Some anime this year impressed me. There are also some anime this year that made me wonder if the creators were high on something. Then, there’s Joshikausei.

There’s an old phrase that you’ve probably heard of before: silence is golden. As a short anime without any dialogue, that’s what Joshikausei represents. The manga itself is also silent without dialogues. So now the real question comes, how would this show work? Was the author high when they made this?

Not quite. It’s actually real simple. The show is about high school girls interacting with daily activities. An easy draw of the show is the character chemistry between the three main girls – Mayumi, Momoko, and Shibumi. The show explores their lifestyle that reminds what normal people would do. Sometimes, characters are represented with fan service in the case of Momoko. The first episode is a testament of this with the suggestive leg shots plastered all over the place. But an important part is to see the character interactions. Joshikausei explores the limits of language and how characters communicate with their expressions. Their emotive performances combined with music carries the mood of each episode. Writing a series like this isn’t easy. For a manga with over 80 chapters of content, it’s not hard to see why. At the end of the day though, it works out just fine and dandy.

Joshikausei isn’t long and ran the risk of looking like a humorless joke. Embedded into the show is simple slice of life storytelling featuring a trio of girls. Every episode is only 3 minutes so it’s an easy way to pass time. The real question is if this silent anime is a golden treat for you. For me, it’s just so-so.

Dem thighs tho! Amirite?

There’s a common misconception that silent films are vacuous works with minimal insights on the human condition. But this could not be further from the truth. Done correctly, the choreography of actors ‘tells’ a story through facial expressions, symbolism, and physical actions. The absence of dialogue, ironically, allows the viewer to focus with greater intent and awareness. Thus, enabling the audience to form a connection with the character(s) that they might not otherwise understand or empathize with. Yet it is paramount that the work has a recognizable theme that the viewer can identify; otherwise, the story becomes a senseless barrage of foolishness.

Joshikausei falls in the latter camp, unfortunately. By placing Momoko in awkward, sexually suggestive positions, the show displays a preference to arouse the viewer. Which is acceptable, if those situations lead to effective comedy. But this is not the case. Perhaps the arbitrary actions of the characters aimed to force the viewer into laughter via the preposterous nature of the situations themselves. For this to be valid, however, the writers should have exhibited a greater degree of audaciousness. No risk, no reward.

At three minutes per episode, you may think Joshikausei is worth a watch (regardless of the quality). Let me tell you something: it is not.

From the studio that brought you Rance 01: Hikari wo Motomete The Animation, Koikishi Purely☆Kiss The Animation, Baku Ane: Otouto Shibocchau zo! The Animation, and Real Eroge Situation! The Animation, comes Studio Seven's latest series entitled Joshi Kausei.

STORY:

It's a story about three “silent” high school girls and the random events that happen to them. You'll watch the girls get involved in some strange skits, such as riding a skateboard, playing with each other's thighs, be concerned about fortunes, and create origami in a family restaurant. And to spice things up, the girls are also involved in some risque situations like wearing wet clothes after a rain shower, having a hand print near one's thigh, and planking over your sleeping daughter. Three-minutes of silent action. It doesn't get more exciting than this folks!

ART/ANIMATIONS:

Be entranced by the stunning plainness of its art and animations, you can appreciate the lack of details from its background art, the rigid movements of non-human objects, and the plain and uninteresting character designs. You can also contemplate as to why Studio Seven's other works, mainly hentai adaptations, appear more refined, animated, and well-produced, considering their previous history, this should be their specialty, unfortunately it doesn't show here.

SOUND:

Watch as the characters move their lips and speak dialogue, but alas, no dialogue is heard because every character live in a world of silence. Watch as the characters create various sound effects from squeaks, squeals, and cries to grunts, sighs, and moans, accompanied with many facial expressions, such as happiness, sadness, anger, embarrassment, shock, and concern. Hear multitudes of background music that successfully fits the mood and tone of the scenes in question. Hear a 30-second ending song called “Silent Days” that is accompanied with moving animations of the girls in different locations through images, playing with water guns, wearing school swimsuits, flying on a rainbow, and looking over the bridge.

CHARACTERS:

Meet our three protagonists: “delinquent” girl, glasses girl, and short-haired girl. “Delinquent” girl is beautiful and cute, has long, light brown hair, carefree, and somewhat unlucky. Glasses girl is calm, composed, and levelheaded, and has long, light blond hair. Short-haired girl acts childlike and is naive, and has short, black hair. Be impressed by the show's lack of character development, its lack of character personalities, and its lack of memorable characters.

ENJOYMENT:

So, sit down and watch as the time quietly goes by, and by the time episode 12 rolls around, you'll have forgotten what the previous episodes were about. But, that's okay, this series isn't suppose to be memorable, its a quick “cash-grab” for a studio whose strengths lies in hentai adaptations.

P.S: Our “delinquent” girl finally says one line of dialogue, and that's “I'm home!” What a fitting line for the end of this series.

One of my favourite short comedy anime is Morita-san wa Mukuchi, where the extremely shy protagonist doesn't speak a word during the whole two seasons and we can only hear her thoughts from time to time. It was a cute gimmick that fueled the lighthearted SoL comedy of that series and made me remember it despite the rather plain "cute girls doing cute things" formula - I'm sure many people would find it boring, but it was well-crafted and relaxing.

Here, the silent cast is not a gimmick. It's a pointless hindrance that adds nothing of value to the whole setup - there are obvious places where dialogue takes place, but we don't hear it. The gags are mostly piss-poor and don't utilize the lack of dialogue in any interesting way. Fanservice is weird and obnoxious, with the opening episode (!) missing the mark in a particularly spectacular manner. Characters are boring cliches. There's nothing even remotely interesting or memorable here, and the humour doesn't land more often than not. I've seen worse shows, but I'm not sure I ever saw one this pointless - truly a waste of time.

Sometimes I just don’t really mind watching a show with a low rating if it contains some cute characters. I watched this preparing for the worst but honestly, it wasn’t all that bad.

Joshikausei is a gag anime in mostly silent film style, but there are occasionally non-verbal sounds such as laughter, gasps, etc. Each episode is about 3 minutes long and you can finish the whole thing in about half an hour.

Story: 3/10. Each episode is 3 minutes long so there is not much story to speak of, but surprisingly each episode at least attempts to set up certain scenarios for each gag. They even brief touched on some backstories for each character. The story still has no substance whatsoever, but considering how short the run time is, plus the fact that there was no dialogue, it’s not the worst thing I’ve ever seen.

Art: 5/10. While it’s nothing amazing, shows this short often have terrible animation made with no effort which is not the case here. The character designs are cute as well.

Sound: 5/10. I felt that the silent film approach was interesting and unique. The addition of non-spoken sounds also helps to express the characters’ emotions better. Honestly jokes can be better sometimes without the tsukkomi unnecessarily butting in. The music is forgettable though.

Characters: 3/10. The characters are somewhat unique and the show does a decent job of giving each character interesting quirks through animation and grunts alone. But you just can’t get very far without any dialogue. In the end the characters are rather bland and we don’t really get to know much about them.

Enjoyment: 4/10. I wanted something I can watch with my brain turned off and it delivers. There was at least one joke that was pretty funny and that’s all I can ask for.

Overall: 4/10. I was expecting a complete snooze fest but it was honestly better than I expected. The length of the episode keeps it from getting too boring and you’re gonna finish it in half an hour anyway. I don’t necessarily recommend this to anyone but it’s not a bad watch if you really have nothing to do.

Also the ED is actually pretty good!
