Perhaps I'm just biased towards shows about people living their lives, but this one felt different and better to me. It's a short series, with the episodes being around 3 minutes, so it's great if you want to get committed to an anime without spending a hilariously large amount of time on it.
Although the comedy is a bit dry, it does a great job of conveying the life of a retail worker, and on top of that, the ending theme (or opening?) is absolutely great.

Not much else to say about this anime. If you're looking for something short and silly and you want a great little song to go along with it, Sore dake ga Neck is your show.

It's hard to describe what That is the Bottleneck is about, and maybe it’s one of those pieces of media that it’s better to just jump in without not much knowledge of it. I didn’t know anything before watching this show as I stumbled upon it by pure chance, I was just looking for a short-length anime and simply found it. In the past, I found Skull-face Bookseller Honda-san and it didn’t disappoint.

That is the Bottleneck is not as good or funny as Honda-San, however, it is nonetheless an interesting journey through the point of view of different characters through its short runtime, both in length and number of episodes. What got me from the very first episode is its dry humor and not in-your-face jokes, one of its strengths is that its comedy doesn't have obvious queues or telegraphs, instead, we got a lot of well-written phrases here and there.

Characters are somewhat disposable, but because of this, each episode it’s like a surprise box where you don’t know what are you going to get but whatever you get it’ll be good. These characters were surprisingly relatable and charm, they also feel pretty real in their way, the setup of a convenience store works wonderfully with them.

The anime has some sort of build-up which has an interesting payoff that I can see how someone would not like, however, I do think it goes pretty well with the show’s themes and humor. For such a short anime with so little run-time, it also has a lot to say, stuff like relationships between people both friendly or romantic, and even work relationships. It maybe isn’t a masterpiece and probably it won’t change anyone’s life but it is a very enjoyable and rewatchable anime that is easy to recommend to anyone who’s just looking to have a nice time.
