In modern times, where virtual reality is becoming increasingly popular, more and more anime studies are daring the "POV" step. Already in 2018, we saw a masterpiece with Opantsu (or let us say the name full in English: I Want You To Make a Disgusted Face and Show Me Your Underwear) that the world has never seen before. The long-awaited successor Opantsu 2 followed one year later (you can check out my review for this one as well). Their faces and underwear will surely be remembered by many of us, how can we forget such a divine sight. The current situation with the pandemic is terrible, but fortunately we are experiencing a real miracle from the anime studio "DMM pictures", previously only worked in dubious works such as "Domestic na Kanojo" or "Kanojo, Okarishimasu". But their latest work "Dogeza de Tanondemita" gives mankind faith and hope for a better year 2021.

Let’s take a look at the English name of the anime first: “I Tried Asking In Dogeza”. Many of you probably had the same questioning face as I did: What is "Dogeza" (土 下座)? After weeks of research I was able to find out that there is also an English term for this: "Kowtow". This information was of course very interesting, but this word didn't mean anything to me either. Thanks to the help of this anime, I was able to find out the long-awaited meaning: Dogeza is an element of traditional Japanese etiquette where people gets down on their knees, places their hands on the ground, arches their back, and presses their forehead to the ground in the space between their hands (AMEN and very hard to learn). You do all the difficult moves just to show the highest respect to the other person and how serious your request is. You really hope they make your wish come true.

Why was all that information necessary? Because in this anime you are the MC (with the name Suwaru Doge) and do “Kowtow” with an urgent request every time. Fortunately, the plot doesn't require 200 IQ to understand it. In every episode (which only lasts 3 minutes, 30 seconds of it is the outro) you are faced with a different girl in another environment. Sometimes your teacher is in front of you, at other times you are in the library or even when refueling you only have THAT thing in mind. Some of them have bigger OPPAIs, for others the famous saying "Flat is Justice" by Confucius applies. It doesn’t matter, you are cultured enough to know all boobs are great. BUT STOP! Breasts alone are not enough of course and so you decide in four of the twelve moments that you prefer to see their underwear. Since it has already worked out at “Opantsu”, the goal will be accomplished here as well for sure. In the end, only the result counts and you passed that with bravur in every episode!

The art style is very beautiful in general, you can see and recognize all the important shapes. The exciting scenes are also highlighted by a special background effect. BUT I think we all agree in this point: We want to see uncensored OPPAIs and PANTSUs. So why is there a crappy, green mini version of us that obscures everything. That of course takes the fun out of it.

Nowadays you can hardly find a more motivating song than the ED "DOGEZA! Do get that!" which is played after each short episode. The music gives you strength and the belief you can manage everything no matter how difficult the path might be. The lyrics are super easy to learn and should be a mandatory song at school, college or work to keep you motivated. With a good "Dogeza" everything is possible!

A masterpiece is no use if it is not enjoyable. As an enthusiastic fan of the VR world, I already had fun watching "Opantsu" and "One Room". You don’t even need an expensive device here, so you can spend all the money for your next waifu figure. Anime shouldn't be compared directly with each other, but there is no way around "Opantsu" in this case. If you are cultured enough, do yourself a favor and watch that first if you haven't. There are no rational explanations why you should watch all of these. But as we all now, men are simple. You can see eight pair of boobs and four panties here, what else do you want more? The only thing that triggers you very hard is the censoring.

Finally, I really can only recommend this anime to every cultured person. But do yourself a favor and don't try the "Kowtow"-thing in real life, the probability of ending up at the police station is much higher than being successful.
We have to learn more about the "Dogeza" culture, so the only question left is:

Luckily an OVA is already announced for the isekai-world but when will we get the second season?

Chase your dreams, even if it means sacrificing your dignity. Who knows? You may just achieve them. Dreams take sacrifice, and you can't expect them to be handed to you without you making the effort to reach them.

I'd say that was the biggest takeaway from Dogeza de Tanondemita, a short anthology which presents this teaching in a quite unconventional fashion.

In all seriousness however, I don't understand why this received such a low rating. What did you expect? I would assume the title of the show alone already set its expectations from the audience and what it was trying to achieve and become. I understand that we all critique in different ways but personally, the main factor that influences my criticisms is based on what the piece wants or presents itself to be, and how well the piece does in achieving those expectations, compared to others in its genre.

Dogeza de Tanondemita already showed what it was meant to be from the start, and did not present itself to be some kind of extensive story, or deep plot with character development. It was concieved for the purpose of satisfying our degeneracy, but what I didn't expect was how comedic the whole endeavor was.

Other animes which aim to do the same such as IyaPan (I Want You To Make a Disgusted Face and Show Me Your Underwear) achieve its goal but sets its expectations from the start. Yes, the whole show centered around this premise, and was satisfactory in how it achieved its goal. There was no deviation from its central premise and did its job. I would personally give IyaPan a 7 (I'm quite the harsh grader), since it was no nonsense and stayed on premise, and also delivered on audience expectations. What sets Dogeza apart however, and why I give it a 9, is because of how the show was able to stay on subject and achieve its goal, but also enhance its charm due to its comedic factor.

Gintoki himself voiced Doge (our main protagonist), and he played the role of our main protagonist very, very well. His lines were really funny and how he synergized with the narrarator was my favorite part of it all. Only critique however is that by being in the POV setting, we would expect that it would be just like IyaPan or One Room wherein we are the protagonist, and we are meant to be making our own lines and playing our own scenario. His voice lines break the immersion and defeat the purpose of the setting, however, other than that I don't really mind it and found that while he breaks the purpose, his comedic prescence overshadows it and really makes for an enjoyable experience.

Those few minutes were very enjoyable, and I would honestly watch a full 24 min show with just this. But then again, we would run out of archetypes wouldn't we. Which brings me to the girls in the show, the main attraction and hook of the show altogether, and why we want to devote time to this experience in the first place.

The female (*ahem, mostly) protagonists change every episode, and present a different story and setting every instance, with our main character Doge requesting a different sight every episode (rotating between oppai or opantsu). I found the girls to be archetypal (which is to be expected from this genre so that isn't a minus point for my criteria). I did find all of them to be amusing however, and seeing their reactions was also very amusing, and personally, how those subtleties are presented is what amplifies the overall experience. Their character design and overall artsyle of the show is also okay, from what I remember, I don't think a majority of the backgrounds were but much effort into, but that's not what we're here for anyway huh. The girls were drawn decently, nothing bad, but nothing too special. The end card however, is the best piece of art I can refer to, and every episode it dosen't dissapoint, so please make sure to watch until after the end credits.

I personally didn't feel the end song, and usually skipped it. It was high energy but it wasn't anything worth mentioning. Sorry, it isn't an Overflow ED level banger. Sound quality however was fine and voice acting was done very, very well. Not just by Gintoki and the Narrarator, but all of the girls as well played their roles quite well.

I'd like to end by saying that shows of these genre are usually alleles of the same gene, or rather, variations of art based on the same template. The premise at its core for all of the shows in its genre is simple, show pantsu and it will be recieved in different ways. For IyaPan it was asking and also making the girls show a disgusting face. For Dogeza, it's to literally get on your knees, and sacrifice yourself to get it.

What sets apart these kind of shows however, and as I said earlier, is how you can amplfy that canvas and give us something interesting, and Dogeza does this by adding to the show its comedic value.

TLDR: Show surpassed my initial expectations and is very enjoyable, it won't be the self insert theme like you're used to, but you'll still love its charm either way.

9/10

Note: I'm comparing to IyaPan alot and that's because that's what I would consider to be its best comparison to. The people who will watch this have likely watched a few episodes of IyaPan and will likely understand alot of the comparisons I made.
