(This is only my 3rd review!) This was one of the first yaoi I watched, I jumped into the deep end with Gravitation and wanted to experience something...well, shall we say more mature.

This, sadly was not it.

There was no tangible plot that I could spot, a guy sees a lovely young boy on the street and follows him around. Well, that's the impression it gave me. It creeped me out, there was no sudden revelation of feelings, no shocked faces and proclamations of &quot;Did you see that guy? I have to find out more about him.&quot;

It was, essentially, an excuse to watch two guys go at it.

And if I could have SEEN the two guys go at it I wouldn't be complaining so much about this anime.

I had no idea mens genitals also came in a clear colour, with extra squelch!

If there were some form of feelings involved, an angsty plot line (C'mon people, that's the only reason some of you watch yaoi!) then I could more than likely overlook the fact that the intimate scenes look like something a homophobic person would draw who'd only heard stories of man parts.

The characters were likable, but nothing new, just the dominant, cold eyed Seme and the feminine, innocent Uke.

~~*OVERALL*~~ If you're looking for your first yaoi then this is perfect, it's not too overwhelming and the characters are a traditional mold of Seme and Uke.

If you're a fan of the genre and like more plot, character fleshing and general scenes of saucyness then I'm gonna go ahead and point you in another direction. This isn't something for the &quot;experienced&quot; fan.

I hated this one. Really, I did. It is probably the most shallow yaoi I've seen in my life. No, scratch that. It is the most shallow yaoi I've ever seen in my life.
