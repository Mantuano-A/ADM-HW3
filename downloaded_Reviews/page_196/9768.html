Okay, so I have played through the entire game and decided to drop the anime after the 4th episode. Spoiler free review ahead:

Short answer: Just play the game, it's the exact same story but the art, music, combat is better in the game.

Long answer:

I was excited for this anime because the game looked really fun, I bought the game day 1 and played through the entire thing immediately. The game isn't TOO hard and it isn't that grindy for an RPG either. The anime itself feels lazy (for Sunrise standards), and poorly implemented. For an anime based off a game with really hype fights, this is just an insult. Sure the game will take you longer than watching the anime (30-40 hours from start to finish), but if you start now, you'll probably finish it before the entire series even finishes in 2 months. When I saw the lazy white and red backdrops during fight scenes, I knew this anime would be bad, but I was hoping it'd do better. It doesn't. There were two reasons I wanted to watch the anime after playing the game; 1) To see if the stories were going to be different (They aren't. Not in the slightest), and 2) To see hype fights animated by Sunrise (They aren't hype, not even close. They're lazy and last 10 seconds).

Normally I don't give scores to my dropped animes since it's not fair to give a score to something that you haven't finished but this is an exception. I've only seen the first 4 episodes but I've played the game so this review will be in relation to the game.

I won't say much to the story since I haven't actually finished Kasane's story and I haven't done Yuito's story. I'll just say that it's actually pretty interesting as the story progresses though it does feel a bit rushed.

Art-wise, it's very mid; probably less than, actually. During the hand-animated scenes, it's enough for me to not get turned off immediately. The real problem is during the CGI action sequences. To put it simply, they're boring. Even without comparing it to the game's action pieces, the animation is just boring to watch. In-game, the combat is fast and dynamic, and fun to watch. Even after 20+hours, I still find all the animations really entertaining. It seems that there was no thought put into the action choreography and decided that just having Yuito throw one box at the other is good enough. They could've even used the attack animations from the game as a basis or just straight up copy-pasted it and it would've been so much more entertaining to watch.

The characters in the are also very mid. With 6 recurring characters, it's hard to fully explore their characters in the span of 12-25 episodes. With such little time to fully explore these characters, you hardly get the chance to form any kind of opinion of them. This is something that the game does really well. In order to get better abilities from the other characters, you are forced to interact with them, giving you the chance to find out more about their personalities, giving you the chance to find some sort of attachment.

I tried really hard to like Scarlet Nexus but I was just severely disappointed. Just play the game.

I have played both perspectives of the game, and as of today (9/8/21), I've seen 9 episodes.
Story:
I absolutely love how the story is basically the same as the game, where it isn't a completely different plotline, since most animes tend to stray from the original. I'll admit, it's strange watching a third of the game go by in two episodes, but considering the anime will only be 12 episodes, I think the pacing is just fine - they emphasize the important parts.
Art:
I know a lot of people dislike the CGI, but it is one of my favorite parts, and I think it's neat, because it definitely gives it a videogame-esque feel. Just like the videogame opening, the character animations could use a little more work, but they still make the characters look good in the right scenes. The backgrounds are based off the game maps, and it makes me really nostalgic.
Sound:
They take sound effects from the game, so that's pretty cool. However, I don't remember hearing any songs from the videogame soundtrack, so that's rather disappointing. At least the opening Red Criminal is excellent! Not only is this anime in Japanese, but they are dubbing it into English and are bringing back all but three of the VAs from the videogame. So excited!
Character:
I'll admit - this is one of the shortcomings of Scarlet Nexus, both gamewise and animewise. There isn't a lot of character development, and the backstories don't really get interesting until much later. The characters mostly have one specific strength/weakness that defines them, so they can be kind of bland at times, but I guess the game's bonding events can make up for what's missing in the anime.
Enjoyment:
With Scarlet Nexus being one of my favorite games ever, this anime was something I definitely had to see. So far, I've really enjoyed it, especially the way it shows both routes at once, where in the game, you're only see one perspective at a time.
Overall:
Scarlet Nexus isn't the best anime out there, but I think this is really eye opening for anyone who wants to play the game but can't, or someone who simply wants a rundown of what goes on. I love seeing the differences between the anime and the game; it makes me smile and laugh, and it also breaks my heart - the anime nailed the emotion factor! This might not be for a hardcore anime watcher, but it's definitely a treasure for gamers who want more! I hope you enjoy it as much as I do!

Ah, What do I have to say about "Too much exposition: The Anime?"

Jokes aside, the fact that this anime has non-stop exposition is at the core of what makes this a thoroughly dreadful show. The other major problem is the writers' tendency to write in plot twists all the time. Some other reviewers have said this is rushed. I agree. The show keeps adding more and more to the world building with each episode, but does not take the time to make any of it matter. A small but densely packed world is much more enjoyable than a huge empty wasteland of cliches.

It's weird because the anime does have pretty decent animation and sound. It's the story and characters that condemn it. To give an example, right in episode 3 we see the start of a coup d'etat. But I haven't been with this character for long! I know next to nothing about this society or the character's relationship with it. Why should I care about this? Later on the show kills off major characters. But again, I don't know these people! Why do I care that they died?

The show does not take the effort to build meaningful relationships or bring this society to life. So when it automatically starts condemning it, I have no opposition to it. Perhaps this makes more sense in the game this show was adapted from. If, as other reviewers point out, the anime could just have taken things slower and given us some filler content, I may actually have been at the edge of my seat.

The fact that I want to keep watching the show despite this simply shows how much of a wasted opportunity this is. It reminds me a lot of Seraph of the End, a show which I enjoyed a lot more. I hope it picks up later on in the season, but I'm not holding my breath.
