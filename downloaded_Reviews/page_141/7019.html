The protagonist, Hiragi Seiichi, is fat, and at the start of the episode we see him being bullied for it. This, in of itself, is not problematic. THIS SHOW IS A PARODY

AND A GOOD ONE AT THAT

So you can immediately ignore all of the people who are bombing this show in reviews and on the forums that are treating this like an ordinary isekai. We've even got people comparing this to Arifueta (a poorly written chuni's power fantasy). This show at no point ever wanted to be taken seriously, and never tried to be taken seriously.

How is it that, the LN readers got this and is reflected on the LN page, but so many people on the anime page didn't get this?


Yes, it is a parody, the show is a comedy. All of those people are going, huh duh, generic plot, generic charachters, generic stage setting etc. Yes, thats the whole f**** point. That is what a parody is. You take a story and use that as the setup for your gag. You deliberately exaggerate stuff for comedic effect (and this show does it in abundance). You go off on tangents because you can. Are you going to tell me that Konosuba did not feature a generic Isekai plot, and had no tropes or cliche whatsoever? A parody takes the tropes, it takes the cliches and it turns them into gags.

/end rant and onto the main review.

The premise:
You've got mr MC who is generic bullied kid in class, fat, stinks and has no appealing characteristics (even his status window considers him, and i quote "barely human"). The only thing he has going for him is that he is 'innocent' and nice?. The class gets transported to the other world, but he is left out without a group. The god transporting them takes pity on him and gives him plot armour. He somehow gets accidentally transported into a the most dangerous forest around and must figure out how to survive.

Everytime he beats a monster, he acquires their skills, and a backstory which is a minature gag, often making light of one trope or another.

Art 7/10
The art is solid, and works in concert with the music. There's nothing overly special about the art, but the mix of artstyles and even dropping into pixelated art is notable.

Music 7/10
The music is solid and makes it completely obvious that this show is a comedy. It is comedy music, and works well with what is on screen. There is nothing overtly memorable, but nor does it need to be.

Characters 7/10
There is nothing special about the characters. However, they are purposefully unique. The writer has taken a trope and made that character the definition of that trope. We've got Mr Abs. We've got calamity girl. Most notable is how the demon army is portrayed (as a circus troupe instead of an actual threat). No character is ever taken too seriously.

Story 7/10
The story is generic, but it doesn't need to be a new dramatic story. If we look at one of the greatest parodies of all time on this genre, Konosuba. Would you say anything about Konosuba's story is not generic? What made Konosuba was not the initial premise, it was the comedy within the premise.

I'm writing this review basically because I haven't seen anyone who has also read the novel do it, and I wanted to be the first to review the series as an adaptation, instead of a simple anime.

With that said…

The anime "Shinka no mi" is a cheap, rushed and a bad adaptation in general.

I'm just guessing, but it seems that, whoever is in charge of this, is trying to compress 5 volumes in a short season anime. That's why I said "rushed". For someone who read the novel, the anime is just jumping from one joke to the next one, leaving any sort of character development behind.

I should say this, when I saw the first and even during second episode I was laughing out loud; however, by third and now fourth episode I finally understood the problem with the story of this anime: there is none. Shinka no Mi is a parody of the isekai genre, especially mocking about the OP-MC trend. But by leaving out the character development, the anime became a generic comedy isekai. In that aspect it reminds me to Arifureta (yes, I'm doing the comparison too); though Shinka doesn't take itself seriously.

Now, about the "cheap" and "bad" part: It is noticeable from the very opening! The abuse of close ups, generic panning of static or looped cuts, and a total lack of dynamism in what should be accition scenes, this anime has it everything to be called "bad animation". And also the fact that is done in 2D makes me think (yes, is just my appraisement) that the budget is lower than that of Arifureta and Kumo Desuga (in its last 6 episodes), which made those mistakes in 3D.

Overall, this anime is a 5 when it comes to an adaptation, and a 3 if you don't have the background of the novel. Why? Because the novel is a parodic comedy and, so far, the jokes are all there. And obviously, without the context you won get the joke; that's why it has a better rating for those who have read the novel and a lower grade for those who doesn't.

The Fruit of Evolution is an anime with no story at all. It borrows from isekai tropes and sprinkles a little bit of absurd humor to create something new. The animation is not great, without being bad either.

As far as isekai go, this is a 'wish fulfillment fantasy of a social reject that gets transported into another world with game elements' kind of isekai. One of many. It comes from a web novel and suffers from very amateurish writing. The basic premise is, as written in the title, that a self-insert gets transported into a forest with a fruit of evolution that transforms him, the rest is improvised along the way. Plot elements, like the fruit of evolution, get discarded as the anime does not appear to know what to do with them.

What will make the anime good, bad -- or very cringe if you were to ask me -- is its humor. The author opted for the no-brain absurd short memory humor the internet/amateur authors are known for. Humor is apparently the only tool the author had to make a story (or in this instance a work without a story) interesting.
As a result, the world is dumbed down for the main character, leading to strange 'humorous' situations. The main character is a simple-minded bland superficial psychopath. I assume he was made from the average internet user and meant to be his avatar in the original web novel story. This also leads to very cringe involuntary humor.

Animation is generic. It just appears to animate a manga and add sounds. I find myself blaming the author because the work of the studio is just that unremarkable.

It is hard to say if the anime is serious or makes fun of its genre, itself, or the viewer. There is entertainment value and I watched it with the same curiosity I would watch a YouTuber-react-to-a-reaction-video video or a movie written by and starring Steven Seagal.
