Brave Express Might Gaine is the first of the 7 brave/yuusha shows i ever saw back in early 2018 and one of my main gateways into the world of Super robot anime. Going into this based on clips i saw way back, i was expecting a very fun and exicting thrillride of an anime, and i can safely say that it mostly delivered on that, while also becoming one of my all time favourite mecha anime.

(WARNING: REVIEW CONTAINS SPOILERS)


Story: 6
The story follows Senpuuji Maito, a rich young man who is the CEO of his deceased father's company and on mission to rid his home City (and the world i guess) from evil, as part of a final request from his father before he and Maito's Mother were killed in a supossed accident. To do this, he'll need the super robot Might Gaine and the rest of the Brave Express team to thwart the ambitions of evil with help from his best friend Hamada kun.

Might Gaine follows the monster (or rather villian) of the week formula, with a new foe every ep from each of the 4 reccuring antagonists, always defeated in the same way. Things do get a little more interseting once the rival character 'Ace no Joe' arrives after 10+ eps, posing a greater challenge to the main protagonist and raising the stakes of each battle. There's also a reccuring plot to do with Ace no Joe trying to discover his Father's Murderer

The main Villian of the show the 'lord of Darkness' to me was pretty disapointing. Only fully revealed around the last few eps, we find out it's actually an evil God, and is responsible for the death of Maito's parents and Ace no Joe's Father. It's motives for such were pretty vague, to do with being part of a Game and outright stating that he created humans just to be apart of it. Whilst i found the Villian quite lackluster, the final encounter with it and it's minions resulted in a very intense and exicting climatic battle, all wrapped up by a very Happy and somewhat questionable ending. Overall the weakest element of the show for me.



Art and Animation/Sound: 7
I really like the art design for the main characters of Maito and Sally, although i think it's a little inconsistent. I presume the main Art/character designer for the show was also responsible for the designs of 'Magical Knight Rayearth, with very sharply drawn faces and very detailed eyes, i'd say this is the prefered look for me. However, some episode will have the characters look like they came out one of the previous yuusha shows like Fighbird or Exaizer, with more rounded faces and less detailed smaller eyes. This wasn't really a big problem for me tho, as it's something i only really noticed later on. The actual animation (outside the Final attacks and combination scenes) is okay at best and nothing to write home about.

I really love the music for Might Gaine, whilst I think the OP is ok, Might Gaine's Gattai/Combination theme sounds awesome, very upbeat and gets me exicted to watch the mecha fight begin. The Great Might Gaine theme was is also pretty epic. The 2 EDs are pretty good too, with ED 2 being my favourite. Most of the background music reminds me of old Noir flims, which i really think sets the mood for the show. Can't really say to much in terms of sound design, so i'll say it's Ok.


Characters: 7
Whilst not the most complex, i enjoyed majority of the characters whenever they were on screen, even the villians. I thought Shogun Mifune was pretty funny, with him being one of the 4 reccuring Bad Guys, his goal was to revive the fedual era of japan, resulting in some pretty wacky moments and funny dialouge. Another Villian 'Catherine Vuitton', i thought more of the opposite of, finding her voice and character very annoying i didn't enjoy alot of episodes with her in it (except maybe the Natto episode)

My favourite Character however, had to be Maito. One of my favourite mecha anime pilots, whilst a pretty basic character with a pretty basic motivation, his views about nature and taking care of the world was a suprising trait. Along with cool design and a Hot blooded, powerful voice from legendary Seiyuu 'Hiyama Nobuyuki', he was a joy to watch. Other characters like his butler and best friend Hamada kun were good and fit into their roles fine, i will say tho that Sally chan is pretty cute and Maito's grandfather had a pretty funny episode about 'Natto'.


Mecha: 8
Might Gaine has in my opinion, some of the best the designs in the Yuusha series, Might Gaine and Might Kaiser were my favourites and the auxiliary ones like Battle Bomber and Guard Diver looking pretty cool aswell. With outstanding designs from Shoji Kawamori and smooth animation from Masami Obari (parricularly in the OP) might gaine's mecha did not disappoint. The combination scenes also looked very crisp, fluid and full of that sweet mechanical detail, truly a treat for the eyes.


Overall: 8
I think Might Gaine was a pretty fun experience, not doing anything new or suprising in the genre but still managing to be engaging with it's great music and lovable goofy characters, it mostly kept me entertained. Fairely underrated in my opinion, this is definetly worth checking out for super robot lovers, anyone curious about 90s mecha and earlier yuusha shows before Gaogaigar. For anyone else....try it hopefuly you'll enjoy

I think it wouldn’t be incorrect to say Hiyama Nobuyuki is one of the best actors to come out of the 90’. His range is deceptively limited, as while his voice doesn’t change much in between most of his roles, the sheer amount of characters it allows him to play can be absurd. He can go from hot-blooded to cold and calculating to creepy all in a matter of a second. Thus, I decided to make this month the Hiyama month, what with his birthday coming up. And what better way to begin than with the show that put him on the map: Yuusha Tokkyu Might Gaine. So, how is it like? Let’s find out.

Story:

The show… how do I put it… it’s what happens when you take an old-school super robot show and put it in the 90’. That’s really the best way to describe it. It has it all, an episodic format, a lighthearted tone (Bar some dark moments here and there) and a bunch of quirky villains who blur the line between ineffective and actually pretty menacing. There really isn’t much to say really, it’s a pretty basic show in the grand scheme of things.

Of course, that’s not necessarily a bad thing. In general, now that I think about it, I don’t think there was any episode I particularly disliked. Pretty much all of them were at the very least decent and relatively enjoyable. There’s also a well-done mystery element which, while it does take its time to kick in, is relatively well-done. Also, this show has this weird charm in that it’s sorta aware it’s ridiculous. It’s like if you took a bunch of classic comic book tropes and put them in a blender. The show knows this and embraces those tropes, which given how mecha Anime had devolved into nothing but angst towards the 80’ and 90’, it’s nice to have a show that returns to the genre’s idealistic roots.

Sadly, it also brings along all of the problems with that classic era of storytelling. While I will admit that I didn’t dislike any of the episodes, none of them wowed me either. All of them settled on being just good and not much else. However Might Gaine’s biggest issue is the fact that she show doesn’t have much uniqueness in it all things considered. Some might say the same about Guren Lagann and a show I’ll be looking at later on this Month, but those shows not only played tribute to those classic series, but they also had their own identity and applied those tropes with more modern storytelling techniques. Might Gaine is basically a show that should’ve been released back in the 70’, but was released in the 90’ instead. The only time the show starts getting ambitious is in its final episode with the reveal of the Big Bad, and sadly it kinda backfires as it doesn’t have much time to develop that idea.

Characters:

To be fair though, I do like this cast, even if they’re admittedly not the most developed bunch. Maito is more or less a more idealistic and youthful version of Batman. He has a shit ton of money, but he’s also in no way spoiled. He’s one of the nicest guys out there, kind to almost everyone and always believes in the best in people. This isn’t particularly unique, but among a sea of Super Robot pilots that are somewhat jerkish, Maito stands out as one of the most unconditionally kind of them all.

His supporting cast is also decent enough. Sally, his love interest, is a likable enough character for me to like her, and somehow she is able to constantly avoid the idiot ball. His more immediate supporting cast (Izumi, Hamada, Osaka and Aoki) are also pretty decent, with Hamada in particular probably getting the most development of the lot. Even the robots are quirky and lovable, with just cracks a big smile on my face.

The villains are… okay. None of them particularly wowed me, but all of them provided an at least decent challenge. The only ones that I particularly liked were Kochou and Black Might Gaine… both of whom appear for only one episode. Maito’s rival Ace No Joe (Yes, seriously) is perfectly fine, if a bit generic. The big bad Exeve is okay, if equally generic, and his boss Black Noir… honestly I still have no idea what his deal is.

Animation:

The show looks average for the 90’. It’s more or less on a similar level to Sailor Moon (From what I’ve seen of that show): Stock Footage is all Sakuga level gorgeous and whatever isn’t is pretty lame all things considered. Still though, it is mostly consistent, so at least it has that much going for it.

The Mecha:

By contrast, the assortment of mecha is fantastic. The regular Might Gaine looks great, and its companion piece the Might Kaiser looks equally great. Of course, both combine into Great Might Gaine which looks equally awesome, and there are so many other Mechas that I would be here forever if I were to list them all. All I’ll say is that my favorites were Joe’s Robots: Hiryuu and Goryuu, both of which looked great.

Music:

The soundtrack is equally great. Right of the bat we have the amazing opening theme, and many of the insert themes are equally great. Even the background OST was quite nice, with all of it having a classically heroic tone to them. As for the ending themes, I didn’t care much for either of them, but if I had to pick a favorite, it’d be the second ending theme, “Black Diamond”, as “Kiken Na Gold” was a song I’m simply not a fan of.

Voice Acting:

Overall the cast is pretty great. This was the show that kickstarted both Hiyama Nobuyuki and Midorikawa Hikaru’s careers, and while this wouldn’t be their big break (Midorikawa would get it in Slam Dunk a year later and Hiyama got it in the show I’ll be reviewing next) this did serve to give them a good place to start off with. Similarly the rest of the cast is comprised of similarly popular actors from the 90’, like Kikuchi Masami, Amano Yuri, Yanada Kiyoyuki, Yajima Akiko, Matsumoto Yasunori and the Late Great Honda Chieko.

Overall:

Might Gaine isn’t a bad show or anything, it’s just an ultimately unremarkable one. All of the things it aims to do it does it fine, but none of it is done in a particularly great way. It’s by no means a show I wouldn’t recommend watching (So long as it gets a better translation) I wouldn’t tell you to go out of your way to watch either. It’s good, but not great.

Final Score: 7/10
