OH GOLLY GREAT...another smartphone game anime adaptation. Look Japan, I know these are quite popular over there, but if there is some heart in making one, please at least make a decent story/plot a.k.a Princess Connect (from the same season).

What happens if you combine generic game characters and gave them overly boring, tropey and cliche personalities? A male protagonist (more like a support protag I suppose), the childhood friend (who's vying for some love), the ACTUAL support characters, and some very tropey enemies (that are always garnered to the good side), all these is what you get with the generic fantasy package. NGL, this formula is very rampant on 99% of smartphone anime adaptations, whilst the mobile games themselves are just "pick-and-choose/play" characters whilst following the usual plot to level up and such nonsense.

As far as the settings go, an adventurer's guild is most likely the "playing on safe ground" option, because it just showcases the main cast of characters on their merry ways, doing the things they love to do in their spare time a.k.a fighting to earn money, experience and whole lot of nuance. It's an action-adventure for God's sake, and thankfully not a whole lot of Slice-of-Life-ish. The plot however, give or take, just give it away, pretty please. With such bland characters, and I re-emphasize this again: how can you expect at least a decent story plot?

The only thing that draws me in is studio C2C's nice artwork and visuals, but that's more of an enticement that would've been too easy to fool you if not for the overarching plot, which isn't all that interesting to begin with. Same with the music, it's cutesy and peppy, but it absolutely does nothing for me to draw in what this mediocre adaptation to ireness.

This show is just too mind-numblingly boring for the most part, I'd suggest heavily to skip on this, you're not missing out on it much unless you've played the mobile game (which is a sad denomination). The marketing teams for games in Japan, please buck up your efforts.

To quote somebody the best: TL;DW; Its boring AF.

Kibou Company - The Hope Company

Do you like your like boss at work? Do you have to work too much and getting paid too less? Luckily this is not the case in this company, they are the hope for all employees. We have a president (boss) who thinks of precautionary about his employees, a president that you really like.

STORY:
A new boss in a company always means various changes. But how do the employees feel about the new boss? That's the question every Shachou will ask himself. Our main character has the honor to take this place in a company because the old boss (his father) cannot be found. He quickly realizes that it is not easy to run a company. There are many things to watch out for. Above all, in the financially part, the Kibou Company is pretty badly positioned at the beginning. Nevertheless, our boss is a positive person, he is very nice to his employees and they also have fun working under his orders. Together they manage many different and dangerous dungeons, which are often stored with crystals, and those are associated with a lot of income. One day our little group wants to collect so much crystals that they have the opportunity to find the old boss again.
Their journey begins...

CHARACTERS:
In addition to the boss Minato, the employees naturally play a major role. Each of them has their own function in the company. In addition to a secretary, a fighter, a healer, we also have other important positions that are filled. At the beginning with only four employees [three women, one man] (plus boss), the company enriched itself with two more [cute girls]. The seven of them have a very good harmony and they can also master a lot. We also experience a little romance between our boss and his childhood friend Yutoria, who asked him to come as a boss, and she is also an employee of the company. Even if that part is certainly not the focus of the action, a little love is never wrong.

ART:
The most convincing part of all. It is a little strange that our two male people in the company also have long hair and could therefore go through as women, but the characters are still very well animated. It is a colorful anime that uses many colors from the bright area. The dark dungeons are also well represented. The most important thing is, of course, that the female characters are presented in a cute way and this is definitely the case here.
What is extremely cool to look at is the font that is used. It looks like an old font (you can still recognize the letters, of course), but here you notice that a lot of effort was given for this part.

SOUND:
The OP and ED are both solo songs from the seiyuu of the female characters Akari and Yutoria. They are not special, but they give a fitting impression of the anime. Both have sung OP and ED in other anime before and their voices are nice to hear. As in almost all anime, the OP is rather loud and happy, while the ED is a bit quieter and more relaxed. Other noices in the anime (such as battle swords during a fighting scene etc.) are present, but relatively average.

ENJOYMENT:
Even though the story is progressing very slow, this aspect didn't bother me. I had fun watching this anime. There is too little said about the characters in my opinion and you'd like to know more about them and the old boss, but I still like the characters. As already mentioned, I find the long hair of the two men a bit strange, but after a while you get used to it and don't mix it with the cute girls in the anime. In addition to these things, this anime shows us how a boss can get along well with his employees. Nobody should feel like the robots and monsters in this anime: only overtime, too much work, no overtime pay etc., everyone should feel like the employees in the Kibou Company: happy and satisfied.

I have already read many negative comments about this anime and I can only agree on many points. I never heard of the mobile game of this, so I only referred to the anime. Nevertheless, everyone should take a quick look at the first episode whether this anime suits them or not. In terms of type, it is somewhat similar to Konosuba, Priconne and something like that. Certainly not nearly as good, but you can relax quite well and experience a nice little company and its adventures!

President, It Is Time To Stop!
A young man stands in front of the tremendous gates and carefully reads the message of his father. This place is the center of the world, which is also called Gatetoria. Before that, however, there had been nothing but vast wastelands. At that moment, a Goddess decided to see the human world and summoned those giant gates, which began to appear in different places of the world. With the summon of the gates, people also discover a new source of energy. Later on, this resource helps develop science and becomes the reason for a huge interest of many adventuring guilds. The series focuses on Yutoria, who decides to invite a new friend to join one of the adventuring guilds. Minato, the friend who joins the guild hopes to hear a declaration of love from Yutoria, however, what he gets is a request to become the president of the guild and he cannot say 'no'. Will Minato manage to be a good president?

Shachibato! President, It's Time for Battle! is an anime that advertises the game with the same name. It is difficult to give any serious assessment of a series that overuses the idea of characters that go in another world, form a guild, guild members start to quests to earn money, and so on and so forth. And the problem here is not that it overuses cliché, the problem here is that the series refuses to even do the bare minimum required to at least be average. It just looks like that the only aim of this anime is to promote the game: it shows some unnecessary mechanics, as well as jokes that are hardly funny.

Characters are, however, not as bad as the storyline, but are still average at best. Minato, the series protagonist, is asked by his friend to be the president of the Hope Company and lead its adventurers. This is the last thing he wants to do, because he is only interested in the girl that invites him to join the company and become its president. Another reason for him not wanting to become the company's president is that he does not have a high level. And it would be absolutely normal, have this show decided to choose the romcom route, it would be a pretty okay romcom, if it were one. But what you have is a male protagonist, who is supposed to be the president, the character, who makes this series interesting and is the reason people watch the series, here, however, even supporting characters are more interesting than the male protagonist, the president Minato.

Overall, this anime is nothing but a cash grab. Though, I do wonder if this series will be of much help for the game, since it does a bad job thinking about the viewer. So why would that viewer want to play that game? There is, however, only one good thing about this series and that is animation, it is quite good, I must say. Would I recommend this show to anyone? Definitely not. This series fails to be even average, it is worse than average.

After reading a lot of negative reviews out there, I feel like I had to write this review, to actually gives some positive response for this anime. At first glance, sure, it does looks like your typical isekai anime. I also felt like the style of the anime kinda copy paste from other isekai anime out there. Sure, if I had to say, there are other isekai anime out there is better than this one. At the end of the day, people are free to express their opinion on this anime so this one's mine.

Yes, sometimes, isekai anime setting is quite weird but that's what makes an isekai, what makes an isekai different. This series talks about Makoto takes over his dad's company becomes the president of Kibou company. At first I was skeptical, maybe this anime has a lot of actions? a lot of fantasy? a lot of adventure? but it doesn't do much. Yup, this anime doesn't do much if I had to say in a nutshell. The story is meh and at some point, predictable, the characters, are cute other than that I felt natural. Just your typical president-employee kind of relationship except it's anime. It would be really great if Minato and Yutoria actually starts to see each other as love interests or Akari actually starts to fall in love with Minato, or better yet, maybe we get to know whether Raiba actually has feelings for Akari or not. Why Valmi has the same vibe as Guide tho? I kinda felt like they might be sisters? Maybe. This anime, has a lot of story potential but they limit it to just that, a president-employee relationship, competing with other companies for more "kirakuri". Heck, Servant x Service did a better job at that.

Since this anime "doesn't do much", it kinda felt like this anime lacks the plot, lacks the story and lacks action (being it in isekai) but still, that's what makes this anime good for me. Maybe a lot of reviews I've read actually trying to search for something worth to watch and fail to find one in this anime, because this anime is better watching if you don't take it too seriously. Yes, this anime if you don't take it too seriously, that's the charm. This anime is just perfect if you want to sit back on your couch, hit a random anime to watch, lose your stress and enjoying your night resting. I'm that one guy that actually enjoys anime like this, anime that you know, sit back and grab a snack and laugh along. Because at some point, you do need a rest from plot-twist, high suspense drama, hardcore action and anything related to those kinds of anime.
