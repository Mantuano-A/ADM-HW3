First of all, why would you devide the class into two groups, when only one is shown? Seriously, this OVA has TWO episodes and for not EVEN A SINGLE SECOND, the other group is shown, other than in the scenes before dividing.
Most of the screen time belongs to Todoroki, Bakugou and Midoriya.. oh wow, oh wow - how unexpected. The plot is basically a copy of the other OVA's training course plot, which actually is pretty dissapointing, since there are a lot of things they could show us, like dorm life, just some hanging out in the city.

Art 2/10:
Oh boy oh boy, that was some bad art. Deku's face changed like few times in a row, the quality overall was pretty bad. It seemed like the whole budget went to Todoroki, that didn't even had a lot of scenes in those two episodes. The animation was usual BNHA animation with even more flaws.

Sound 3/10:
Well, in those episodes they did not fail on the sound effects, but we did not hear any new tracks. Just the usual You Say Run and other stuff. 'Wish they could add some new ones one day.

Character 1/10:
Godness, that was the worst part of the OVA. Everyone felt... kind of dumb. I could continue for hours, but if i had to point out who actually did something, it was probably Bakugou. And he didn't even do much except continuing the mission.

Enjoyment 1/10:
Honestly, i think i wasted those 40 minutes for this. Watching this was a pain.

Yes, I am someone who is easy to please when it comes to anime, and watching this ONA after season 4, while felt a little odd due to the timeline, it was still a blast.

What I like the most about this series is how it manages to be so intense and action-packed even without villains or their backstories. This formula is repeated even here, and the result thus is also impressive as expected. Think of it as just a made-for-television action flick with enough superhero action, peril and challenges that can make anyone's day, and there you have it.

The pacing was good, the animation looked finely done and the voice acting was impressive as usual. The mission it showcases is actually plausible and has a suspenseful feel to it given the backdrop. Might remind you of some Hollywood disaster flicks and the premise might not seem very original, but that's where this ONA's success lies: to turn used ideas into something that befits the show's universe in its own unique style.

Plus, we also get some much-needed character moments (a lot of you probably know what I'm getting at) and the action feels different because it's more about fighting the forces of nature than rivals. So expect intensity, fun and loads of fun. If you love the show, you're gonna love this one. It's just as simple as that.
