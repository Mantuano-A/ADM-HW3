It pains me to say this, but Bijukubo is one of the most mediocre hentai I've ever seen. It's also increadibly inconsistent in its mediocrity, which is the part I find most interesting. So let's end the foreplay and dive right in!

Bijukubo is a two-part OVA by Y.O.U.C, which is a studio that has produced hentai of varying quality. I generally like thier work, but that has more to do with nostalgia than anything objective.

I found the plot of Bijukubo very challenging to wrap my head around, but this is what I've come up with. The main character, whose name is "You" (this has to be a mistranslation or something), lives with his mother and sister. But on his 18th birthday, it's revealed that You is adopted, and his mother is actually his aunt and his sister is actually his cousin. Okay that's fine and dandy, but then another woman shows up who claims to be another one of You's aunts, and she offers him an inheritance... or something... I dunno.

The first episode treats this story very seriously, but it's ultimately rendered worthless because it's dropped in the second episode.

I have nothing to say about the characters. They're not bad, but they're not good either. They're reasonably attractive and that's good enough.

I do have something to say about the art though.

The first episode has some decent artwork. It isn't anything spectacular, but the lines are crisp and the characters are drawn well. It's just a shame a lot of it gets covered up by some heavy-handed censorship.

For the second episode though...

...the artwork is ABOMINABLE!

It looks like shit! Hot garbage! Absolute trash!

I don't understand what went wrong, did they spend all their budget on the first episode and only had a couple yen left? Whatever the reason, it's unacceptable. Something having consistently bad artwork is one thing, but having good artwork and following it up with shitty artwork is even more disgraceful.

The moment I caught a glimpse of what the second episode looked like, I knew it was over. I was willing to overlook some of the flaws if it looked good, but that isn't the case now is it?

If I were just reviewing the first episode I would give this OVA a 6/10 like I wanted to and be done with it. But I'm reviewing the full package, and I must score it accordingly.

I enjoyed the first episode, though it was far from perfect. The second episode I hope to never view again... 5/10

So, would I reccomend you watch Bijukubo? Despite how harsh this review is, I actually would recommend you watch the first episode. But do yourself a favor and avoid the second episode at all costs... your eyeballs will thank you.
