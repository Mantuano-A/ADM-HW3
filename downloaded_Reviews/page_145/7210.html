It’s nice when a show knows it’s a meme and embraces it. One of my only gripes with an anime is when it takes itself too serious for what it is. Ore Dake is aware that it’s an ecchi fantasy full of waifus and an OP MC, but it embraces it and brings everything together in a way that still allows you to enjoy it.

Simply put, Ore Dake is a good time. There’s no real plot here other than rather shallow excuses for fan service-y interactions between the MC and the girls, but you know what? That’s ok, because they’re great and hilarious. Every anime doesn’t need to reinvent the wheel to be great. This is the type of show that you shut your brain off for and sit back for some mindless fun. Week after week I was amazed at the ridiculousness of the antics and just had a blast with this show. It’s actually quite funny, if you asked me to give you a story recap of the show, I honestly couldn’t. All I can say is that I loved it.

The art is really nice in the show, the fight animations were a bit on the meh side, but let’s be real. No one is watching this for the fights. You’re here to laugh and for waifus, and the art is more than good enough on that front.

I don’t think Ore Dake is a must watch, per se, but it is a very enjoyable show that I loved and think many will enjoy. If you’re like me and don’t think every anime needs to be a Monster, Monogatari or Geass in terms of writing, you’ll love this show. Ore Dake gets 9 kisses out of 10.

DanMachi meets Monster Musume.

When someone says “anime is trash and so am I” in regards to liking an allegedly degenerate anime, half of that statement is correct no matter what - the latter half. Because either the anime in question is genuinely degenerate, and they are a moral failure for knowingly endorsing degeneracy (whether they’re doing it “ironically” or not - there is no such thing as pretending to be retarded); or the anime in question is not degenerate, and they are a moral failure for spreading slander and falsehoods. I, for one, am sick and tired of hearing this sort of mental diarrhea about how black is white, two plus two equals five, and Hidden Dungeon is something other than an intelligent, tasteful, and morally upstanding anime show.

There is little doubt that Hidden Dungeon owes its existence to KonoSuba’s success as a comedic/ecchi isekai. You can see nods to that in things such as characters having random English nouns for surnames or mobs being just oversized versions of regular animals. It would, however, be a mistake to peg Hidden Dungeon for a KonoSuba clone based on those surface-level similarities, as their substance is very different. As I already said, this show’s actual substance is “DanMachi meets Monster Musume.”

How it is similar to DanMachi:
A lighthearted, yet serious JRPG setting that actually puts thought and effort into its own JRPG aspect for a change.
The premise of the story is the MC acquiring a cheat-level reality-warping power... that he has to cast from his literal Life Points, with brutally limiting numerical costs. It’s the stuff of a “How to write a magic system well 101” writing class - the less all-powerful and able-to-do-anything magic in a story is, the more interesting it is to see characters working around its limitations when applying it like a tool to their problems. And Hidden Dungeon adheres to this rule splendidly, with its protagonist having to literally Get Creative in order to overcome even the tiniest challenges.
For example, here’s what Noir does when he needs to acquire some dragon loot: 1) Gain some LP. 2) Spend it to give the Dragonslayer attribute to his entire party. 3) Spend reward money from a previous quest to buy a shit ton of thrusting weapons. 4) Consult his guild handler on where do dragons live. 5) Arrange for a friend to transport his party to the area. 6) Pinpoint a dragon with his innate magic wikihow. 7) Make a pit trap with those thrusting weapons. 8) Serve as bait to lure the dragon into the trap. 9) Have the rest of the party jump the dragon when it’s incapacitated. 10) Profit.
That’s a solid eleven-step plan that makes use of available assets and brainpower. This series is a breath of fresh air compared to almost every other isekai with the overpowered protagonist premise in existence where MC would just spam infinite attack magic and strategy isn’t a thing.

Incidentally, the way to gain Life Points is experiencing “life’s pleasures” - be it eating good food, acquiring riches, or getting frisky with girls - that’s the setup of the show’s ecchi element.

How it is similar to MonMusu:
Nigh perfect execution of the ecchi harem formula with a sex-positive attitude. This point will be further divided into two sub-points.

Harem formula:
Just like MonMusu before it, Hidden Dungeon provides a great case study for a “How to write ecchi harem well 101” class. Write it down, it will be on the test:
1. The protagonist should be likable in general, and above all else, convincingly presented as attractive in the eyes of his harem members.
For example, KanoKari is a bad harem show because its male lead is a misogynistic incel, and Highschool DxD is a bad harem show because its male lead is a cumbrain pervert. Women are not attracted to guys like that, so the entire premise of the story falls apart. You also can’t have a harem lead that is just “nice” - realistically droves of females would not be lining up to suck the MC’s dick because he picked up an eraser once - he has to be ATTRACTIVE in a conventional masculine way.
Here in Hidden Dungeon, we have Noir who is a good-natured himbo with shining future prospects due to his OP talents, as well as your friendly neighborhood savior of distressed damsels. Every harem member likes Noir for their own meaningful reasons and has a unique relationship dynamic with him.
2. Harem members should have actual character. Again, you can’t have personalities that end at “wants to suck the MC’s dick,” “has big boobs,” or “wears a maid outfit” (say hi to Rem). To be good characters a harem show’s female cast members must have their own goals, motivations, and purpose in the story. Put simply, a good harem must be an ensemble cast show - and Hidden Dungeon is compliant with this rule.
3. Harem members should meaningfully interact with each other. This is basically an extension of the ensemble cast idea, a good harem anime would pass the Bechdel test - and Hidden Dungeon does just that.

Sex positivity:
From Wikipedia: “Sex positivity is an attitude towards human sexuality that regards all consensual sexual activities as fundamentally healthy, encouraging sexual pleasure and experimentation. The sex-positive movement also advocates for comprehensive sex education and safe sex as part of its campaign.” Bear with me, this is, in fact, still an anime review of the Hidden Dungeon series. In a cosmic coincidence, the Winter 2021 season happened to air not one, but three isekai titles featuring heavy sexual themes:
- Redo of Healer, featuring a protagonist that enjoys taking it up his ass in a rape dungeon so much, he travels back in time just so he can repeat the experience.
- Jobless Reincarnator, featuring a little girl masturbating on a hallway floor while peeking through a keyhole on adults having sex, as well as an MC that is a straight-up ugly bastard from lolicon hentai that skips the funeral of his parents to masturbate to hidden camera footage of his underage niece taking a bath.
- And the worst of all, Hidden Dungeon - featuring two teenage childhood friends passionately kissing once per episode as an expression of their love and having an amicable relationship most likely leading to marriage (the depravity!)
I’ll give you three tries to guess which one of these has a MAL mean score of 8+, and which one is panned as smutty borderline hentai. This seeming paradox can easily be explained - in a place that is not this review section, where a reviewer cannot, quote, “address your reader or any other viewers of the series, in either a positive or negative fashion,” end quote - regardless if it provides context relevant to the reviewed subject. The abridged version of it is that it’s very common to see attempts to push a false narrative about shows with a sex-positive tone - that they have bad writing, or bad characters, or other “objective” flaws - which is pure lies, as I’ve demonstrated in case of this series with the first half of my review (there is no limit to how patently absurd and detached from reality these lies could get - you can see even literary masterpieces like Monogatari or Kuzu no Honkai being called badly-written).
Here’s the actual truth: Hidden Dungeon is a high-brow erotic comedy about a realistically portrayed horny teen having a social life and being successful with women. The boy shows a healthy interest in sex without being an obsessed cumbrain, and so he acts upon this interest by engaging in consensual erotic interactions with members of the opposite sex (also known to people that have ever been outside as “flirting”) - to the mutual enjoyment of involved parties. And since their interactions are reciprocal, consensual, and enjoyable - they lead to these characters developing a mutual interest in each other. Hence, “the MC being successful with women.”
Furthermore, Hidden Dungeon is refreshingly tame by the ecchi anime standards, the sexual scenes consisting of things such as kissing, staring at cleavages, or having boobs pressed against the MC’s body. The lewdest thing that ever happens is accidental panty-shots. There is a caveat that the viewer needs to have morals that allow them to comprehend the difference between an MC that consensually french-kisses his future wife and an MC that steals and sniffs the panties of a nine-year-old (another Jobless Reincarnator example) to appreciate this show’s tastefulness. It’s telling that yet another seemingly paradoxical (*explanation is omitted because muh addressing your reader or any other viewers of the series, in either a positive or negative fashion*) “criticism” Hidden Dungeon gets is that it has “bad writing” because the MC doesn’t just molest everything that moves in order to min-max his Life Points gains (“The MC acts like he’s a sane person inhabiting the real world with real-life logic and real-life consequences - as opposed to acting like he’s a hentai protagonist within a hentai” is, in fact, an example of very good writing).

Unrelated to anything above, one of the main characters is voiced by Horie Yui and her performance is amazing. I almost never mention voice acting in my reviews (because you’ll hardly ever find an anime where voice acting isn’t good), but the Shaft All-Star VA Team are just on a whole nother level.

In summary, Hidden Dungeon is a pure joy of a series with a protagonist that is a great role model for teen boys, nuanced three-dimensional characters, a deeply thought-out fantasy setting, good dialogue writing, jaw-breaking comedy, plus great artwork and sound - and no amount of slander and falsehoods can change this reality.

9/10 for a wholesome fun show for people with high IQ and moral standards.

Another action, adventure, comedy, fantasy anime? Well…why not although we had a lot of those in the past already. It has lots of Ecchi scenes and the fanservice is god-like? REVOLUTIONARY. Count me in.

The studio "Okuruto Noboru" enters a “dangerous dungeon” for the first time with the anime "Ore dake Haireru Kakushi Dungeon". Previously only worked in a few OVAs, they venture into the fantasy, adventure world. Although this genre is nothing new in itself, they managed to do it in a way which doesn't make us bored and asking for more.

Our adventure begins with Noir, the third son of a “beggar noble” (the lowest position for a noble). Because of his position, he didn't get his job in the library. Thereupon he decides to go to the "Hero Academy" in order to become stronger and to get a secure job as an adventurer after graduation. Noir was very nice to his fellow men, but he was never particularly strong. One day he discovers a secret dungeon that only he can enter with a secret spell. This is where he started his secret training to get stronger and stronger…

There is a beautiful woman with a large bust and hips in this dungeon: Olivia. She was one of the S-class adventurers and thus one of the strongest at the time. She entered this dungeon, but after defeating a monster, she was handcuffed in death shackles. When Noir met Olivia by chance and appointed her as his master, she decided to pass on her special skills to Noir.

LP (life points) are required to use the special skills. How do you get this? Noir's childhood friend Emma is the main contributor to all of his LPs. Emma has laaaaarge breasts and is very popular with the opposite sex because of her cuteness but she is only interested in Noir and really loves him. She is willing to do anything for Noir and to get him the necessary LPs with kisses and well… “other things” like that. Everything that is "fun" for a simple man increases the LPs. Noir and Emma has a very close relationship since childhood. They did everything together. Both of them also decided to take on the job in the library. But after Noir was rejected, Emma decided to follow him to the Academy. She really is the vital supporter for Noir.

Let’s take a look at the other two main characters and their role in this little “Noir-clan”. First, we have the Odin Guild receptionist Lola. She is a beauty of the calm and collected sort with an air of maturity around her. Lola was the one who welcomed Noir when he joined the guild, she was always on good terms with him and Noir also helped Lola in receptionist battles. She is clever and knows how to motivate her guild and especially Noir.

Lola's best friend Luna is the last girl in the “Noir-troupe”. All in all, she’s a super beauty and has a good personality. Luna is convinced that it is her mission to save people and make them smile. But whenever she heals someone, it uses up her life energy and this shortens her lifespan. Noir managed to edit her skill in a way not consuming her life-force. Since that point, she fell victim to his charm and decided to stick with Noir as well. Even if all the female characters certainly love their hero Noir, it must be mentioned that they also support each other.

In addition to our main characters, we have the teacher Elna who rewards her male students by sitting on their faces. Maria, a real beauty who really has her heart in the right place as a duke. Her loyal bodyguard Amane, who always takes care of her. Of course, we cannot forget Alice, Noir's younger sister, who has a brother complex and adores her brother above all else. She has a special power which allow her to sense when her brother is getting intimate with other girls. Besides these girls, there are a few more who play a minor role. But one thing is certain: All of these girls are top tier!

It cannot be denied that the art really stands out. All the girls are shown amiable and the fanservice is of high quality. The surroundings are also very beautiful and detailed. Everything fits together perfectly.
The adventure of our group begins with the OP "Pyramid Dai Gyakuten" which tell us to be ready for some action. Prepare yourself for a new adventure that will come your way! The ED “Nemophila”, on the other hand, is about the deep relationship between Noir and Emma. Watching the animation during this song is particularly nice. Each picture tells a little story from their past together. They are a perfect couple. Besides the music, the Seiyuus did a great job performing their role. Their voice and all the emotions go very well with the character.

As someone who generally likes this genre and also watched similar anime like "Konosuba", "Shachou, Battle no Jikan Desu!" or “Princess Connect! Re:Dive “ before, I am also very enthusiastic about "Ore Dungeon". It's a typical anime where you can just switch off for a bit and enjoy the things. Don't think too much about it, just follow the adventure with our characters. The fan service part is very successful and I mean we already experience more kisses in the first episode than in many romance anime. Sure, it's not the same, but it's certainly not a bad thing in my opinion. Noir is surely a lucky guy. I agree that the plot is uncreative and we have had such things before already. Still, I don't see any reason not to like "Ore Dungeon" just because of this point. If you enjoyed other similar anime, you will like “Ore Dungeon” as well.

In the end, I can only say: Just enjoy the adventures and all the S-tier waifus. Let's hope for a second season with even more adventures from our little group!

You can argue that Ore Dake is yet another generic fantasy anime with an ecchi harem plot, and you’ll be right, because it never pretends to be something else than a generic fantasy anime with an ecchi harem plot. Ore Dake Haireru Kakushi Dungeon centres on Noir Starga, who is an ostracized noble because of his low peerage; however, Noir has the unique power of consulting a Sage to help him in varied circumstances, but severely draining his energy as an after effect. One day, after wandering about in the land, he got lost in a dungeon and met a young immobile woman called Olivia, who only communicates by mental waves, and gives him new powers to survive. And with the help of de facto girlfriend Emma, sensual receptionist Lola and eccentric half-elf Luna, Noir will try to become a Hero and your average merchant.

Please take in account that entering this dungeon review will show you spoilers.

Story (8/10):
Noir Starga is a low level noble who has the access to unlimited knowledge to a sage, only to drain his life for using it, so he uses on restricted occasions to avoid fatal consequences. He only has a normal dream of becoming a librarian, along with his childhood friend and arguably girlfriend Emma Brightness.

The same Noir, while exploring the land, gets into a mysterious dungeon where he enters a secret chamber only to meet a vegetative girl bound by chains called Olivia Servant; this young woman offers rare powers to the young adventurer by telematics. After acquiring those new abilities, Noir returned to the town, and then he decided, against the wishes of his family, to enter the Hero Academy, along with Emma.

In order to enter the academy, Noir and Emma need to do certain things to reach a passing grade, this in spite of the massive hate he receives from the students with higher peerages because of Noir’s baronet peerage. Despite their entire negative comments during the gradings, both easily passed the admission test in the first place.

Much to the chagrin of Emma, Noir starts getting along with the academy girls by using his charms and the creative powers he keeps acquiring, specially the frail Maria, and even his main teacher, and this not only applies to the school. That’s when they go to the adventure guild to earn some coins, they meet Lola, a pretty girl who is the receptionist, and clearly falls in love with Noir, even considering his total inexperience on adventuring. Maria’s ever worsening health prompts all three to reach Lola’s healer best friend, the half-elf Luna.

Initially, Luna totally rejects the job due to her also ever decreasing lifespan due to habitual healing and Maria’s extremely demanding curse; then Noir intervenes to overwrite her attributes to neutralize the curse with a reducing wealth instead of a reducing lifespan, forcing the three to enter a contest to get a harem. Finally, after earning so much geld to amortize possible debts, Luna finally cures Maria.

With the worst already behind, Noir and his harem, decided to start focusing in creating a shop about rare items they’ve gotten during their adventures. Alongside, Noir and Emma keep getting closer and closer, to the point that the Brightness family invited him to be Emma’s guardian to prevent her for being kidnapped; their close physical contact and kissing became a constant, evolving from only kissing Noir to alleviate his draining, to being a sign of mutual trust.

Their increasing relationship pushes Emma and Noir to travel the hidden dungeon to search for Olivia in order to save her, only to be attacked by an Olivia clone that was bound to Death Chains and severely hurting Noir in the way. No matter how much the Starga primogenitor tried to attack Death Chained Olivia while sacrificing almost all his acquired powers, she always one upped him, even with the help of his harem, Emma, Lola, Luna and Lelia. Noir, reaching a breaking point and sacrificing almost all his creative powers, finally destroys the Death Chains and saves Olivia without killing her.

Art and animation (7/10):
The animation seem too standard and nothing inventive, the characters look distinguishable and well-drawn, highlighting Emma Brightness (yes, I do like big breasted blonde girls, and I’m not ashamed at all) and the sensei Elna; however, the studio Oku Noboru is completely new, and Ore Dake is one of its first forages onto TV anime, the next one will be another generic fantasy anime with an ecchi harem plot, Isekai Maou.

Backgrounds seem decent, relatively well detailed; the colour panels are pretty colourful, but never getting too oversaturated like in fellow Winter 2021 anime Wonder Egg Priority.

Sound (9/10):
The sound is pretty tranquil and happy, the seiyuus are pretty serviceable, but already established, Ryouta Osaka has already got experience on harem leads like Nariyuki Yuiga (Bokuben), Miyu Tomita voicing Gabriel White (Gabriel Dropout), Rumi Okubo in doing annoying girls like Chinatsu Yoshikawa (Yuru Yuri); and the more well-known voice actress like Akari Kitou doing Luna, Sora Amamiya as Master Elna and Yui Horie hanekawazing Olivia Servant.

The opening Pyramid Dai Gyakuten, sung by Spira Spice is quite upbeat, who also sang in the controversial anime Okaasan Online; Coala Mode sings the more depressive Nemophila. The background music is well written, never too loud and never too quiet.

Characters (8/10):
The characters are pretty generic, but all of them serve a determined purpose, I’ll only talk about Noir, Emma, Lola and Luna:

Noir Starga is your typical generic adventure hero, gets all the girls without any effort at all, but I would highlight that he doesn’t shy away of girls and he’s very protective of them, and has a very positive relationship with Emma, even considering the initial kissing motives.

Emma Brightness is the classical childhood friend who becomes too well endowed and gets all eyes put on her, but breaking the traditional and always negative ending of osananajimis, by finally being the de facto girlfriend and much needed companion of Noir.

Lola is clearly the traditional oneesan-like receptionist who after questioning heavily Noir abilities gets too infatuated on him. Competitive by nature, she always tries to get Noir to her side without withholding any barrels, and trying to be the best receptionist of her guild.

Luna is the all too well-known half-elf who is helpful with anybody by curing their aliments, even putting her life in risk, a master on healing, she too became enamoured on Noir, but gives him the right space and doesn’t pressure him.

Enjoyment (10/10):
How can I enjoy an anime that is way too generic and doesn’t add anything new to the genre? It’s simple, the simplicity and the amicable character of the anime, never going too overboard on fanservice, maintaining all the tropes that characterize the generic fantasy anime with ecchi harem plot.

The show is no magnum opus, nor nothing highly venerable, it just works well within the limitations of the genre, only trying to break the mold of the osananajimi finally getting the ultimate prize (if she didn’t already got it).

Those who see nothing valuable in Ore Dake are the same overly purist to never like something generic or unimpressive, I do find valuable Hidden Dungeon for the sole purpose of entertaining and giving us a nice big breasted blonde girl who can stand up on her own. And yes, I’m partial to Emma.

Final Score: 9/10.
