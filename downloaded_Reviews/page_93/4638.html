Story-7

A young princess sent to the other kingdom to marry one of the prince to make an alliance between both countries. While being on the ship, she brought her friend on the ship and command her to disguise as the princess. The ship was protected by tons of guards and soldiers, but they were attack by the other nation so that the marriage didn't happen and the alliance wouldn't be made. Their attack was successful and the princess was captured, but then she was saved by her fiance. Then she was brought to the kingdom and successfully have the marriage, but the enemies aren't happy with this and they will always try to kill the princess since she's so weak.

Art-8

Beautiful art and great scenery, the chinese artstyle fits the slice of life and romance in this anime. the action in this anime is really good despite it being a slice of life but the animation is really cool. You would understand they put a lot of efforts into this anime.

Sound-9

The best thing about this anime is the music in it, the opening sequence was a really well made music that is so relaxing, it could be used as a background music for studying. The ending is also great, but i prefer the opening more, the voice acting put in this anime is ok, it just gives a different feel than normal anime. As someone who is used to hearing japanese in anime, chinese aren't that bad too.

Character-8

Really well developed characters, the design is good and it shows a different and more chinese hairstyle. it has a lot of traditional uniform and accessories, there's a lot of character in this anime but it really focuses to the princess and the prince. They developed relationship throughout the story which when they didn't really liked each other at first.

Enjoyment-9/Overall-9

I really enjoy this from the characters, the arts and the songs in the anime. There's a little bit of comedy element in this anime that makes it more enjoyable and not to mention that the action is really well made. At first it didn't really appeal too much on the first few episode but it starts off being good when they had the marriage. The opening in this is really relaxing and beautiful, the same goes with the ending. Overall this Donghua is a really good romance/slice of life comedy show and i would recommend this to someone who enjoys light anime.

I am so pleasantly surprised with this. It was my first Chinese animation show, and I am shocked more people don't talk about it??? It was STUNNING: visually, emotionally, and plot-wise. The characters were all beautiful, the dialogue was hilarious and gripping, the plot was emotional and captivating, the music was gorgeous, and the entertainment value was 100%. I was completely obsessed with this show and could not stop watching. I had to literally pull myself away to get to my real life.

The romance was seriously so fun to watch and the development is realistic and raw. The characters make realistic decisions, have genuinely realistic emotional responses, and say relatable things. I am so so impressed with this show.

I really encourage you to give this show a chance. You'll be into it within the first few minutes. The Chinese language takes a few minutes to get used to it and you stop noticing after a while. I could not get enough of this show and I will be so sad when season 2 ends! As I write this, I am already halfway through the second season.

Some people complain that the show is quick and not many minutes, but I really love that I could binge this within three hours.

Excellent! I didn't expect that ending. It's good. Ok, lemme recollect my thoughts. The Art department in Chinese anime is never short of praise. Its unique, orange-ish, and bright color palette adds up to the quality and further accentuates the Chinese autumn vibes that the show was trying to build up. The visuals in the opening are magnificent, full of ambiance; the way its story boardings and visuals alone managed to message a bit on the emotional struggle a housewife faces, namely solitary, seclusion, feeling of disconnection, longing for martial family, and so on especially prevalent among brides wed through an arranged marriage in Asian culture is fantastic. The music is great in itself too. In Asian cultures, many brides are wed through arranged marriages, this show exactly tackles what it means to marry into someone's house without building any former love and friendship first. The bride is met with expectations on etiquette, manners, and other trivial pieces of stuff rather than feelings. Love is prioritized second here. Our protagonist, a bride used as a means for political stability between two countries has to face many adversaries in her palace, constantly being swayed in the kingdom's politics game, and being left alone. I like this because this topic is uncharted and unprecedented in Japanese anime, at least from what I've experienced. One might argue, however, kingdom settings and arranged marriages kind of stuff is common in Chinese, I believe this is the best one in this department. Most people complained of romance starting at the end part of the series but this is perfectly fine, the show gives an extra emphasis on how politics/ kingdom move and lays out a framework for the main character to find herself in this newfound environment of her, she takes time being used to the palace and contacting with side characters and her husband. There's not much romance between the main heroine and the hero in the first season however, there's this one bittersweet love story of a side character that more than enough warrants my recommendation, which I'll not dwell much on because of spoilers. Stick to the ending and you might be surprised as well hehe. Fight scenes lack fluidity and it's okay; it wasn't trying to be Naruto V2, so it's fine to not have jaw-dropping actions, though the choreography was decent. It's lacking in cinematography; the lack of sunlight effects and incessant use of smudges made the visuals look less pristine. Though the art is fantastic and always pleasing to look at, they seemed to lack luster at some points, not giving the justice the art department deserved. Simply speaking, a common sunlight effect is more than enough to make it reach Fog Hill of Five Elements level-of-filming, well not that high but still, you get the point. Anyway, back to the plot, the side characters are interesting. The plot is hard to follow at times, not necessarily due to pacing problems or another, but because the way Chinese is spoken is incredibly fast and the translation was grammatically correct all the time but lack of fluidity and naturalness affected me. I had to sometimes pause and replay in order to reread subtitles due to fast voice acting. I don't mean to insinuate that the voice actors did the lazy job or fast-paced job but for Japanese anime watches, Chinese is relatively fast and the voice acting in this anime is really great as well unlike other Chinese anime who fubble at this part. The first season is more plot-oriented and the focus on characters at the first 5 episodes is meager but as the plot escalates the side characters are given deserved highlights. A storyline packed with political chicanery is always welcome in my book and it doesn't take tedious time to do things, characters are well written as well. Episodes are very short, the openings and company credits take the first 2 minutes 40 something seconds, the ending song is unusually long and there are advertisements and promotions at the end which goes from about 17:00- the end of the video. So an actual episode is something from 2:40 to 17:00, about 15 minutes each episode- not too long. I finished it rapidly. Voice acting is stellar, especially the main character. Wrapping up ideas, it's entertaining to watch, fantastic art, not-so-awesome effects and filming though, and of course great intentions and themes, great voice acting mainly the mc, she is honestly so pleasing to just hear. There's much to talk about the bittersweetness of its story but I don't know how I should articulate this without spoiling, anyway thanks for reading. Hope you enjoy it too.
