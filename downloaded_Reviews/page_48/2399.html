This OVA features the band formed by Elsie and some of the girls that Keima had already conquered in the past.

The story is pretty much a spoof of K-ON! which I liked since I'm also a fan of it. The girls also struggle in forming a light music club. Keima only had a short appearance in this OVA. Because I never really able to read the manga version of this, at first I thought Keima wouldn't appear at all. Don't get me wrong, Keima still has a big role in this OVA. There's this moment where Keima believes that the girls he already had conquered still have some remnants of their memories with him in the past.

The art is for me is just pretty just like the TV anime. The chibi art is still really fun to see especially Elsie's chibi version.

I really like the Kanon's Opening theme of this one. The pinkish theme really makes it obvious that it's a Kanon theme. It features Kanon in her swimsuit and her concert. Great art and effects which Kanon fans will really love to see. The Ending theme on the other hand features Elsie's band playing, which is really good way of ending the OVA.

It is really great to see all the past characters again in one episode. You get to see how they have progressed after Keima captured their loose soul. Fans of even one of these characters will really love it. I guess loosing their memories is really the best way to make them appear again on an episode without any trouble if ever they meet or see Keima again.

All in all I really enjoyed this OVA since I've been really waiting for this. It's really worth waiting it. I still wish that there will be a season 3 soon! :)

This is my very first review, so apologies for some grammatical errors and if I give a little bit of spoilers.

Ok, so now that I've watched this 1 off OVA, I have to say that it's not bad at all, if we're to look at it, it is more like a parody which is pattered after another anime "K-ON" which a group of girls gathered up to form an all girls band, so far most of the genres of this anime's prequel no longer applies to its OVA, the only genres apply were music, slice of life and comedy. but just like from the said anime, there are challenges abound for them, like:

1. Fine tuning not only their instruments but their capacity to play it as well.

2. Finding a place where they can practice and their prospect- you got it, in school.

3. Asking permission from the teacher to allow them to form a light music club which is actually just their front for practice.

4. Finding another band member ,which by the way they chance upon pop idol Kanon.

5. Getting a perfect 100 score in English as a precondition by the teacher to have their own club.

6. Fining a way how they can get a perfect 100 score in English where everyone sucks.

As expected,in the end everything turned out 98% ok, since Elsie still sucks in English, Kanon is too busy with her schedule and the teacher just allowed them to form their club anyway, finally everyone except Kanon get to have a room and time for practice and it ends there. So for the story I gave it a "Good" 7 since the OVA's plot is all for them girls.

The art of this anime hasn't change so there isn't much for me to say anything,so for the art a "Mediocre" 5 would do.

The sound, well I like Kanon's song and the alternative Chihiro's ending theme. so it all "Good" number 7.

For the characters, well, 3 of them form the anime are there like Chihiro, Ayumi, Elsie and Kanon, but there is a new one who used to be from the manga Miyako Terada, so even though some of the gang are here, it's still can be considered as a "Mediocre" number 5.

Enjoyment and overall, a good number 7.

Well, this isn't anymore about Kiema and Elsie hunting down loose souls, this is all about Kanon and Chihiro singing, which is the enjoyable part of this 1 off OVA. Overall the real cast of this OVA are it's theme which is Kanon's opening and Chihiro's closing theme.,

This OVA is the anime equivalent to game DLC you really like but bought more to support development rather than it being genuinely worth it. Kami Nomi is probably one of the better romance shows I’ve watched as far as the more fan-service oriented counterparts of that genre goes but this is just another filler episode in disguise.

The simple premise is 4 of the show’s girls starting a band, but it only takes up a minuscule amount of time in this episode and the rest is them studying for a test? The absence of Keima for about half the OVA stings too. Bland character interactions don’t make a case for Kami Nomi’s usual wit and standout personalities; the sole interesting moment was Keima meeting Kanon again. Basically it’s ok to skip this. It’s not in any shape or form important to any story of the show.

While it was nice to see the results of her development, it just feels like any other "high school friends working hard to form a club" anime out there in a single episode form. Although it was quite generic and nothing special, it did managed to move me a little like most slice of life anime do. The only other notable things about this OVA are: Kanon showing signs that she remembers parts of her conquest, and both Ayumi and Chihiro are only showing hints of theirs. That's about it.
