I almost never write reviews, but the low ratings from disillusioned Naruto fans that only have watched 30 episodes have become a bit much for me..

I have caught up to the recent 155 episodes and have to say I really really love this show.
Sure, some of the arcs and fillers are BORING, but the main characters and ultimate storyline are super fun and interesting!!

Boruto is a natural-born genius with lots of friends and a loving family. This greatly contrasts Naruto's childhood, so I understand why people could initially write Boruto off as just a spoiled brat... However, through various events, Boruto does finally come to realize how fortunate he is in contrast with his dad's lonely and sad upbringing and grows out of the wanting acknowledgement / attention phase, especially as he starts to admire Sasuke who protects from the shadows.

In the end, Boruto is just a kind and lovable kid with his own ninja way of never abandoning his friends and goal of becoming a powerful ninja who can one day assist the Hokage. His abilities and growing powers leaves me really excited for what is to come in the future. Additionally, it's great to see the happy grown-up Naruto characters, finally living their dreams or having peaceful lives after the war with their families. The kids are all so cute and it's cool to see them utilizing the combined abilities they inherited from their parents. The parallels that happen between the two shows also makes it a fun watch!

If it's not for you, it's not for you, but I just definitely wouldn't go into Boruto thinking it'll be the same as Naruto... Boruto is NOT Naruto, but that's okay. I think the show itself fits/reflects the transition into the time of peace very nicely.

Thank you for reading this if you did. These are just my opinions. :)
There ARE people who are enjoying this show!

ART

SOUND

Lesson 2 : don't care about the rest, people will follow as long as they have the bait in sight. You don't even need a clean job, just be as lazy as you can get and relax.
Lesson 3 : you lack material? Just take what already exists. People won't care if you show them a story they've already seen not so long ago.

I've learned all that thanks to Boruto. Because that's what it is : a PURE, DISHONEST, DISRESPECTFUL CASHGRAB.

Boruto: Naruto Next Generations follows the Naruto series much like one generation follows the next; the series and it's elements have been passed like a torch to the children of the series.

The opening statement for this review should be that the main audience this show is meant to appeal to is not the prior Naruto audience. This anime has tasked itself with appealing to two different audiences; the prior generation whom followed Naruto Uzumaki and the current generation who will now be following our new protagonist Boruto Uzumaki.

Boruto Uzumaki is a solemnly different character than Naruto was; he's spoiled both in the sense of material wealth and in his ease of acceptance by the village. He does not bear the same burdens that Naruto did as a child, and for this reason he has troubles with entitlement and acting, for lack of better phrasing, "bratty." But, with this said I do not think the show lacks in proper characterization or character development. Boruto is a child, and he acts as such. He lives in a modernized version of the ninja world; one where there's abundant peace, computers, video games, and a lessening need for the occupation of ninja. Boruto (at this present point in the series) does not know struggle; what he does know is that his father's work is keeping his father away from him, and having shared happy experiences with Naruto prior to his inauguration as Hokage he is bothered by this fact.

Boruto acts very much as a child would in this situation. He meddles, and causes trouble; but still bears many of the same traits as Naruto (being someone who is there to help others). He's simply living a different young life than Naruto; and for this reason expresses the same traits Naruto did at a young age but for different reasons. In other words he acts up due to lack of attention from his father; rather than a lack of attention from the village.

What makes this show interesting to watch is this very characterization. It gives a chance to those of us who have followed Naruto through his journey to see how his child acts; whom knows little of his journey because he was not there himself. It gives us a chance to see how these new ninja students live in a modernized world that's in a state of peace. It allows us to again watch Boruto grow and change; much like Naruto did, but with a different start and a different end.

Boruto is meant to appeal to the new generation; someone they can follow and admire. The current generation likely does not know of Naruto's accomplishments or has not followed the series, and so they can echo the sentiments of Boruto - they don't know the story. This anime gives both the prior viewers a chance to follow Naruto as he learns to be a father and Hokage; but also serves the purpose of showing the current viewers Boruto's journey - which will likely contain the same character development Naruto did.

Having been a heavy advocate of the Naruto series, and having watched the entirety of it; I enjoy Boruto. I fully recognize that Boruto is not a show made for me, but rather for the kids of the current generation. I think the characterization of the children is done well, and they bear enough resemblance to their parents whilst expressing unique traits from their different lifestyle growing up that it makes sense. I'm interested to see the series progress with a mix of enjoying the company of my own hero, Naruto; and seeing how the new children develop into fine ninja as well.

Boruto is a follow-up to the Naruto series that I can advocate. It knocks at modernism as we see skyscrapers accrue, to-be ninja playing game consoles, and clothes which were prior tattered and torn after training (such as those worn by ninja students) looking pristine and clean. It gives us a chance to follow another story of growth, hardship, and much more; and for that I'm excited. It's the next generation of Naruto, and I'm looking forward to seeing where we are headed.

In my opinion, Boruto is highly enjoyable. Everyone has their own opinions, and this is mine. There are also many things that I want the anime to tackle, but just because it doesn't, does not mean that it is a bad show.

Story: Based on the direction that the series is taking, I believe that it will tackle the major plot point introduced at the end of Naruto Shippuden: the Ootsutsuki. It will also focus on the story of Naruto's son, Boruto and another boy named Kawaki, who was seen in the first episode. I really enjoyed watching the buildup from the academy to the Chunin Exams as it shows a young Boruto who is discontent with many things, evolve to a mature boy who finally understands the role of the Hokage, his father. Plus, his resolve begins to change as well. Boruto acknowledges that his previous behavior pertained to one of a brat and he grew up from the experience of the Ootsutsuki Invasion, realizing that the easy way out, is not always the correct way out. The story begins to build from there and creates more excitement than ever, as there are new foes and powerful enemies. Many of my favorites haven't been adapted in the anime yet, but I can't help but become very hyped for their entrance! Another thing I enjoy in the story, is the connection throughout the arcs and character development. Since the anime can't adapt the manga immediately, there are world building anime canon arcs that are enjoyable as well, even if they aren't as plot driving as the manga canon. The ending of the fight with Momoshiki was a major moment as Boruto receives an interesting message from Momoshiki regarding his interesting fate.

Art: The art is wonderful, the adaption of Naruto Gaiden: The Seventh Hokage and the Scarlet Spring in the anime in addition to the Nue Arc, The Academy Graduation Arc and the last couple of episodes in the Byakuya Gang Arc during Ryogi's breakdown, all within the first 50ish episodes had great animation. And the animation in the Chunin Exams Arc during the battle with Momoshiki vs Sasuke and Naruto is one of the greatest animated fights I have witnessed. The father-son rasengan was very well animated as well as touching. Mitsuki's Will Arc was filled with many well animated sections towards the second half of the arc, this served as a nice balance to the first half of the arc which was focused more on the story telling, and the ongoing question of one's will. The Jugo Arc had very nice fight animations as well. The Mujina Bandits, and now currently the Kara Actuation Arc have very stellar fight animation sequences that are very enjoyable to watch, I can only imagine what the manga adaptions will look like once they get animated.

Sound: A very great soundtrack so far with a good amount of variety, I believe there will be more soundtracks that will be added, especially with the upcoming manga adaptions for the anime. The sounds capture the moment very well, a few osts that I enjoy are Spin and Burst, Sarada's Heartache, and Sarada's Fighting Spirit. The openings and endings are very nice as well; my favorite openings would have to be 5, 6 and 7, and my favorite endings are 2, 3, 4, 5- practically all of them. The endings especially capture the essence of the arc. Ending 1 captures the lighthearted beginning of the show that reflects the peace time that has settled on the ninja world after the 4th great ninja war, ending 2 and 6/7 depicts Sarada and Mitsuki's inner turmoil, ending 3 captures the academy lifestyle and Boruto's energetic drive, endings 4 and 5 are pretty linked as ending 4 signals the oncoming storm and turbulence of the next arc, while ending 5 is an ending that hits hard after the episodes in the Chunin Exams arc regarding Boruto's fate, his shame, the Ootsutsuki's descent and Boruto's resolve. Endings 8, 9, 10, 11, 12 and 13 don't really connect to an arc, but are really nice to listen to. Ending 8,10 and 12 seem to have a lot of foreshadowing of the future events in the series, while 11 is about a card game that depicts team 10's compatibility. Endings 9 and 13 illustrate the amazing chemistry between team 7 and also showed the viewers how adorable they are(lol).

Character: I personally enjoy most of the characters in the series, the new team 7, with Boruto, Sarada and Mitsuki, is my favorite team in the new gen. I also found it funny that Naruto, Sasuke and Orochimaru, all of whom had a very strained (and that's putting it lightly) relationship throughout Naruto and Shippuden, now have children in the same squad, who unlike them, have a very wholesome relationship and support each other.
I also find the parallels between Boruto and Sarada's character development very interesting. They were both initially at odds with their parents for either withholding information or not being there, and thanks to another figure, the other one's parent, the embarked on a mission where they saved a parent with help and returned to the village with a newly formed goal and a stronger resolve. And now both of their goals are intertwined as Boruto is willing to protect Sarada as she pursues her own goal. Mitsuki and Kawaki are also very compelling characters, the latter whom I can't wait to see in the anime. Mitsuki also has quite a similar path to Boruto and Sarada, except his path is more Boruto oriented, although, he has shown Sarada a great amount of support, especially in volume 11 during Team 7's latest tussle.
I am still very eager to see more characters from the manga get adapted into the anime!!

Enjoyment: Everyone has their own opinion, and in my opinion, Boruto is highly entertaining. Each chapter is something I greatly anticipate, and the anime adds more depth to the manga storylines.

Overall: I enjoy Boruto, but there are still many things I do want to see, I can't wait to see more and I am truly hyped for more of the story. And reminder, just because something is not to your taste, does not mean it is horrible. It is impossible for everything to go exactly the way you would like it to go, so please don't be a child and throw a tantrum over a fictional universe.
On another note, I like the direction of the story and I wish for more episodes and chapters!
