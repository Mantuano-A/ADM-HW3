Keep in mind, this is based on an otome game. The story and characters are pulled from that. In the game, each army is designed by a different illustrator and you can sort of see that in the character design but the images in the game are BEAUTIFUL. The images in the anime...well, the animation really failed this one, even if I was entertained by the show.

The first few episodes have horrible lighting issues but seem to even out a bit after about episode three. Sadly, overall the animation is poor. Characters are often distorted and what could have been epic fight scenes were mostly stills with shakey camera tricks or 80's style lasers for sword cuts. It was.....beyond bad. Also very disappointing. While I was watching I couldn't help but think about how better the animation could have been.

Script wise I'm not sure this one knew what it wanted to be. Reverse harem or full on Sengoku story. It seemed to only have small moments of a reverse harem but the story of the fighting warlords was never fleshed out very well. I wish they would have decided what they wanted this anime to be because it could have been a fun time as a reverse harem or it could have been an excellent Sengoku piece with amazing fight scenes but it chose to do neither well at all.

Again, I did enjoy watching it, but it had so much wasted potential.

This is based off of a otome mobage game, that only came out in the spring of 2017, so you can tell they had it set up for the anime. I can not express how much I hate the art style, beyond Sanada team which it might be an improvement there but that is also my own tastes. I just feel like compared to the actual game the art doesn't do it any justice what so ever. In the game each army is done by a different artist, and they're the top in the industry at that! So that is my MAIN big problem with the show is the art doesn't do it justice. Otome/ Reverse harem shows, hell harem shows in general, don't usually have a strong story. You watch them for the individual character, which I do feel the anime is rushing a bit compared to the game. Those are standard things that happen though when doing an anime for this type of game.

This one is no different, in fact its almost laughable, but its not bad enough to make me not enjoy it. Basically Himemiko ( The Mom ) decides to peace out, and supposedly sends Yuzuki from the real world into here to possibly replace her? Yuzuki might be inside a cellphone, the anxiety riddle tanuki adorable companion minion Imari probably the only one that knows. its not clarified in the game either. To make everyone get along she created the Moon fang tribe, consisting of two vampire armies, and 4 werewolf ones. They rule over the normies and fight these monster things called Yakuma ( WHICH DONT LOOK ANYTHING LIKE THE ONES IN THE GAMES FYI ). Since Mom disappeared without a trace everyone freaks out and starts fighting over unifying Shinga, kinda like how they fought over unifying japan in sengoku period.Like a bunch of kids wanting to acomplish something big to show off for when they find Mom ( Himemiko ) again. Rather than just get along and keep the peace like they're suppose to...Yuzuki's blood awakens them to a new color scheme and power level, which could give each army one hell of an advantage. So everyone is going to fight over yuzuki, while she helps each army in their own little way and probably in the end makes everyone get along.

Its a general, overplayed, theme and type. Yuzuki is your typical bland sweet MC.... annoyingly so. However the voice acting, is pretty top notch, and I really hope the show continues to show more character development though it will probably only focus on one or two relationships at most. The game is amazing for mobage, and so far the anime doesn't disappoint to badly. Getting music for it has been fun, I'm loving the sound track! 7/10.

The only thing I *kind of* liked about this anime is the opening theme song and possibly some of the voice actors (i dont blame the seiyus for their awful lines). That's it. The rest are all poorly, if not dreadfully executed.

I stick with the series because of the Sengoku theme but there have been a few times that I wanted to just drop this show altogether as it was ultimately lazy and boring so to speak.

As usual it starts with the reverse harem theme: Ordinary girl gets transported to another world where its mostly dominated by guys and they all swoon over her for no reason. As a reverse harem fan like me, I don't mind if the heroine is a little useless, annoying as they seem, I usually give them the benefit of the doubt and wait till they reveal their redeeming qualities that makes them a little bit likable.

Yuzuki (MC) on the other hand is way too useless, even her lines are so lazy and it just made the show so bland its aggravating. There was no utter motive why everyone likes her. She says one useless thing then bam! instant like from a warlord! lol

The plot started with everyone fighting for unification then a girl comes along with her special blood but no one even dares to drink it just because... (I've heard in the game they actually do). Out of the blue some evil spirits (yakuma) enters the scene because they are after her blood too and it wasnt explained why. So in the end everyone decided to set their differences and fight together for her sake. No plot twists whatsoever. It feels like its just a show for the sake of hyper commercialism on the game.. Like "you wanted more? Play the game!" sort of thing.

So no, I do not recommend this anime. I do not like how Toyotomi Hideyoshi looks in this show either... what's up with the get up?!

Over all I rated this a 3. Tbh It's not even a good show to pass the time. Rather it puts you to sleep.

This is an Otome game in a nutshell. Generic unrealistic summarized story of girl finding herself in a parallel alternate path of history with beautiful bishounen puppy boys and vampires that are all warring. She just so happens to have magic blood that gets all the boys in the club tipsy. She makes her way from warlord to warlord making them all swoon by existing as a normal human being and looking and acting very normal. There's basically no complexity to the story, no depth, and the animation of the fight scenes could have been portrayed better as a flip book.

On the other hand, the actual art is pretty decent. If you want to watch exceedingly pretty men for 12 episodes, this will give you that fix. The protagonist heroine has zero personality, so she's easy to ignore. To the staff's credit, the voice acting is very good, and I would give it a 9/10. Character development is mild, and backstory is generic, shallow, and pretty much the anathema of the typical juicy backstory one usually craves in anime.

Overall, this is an anime that is crap between 2 pieces of bread, but for those of you(like myself) that enjoy garbage every now and again, just look at the crap sandwich from the above view(i e zone out whilst watching the bishies and listening to their sexy voices) and all you see is nice toasted bread.

5/10 would watch again,
would not recommend.
