One sentence to describe this anime/hentai: horny for the sake of being horny.

Is that a bad thing? No, not necessarily. Especially when you go into this anime with zero expectations like I did.

Listen, this 'ecchi' anime is pretty much a hentai and you know it (watch the first episode and you'll get what I mean). But then again, it's also more plot-heavy than a hentai. The truth is I'm not the biggest hentai-watcher but even then I'm so so grateful for ecchi anime targeted at women. Like??? Was about time???

Story - 4
It's okay, decent, so-so, nothing special. It's essentially about an office lady in her mid-twenties falling in love with her hot firefighter childhood friend. When I first read the synopsis of this anime, I was honestly expecting the 'fire-fighter boyfriend' to become the centre of the plot. But nope, I was wrong. Rather than actually using the profession to its advantage, the writer doesn't dwell on that aspect to build up the main lead's character at all. Sure there are scenes at his workplace that gets the spotlight, but nothing that actually justifies why this firefighter profession was necessary in the first place.

The story is all over the place and extremely rushed but it's still pretty coherent. Unlike many hentai anime, you won't see any major skips in the plot to get straight to the... y'know the good bits. It lays out the basics of the context, enough to actually make you understand the reason why they're doing what they're doing. Well... kind of. It really has more to do with the characters and I will explore that in a bit.


Art - 6
For such a short anime series, the art is pretty good. The main characters are eye-candy material and since this anime is targeted more at women, the animators definitely did a good job at making the guys look hot. It's no KyoAni Free! half-naked boys typa good, but it's pretty damn great at giving the 18+ scenes a bit of spice. Our main character Souma, and his firefighter colleagues are hot. Period. But at the same time, you can definitely see how the animators paid extra attention to making the main male lead hotter than the rest.

One comment though; their proportions are wack at times??? Like Souma looks phenomenal during the bed scenes (with his shirt off), and yet he looks like a Lego block with his clothes on... something about the way they drew his body proportions seriously irked me but not enough to affect my enjoyment too much.


Sound - 3
Irremarkable. That's all I'm gonna say.

The anime doesn't have an opening - considering it's only 3-4 mins per episode. The ending song is a wreck as well. It's so forgettable I don't even remember a single tune from it.

The OSTs were also forgettable. None that really struck my attention at all. They tried but not hard enough, I guess.


Character - 5
One thing I get so frustrated about with hentais are the fact that the characters feel like an empty shell - as if they were created just to y'know, get at it. And that's fair, it is a hentai after all. No one is going to watch a hentai to get the feels or to experience a good story. Still, it's a bonus when characters are not just there for the sake of getting you off. Ryo and Souma are horny pieces of sh*ts but you can actually feel that they look at each other beyond just bodies.

There are attempts to build up their feelings (albeit poor but I mean it's there), and the author actually throws in typical shoujo anime tropes like jealousy, cliches, and whatnot to make viewers feel something.

But here's where it gets kind of iffy. The characters have some major issues; Ryo, our main female lead acts like an outspoken yet timid lady - she wants to set clear boundaries between her and Souma only to break her own rules and run after him while repeated saying "S-Stop it." Not gonna lie, it gets really confusing because as a reader, you're unsure of her true feelings. Does she want him or does she not? Is she giving consent or is she not?

Souma on the other hand is a very, very flawed character. He seems to be genuinely concerned with Ryo, saving her from a fire frantically and inviting her to take refuge at his place without hesitation. BUT, the show is really struggling to convince me that he likes her beyond just her body. He goes through such a stark change in character when she slightly shows interest, almost as if he's a completely different person. He becomes almost sadistic (minus the violence) and I personally think it would be much more exciting if his character was more devoted and gentler on Ryo.


Enjoyment - 7
This doesn't come as a surprise but despite all its very evident flaws, I enjoyed the show. It's the biggest guilty pleasure and it does a great job at catering to its female audience. I don't think I've really come across an ecchi/hentai anime meant for the female demographics so this was a breath of fresh air.

If you enjoy looking at hot men and a main female lead actually being pleasured right, you'll love this one. It's a no-brainer show for days where you just need a good trashy, degenerate show to fulfil your fantasies. Plus, you don't even have to commit to it fully since each episode is 4 minutes long.


Overall - 5
It's a decent show and a fantastic hentai for women. That's really all there is to it. It's satisfying without raising red flags and it's surprisingly very light to watch (and read). I - not to sound cliche - actually prefer the manga over the anime as the artwork is so well drawn and the plot is a lot more polished in the manga. But the anime is not a poor adaptation at all.

Giving it a solid 5 is the best score for this anime in my opinion, no more and no lesser than that.

Go on, don't be shy, watch it right now.
