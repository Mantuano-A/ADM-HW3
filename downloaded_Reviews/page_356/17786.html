Let me just say EVERYONE has high expectations going into this hentai adaptation, including me. I mean its one of the most popular and highly rated manga's so of course the fanbase will expect something that delivers. Did Bunnywalker manage to succeed at the grueling task of adapating one of the most famous hentai dojins or did they fail disappointingly known as "Joshi Luck!"? Well let's find out.

Story: 4/10.
Now as someone who has not read the manga, please remember that all of my critisms are directed at the anime and not the manga. With that being said, "Joshi Luck" fails in many regards to stories in my eyes. I don't know what it is but I have kind of starting warming up the whole trope of "harem of sports girls", when it is done right that is. I feel Joshi Luck never really gives time for things to develop, kind of like something else I recently reviewed. I am going to be honest these 16 minute hentai are really crossing the line but I guess it doesn't matter since no gives a fuck about story and characters rather than me. Look, Joshi Luck's premise is not bad and I do like somethings about it's story development but let's be honest, there is not that much story here. It's not as bad as the other hentai I have reviewed but it's getting to a point where I am legitimate scared of there new releases as they keep getting more and more "lifeless". I don't like hating on series that I legitimately enjoy but when they keep pulling shit like this it really makes me concerned about where hentai is going to be heading in a few years, to "nonsense full or NTR and no plot harems".

Art and Animation: 10/10.
The art in Joshi Luck! is amazing. Well it does seem hentai is regressing in terms of story it is defiantly making big strides in terms of art and animation. Now I described in my review of "Ecchi na Onee-chan ni Shiboraretai" that the art had this 'indescribable shininess and immense contrast to it', that made it lower than a 10/10. Well... doesn't Joshi Luck! have a similar coloring scheme? Yes it does. However, I feel that Joshi Luck! handles this better because the art style itself has a more unrealistic undertone to it which makes it work so well with the "indescribable shininess' I said earlier. Animation is top notch and choreography of the shmex scenes is also amazing as well.

Sound: 10/10.
Both VAs of the leads for this hentai and the other girls all perform exceptionally. BG music and such is superb too.

Character: 5/10.
This low-score honestly just has to do with what characters were given screen time compared to others (I'll talk about this later). But a flaw I have with this is that, the two girls who are the center of attention for the series: Kise and Yuri don't get much time to shine when we don't look at the shmex scenes. Kise has a bold personality but I feel that she barely gets any time to be even shown to the rest of the audience. Yuri does suffer less as she gets more time to develop but ultimately, she could've had more time too. But by far my biggest flaw with this section is how the other three girls get NO shmex scenes whatsoever. Look, if there is more episodes that get released, or another season is announced I'll bite my words and except my mistake. But as of right now this was a horrid decision. The person who was on the cover, Aya does not even get a single sexual moment and the other two are discluded as well. Look, if you are going to make a harem MAKE SURE THAT IT IS A HAREM, not a half-assed attempt at it. There probably will be more for this series but that does not ignore the fact that more than half the cast went underutilized.

Enjoyment: 9/10.
Ignoring all of my complainants I really enjoyed Joshi Luck! as a hentai. It had amazing art and animation and even better directing for the shmex scenes. For "enjoyment" scores I tend to not look at story at all as I feel that does not dictate my enjoyment. I do however look at the characters as they are an integral part to the hentai, which is why my enjoyment score is not a 10. Both Kise and Yuri were phenomenal... but if more time was spent to polish them they could've been even better!

Overall: 6.5/10.
Overall Joshi Luck! is a decent hentai. I'd say my standing as far as recommending goes for the hentai is similar to "Ecchi na Onee-chan ni Shiboraretai". As a reviwer I have many flaws with it, but as a lover of hentai I can't find myself saying anything to you but: GO WATCH IT NOW! It may have its flaws, but I still loved many things about it and I am sure many of you will too!

Joshi Luck! is a short OVA series by Bunnywalker. And when I say short, I mean really short. This series isn't bad but I can't say I was that engrossed either. Also, just before I start this review I'd like to preface; I haven't read the manga, so this is purely based off the anime adaptation. With that out of the way, let's begin!

The "story" of this OVA series isn't much of a story, I can't really pin down much of a plot. From what's shown the main protagonist coaches a group of high school girls. And like most high school girls, they're rather uncooperative, and our protagonist has some bad luck. Cue standard hentai plot.

The art is pretty good, although I do have some gripes. Some of the faces can look rather derpy at times. And there're some shots that look really flat and overly 2d. But most of the characters look fine, the color palate is vibrent, and the motion is good.

The sound is fine. The voice actors are expressive without being overly loud. And the audio does its job well enough to keep the episodes from becoming sleep-inducing.

In addition to our bland-as-toast protagonist there're several girls as well. I think they're all supposed to have unique personalities, but you'd be forgiven for thinking otherwise. Since there're only two 16 minute long episodes there's not much room for character development. Given these constraints, Bunnywalker probably made a smart move, and decided to focus each episode on one girl. Of course this leaves several of them with next to no screen time. I wish there were more episodes so everyone could get focus, but alas, tis not to be it seems. Of the girls that get significant screen time, they're alright, nothing special but not bad.

In fact that pretty much sums up my thoughts on this series as a whole. It's not bad, but it's nothing special. I enjoyed it somewhat, the second episode more so than the first one. But it left me feeling like there wasn't much. 5.5/10 (I'd give the first episode a 5 and the second one a 6.)

Overall I still think you should watch this, especially if it gets a sequel, but temper your expectations.

I love sports anime and you know when I heard this was a lacrosse hentai, I won’t lie that there was an inkling of hope in me that it would embody some of the sports anime energy that gets me so pumped. Looking back, I was a real idiot to have hope because believe it or not a lacrosse hentai barely has any sports scenes and its just plain fucking. This is just another run-of-the-mill hentai with minimal extra content between the sex and its just kinda boring despite what a colorful cast of characters would have you believe.

Story : 5

Basically this hentai has the barest semblance of a plot where we have an introduction to a situation leading to a conflict or problem that is resolved in the final act usually via fucking. If that sounds boring than as a person constantly immersed in hentai then yeah it definitely is boring. The hentai takes an episodic approach with the first episode serving to introduce us to each character in an overplayed narrated sequence going over each character’s personality, which is frankly just lazy, then leading to fucking and we just get more of the same in the second episode.

It is just bland and uninspired as I have definitely seen better approaches to an ensemble cast of characters and being able to use every character effectively such as Eroge! H mo Game mo Kaihatsu Zanmai. Given such a great lineup of characters that were unique in your own way, it felt like a waste not to use them all by cutting down on the sex scenes and giving us more variation which I believe even the most degraded coomers wouldn’t be opposed to as it just creates a more exciting experience for everyone.

Art and Animation : 7.5

Surprisingly this hentai is quite well-made despite my qualls regarding the basic plot structure and overall content of the show. Nothing feels too repetitive and it has a kinetic energy about the scenes that help you feel a little alive in such a dull viewing experience. While I still hold up my stance regarding motion blur in hentai, especially in Master Piece the Animation, I will admit it is done well here. I’m not sure what exactly differentiates this approach to Master Piece but it didn’t give me a headache which I would consider a vast improvement. If I were to take a shot in the dark, it would be that there was a general rhythm to each scene that I could follow and didn’t disorient me.

Furthermore, the character designs are quite well-done and no two characters feel alike which enhances the voice acting as paired together they embody a well-defined character. All of them have a distinct style and seeing as first impressions are important they help reinforce each identity to a great extent.

All in all, this hentai definitely put in some effort visually and is a stark contrast to most hentai that have this “blurry” style which really takes me out of the experience most of the time. I would say the first episode left a greater impact visually with the night time setting and every gleaming surface enhanced by that midnight backdrop.

Characters : 7.5

Imma be real with you here chief, I watched the show yesterday and besides the two main girls most of the characters left my mind so while on first viewing they all individually stuck out to me, by no means were the characters ultra memorable. Of all the girls, I felt that Kise had the greatest character arc with a decent resolution but it felt like every other girl was left to the wayside, including episode 2’s star Momoi. Within this group, there are a dynamic bunch of characters from the tsundere to the kuudere to the childish tomboy with this diversity accentuating the identity of each other. There was great potential for an exploration of each character or even storylines involving multiple girls at the same time which would’ve also given us deeper insight into the characters through their interactions. Instead we got nothing.

Kise and Momoi feel like they’re on their own islands apart from the other girls who serve as minor characters which they aren’t set up to feel like at all. Momoi has the weakest storyline with her development being “sensei fucked, horny gone”. Meanwhile Kise had a rather strong arc as she went on to defy character expectations of, even me the viewer, along with the sensei through which experience the hentai. We got a peek into her internal struggle of fitting the mould she was cast into as well as her true identity which comes to a head when she forces her sensei to be the first person to acknowledge that side of her own she had ever known. It is a great subversion of a hentai/anime character tropes and actually makes the audience develop a bond with the character.

I’d also like to give a shout out to the voice actors because they exceeded what was expected of them for this being a hentai. Each girl got their own distinct voice and despite this hentai’s attempts to sabotage its own character development, with the character designs and VAs genuine effort they established a strong foundation for each character that shone through, so props to them.

Sports Anime Spirit : .5

I mean they showed some sports at the end and showed the equipment as well as kinda explaining some of the roles in lacrosse but overall there was nothing here for a sports anime fan like myself. Like come on, don’t cheapen the breakthrough of a character’s internal struggle to overcome a wall they hit by “do thing once and you’re good to go”. I don’t know what I was expecting but with the lowest of expectations I was thoroughly disappointed that they didn’t give me anything.

Enjoyment : 6

Overall this hentai is fine with good characters, one somewhat compelling character arc, great voice acting and above average animation. If you’re looking for something deeper or any actions besides fucking you’ll be hard pressed to find it here, but if you just need to get your rocks off then be my guest. You won’t regret picking this up, it's a very safe bet and you’ll part ways with it amicably.
