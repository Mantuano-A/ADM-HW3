Abuse comes in many forms and Baka na Imouto wo Rikou ni Suru no wa Ore no xx dake na Ken ni Tsuite is a work of art that deals with the controversial topic of sibling and disability abuse.

Hana Mitsui is the main protagonist's sexually attractive but mentally disabled younger sister whose voice is out of sync with the rest of her body's development during puberty. Hana's character and interaction with her brother challenges many of society's views of disability and can be interpreted in ways both positive and negative to the portrayal of disabled people.

Many can agree that among the reasons for the (il)legal status of pedophilia in developed countries is so that children who don't know much about how the world works won't be deceived or manipulated by people such as the main character of this anime, who tricks his unknowing sister into believing that his sperm is actually milk which can cure her absurdly low IQ and/or mental illness. The plot of the story can be considered rather offensive to those mental disabilities due to the protagonist's outright usage of her imouto, heavily implying that disabled people and fe-males are only valued for their contributions to a male dominated society. While not an outright fact, it's my personal belief that the protagonist is also a bad role model for children watching this series due to the way he manipulates his little sister and could possibly harm her in the process. This wouldn't be so bad if the character development wasn't so non-existent and the main character learned the consequences of his behaviour as a lesson to any children watching the show, but as it is, he seems extremely satisfied and completely unrepentful of his actions, showing kids that these actions are acceptable in society and encourages them to abuse their siblings in a similar fashion.

However it could also be argued that the protagonist's actions are very progressive towards the disability rights movement. The Pet Girl of Sakura House is a show that deals with very similar issues, how a boy accommodates for an attractive, mentally disabled girl. Many people would argue that Sakurasou depicts mentally disabled people in a much more respectful manner, however Sakurasou depicts mentally disabled people as people who should be shunned from society, coddled by those who know what they're doing because they'd be a threat to themselves and others otherwise. On the other hand, Baka na Imouto, while it does portray the manipulation of disabled people, attempts to integrate disability within regular society, doing things that any other person would do, not giving them special, extra kind treatment just because of their disability. So while Sakurasou keeps the disabled out of harms way (and the public view), Baka na Imouto respectfully attempts to show us that mentally ill people are just like us, and deserved to be treated as equals, not like incompetent babies.

Despite the deep message this anime tries to impress upon us, potential viewers should be aware that the art is rather generic for an anime, no special VFX, or flashy sequences. I also think the character designs don't match their intended age accurately at all, in addition to absurd amounts of bodily fluid being released compared to what would happen in real life. The show's audio is also rather lacking, there's no great pieces of music composed for this show (or any at all), and the voice actors seem fairly low effort and don't really put much emotion into their characters.
