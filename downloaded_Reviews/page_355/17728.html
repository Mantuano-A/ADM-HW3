The first thing you notice about this ero anime is how different the character designs look from your standard "hentai" fare. This is the only ero anime I've seen that uses the "moé" style. That is to say, the characters look similar to the heroines in popular anime such as K-on! or Clannad. This made a huge difference in the enjoyability of watching this for me because the portrayal of the female body in hentai normally comes off as either cheap looking or simply ridiculous. Mai and Reo are neither—in fact, they're probably the only characters from a hentai that I would consider cute, and that's saying a lot. The production values are also higher than your standard hentai as well, although this may be because it is only one episode. Regardless, the important parts are animated fluidly and there are times when you even may forget that you're watching an ero anime due to the smoothness of the intermediary animation. This was a tremendous relief to me, as I had almost gotten used to seeing characters in hentai move around like paper cutouts on popsicle sticks.

The story is there, and from what I understand is adapted from a visual novel. I have not read the visual novel, but there is enough tension to properly set up the action so that it's not just wham bam thank you ma'am. That being said, I don't usually expect much of a plot in porn to begin with. However, the confession scene and the "nursing back to health" scenario work very well at conveying the tenderness between Mai and Reo. The story is also devoid of rape, demons from hell, old men, aliens, and all the other banalities you may be prompted to think about when you hear the word "hentai." It was a refreshing change for me.

The soundtrack consists of violins, piano, and in one particular scene some rather err, "celestial" sounding synth choruses. It is nothing to break the mood, although the "climax" of the story may come off as a bit too... grandiose. If there is a weak point in this ero anime, it would have to be the soundtrack as it was overly conspicuous at times and ultimately forgettable.

On the other side of the audio, the two characters Mai and Reo are excellently voiced, if only because they avoid the irritating wailing and groan-worthy pillow talking common in other hentai. The sense of hesitation, embarrassment, and at the same time longing in the dialogue is also carried through superbly. As for the personalities of these two characters, Reo falls on the "tsundere" side of the spectrum while Mai plays the slightly more cool-headed, but persistent-enough-to-take-the-lead "onee-chan." The dynamic between the two plays out wonderfully.

The main enjoyment of this ero anime is not derived from hardcore depravity as it is with other hentai. It is more of a drawn-out, passive warmth than a quick and dirty flash. Of course, this may lead some to call it "boring." And admittedly, it's not for everyone. Yuri fans, however, needn't ask the question any longer—the answer is "Yes! Watch it!"

Ah... I thoroughly enjoy the Sono Hanabira series of visual novels (as of present, there are ten mainline VNs, half of which are translated, and the spin-offf Hanahira!), sowhen I first noticed this, I knew I had to download it. Well, after about a full year and a half after the fact, it is time to impart my thoughts upon MAL.

First off, as a warning, this is a yuri hentai, so make sure this is what you are looking for. Also, the ratings reflect this, too.

First, I should handle the intrinisical elements:

STORY (6/10)

Now, being a hentai, story usually is not the main concern. In fact, all we get from this is situational framing for sex scenes and a flashback to show how the two main characters, Reo Kawamura and Mai Sawaguchi, got together, and it doesn't even explain what happened beforehand. However, you then have to consider that this OVA is likely meant to be supplementary material for those who have played through the visual novels (specifically, the third, and you can watch the third without playing the first and second). When put into that context, this is more like a retelling of a scene with some additions, so focusing on plot likely wasn't a priority. Still, it would have been nice if they included more background for the uninitiated.

CHARACTER (10/10)

I've played a good number of the Sono Hanabira VNs, and I can assure you, the characterization of both Mai and Reo has been kept intact. In fact, some may say that the characterization is actually more realistic, considering that some people would see Mai's and Reo's dialog in the VNs as a slight flanderization of their personality type. In case you're wondering, Reo is somewhat shy, in addition to fitting most characteristics of a type A Tsundere. As for Mai, she's still pretty much the pushy flirt that tries to get Reo more active in their surroundings. But what perhaps is the most important aspect is the relationship itself.

Sono Hanabira, unlike at least 90% of all hentai and eroge available, takes a lot of time and develops a deep relationship between characters through a sweet intimacy that, from what I've heard, can only be matched in yuri hentai by Shoujo Sect ~Innocent Lovers~. Yes, they may have a LOT of sex, but its painfully obvious they care for each other, as the intimate moments speak enough of such. Then again, the way the scene shots were sequenced help as well.

ART (10/10)

I will tell you right now, forget about the average art quality you would expect from most hentai, because the artwork for this one OVA will blow the rest out of the water (yes, even Milk Junkies and Striingendo). The color pallete goes excellently with the attitude the OVA is trying to bring, and the line detail is enough to compete with stuff by KyoAni. Additionally, whoever directed screenplay for this OVA deserves a gold medal, because the way the screens created progression, it cut down on unnecessary fanservice and intensifies the intimacy between Mai and Reo, be it cuddling or making sure they are both equally satisfied with each other. In fact, I dare someone to find a hentai that does better.

SOUND (10/10)

One of the things that I like to see is when people keeps things simplistic and beautiful. The soundtrack, being comprised of only a few simple piano pieces, pulls off that task admirably well. I may like a complicated soundtrack for stuff like K-ON!, but leaving a background piano enhances the atmosphere being drawn up by the art and action. As for the seiyuu... I don't feel I can make a good judgment on such.

If you want something different from your every-day, run-of-the-mill, conquer them all hentai, this is most likely your ticket. The only taste that will be left in your mouth is a light sweetness, so if you're into that kind of feeling, enjoy.
