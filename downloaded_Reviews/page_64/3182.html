HOLY HELL THANK YOU MAL FOR ADDING MILGRAM ONTO THE SITE.

Theme and Story Interpretation spoilers ahead

For those of you who don't know about this legendary music video series, MILGRAM, it's about the stories of 10(?) individuals who have committed 'murder'. Yes, that's right. 'Murder'. Every music video is told from the perspective of the 'murderer' and is rich with symbolism and contains some of the darkest backstories you'll find on the planet. Because the stories are told by someone who has committed a crime, it might make them an unreliable narrator you can judge~ In fact, if you keep track of the latest music video, you can go on ahead on the milgram.jp website and cast a vote on whether you think the person in the video should be 'forgiven' or voted 'guilty'. That decision will affect what happens to them in the next trial. Their story will be 'altered' in a way I can only wonder about! Just know that a guilty vote may end up being their 'death'.

As for the sound, oh my my. The music hits you like a truck (no isekai pun intended) with some of the best J-pop songs I've heard in my life and I have literally 1000+ songs in my YouTube playlist. These J-pop songs can either resonate so deep in terms of the struggles the characters go through or bring awareness to an issue that is not talked about often in some of the most subtle ways possible. In order to truly appreciate this series, you will want to rewatch the music videos, dissect the lyrics, as well as pause a lot for anything of interest~ I've literally replayed After Pain, half, Undercover, Bring It On!, and Weakness at least 100 times respectively! I will admit I'm rather garbage at symbol interpretation at times so I peek at the YouTube comments for other people's thoughts. You will be delighted to discover that on the milgram.jp site, there's a vast amount of lore that gives you information on a character's behavior and personality and how that hints to their murder. It is absolutely the best music video series I have ever seen. It's free to watch on the MILGRAM YouTube channel, so please take a look!

About to spoil the content of all the videos and Mu's story/interpretation right now, so there's no turning back from losing out on the mystery once you read the next 4 paragraphs!


In no particular order, this music video series tackles individuals who have caused the death of another as a result of: cancel culture, toxic relationships, child neglect, cult brainwashing, possibly death penalty, infidelity, bullying, abortion, and organ trafficking. It is absolutely fantastic at hinting at these themes without being easy to see. The best part about it is the moral ambiguity and the divide between the audience. Is this crime something you can forgive? It's a democracy trial where the public votes whether the circumstance leading up to 'murder' are justifiable or not. I highly encourage you at least take a listen cause you'll honestly be chilled to the core after hearing Undercover, the first video.

Undercover is about the non-binary prison guard, Es, and their introduction to all the criminals in the series. If you're taken away by the lyrics of Es's song, you'll notice Es's struggle of whether or not to forgive the prisoners for their sins. The song is absolutely fire.

Additionally, starting with Haruka and Yuno, the second and third video, there is an interesting pattern between Haruka and Yuno and every following pair. They are opposites of the same spectrum. There are pairs who have different experiences with: bullying, knowing why they are being charged for their 'crime', promises, loneliness, and love.

I want to talk about the After Pain song in particular because it is my favorite music video of all time. It's about a girl named Mu, a victim of bullying. The metaphor is very clear as she is trapped in an hourglass with liquid falling down until she drowns in her own despair, until her mental health is damaged beyond repair by the 'friends' who exploit her. Based on the images revealed from the music video, it's evident Mu is not fighting back, likely because her bullies are her closest 'friends'. I will admit I do not think it's completely clear what the cause of bullying is, but the reason is irrelevant. It is clear she is being taken advantage of for her status or wealth which makes her an easy target. The lyrics indicate Mu self-blames herself for the awful things that has happened to her as she is unable to handle the problem on her own until she completely breaks. There are indications of Mu's suicidal tendencies in the lyrics when singing about "postmortem makeup to hide my heart" and "the stabbing of the little devil's voice" as well as the subtly hidden noose imagery within the hourglass. When she finally works up the courage to ask for help from another girl, Mu's love interest, Mu's world shatters. When Mu pleas for help, the rejection by her last hope leads to the hourglass breaking and the murder of the only person Mu loved in that moment. The help she didn't receive was more infuriating to Mu than any bully who hurt her. It's very clear what the message of this music video is: "The bystander is worse than the bully." The words ""I'm sorry" can't be heard by anyone" repeat at the crime scene and the song ends with the same chilling lyrics it started with:

♪If I was gone,
If I had just disappeared
I overheard,
I found out
How much I'm not needed♪

The passion in the singer, Arisa Kori and her voice, broke my heart so many times after so many re-listens. I used to cry on a regular listening basis because of how heart-breaking this story is. I know there are other well-done music videos in the MILGRAM series, but I wanted to give a shoutout to this one because it's something I hold personally significant.

Please listen. I promise, you won't regret it :,)

So, after 14 eps:
1 - "This is Milgram" (kind of prologue)
2 - "Character Voice Trailer" (Presenting all the characters)
3 - "Es - Undercover" (Music from the Judge/Warden)
4 - "The Song Of Prisioners" (Sample of all the prisioners first musics)
5 - 14 - Full MVs from the prisioners except for the 13th which is a cover of the 4th prisioner music by the Judge/Warden

What I have to say is, this is an amazing project, with amazing music and excellent short stories, but better than that, is the interactivity it has with the viewers, in which, "us", the viewer, work has the Judge/Warden, and can vote in the official website if we think the character is guilty or innocent.

Even though my ratings are high, I´m giving an overall of 7, since this is an anime review, and as an anime, it lacks the same kind of dialogue seen in episode 1, hope that gets adressed later when all the first trials are done with some kind of dialogue between the Judge/Warden and each of the prisioners before their next song.
