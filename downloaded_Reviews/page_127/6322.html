tldr: The animators are a bunch of gumbies.

Climbing is a very technical and subtle sport - unlike football, where the team plays out clear tactics and strategies that can be explained and visualised relatively simply, climbing is more to do with balance, body positioning and coordination. Now, these things could be impressed upon an audience but would require pristine animation and direction which, unfortunately 'Sport Climbing Girls' doesn't have a lot of...

It seems to me that whoever thought up the whole premise knew something about climbing but the staff actually making the anime haven't a clue: puzzle games to climbing is surprisingly more realistic than it sounds - 50% of climbing is problem solving, working out the correct and most efficient sequence of moves to reach the top; lead climbing takes a while to learn and can't be done without training as is shown in the anime, the girls back clip which can be potentially very dangerous, their footwork is all over the place and body positioning is generally not shown very well.
And as with all sports anime, the Miss MC is stupidly op. I don't know much about other sports but in climbing it takes a very long time to get good; it uses muscles not often used in other sports and building finger strength takes years - if you go too hard your tendons WILL pop (trust me) - so sending a V6 highball after a month and a bit training is stupidly unrealistic.
The competition setting is fairly unrealistic too. The climbing community is generally very supportive and friendly which isn't really represented in the anime.

From a generic anime standpoint it's pretty meh. The animation is bland and uninteresting, the character design is whack and the story is pretty generic - mc wants to be best in japan, makes friends and rivals along the way - but if all you're looking for is naked feet and tight fitting sportswear with convenient camera angles this anime is for you. Hopefully it gets people interested in climbing like Dumbbell did for weight lifting but I'm afraid it's sub-par in that regard.

Who should watch this show? People who are into CGDCT, people who are curious about climbing or enjoy outdoor activities in general. This is a quick review.

"Iwakakeru Sports Climbing Girls" is an interesting show, it tries to explore a relatively unknown sports called "Rock Climbing". I absolutely had no idea that even this game is played on highschool level. Rock climbing, well as the name suggests, is about climbing rocks. To achieve this, you need to have strong muscles as well as strong observation skill to know where you are stepping in next. You need to be physically strong as well as have to develop strong mentality. It interests me to this level, that I want to learn how a player can gain admirable skills to achieve his goals, aka reaching the peak.

That's of few positives this show has carried so far.

To talk about the negatives, just reminding you it is an anime, means you are getting to use both audio and visuals for your storytelling. Unfortunately, Iwakakeru fails in both category. Visuals are cheap, and it doesn’t help elevating its storytelling. Audio is rather forgettable. Rather good visuals, we get some good fanservice. Muscled girls with strong legs and busts, it is sexually arousing. The focus on cleavage and butts can easily distract you to concern with the characters.

Rather it's disturbing. Characters themselves aren’t anything special. Lead protagonist,Kasahara Konomi is an ex hikikomori, and a national champion on Candy Crash 🙂 with enrolling into highschool she wants to change herself. Why she wants to change her lifestyle, why she ran out of interests with mobile games, is surprisingly unknown. We are just asked to believe it since everyone knows what development means to a hikikomori. The 2nd main girl, Jun (voiced by Ishikawa Yui) is so serious on rock climbing that she poses strong problems to newcomers in order to prevent any vagrant kids to join. Like many characters voiced by Ishikawa, she is another emotionless teen ager whose asked development will be, learning emotions. Amongst the 4 main girls the president attracts me the most, I feel there are lot more hidden with her abilities and it is engaging to know more about her. Side characters are forgettable too, except for one that's so scary to look at and another (voiced by Dio) who is the only male character in the show and is too hot for any girls, despite being a father of a 10 year old girl.

Animation, is quite good compared to its low effort on visuals. There's a cut in first episode where Konomi tries to solve a rock climbing route using her knowledge with Candy Crash which was impressive. Camera movement is nothing special, usually it stays focused on busts and butts, though it moves and that makes it better than most ecchi shows.


My final verdict for this show is it brings something new to table, it's refreshing to know more on athletic sports and how it ensures growth in its players, how they become a better human being and how it helps us to understand more varieties among humankind......but It's ugly character design, bad visuals and forgettable soundtracks surpassed the few good things. You can say it has good concept but the execution is lackluster.

I don't normally write reviews, but I wanted to for this series. I would like to hopefully encourage people to try it themselves before writing it off, as with only one episode left to watch when it airs, I've enjoyed the show quite a bit.

I'm not going to rate the show by different categories, as I tried that and it sounded boring, but I did find it more enjoyable seemingly than many other reviewers here did, and wanted to express that.

It doesn't stand out too much in any area, and I doubt it will join anyone's list of favorite anime, but I've found it surprisingly entertaining and a fun watch so far at episode 11. I enjoy many of the characters, the arcs that some of them have had, and the actual climbing parts are very engaging to me, despite my complete lack of background in or knowledge or the sport. Although the climbing is not shown with too much detail I imagine compared to the real sport (I don't feel like I've gained a whole lot of knowledge while watching or anything), the characters' motivations and stories help make it entertaining.

Also in case the poster art made you think of avoiding it because it would have heavy fanservice, fear not. I actually don't think the fanservice is very heavy at all - despite some angles while climbing seeming a bit that way, the show itself seems quite intent on depicting the characters' stories seriously and not undermine that with leering camera angles the majority of the time.

One moment that made me a bit uncomfortable and wish it hadn't been included was *SPOILERS* one of the characters - who is played as a bit creepy, but in a funny way - punishing a climber who tried to sabotage a promising newbie. She creeps up behind her and scares her by not letting her collar go (and also being creepy is kind of her gimmick, but usually in a pretty harmless way), then when the girl insults her, she says "Come with me to the dressing rooms... I'll give you a good dressing down there." She then drags the scared girl away with her while creepy music plays, while the girl screams things like "Spare me!" This is the most uncomfortable moment in the show in my opinion, (with the exception of one more moment I will mention next), so unless you're very sensitive to this type of "joke", it's quite easy to look past in my opinion. It's just a shame, as if they'd just left out the threat of being forcibly undressed, it would've just been a funny scene where someone got payback for trying to sabotage a fellow climber, but as it is, it's a small blemish on the show in my opinion.

The other uncomfortable moment in my opinion was *SPOILERS* when a character is called "chubby" with no visible chubbiness to speak of. This happened in the middle of a training episode, and while the character definitely needed extra muscle to be able to compete, she never appeared even close to "chubby". To add insult to injury, I can't even tell a difference between her weight before and after she gets stronger, so not only is it worrying seeing a visibly quite thin girl being told she's "chubby", her body when fit is drawn the exact same anyway, indicating it looks plenty thin enough to be fit for competition before. This is another part that I just have to sigh and ignore, as it is only a few times in the episode it's mentioned, and weight issues, from memory, are never mentioned again outside this episode, so it's quite easy to pretend this is her teammates positively encouraging her to gain muscle and increase her fitness, rather than making someone think they're overweight when they're perfectly thin already.


Overall, I don't think you should enter this show expecting to love it, as I don't think its appeal is that broad or strong, but I started it just wanting a simple show about a niche sport, and I came to really like it. To me, it's one of those shows you don't really include with your favorites, but because they were such a pleasant surprise, you become a bit of a special series anyway, and I hope this might encourage others to give it a go and maybe come to like it as well.
