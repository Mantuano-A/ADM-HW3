The Story of 15 Beautiful Girls. A title like that has to spark some kind of thoughts in any mans head.

Its always refreshing to watch an h-anime like this one, something that doesn't make you feel the slightest bit sad, empty, or rage towards the animation. If the viewer does happen to feel anything it would be envy towards the sole male character, wishing that you were the one in heaven surrounded by 15 angels. As a viewer, you can immerse yourself in your wild fantasies and just place yourself in the unrealistically pleasurable scenarios. It certainly is one of the benefits of actively watching shows in this genre, your imagination in this area tends too become more creative.
But in the end H-anime like this are always a nice watch, because they almost always have a fun, laughable, or amusing ending.

Now onto what i think of this particular H-anime. 15 Bishoujo Hyouryuuki certainly was one of the more cheerful and amusing hentai i have seen. the plot was very linear, but the only plot that revealed to the viewer is that there are 16 people who get stranded on an island, only one of whom is a male. I never base my score on H-anime on the plot to begin with. The idea of rating a H-anime based on its plot is pointless, these kind of anime aren't made for their plot, there made for their artwork, so why rate them on something that isn't even focused on by the makers. What i base my scores for H-anime on is the quality of the art, voices/sounds, and how satisfied i was at the end of it all.

The art for this one was great, but sometimes the whole characters body would move as a whole, like she was floating in air, which was really odd, but usually i didn't notice it too much, due to the constant camera changes.

the voices were good, but mainly because they didn't repeat the same thing over and over and over again.

'15 Bishoujo Hyouryuuki' is a hentai with a very believable story. 15 girls stranded on a desolate island with a single guy... and you know he's going to take advantage of that. Now how is that realistic? Well it turns out there is one realm where dreams come true and I've already said too much there.

That said, the plot isn't really that complex and switches from one girl to another rather quickly. So yes, the story is believable but at the same time shallow and lacking in substance. What 'Bishoujo Hyouryuuki' does have however is flawless comedy. This show has the knack of justifying any sexual scenario and enabling the MC to have sex with all the other characters through comedic means. And while some of them might have been predictable, most have actually caught me off guard. A prudent scientist character who doesn't really want to get involved in any inter-personal relationships? No way, right? Turns out she gets captured by a jelly monster and now the MC and her need to mix bodily fluids to defeat it. Yeah, I'll leave the rest to your imagination. This show is filled with such surprising and indeed, eyebrow raising moments.

The characters themselves are what you'd expect a character in most hentai to be like. Full focus on sex and that's about it. That said, the artwork compensates for the apparent shallowness. Designs are fantastic, animations are fluid and change constantly, i.e you won't see the same scene repeat for 5 minutes and see it reused in the subsequent episodes. Everything here is top-notch when it comes to animation.

The sound is something else entirely. This hentai features an opening and ending song. WHAT? First time I'm seeing that (and hearing it too). Even more surprisingly, it's quite catchy. Voice acting is indeed very good and the ambient sounds of waves, trees, sand etc. are all here. Superbly done.

All in all, a great mix of comedy & stunning artwork and sound. The story is believable but superficial, though what it lacks in depth it more than makes up for in sheer comedy value. Characters are shallow - that has to be said, however I wouldn't really expect anything else out of a hentai like this so they get a good rating nonetheless. There's simply not enough time to delve any deeper into their stories etc. and why would you. It's a hentai.

Not a masterpiece like "Hatsu Inu" where even the story was done flawlessly but definitively a good watch. Recommended to hentai connoisseurs and casual watchers alike.

Have you ever watched something and had this nagging feeling in your mind, almost like something is... off?

Well, that's how I felt watching 15 Bishoujo Hyouryuuki.

This three part OVA series was made by T-Rex, a studio that generally makes decent hentai. Nothing spectacular, but nothing abhorrent either. And 15 something ... something falls into this category. It's frustratingly average, or at least it would be if I was even slightly invested in it.

But enough with the preamble, let's dig in!

The "story" is fairly simple. A guy and 15 young women are stranded on an uninhabited island. Several working cameras wash up on the island over the course of the story, and the guy uses them to capture the girls doing sexy things. Each girl has a fairy distinct personality. There're lolis, athletic girls, androids, fox-girls, scientist girls, milfs, and plenty more. The only problem with these characters is how little they're used. Cramming 15 girls into three 20 minute episodes goes about as well as you'd expect.

The sex scenes are pretty much all the same, they might be different on paper, but they all sorta blend together. As a result, it's the personality of the girls that really drives the scenes. This isn't a bad thing in my opinion, but it does mean that your enjoyment will largely depend on what characters you like. For me personally, I liked the first and third episodes, but not the second.

The art in this OVA series is pretty good. The characters are all very well designed, and I found the chibi elements to be quite cute. If I do have one complaint, it would be that the animation is a little stiff, but I personally didn't mind. The voice actors also did a decent job. I was surprised to see that this OVA had an opening song, as that isn't very common for hentai. It isn't anything special, but it's still a nice touch.

Overall, I enjoyed 15 Bishoujo Hyouryuuki. It's well produced and has well designed characters, but it still feels like it's missing something. This probably could've used more episodes... 6/10

So... would I reccomend you watch this OVA series? Yes, but there're probably other hentai more deserving of your attention.

SUMMARY
A quick paced anime which hops from one mini love scene to another, story is kept to a minimal but well worth a watch for those unfamiliar with the genre.

STORY
A young man finds himself marooned on a small island with nothing but girls for company and a camcorder to keep a diary of everything he gets up to.

He quickly seduces each of his female companions, three per episode for a total of three episodes.

Character building is nonexistent, none of the girls appear again after their scenes and each segment feels more like a stand alone mini-movie.

VISUALS and EROTICS
The anime has a pretty good look. all the girls are quite hot in a vanilla way and although censored it doesn't feel too obtrusive. Overall it isn't anything to get too excited about but it serves its purpose well.

The rest of the scenery is either a beach or jungle, it is hardly worth talking about.

HIGHLIGHT MOMENT
The second scene in the third episode which features to robot nurses was pretty enjoyable to watch. But each scene is all pretty consistent in quality.

CONCLUSION
This is a pretty good introduction hentai anime which dips its toes into almost every vanilla sub genre of hentai, it is pretty fast paced and manages to keep things fresh over the three episodes. As a stand alone anime it really lacks any story or character development, but if it is seen as compilation for new hentai fans, then it really holds its own.

OVERALL
