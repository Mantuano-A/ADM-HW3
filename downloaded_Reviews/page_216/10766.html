HEY! Listen!

“Listeners” is fucking garbage, and in most cases, I’d say you’d have to see it to believe it but I pray no one besides myself has to lay themselves bare to this level of unfiltered drek.

If the litmus test for a half-decent anime screen-writer is having listened to the musical stylings of Jimi Hendrix, Prince, and Pink Floyd (among many...many others), then you can consider most dads in the continental United States accomplished enough to pen something equal to or better than Listeners. This isn’t a show for music nerds or people who can appreciate the hallowed halls of surface-level dad-rock, no, no, Listeners is a bit more sinister than that. Listeners is 12 straight episodes of some smug prick patting himself on the back for having listened to the most bare-essential “classics” in the history of popular music. You like Nirvana? So does the rest of the world, dude.

Besides its flashy concept and presentation, Listeners is one giant nothingburger of an anime. Its intentions are pretty damn transparent, to the point where its disjointed narrative throws caution to the wind in order to fulfill the fucking reference quota in any given episode. The dialogue in this script simply acts as a placeholder for more fucking references. I shit you not, entire sequences of conversation in this show can be boiled down to two Talking Heads (see, I can make references too) reciting famous song lyrics to each other in place of substantive discourse, exposition, or character interaction. I’ve seen some call this “impressive” for sheer virtue of the fact that you have to frame this in a manner that both visually and narratively fits the story… PUHLEASE! “We don’t need no education”, frame that entire lyric on a shallow vignette that barely touches base with what Roger Waters was intending when he wrote that it and make it a slice of life school anime, that's Listeners' brand of storytelling. It’s not hard people!

Mu, of course, the mascot character and strong female deuteragonist. Another mysterious Mary McGuffin who lacks social graces, sports a bitchy attitude, and is generally unlikable by most standards. Complete with a nebulous past that is somehow directly involved in the plot, lovely, haven’t had enough of those. But she wouldn’t be complete without “Echo”, what a lovely character. An intrepid young lad with an appetite for (reconstruction?). These two may as well just be Kamijou Touma and Index but even they had better chemistry than these two buffoons. Your average character interaction between them will generally involve Echo regurgitating essential world-building and Mu calling Echo a weirdo for doing so, brilliant.

I get that this show is supposed to be some kind of kitschy tribute to artists of old but there’s little to no consistency in terms to what it chooses to riff that the references at best come off like a Spotify playlist of rock essentials. The world of Listeners suggests a reverence for rockstars, but the references wouldn’t lead you to believe so. This makes me question references to MBV and Robert Johnson, I mean does the type of music Kevin Shields produces exactly scream “rock star?” People under the supposition that this show has consistency in terms of what music it chooses to pay tribute to are gravely mistaken. Additionally, artists like Kurt Cobain who famously repudiated this degree of blind idolatry have flagrant references strewn about like kitschy decor, which is stunningly ironic.

It would be one thing if all these on the nose winks and nods to popular music acts from back in the day were for the sake of aesthetic (take Jojo’s for example), but it couldn’t be that simple, this shit is intrinsically tied to the narrative. Everything from “Teen Spirit” to “The Wall” has some form of off-the-cuff functionality in the world-building that lives and dies with whatever episode it was mentioned in. Also, I have to question the rationale behind making the Jimi Hendrix (widely recognized as one of if not THE single most important black musician in the history of music) stand-in, a generic pasty-skinned rock god catch-all. What kind of optics are these? Who the fuck co-signed this shit? The same dude who tried to cast Julia Roberts as Harriet Tubman?

The CG models for the mechs are chunky and distractingly out of place which makes for some of the most awkward action sequences I’ve seen come out of an anime in this year. Give this concept to Trigger or David Productions and I’m sure they’d at least be able to cobble something together with the visual panache to distract from its cliched sensibilities.

Yeah...so...Listeners, that was a thing, I guess? Alright, listen (okay...I’ll stop), perhaps this show wasn’t made for someone like me who is cognizant of most if not all of the musicians it proudly flaunts, but I have to question if this show even really has a target audience I could recommend it to. If you’re someone who is completely oblivious to all or most of the references, what leg would this anime have to stand on? There isn’t a compelling story, not a single noteworthy character, or even just a brief cut of decent visuals that isn’t just lazily aping the music it pays tribute to. It’s just utter nonsense and sci-fi gobbledygook.

Needless to say, this anime made me foam at the mouth, and not in a good way. This show is so eye-roll inducing that my sockets have ballooned by at least a centimeter over the course of its 12 episodes. Fuck you Jin.

In a Nutshell
Set in a post-apocalyptic world that has been terrorized by shadowy creatures known as Earless for far too long, the humanity is now to battle their inhuman enemies. The series focuses on a boy called Echo, who wants to join the fight against the Earless.

FLCL on Shrooms
It is quite obvious that something like ‘a boy finds a mystery girl and together they save the world’ sounds way too overused. Here, however, the show at least gives the girl with amnesia a personality. Let us see if this show even tries to be ‘somewhat’ unique. The boy is your Takkun (FLCL), or Simon (Tengen Toppa Gurren Lagann), in other words, weak, nerdy boy, who want to escape and change something in his life. The problem, however, is that he shows even less development than Takkun and Simon, he is just your average Joe that you have seen 100000 times already. The girl, μ, has an amnesia (I am not going to stress the fact how many times it has been used in other series). She looks like a big human amplifier, do not ask me why, Japan is weird, yes. While she does have 0 recollection of what she is, she does not care about it and keeps fighting. People like her are called players, and they are known for bringing music into the world. Unfortunately, players have been banned from the town Echo lives in, because earless monsters might destroy it, if they were to cooperate with any players.

It Is Just Way Too Generic
The show does focus on music, which is something you will rarely see, ehem Macross, ehem and it does manage to be somewhat unique, but it just fails to be anywhere entertaining. A series that focuses on music, has such a weak OST, please. Listeners does use mecha fights and does it quite often, I must say. But these action scenes are just way too boring. I mean, mecha fights must be epic, mecha means epic and here they are just anything but epic.

Too Many Things Going On
Listeners is a great example of a show that tries to be everything, but fails to be good. You are introduced to the world of Listeners, however, you do not get a normal explanation about where the Earless came from, what the mechs are exactly, where Players came from, who are the Earless, what they want and so on. It is perfectly fine, if you do not want to focus on the actions scenes, no problem. But do pay enough time to the world-building and characters. Already the main protagonists, let us be honest, are nothing special, but the show introduces more and more characters that do not receive enough development, as well as the screen time for the viewer to like them, to understand what they are and what they are trying to achieve and many many more.

Not Recommended (5/10)
But it does not mean that you should not watch this series. It is not bad, it is just average. I will not try to deny the fact that it did try to be unique and it could have become quite a successful show of this season, if more attention has been paid to the characters. However, this show is just way too generic, it shows way too many things, I have seen too many times already, so seeing it one more time was not quite what I was looking for.

Spoiler-free review:

Listeners is a show that had a lot of promise. As a huge fan of the Kagerou Project and Jin, when I heard he was working on a new project I was hyped. Then when it came out, I watched the trailer and it had everything; mechs, cool character designs, and most importantly some ROCK AND ROLL!!! I was so ready for the next crazy show full of nonsense and awesomeness just like FLCL, for example. However so far, Listeners has not delivered.

I'll start with the good: the music is outstanding, the characters and mechs have really cool designs, and the classic rock references sprinkled throughout each episode are a cute touch. However these neat surface level things are overshadowed by the big problems of the show: one of them being the plot. There's a lot going on, new characters introduced every episode, but it feels too fast-paced for the viewer to really actually care at all about any of the new characters. The world building is similar, every episode jumps to a new setting/city and before the audience even gets a chance to learn anything interesting about it, the next episode just shows our main characters at the next place. Other than that, the plot is basically your average show about a threat to humanity and those who fight said threat. A more lighthearted spin on more serious post-apocalypse threat-to-humanity shows like Evangelion or Attack on Titan. I digress.

The other problem with the show is the action sequences. The show is supposed to be about Players, who fight in their mechs not only for sport but also as a means of being a bastion of hope for humanity to fight off the Earless. So one would assume the mech battles should be spectacular and the highlight of every episode. And while they are *placed* at the climax of most episodes, the animation (or lack thereof) really causes the scenes to suffer. The clunky CG looks terrible whenever they try to do anything besides fly around, and I'm sure the animators know that, so instead they get around it by having the mechs pretty much just emit light???? during the huge climaxes of these fights, and whoever emits more light wins. It's a lazy cop-out to the show's overly ambitious ideas.

Overall, Listeners is an anime I was really excited to watch, but its own ambitions have ended up becoming its downfall. The many characters, settings, and just random ideas (mechs + music + kaiju) all mashed together into one show has created some weird amalgam of a bunch of really cool ideas that all just fall short. That being said, it's still entertaining enough for me to finish the series at least. I still feel the need to watch to the end to see if our main character Echo undergoes any character development at all, and the bangin' rock n' roll and colorful art/character designs are often enough to make me overlook the terrible CG fight scenes and keep me somewhat entertained.

6/10, not going to drop but definitely not a "good" show by any means other than its soundtrack.

WARNING: MILD SPOILERS.

My entire viewing experience of this show can be described in one sentence.

I don't get it, but Mu is kinda cute.

The concept has a lot of potential, but the story suffers from less-than-average world building. Supposedly, music doesn't exist in this world, but the anime does a piss poor job of exploring that. There was something about Echo not knowing what humming is, but that's pretty much it. It might be too early to tell, but it isn't heading in a good direction.

The "Equipment":
This is one big question mark. Where do these mechs come from? How do they work? How does a Vox amp turn into a giant robot? How did some kid gather some junk and turn it into a giant robot? This is probably some sort of twist or mystery that will be explained near the end of the series, but it sure as hell doesn't feel like one. It feels more like, "Yeah whatever, anime logic."

Speaking of the mechs, they are damn ugly. Simply unappealing and tough to look at. It's not even the fact they're CG, It's just bland design that doesn't feel unique at all.

The Rock References:
It's just not for me. I don't get the references. If you do, then good for you. Every time an alleged reference comes up: best case scenario it goes over my head, worst case it's just cringe. References and easter eggs should be there to add to the experience, not to take from it.

Mu and Echo:
I have to admit, I have a strange itch for this kind of naive, youthful relationship dynamic. Some of their interactions and dialogue put a stupid grin on my face. That's kinda it. Neither character has anything special about them right now. Though, to be fair, there hasn't been time for meaningful character development this early in the series so I'm going to leave it at that.

The Art:
I like the art style. I really do. The animation is good too (Especially the opening). It's just the mechs leave such a bad taste in my mouth. For a mecha show to have generic mecha designs is incredibly disappointing.


Final Thoughts:
I really hope this show gets better. I want this concept to work. There was an interesting bit about the traumatizing aspects about being a Player, being in the army, the fear of the Earless, and resorting to drug use. I hope the serious tones of this anime get better developed. For now, If you're a Mecha fan that doesn't mind generic mecha designs and has a throbbing itch for music and rock references, then give this show a try.

The best way I can sum Listeners is: Musical references of the glorious old days, the Animation. If the show and its episode titles aren't obvious enough to make you think otherwise, then I don't know what else will do.

Music composer Jin (famous for his work on Kagerou Daze), series composer Dai Sato (famous for his screenplay on Eureka Seven), and director Hiroaki Ando (famed for the Ajin series). Put the 2 composers together and the director, and you get Listeners: a blatant excuse for a story and plot about "recapturing the art of lost musicality". A "history" so tainted that goes on for generations, it's easy to put two and two together, with an execution so bad it becomes second-rate nonsensical. I mean, Jin back in the mid-2010s, with his crowning achievement of the Kagerou Project (compromising of Mekakucity Actors and its many spin-offs) taking off, it's for sure that creating scenarios lost in music would be a good match with the lucrative artist. Heck, even in a Funimation video where Jin explains the history of this show was founded on goodwill (that spans years) because he and other staff members envisioned a world without music, it was fine really.

To quote NASA's timeless famous quite (in the musical form): "That's one small step for a boy, one heck of a giant regressive leap for mankind." A lone boy by the name of Echo Rec, seeking out people known as Players and their weapons, known as Equipment, all in the name of a slumdog kid having big dreams for the world, only for the balance of powers to negate his short-lived dream, and in comes Mu (as in the mathematical symbol) as a Player to help him seek out the other Players for his collect-athon-ish languishing behaviour, as innocent as people could see from afar. Sounds like a simple plot, but the wayward directions truly are the definition of nonseniscal, as if we're being taken for a ride that has a short-term range goal but with a long-term sickness of longing for more.

Even worse, ar lack thereof are the lifeless characters themselves. I get it that the show must be overly robotic-mechanical, but characters are just so one-note-ish that only their convictions tell the entire story...yeah. The very last thing we need in a show like this are the Mary Sues, and this show has tons of it just waiting to unleash its "full" potential by being objectively bad. WOW, just wow, totally lacking of any form of confidence at all, and are just waiting for things to happen before they can move strategically like chess pieces on a gigantic world. "To save, or not to save" as I quell in horror of the blandness of this show...

Animation-wise, it's MAPPA, so it's kinda a hit or miss depending on the studio's recent repertoire of adapted shows under their belt, and Listeners here is kinda on the OK fence for the studio, save for the "creative imagination" that the creators wanted to show under their "years of experience" belt. Lots of action, some 3DCG (which can turn the audience off, but to credit that it isn't bad) and nice visuals. It's like the studio has turned into the modern J.C.Staff for the new decade going forward, and honestly, it looks promising too. Definitely no faults here, that MAPPA always does a good job, sasuga.

The only ONE thing going for Listeners, is the music department. Look upon this show's Wikipedia page for context, and you'll see the myriad of artists that have been smartly referenced and featured as part of the episodes' contents and context. But no matter what the social trail would say in adapting the "musical cores" of these classic and timeless songs of generations gone-by, the fact remains that the episode titles are reminiscent of the songs' context, and nothing more. Same goes with the OP and the episodic Rie Takahashi endings, they sound good, but the longevity of its appeal wears down progressively quicker.

To preface my experience with the creators' works, I honestly can't remember Eureka Seven being a bad show (but due to the rancid development it arguably destroyed my experience with it overtime), but since Dai Sato was involved with the concept for Listeners, yep, he was the scapegoat I was looking for to blame when the story and plot does not hold a candle to the settings. As for Jin though, the infamy that's after the success of Mekakucity Actors, just like this show here, the music is meant to elevate the experience as a foreshadowing and a plot pointer for things to come, and sadly that execution was somewhat lost in the mix. The same mistakes made back then does not excuse for this BS of a show.

Overall, as much as what creators Jin and Dai Sato would have you believe that Listeners was something special, hold your horses there, because it's not. The target audience a gutter-ball miss, neither strong nor capable characters across the board to get invested into, and the immense paying of tributes to timeless music being just a cover to hide this bare-bones of a show.

Don't ever watch this show, please? It sucks, BIG TIME.

On paper this show should have been perfect for me. I've played guitar for over a decade, I love rock music, and I build amps as a hobby. Based on the description, this show should check all the boxes for me, but it doesnt.

I found Listeners from its icon on MAL. When I saw the characters posing with an accurate depiction of a Vox AC-30 I was incredibly intrigued. With rock music only having a fraction of the popularity it once had, the idea of a show that appeals to rock fans using the existing mythos they themselves have built around their idols caught my eye. Unfortunately, the show we got falls way short of the mark making the whole thing come off as more of a teen's fan fic than a entertaining TV show.

I have no reservations in saying Listeners is one of the worst written shows I have ever seen in my life. Each episode is crammed full of references to rock music across the decades to the point where each episode feels like a rushed attempt to pay homage to literally every rock band from the past 50 years. Dozens of musicians immortalized in the hearts and ears of rock fans world wide are represented through various characters along with brands like Marshall, Fender, and Vox who are familiar names to any rock musician. And the references go even deeper. There are plenty of nods given to amp-heads (pun intended, I guess?). For example the main characters name is Mu which is a reference to the measurement of resistors, something well known to anyone who has built or modified an amp. So many references like this and more are in every second of the show to point that it seems like the writer was so focused on including a nod to every one of their favorite bands, they forgot to put the same love and care into the plot. Somehow, this music anime is actually a mech anime with famous models of amplifiers transforming into fighting robots... Im not joking, stop laughing. Whats worse is that the mech fights are terribly boring to watch so I guess its a good thing they last about 5 minutes max. The rest of the episode is usually just a game of how many references per minute can be made. Episode after episode of the same formula. 15 minutes of rock music references and build up to 5 minutes of a boring mech fight then 5 minutes of set up for the next episode then roll credits.

I cant recommend this anime to anyone. Not music fans, not anime fans, not my dog. 3/10

Also, why did they make the character based off Jimi Hendrix white??? C'mon...
