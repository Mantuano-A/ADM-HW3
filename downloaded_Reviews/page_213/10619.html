Funny Pets

While the characters in season 1 were shallow husks being exploited in simple formulaic episodes, season 2 gets to explore these characters' personalities in-depth and reaches into their souls on a truly deeper level.

In season 1 the characters were very basic; Crescent was jealous and angry, Corona was an overpampered crybaby, and Funny was just vain and selfish. In this season they are given motivation for the way that they act.

If you have not seen season 1, you can easily start at season 2. All backstory is explained in the visuals of the theme song that has been the same since season 1. Ryuuji Masuda, the genius behind Popee the Performer, manages to captivate the audience with an ongoing story where every episode can also stand by itself. You can start from any episode. If you are to only watch one, I suggest episode 5.

If you made it this far into this review, what are you doing? Just go watch an episode, its only 6 minutes. You can tell right away if it will be your sort of humor or not.

Often a sequel fails to live up to it's predecessor, and while in many ways this is the case here, it still manages to be quite interesting in it's own right.

Very little of the presentation has changed for this bout, same opening, same voices, largely the same tone as well, but something just doesn't quite "click" the same way it did the first season. Many of the scenarios fail to capture that same surreal magic of the original eps, with many of the stories feeling too grounded comparatively. However, there's still more than enough gems present here to be to worth a watch regardless.

One thing that didn't quite sit well with me was the last episode's disregard for the usually unexplained returns to status quo, this time unnecessarily opting to go through the process of how it happened, however with quite the dark twist (lowkey one of the most heartbreaking moments in anime I've witnessed to date).

All in all gonna still drop a fat 4 outta ten on this one, comes highly recommended, but only if the first season truly moved you the same way it did for me, and you crave more FP action.
