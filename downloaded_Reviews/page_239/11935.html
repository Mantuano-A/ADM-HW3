Many fans would love to see a good horror-movie based on the game 'Ao Oni' but instead they get this...

Sadly, it has very little to do with the game. It has the monster, the chase music, and they talk about 'Ao Oni' being a video-game. That's basically all it has in common with the source material. The plot isn't terrible but the execution is very poor. The pacing is slow and awkward. Every scene feels like it was slowed down. The characters aren't likeable or interesting. They had plenty of time to make them likeable before the threat comes into play but nope. The characters are very forgettable.

It's 3D animated and looks very cheap. It's very choppy making it unpleasant to look at. Most of the time it has an average of about seven frames per second. In most 3D animation software, tweening is very easy and automatic. They must have decided to render it out with a lower frame-rate to make it appear more like traditional 2D animation. The animation itself is awkward and looks very unnatural. The characters move like robots. With this kind of animation and choppiness, you would fail a 3D animation course.

The music isn't good except for the music during the end-credits which is actually alright. Overall, it's awkward, cheap-looking, and boring. I don't recommend this to anyone. If you're an Ao Oni fan and you're really curious, then maybe watch it.

If you thought the Ao Oni comedy shorts were bad, wait until you see this - it's even worse. The comedy shorts are a masterpiece compared to this tripe.

Story: This is apparently a story based off the game; too bad there is no story. The majority of the hour long movie is the characters sitting around talking and nothing happening. It takes place in a school, not in the usual mansion. There is a story there somewhere, but it's lost in the boring, awkward telling.

Animation: Studio Deen, who brought us such animation fail classics like Axis Powers Hetalia and Higurashi, are the animators of this. I was hardly surprised when I found out they were responsible. Needless to say, Studio Deen have kept up their trope of animating badly, leaving the series looking like a slightly updated version of MMD. The expressions of the character are poor and almost ridiculous even by anime standards and the characters move slow and stiff like machinery. And the animation of the monster is just terrible. It's also in 3D, not that watching it in 3D makes it any better or easier to get through.

Sound: The voice acting is pretty decent but not great. The music is irritating and drove me to despair. I had to pause every few minutes just to take a break. Honestly I wonder whether the voice actors will have careers after this.

Characters: The characters in this movie aren't Hiroshi & co, but a bunch of new characters. There's zero character development, the characters are one-dimensional, boring and have the personality of a gnat. There's no way you can form connections with the characters or even like them. I don't even remember their names.

Enjoyment: I hate every single second. I get more enjoyment from watching paint dry. There's no horror, no nothing.

Overall: 0/10. This is the worst thing I've ever seen and if I could give myself amnesia so I could forget this, I would. Avoid this and the comedy shorts, watch something better.

Alright, so I've never really written an anime review before, but this one was so bad I feel like I have to let people know what they're getting into.

The first thing that will catch your eye about this movie is the animation itself, even though it's done by Studio Deen who has fully shown they're capable of quality animation with Fate/Stay Night and their extensive list of helping with animation on other notable anime such as Neon Genisis Evangelion, they decided to use 3D animation. Not only did they use 3D animation with the same quality as if it were done in MMD, but the frame rate is terribly slow. The animation is extremely choppy with the exception of maybe 2 parts, one being where a bee flies into the room. At multiple points in the movie you can see the characters' clothes clipping through other pieces. The reason I'm giving the art a 2 instead of a 1, however, is because even though the animation is garbage, the backgrounds are pretty nice.

After that, the story begins. The movie is only an hour long, but the first 30 minutes felt like an eternity. The pacing is really weird, and while they are expanding on the story they never really expand on the characters. There's only one character, Murakami, which is given some sort of a personality, otherwise the characters are completely bland. Each character is a walking TV trope.
The story itself started out fine and then slowly became a mess. This movie actually is barely based off of the game Ao Oni at all. The only similarities are that there is the Ao Oni itself that behaves the same, and that people are able to turn into Ao Oni, which is unexplained in the game. The movie basically makes up its own backstory for the Ao Oni, which has no relation to the game.
Not only that, but the story they came up with is ridiculous. When they are trapped in the school at some point, they learn that one of the characters had sex with a mysterious flower in the basement, who he claims is a beautiful woman. We are even blessed with a scene where it somewhat shows this. After that we learn that having sex with said flower causes your genitals to rot and fall off, and then they turn into an Ao Oni. I would maybe be able to excuse this plot if it wasn't trying to tie itself in with the video game, but this really does not make any sense. At the end of the movie as well, they try to throw in some kind of "protect the environment" thing.

I did however, give the sound a better rating because it was probably the only good thing this movie had going for it. The voice acting was good, and the music was pretty good too. They also used the classic Ao Oni theme from the game.

Overall, I give this movie a 2 for all the reasons listed above, and because I don't think I'll ever be the same after watching this.

This movie, solely on it's own merits, is one of the worst mystery anime I have ever seen, without even needing to compare it to the game it was based on. Mainly because it's incomparable.

Story: 1

To illustrate how bad the story is, I'm just going to describe a very early scene, so minor spoilers follow: when two main characters go to the basement I have many questions.

1. How did they get the basement key? (Which is a bit of a leap in logic.)
2. Who is the guy even doing stuff in the basement? (Only mentioned by name right in the conversation before they go to it.)
3. Nobody else has gone down into the basement after who knows how long, and the main characters didn't report this to a teacher?
4. Why is their first thought to find the guy is to go down to the basement, when one of them was skipping class?
5. Jumpscares? Seriously?

Basically, this scene illustrates most of the issues of this travesty. Leaps in logic that make no sense, horror coming solely from the shock value, plot conveniences with no explanation that could be considered plot holes and an utter lack of foreshadowing.

Character: 1

Characters exist in one of two formats:

1. They are only there for the expostion, and as such are flat and bland, never developed upon.
2. They are part of the anthropology club, given a few character traits (or not) and are given zero depth.

As such, this is another failure.

Art: 3

The CGI is stiff and makes average characters look much creepier than they should be. At points, they look like puppets the movements are so robotic. The only points I find them to be OK are during the reaction shots in tense, horrific scenes because they already look so out of place.

There is also a bit of an oddity in the opening scene, where it feels like the models of all three chracters were just copy and pasted then the colour of their outfits were changed, which just feels very cheap.

The CGI monsters, however, just look hilariously bad and as such aren't scary.

The 2D backgrounds used in a couple of scenes, although still images, do look really pretty, and as such deserve some credit.

Sound: 3

Good voice acting I think, which is why this has been given a 3. The music and sound effects sound like they come from stock, and the usage of them are derivative, and as such is a let down.

Enjoyment: 5

A lot of this is bad to the point of funny, which is it's saving grace, and surprisingly the art is better than some other works I have seen which made this feel better in comparison.

Ah, Ao Oni. A horror game many fans would've loved to see a great, faithful adaptation of, but instead they get comedy shorts and... this.
Review:
Story/Plot/Writing: I personally found the story to be rather intriguing, more so all of the backstory in this world. Unfortunately much of the narrative is lost through the excessive dialogue. Everything is explained through dialogue. EVERYTHING. Why'd it all have to be an info dump? Lots of dialogue isn't bad if it's interesting, I mean, I was invested in the mystery that was going on for the first half of the film somewhat, but it really could have been presented differently, because it just feels like a load of talking. Now the plot isn't terrible, but it could have been executed so much better and the pacing was really slow. The film doesn't really have anything to do with Ao Oni either, only a folktale, Ao Oni itself, and mention of the game are in here. Holes are very obviously seen in the plot itself, and the ending is as unsatisfying as licking sand paper. Yeah, not very pleasing.
Characters: They had the time to make them likeable and add some personality to them! Murakami is the only one with some kind of character in this film, meanwhile the rest are wet cardboard! I've seen girls with more personality than them! Some have somewhat interesting backstories and relation to each other, but there isn't enough run time to flesh out everything and make the viewers care for them. Not to mention that they cut out the cast of Hiroshi, Mika, Takurou, and Takeshi for whoever the hell these boring characters are.
Art/Animation: These 3D models are ugly, man. The humans look so bizarre, and Ao Oni itself looks like an amalgamation! Why'd it have to be cheap CG? Did Studio Deen just say "Fuck it! We don't know how to make good 2D animation so let's do 3D!" The shitty animation also doesn't help, it makes it so much worse. Framerate lower than my self-esteem, clunky, robotic, unnatural movements, it's just all around very unpleasant to look at. Asylum Session, a film with some of the worst CG I've seen in this medium, at least had a unique and cool looking art style, cool character designs and good framerate, whereas this shitty CG film does not. Oh God and the way Ao Oni is animated makes me want to puke. This is the kind of half-assed ass-pull that would get you kicked out of an animation course. However, the gore was a very pleasant and unexpected surprise! It looks like shit but hey, still gore!
Sound/Music: Nice ending theme, passable voice acting, and music that sounds cool but would be much better off in a video game than in an anime. Other than that, nothing noteworthy.

Overall: It's bad. It's really fucking bad. Fans of Ao Oni will be disappointed by this heaping mound of fecal matter, or humored by the inclusion of a plant that makes people have sex with it, rots the person's genitals off, and then transforms them into Ao Oni. What... was going through the writer's minds when they came up with this shit? What were they thinking!? Horrible visuals, flat characters, a story that went nowhere, shitty "horror", and an insult to fans of the Ao Oni game... this is a disaster of a film. I enjoyed it somewhat though.
