This review is actually quite hard to write; quite strange for a five-episode show that total under ten minutes.
How do you judge something that doesn't aim high? When your anime is one big shitpost, how do you judge it?










Well, I lied.
It's actually quite simple, actually. It's one big shitpost, and it doesn't aim to be anything but that. And it's a good stinkin' time.

Story:
There is no story.

Art:
The artwork is quite good flat animation paired with passable CGI. They fit the aesthetic well, and the lacking parts of the CGI are actually used to the advantage of the comedy.

Sound:
The sound's passable. Not much to say. The use of the main theme would get old after a while if this wasn't so short.

Character:
The designs are cool, in a "what the hell, man" kind of way.

Enjoyment & overall score:
Yes, this anime gets a 10 for enjoyment. Everything in this anime is stupid, and intentionally. The concept is stupid, and the producers know it. The transformation sequences are laughably bad. The transformations are abrupt as hell. This gag would get old fast -- But this entire anime is less than ten minutes. So cut down and distilled, this is a fun collection of bad jokes. It's that friend who cracks stupid puns constantly. The joke's stupid but you laugh at it, with him and at him simultaneously.
But in the end, it's just a stupid joke. It's fun as hell, and you have to admire how well it's done, but...
It aims to be a stupid little joke. And that's all it is. Is it a fun hell of a time? You bet. But is it anything higher than the low bar it aims at? Not really.

Decent is the right word here.

Ever want to turn into an airplane and fly away from the suffering that is life itself? Now with Henkei Shoujo, you can embark on a journey to do just that!

Story (10/10): The premise had a lot of potential. It took that potential and twisted it into a masterpiece. It was an exhilarating ride going from tragic losses to inspiring teamwork. It is truly a heartfelt experience that I have yet to see in any other show. The way the plot was presented was masterful. A lot was left unspoken which allowed the viewer to interpret it as they want. There are countless possibilities, and that is the beauty of Henkai Shoujo.

Art (10/10): One word- Breathtaking.

Sound (10/10): The music was very calming at the beginning, and it perfectly fits the scene toward the end.

Characters (10/10): Let's see... School girl airplane? Car? Mech? Ship? Check!

Enjoyment (10/10): One of the most enjoyable shows out there. It caught me off guard and gave me an unexpected and unique experience.

Final Thoughts
This show, hands down, is one of the most beautiful pieces of work I have ever encountered. From the calm atmosphere to the wild chase scene- everything was done perfectly. It showed the journey of a girl's struggle, and how her strong will prevailed at the end by demonstrating that anything is possible through hard work.

Update: Episode 2 was a treat! I never expected the show to get even better! One moment a girl is skipping down the road, the next she's stealing your precious gas! It's okay thoughw because guess what? She's a car!

Update 2: Episode 3 just dropped today, and I'm am absolutely blown away. They brought an amazing VA HanaKana! Once again, the perfect music, art, and overall atmosphere deceived me. It all came together to the best transformation yet! What ingenuity from those bra cutters!

Update 3: Boy I was shocked again by episode 4! I thought the show would just repeat the same formula over and over again, but the twist got me good!

Update 4: Wow! I didn't think any show would top Shokugeki this season! We once again find a transforming girl, but this time it's something practical!

My rating is gauged significantly different than if I were to rate more typical anime. As a meme, I'm pretty sure 9/10 is spot on.

There is simply no way to predict what comes next. Perhaps this is a hallucinogenic tribute to mecha, and in particular, to the Transformers series.

Beyond developing the notion of mechanical transfiguration, I think it is fair to say that one cannot simply guess what exactly these heroines would transmogrify into, and that adds to the beauty of it all.

The shock value was spot on. The art is unassuming, one would not expect the skits to turn the way they did given the style.

Overall, it's a good meme.
