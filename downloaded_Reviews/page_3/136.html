"If Naruto was McDonalds, Demon Slayer is Heart attack grill"
**SPOILERS AHEAD YOU'VE BEEN WARNED WARNING**

"Milking the cash cow" is a metaphor that is used when a company utilizes the profit gained from a very successful franchise to make cheap remakes or recycle their trash with intentions to make more money, to satisfy their insatiable greed.

Throughout the history of Anime, several series have set the benchmark for the battle Shounen demographics, the Japanese equivalent of Marvel. From legendary classics like Naruto, Bleach and Dragon Ball to overhyped sakuga abound contemporaries like Jujutsu Kaisen, Black Clover and My Hero Academia (All being nothing but Bootleg version of HunterXHunter) to niche sports like Haikyuu, which is a poor man's version of Slam Dunk specifically targeted towards teenage girls who love shipping boys.
But only one series has managed to steal all the spotlight, despite of its failure to deliver to deliver any distinctive storyline.
This franchise is known for its world record breaking performance in all directions: Manga sales, DVD sales, Television audience, Movie Box Office success etc.

However, history has proved that popularity does not always equal quality. For example, Shawshank Redemption is known for its very high critical and audience reception despite of its Box office bomb. Steel Ball Run sold the least out of all the 8 JoJo Parts despite of being not only the best JoJo Part but also one of the best written Mangas of all time.

Demon Slayer has no novelty other than being an amalgamation of all the basic Shounen tropes such as contrived character development, poor worldbuilding, one note characters, cliche' antagonists, incessant plot convenience, copious plot devices and thicker than Bedrock plot armour. It copies the same generic equation of every other New Gen Shounen and only increases the variable of animation quality but the result turns out to be the same. Yet the praise it manages to receive has baffled many Veteran Fans.

STORY 4/10
The first scene looks like something out of a bland generic horror show. Demons kill the lonely conductor in an isolated railroad car which is one of the most predictable starts in such a premise. The whole plot revolves around hunting down one dimensional demons that has been terrorizing a train's passengers and crew.

The first half consisting of the buildup is very tedious and very predictable. The main Protagonist group hasn't changed much, they're pretty much the same. They spend their time doing bullshit inside the train, everytime there was a scene involving Zenitsu or Inosuke I had to turn down the volume or skip the scenes especially the dream sequence of Zenitsu and Inosuke were so cringy.
One positive element here was that the Character focus shifted from our Main Protagonists to a better written, experienced Flame Hashira, Rengoku.

I expected the cast to get some character progression but they remain same after the fight. They only gets more free power ups without any major change in his fundamental personality.

However, the fact that more than half of the duration of episodes in Cringy corny COMEDY veils the dark and gritty apt tone of this arc has instead made me laugh occasionally throughout the whole show, not counting the one dimensional comic relief Characters.

One third of the battle scenes are spent in exchanging monologues between the pure evil Villains, the demons and our righteous heroes, protectors of the weak, the Hashiras. The fights are predictable as they use the same Shounen formula devoid of any strategy and thematic relevance with no build up, tension or consequences.
The show tries so hard to create a dark gritty atmosphere to fit the tone of this Arc but it makes me laugh instead because of the one dimensional predictable Characters as well as the plot.

The whole demon hunting organization is just poor man's Hunter Association similar to the cursed technique college of Jujutsu Kaisen.
The worldbuilding is almost non existent. How did the demons come into existence? How does the civilization and society of demons function in the absence of humans? What is the history of conflict Humans between Demons? What does rest of the world outside Japan even look like?
We shall never know.

CHARACTERS 4/10
All the characters are one note, they all have paper thin personalities.
Tanjiro is our bootleg of Naruto, but worse. He is the epitome of a Gary Stu and embodiment of pure Innocent Kid archetype. He's perfect in every single imaginable way. He is even merciful to the demons.
The side characters are even worse than the cast of Mid Hero Academia-
Nezuko feels like a Background fodder who does nothing for 90% of her screentime and is completely useless.
Inosuke is a combination of BAKUGO and ASTA (I don't even wanna elaborate as they're both are equally terribly written)
Zenitsu is the worst of the team. His annoying personality and screams makes me skip entire scenes. They're both just comic relief cardboard cut-outs, that never contribute anything significant to the story other than either making the audience laugh or enamor them with their Badassery.
Rengoku is the only interesting character of this whole series, his intriguing backstory at least gave depth to his character and saved this show. Other than him, everyone else is forgettable.

The Antagonists are all CLICHE' with One dimensional goals and there's this poor attempt to give them a tragic backstory to feel sorry for them, but since it has been used numerous times it becomes rather predictable.
However, this time our Antagonist is even more dimensionaless, as her motivation corresponds to a serial Killer. She looks like poor man's Sakuna , and her method of killing is to first out her victims into sleep like an apparent coward, which enables the viewers to get a deep insight into our characters subconscious personality, but still fails to provide them with even an inch of depth.
Even Kyubey from Madoka Magica who shows no emotions has more depth, Genthru of Greed Island Arc and Ndongo from Phantom Troupe have more personality traits than any of the characters of this show.

Honestly, I didn't want to write this review but the score inflation as well as useless hype around JUST A SMALL RECAP prompted me to act.
Instead of wasting time on mindless eye candy Battle Shounen maybe I should've rewatched either heartwarming Slice of Life like Aria or Haibane Renmei, or Actual DARK GRITTY but thought provoking and thematically relevant Seinen like Madoka Magica, Ghost in the shell, Monster, Ergo Proxy, Shinsekai Yori and Phantom requiem for a phantom.
Now I'll just return to revise some chapters of HunterXHunter, Berserk and Houseki no Kuni or just scrutinize Steel Ball Run aka PEAK FICTION.

OVERALL 44/100

As of this review, there have only been 3 episodes of this section of Demon Slayer. I am aware that the remaining episodes will have 70 new animation cuts and my enjoyment will possibly rise from a 5 when the time comes. This is my review of what I have seen.


A good overview of events concerning the Demon Slayer Mugen train movie. As is usual with Ufotables adaption art, sound design and the story are of high quality. Sadly, while the original scenes integrated with the movie are good they are hitting one note. Killing a random demon. If you don't mind this I won't argue with you but when you have watched the movie and all the amazing repeated scenes don't excite you anymore surely you would want more from a "new section" of Demon Slayer. If you haven't watched the movie you should love this mini-season yet people who want a very original part to the series may only enjoy the remaining episodes and bar the first can possibly skip parts of the season as you would have seen it in the movie.

When I say killing a random demon I mean killing a demon with no backstory and in one episode for the sake of it. Instead of an interesting fight, you get mundane demons apart from one slightly cool demon in the first 3 episodes.

My Opinion
Well this is just a little detailed version of the movie. The only new thing would be the first episode. I don't think much new things have been added in the anime compared to movie. Still, it's pretty good

Story 7
As I said earlier there is not much new thing in the anime. The movie was relatively fast paced compared to anime. And the anime starts off pretty slow explaining each & every small details which where not covered in movie. The good thing is that despite being slow paced the story doesn't bore at any point. So, my rating for story would be a 7.

Art 10
No one would argue on this. Ufotable is the best studio in animation & this shows why is it. Demon slayer had an average art style but ufotable took it to a whole new level. The animation is also amazing. So, I would give it a perfect 10 in art & animation.

Sound 8
The opening & ending where done by lisa. The was opening was really good & ending was decent. The OST's were also new & where pretty good. So, I would give this an 8 in sound.

Character 8
In the movie we didn't get to see much of rengoku. So we here we are with the series where we have a pretty good character development for rengoku. And other character had a pretty good screen time as well. So I would give a solid 8 in character.

Enjoyment 9
A pretty good OST, amazing animation & decent character development makes an anime fun to watch. Despite being relatively slow paced compared to movie this anime doesn't bore you even for a while. And I enjoyed watching it. So I would give it a 9 in enjoyment.

Overall 8
Now judging this anime as a critique. No doubt the anime is pretty good. But still it has some flaws. And few things could have improved & should have been better. But still the good things of the anime covers the bad things. So I would give this anime an 8 overall.
