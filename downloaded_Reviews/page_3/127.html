Alright, so, just to preface, this review is coming from someone who hasn't really watched much hololive stuff. I've seen a few clips here and there on YouTube (As most of us have at this point, it's inescapable.) but I haven't exactly dedicated any time to watching full streams or remembering many of the members' names. With that out of the way, here's my review of what I've seen of this little anime.

Story - 1

There basically is no story, as each short is a disconnected gag. The stories in the individual episodes are barebones at best, but I understand that this isn't really something you ought to rate on its story, so I'm not going to really go into detail here.

Art - 5

It's always nice to see 3d anime, but this one is nothing to write home about. The character designs are nice for each character, if not a bit overdesigned (However that's just nitpicking). The animation is very rough, with complex movements looking awkward, but they keep it snappy enough with lots of things going on that you may not notice it. The backgrounds are mostly either plain colors or seemingly cheap 3d models, but with all of the other things going on this is also hard to notice unless you're paying a lot of attention to detail.

Sound - 5

The only thing that stood out about this anime's sound was the near-constant sound effects and music. While I understand that it's a fast-paced ONA comedy series based on vtuber streamers, this was jarring.

Character - 3

Again, I'm not experienced with hololive. if you're a regular fan, you'll probably love all of the jokes which I don't get. Each episode seems to have a different combination of characters, which keeps it visually interesting.

Enjoyment - 1

I didn't ever feel any particular attachment to these characters, settings, dialogue, etc. It was an experience, but not exactly one that resonates with me, or probably anyone who doesn't really care about hololive.

Overall - 1

I decided to write this review as a different perspective than from what a lot of the hololive fans are saying. I can recommend this to just about nobody other than people who like hololive, or have nothing else to do. If you don't like that stuff - just find something else to watch. Hololive fans though, go for it!

I think Holo no Graffiti is an exquisite work of art. Every episode, it shows how unconventional that makes you think "wow that sure is weird huh" but in actuality it depicts life as it is. No aspects of life are truly conventional, in fact every weird thing that happens in our lifetime happens because the Greater Being grants us the cruel punishment that is life. Another interesting design choice that "Holo no Graffiti" shows is the rapid, moving dialogues and animation that is present in most of its episodes. Rather than being done for the sake of comedy, it is actually a reflection of life itself. While yes, life itself is the longest chain of events that we, mere humans, will ever experience in life showing that life is a rather rapid process. Every day, month and year we always thought to ourselves "Wow it feels like yesterday where I celebrated Christmas with my grandmother back in 2008", this is the thing that the author and director wants to show to the viewers. Life is but a fast, no brake zone, it teaches us that we must value every milliseconds that we spend in our lives, be it good or bad events, it's a very Nietzschean approach in terms of storytelling. Lastly, Holo no Graffiti is uploaded to YouTube, one of the most rapidly growing social networks in recent years that features "loop" features. The author and director's decision to upload it on YouTube is not because of public accessibility, but it's actually because they too, wish they can just replay certain moments in their life. If only life doesn't move too fast. If only a person can turn back time and replay the things that we cherish most. The universe is truly a cruel mistress.

Ok, so I'm not exactly a fan of Hololive; Not that I have anything against it or v-tubers in general, I just never got into it. I have a vague understanding of some of the more popular characters from memes and stream clips, but I've never sat through one of their streams. I do, however, have an Hololive-obsessed friend who showed me a few of these and now that this show has been added to MAL I am bound by the competitionest's curse to see this through to the very end.

Story: I'd like to say that there is no real story holding the episodes together, but seeing as 22 episodes are privated and unavailable as of this post, there could be some light continuity that I am unaware of. The comedy can be pretty hit-or-miss, but I like the way it hits when it does. The jokes are often snappy and some of the references were just obscure enough to give me a brief sense of satisfaction.

Art: The models themselves look pretty good and that's to be expected; after all, they were carefully and thoughtfully designed to be visually entertaining enough to keep one's attention for hours on end. However, the backgrounds and props are an entirely different story. They are often low polly, poorly textured, and downright ugly. This is often used to heighten comedic effect, but that isn't enough for me to count it as a net positive. The animation can also be very sloppy at times.

Sound: There are many episodes in which I notice slight differences in microphone quality. This drives me absolutely nuts. Also, I think some of these girls just aren't cut out for doing voices in scripted media.

Character: Holo no Graffiti is carried entirely by its characters and that's to be expected. After all, they're the reason why everyone (other than myself) is probably watching this show. There is no character development (let alone arcs), but this is one of those times where I'm completely ok with that. Each character carries with them their own chunck of the Hololive lore, and while I'm not exactly familiar with them I still find them pretty neat for that reason.

Even as an outsider to the Hololive Fandom, I'm having a pretty good time with this show. I still don't see myself becoming an avid Hololive fan anytime soon, but I'll admit that my interest has been piqued. If anyone has a link to a place where I can watch the 20-or-so privated episodes, I would greatly appreciate it if you'd send it my way. While you're at it, pitch your favorite v-tuber to me if you want.

This review is coming from someone who used to enjoy Hololive contents from August to December last year. I used to enjoy watching the members' streams with excitement because it was such a fresh concept to me. With that being said, let's get going.

TL;DR: If you like Hololive, you'll love this. If you don't, do not waste your time watching this.

Story - 1
I'm going to get this straight out of the way. The story is nonexistent. Holo no Graffiti does not have a plot or story whatsoever. Each episode is a stand-alone episode, thus why I give this a one.

Art - 5
I have to say the art is not bad but it's not good either. The models look cute enough for people to think they're cute, but they don't deserve the praises Holo no Graffiti is receiving. They look bland, the eyes look dead. The animation looks very weird all the time, especially when they move or do anything at all (which they do those a lot). Granted it is not a big studio with unlimited budget handling the show, but they can probably do better than that.

Sound - 1
Now this is where I have most of my problem with. They're not voice actresses (they are in a way, but not professionals), they're streamers. They haven't received voice training like actual voice actresses have (probably they have, but still not on the same level). They're not very expressive, and you can barely understand what they're saying because of how fast they talk and unclear their voices are when speaking. Not just that, but they always sound very tone-deaf, too. Because of that, you can't really tell what they're feeling from their voices, which is crucial for voice actresses to be able to do that, but they're not voice actresses; they're regular streamers. Which leads back to my initial point: they are very bad voice actresses, even worse than amateur ones. Oh, and they're very loud so be careful. Other than the voice actresses themselves, the other sounds are very mediocre.

Character - 2
The characters are, as you expected, the Hololive girls. I get that virtual youtubers are supposed to be playing a character, but their characters in Holo no Graffiti and their characters in their own streams are different. So much so they actually feel like an entirely different people. What this does is, it leaves a confusing feeling in your brain. It's like a character playing another character; which doesn't do well in my opinion. Because the characters are just themselves, you shouldn't expect any development from any of them or you'd be disappointed.

Enjoyment - 2
Despite everything I've said about Holo no Graffiti, I enjoyed watching it, albeit a little. I would watch Holo no Graffiti on my bed before a good night sleep. You know, just watching it to make myself feel sleepy and that's about it. Other than that, there is not a single occasion I enjoyed this show at all. Watching a single episode of Holo no Graffiti is like having a mediocre dream. You won't remember it the next morning you wake up.

Overall - 1
I used to watch Holo no Graffiti when Hololive was booming. Looking back on it, now that I don't watch as many Hololive streams as I used to back then, I can say with a high degree of certainty that it's garbage.

Conclusion.
If you like Hololive, watch their streams every day, or even buy their merch, I think you will love this. This is like a godsend for you and that's fine. However, if you don't watch Hololive or haven't heard about it before, I suggest you to not watch this because it will only waste your time. If this is a joke, it surely isn't a funny one.

Top tier anime, no complaints. It's short, funny, entertaining, Haachamachama and very light-hearted. It doesn't try to take itself too seriously, yet comes off as a total masterpiece.

The story isn't connected, and each episode differs in plot. However, the overall themes are consistent, and the personalities of the main characters(vtubers) are well represented. In fact, each story in itself is a masterpiece. The humour can be subtle, yet blunt at times, and often, prior knowledge of the vtubers is needed to fully grasp certain contents in the show.

Both visual effects and sound quality are top notch, on par with other 3D styled animes. I find that the fast-pacing makes the show more exciting and concise. It eliminates any filler content that is so littered in most mainstream shows.

Overall, it provides a simple yet effective form of entertainment that is unparalleled in the anime community.
