*Note: This review covers both parts of Date A Bullet Movie*

Date A Live is one of the most popular series in the anime industry in the last decade, Whether you hate or love the series, it is undeniable that it has garnered many fans. However, At the end of 2019 year The official website of Date A Live announced a new anime for the series which is name is Date A Bullet. Date A Bullet is an anime film that is a spinoff to the original Date A Live series and it consider a sequal for Kurumi Star Festival OVA.
I don't want to prolong you, so let's move on to the next paragraph dedicated to talking about the plot of the movie.
*spoiler alert*


Story and Plot:
After Kurumi Tokisaki death, She wakes up in the neighboring world, and meet a girl who is name Hibiki, In order to kill the mysterious girl, Kurumi leads Hibiki to a school. When arriving at the school, a number of girls, known as quasi-spirits, gather together. Now, a new battle begins. The plot of the movie is mediocre, it is based on the concept of battle royale, The battle royale concept has been decent presented, All the girls on the battlefield had died in the previous life, and then they found themselves in this strange world like what happened to kurumi. As I said, The battle royale concept has been decent presented, but at the end it feels a bit rushed especially on kurumi and sawa backstory, The only thing we know about their relationship is the short scene when they were in school during their previous life, We know that kurumi kills Sawa because she turn into a monster, But We do not know how sawa turn into a monster. I wish the movie was 15 minutes longer so they could clarify some things more. Anyway, I can say that the story of the movie was decent at least.


Characters:
Kurumi Tokisaki is the main character of the Date A Bullet spinoff series. But in the movie, She is a clone of Kurumi Tokisaki that ended up in the Neighboring World after her death by the real kurumi. Kurumi Tokisaki is the best female character in my opinion, Not just in Date A Live but in all anime in general. Kurumi Tokisaki is a mysterious and powerful spirit in the Date A Live series. She has a killing streak of 10,000 people, but her looks can deceive even the most experienced person. She has shown her sadistic side several times in the anime and refuses to bend to anyone else’s view. Even though her picture of the world is comparatively psychotic, she has a logic behind each kill. As a Kurumi Tokisaki clone, her initial personality is very similar to the original. She can be coy, seductive, and sarcastic at times. However, her primary objective is finding the boy from the other world, who she vicariously fell in love with again after seeing the Spirits' memories through the Compile crystals. To that end, Kurumi is determined and focus driven to return back to the real world as her main objective.
Hibiki Higoromo is the second main character in Date A Bullet, Hibiki is a sweet Quasi Spirit with a degree of kindness that can often be mistaken for naivety. Despite her accommodating demeanor and occasional clumsiness, Hibiki is actually rather intelligent. She share some funny moments with kurumi. Like most Quasi-Spirits, Hibiki didn't retain any memories of her past life as a human.
White Queen (Sawa Yamauchi) is the antagonist in Date A Bullet, she is also known as White Kurumi. It is revealed that her dominant personality is Kurumi's friend. The White Queen has a composed and regal disposition, she sees her followers as pawns for her to use and sacrifice. The White Queen was originally Kurumi's childhood friend Sawa Yamauchi. Unfortunately, my problem with her is that we do not know why Sawa Yamauch turn into a monster.
The remain cast of characters I can not say anything about them, because unfortunately they just share a small amount off screen time and we did not have enough time to learn about their past and their goals.


Character designs and Animation:
Date A Bullet did not share the same staff of Date A Live, about character designs, I prefer the main series art style, the new art style fell a bit awkward, I agree that they try to make the characters more beautiful and attractive, But the problem is that it does not fit on the most of the characters like Kurumi and White Queen, but overall I think the new art style is decent at least, it dose not bad at all, but it has a downgrade quality from the main series art style.
After we saw the animation in Date A Live III, everyone thought that the new series will share the same problem, but Date A Bullet had a different opinion, the art and animation in Date A Bullet are very good, I didn't notice any moments of the animation level going down. The missing thing is, they have to rely on main series character designs style to be as same level as Date A Live season 1.


Sound:
As always, Date A Live is one of the best series in term of soundtracks. Sakabe Gou is undeniably one of the best music composer in anime industry, wonderful fun music, sad music, and scary music. He also give us some new soundtracks in Date A Bullet and the best one is (Rhapsody Sorrow ost). However, I consider Date A Live as one of the best series in term of soundtracks, if not the best.


Overall (7.5/10) :
Date A Bullet didn't offer all the advantages it could offer, it can be somewhat better if they did not rushed the plot and the backstory, this rushing caused some parts of the plot to not be clearly. However, I can say that is Date A Bullet is a good movie, it has a decent plot, interesting antagonist, great soundtrack, some good action scene, and of course it has Kurumi Tokisaki as a protagonist.

I watched Dead or Bullet a few years ago and I thought that now might as well be a good time to finish this story. So, was Nightmare or Queen any good? Well, let's hop on and find out, shall we?

Warning! Spoiler alert!

Story
Kurumi's journey throughout the Neighboring World continues as she faces off against the mysterious White Queen. Meanwhile, the Quasi-Spirits (yes, Quasi-Spirits) are fighting because... uh, reasons, while Kurumi is trying what the battle royale is all about. The story aspect is something that Nightmare or Queen could have handled better. Not only is the battle royale filled with characters we don't really care about (I'll write about that later), the story itself barely makes any connections to the main Date A Live saga. I mean, what's the point of making a spin-off if you won't make a reference to the source material? Was a Shido cameo too much to ask? (5/10)

Characters
The only characters worth caring about in this movie are Kurumi, the White Queen and, oddly enough, Hibiki. They're the only ones with any semblance of agency throughout the story, while the Quasi-Spirits are still engaging in their pointless battle. I'm not sure if anyone cares about this, but most of the Quasi-Spirits die. Yeah, they were all that unimportant. Not only do their deaths fail to impact the story at all, we don't even get to know them as characters and we're left wondering why are we supposed to care that they're dead.

Anyway, in order to conclude the character section with something more positive, I did enjoy the relationship between Kurumi and the Queen. That was the keystone and the climax to whole movie and it didn't disappoint. I also liked that Hibiki was the only real friend Kurumi made during this whole mess; otherwise this story would have ended on a sad note. (7/10)

Art
GEEKTOYS continues to impress with its handling of the visual department. Everything, from the character designs to the fight scenes, looked beautiful to watch. (9/10=

Sounds
The music was good, but ultimately nothing special, and while I did appreciate the vocal talents of the cast, especially those of Asami Sanada (Kurumi), Saori Onishi (Queen) and Kaede Hondo (Hibiki), most of the VAs had to work with characters that felt more like plot devices than, well, actual characters. It's very sad to see talent go to waste like this. (7/10)

Conclusion
Nightmare or Queen is... okay, both as a standalone story and as prelude to the upcoming Date A Live IV. It was fun and definitely not a time-waster, but it's real purpose was to excite people for Date A Live IV, and it accomplishes that mission with enourmous success. (7/10)

They say a 10 is something you can watch over and over again. This is one such show and not just because Kurumi Tokisaki is the best girl to come out of all of anime.

The story continues directly from the last Date A Bullet segment and comes to its finale that then leads us to season 4 of the main Date A Live show when it comes out. It paces itself really nicely and has a nice twist to it that makes for a great episode.

The art is superbly done and very high quality.

The soundtrack and sound are of really great quality. The OP and ED are both awesome. Kurumi's personal soundtrack is awesome as usual and iconic nowadays.

Characters primarily around Kurumi develop and you really understand her much more than ever before if you are anime only. You see her when she was not even a spirit which was super cute, as well as her friend Sawa. Hibiki makes a great character on this one too. Should see more of her going forwards it would seem.

The episode is really great and memorable, definitely a fine addition to the series. 10/10.
