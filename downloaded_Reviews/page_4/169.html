**WARNING TO ALL KOMI SAN SIMPS SPOILERS AHEAD**
"Why use style over substance, when you can use style to fake substance".


Among the mediocre passengers of the coal driven Hype Train, there will be always one damsel in distress who is alloted the first class cabin, her appearance so alluring that it completely makes her neighbours forget their her origins and ignore her actual personality. This damsel is also known as Waifu, and her superficial feature has been used as a marketing bait numerous times.

Guilty Pleasure can be defined as a piece of entertainment that one relishes despite of the uncomfortable feelings he/she gets by reading or watching it.
Comedy is a great way of rendering relaxation to our minds. For me it's a way of taking break from my proclivity to serious tone shows. However, writing comedy is also a form of art that not everyone can master.

A legendary forgotten studio that once animated Pokemon, the childhood of every one of us and Berserk, the most underrated television series in all of humanity, bestowed us with another modern classic, Odd Taxi this year. However, there is one unworthy show that is stealing the spotlight instead.

STORY 3/10
The story is about this Mary Sue who is suffering from Communication disorder (Remember Claude from GTA 3) but she is desperate to make at least 100 Friends. I never expected the story to be like Silent Voice with great character development and exploration of themes on the disorders children suffer from but at least they could've used a better comic relief character. This show seems to exploit the disorder for comic relief and humour by using a Waifu bait, which can be offensive to those suffering from such disorders.

The plot is so generic, predictable and repititive with no substance but just style, which deteriorates further as the show progresses until even the style degrades in novelty. It uses the same formula with no progression whatsover.

-Introduce Some One dimensional Simp
-They attempt to simp for Komi who is in trouble
-Randomly solve the situation because KOMI'S GODDESS (This isn't even plot convenience because there's no Plot at all)
-REPEAT THE SAME JOKES, NOTHING HAPPENS NO CHARACTER DEVELOPMENT, NO CHANGE IN ANY JOKE OR PLOT STRUCTURE

COMEDY 5/10
Humour writing is like the Battle of Stalingrad, any retreating joke that fails to follow the punchline gets killed and is not funny anymore. A well structured joke has a punchline, which is the big reveal or the climax in context of writing. If the setup of the joke fails, the next joke should pick up and follow through the punchline or it becomes meaningless and unfunny.

In case of Komi-San the jokes, extend the punchline instead of delivering it at the right moment. The jokes are repititive, boring and sometimes complete bullshit with no logic.
Even the fight between Naruto and Sasuke made me laugh more.

"Imagination was given to man to compensate him for what he is not; a sense of humor to console him for what he is."


CHARACTER -10/10
Apart from our Mary Sue who is the living vibrator our MC, Tadano is a generic self insert harem Protagonist. He's shy, the target of bullies. He's a loser and the most average guy in the school.
Najimi is kind of a foil to Tadano as he/she has extrovert personality but still trash.
Himiko is literally one of the worst and cringiest in the entire show, her masochist and submissive personality made me skip entire episodes.
And then we have Ren because without a Yandere, a harem is incomplete. She literally fuckin kidnaps Tadano for getting too close to Komi.

Every time a new Character is introduced. They're all limited to only one personality archetype. So they're all forgettable as they're NEVER SEEN AGAIN.

At least Kaguya Sama and Aria has some decent character development, this show is just pure Mary Sue waifu marketing bait, neither substance nor proper style. If you want to know how Real Comedy is written, watch Gintama and Saiki K.

CONCLUSION
This is just your Average Predictable RomCom with even worse repititive jokes with no substance and unnecessary extended punchline. This is the PINNACLE OF WAIFU BAIT SHOW that people hype it up only because of Komi and her wholesomeness. A Reverse Harem (Females included) disguised as a gag comedy with lowest common denominator level of comedy writing. This is basically Non ecchi version of High school DxD where Komi is literally the Harem queen.

Better Anime recommendations instead of this overhyped shlock-
Gag Comedy: Saiki K, Dragon Maid, Welcome to the NHK, Kaguya Sama, Gintama, School Rumble
If you want a story with well written female characters or just Waifus for you, go watch Shinsekai Yori, Monogatari, Ghost in shell and Madoka Magica
If you want to experience wholesomeness and tranquility watch Yuru Camp, Natsume Yuujinchou, Musishi and Aria
Hidden gem of this season: Ousama Ranking
Better comedy: Vampire dies in no time

Overall 22/100

You may be thinking, "Why the hell did you rate this masterpiece of a show just a 5?" I know it may be crazy for you guys to believe in, but please have the decency to explain why this show shouldn't be considered as a 10/10.

Story (5/10):
I'm sorry but like this show's plot is pretty underwhelming. To anybody who doesn't know what this show is about, it is pretty much showcasing the journey of a high school teen with a communication problem who is trying to make 100 friends before the school year ends. That's it. Although it is "supposed" to be romantic story with the side of random shenanigans mixed with it. It just another technique that all slice of life shows use to show their audience that it isn't just "normal" high school life the mc is dealing with. Because of this, it just makes the show just generic, and later on repetitive, as seen in the manga.

Art (7/10):
This is the 2nd thing I like about this show as it's animation quality fits well with the issue that Komi and her friends faced throughout their school year. I'm actually pleased to see that the animators did a great job with following with adapting the art style of the manga, and I do believe it does show the elements of the show better than any other slice of life shows that I have watched recently.

However, one thing the animators forgot to was add details to the show's backgrounds. The backgrounds of this show in the last 3 eps felt "empty" and had no "wow factors" that didn't want to take my attention away from the characters. I appreciate how the filmmaker and writers did not relegate these minor people to the background. Not only that, but I appreciated how they didn't make them into stereotypical side characters, since most of them had their own unique personalities and traits that make them neither boring nor irrelevant to us. But, aside from that, that's all there is to it.

The comedy was mildly entertaining; but, the emotional sequences failed to connect with me in the same way that other sad episodes had done in the past. All I can say is that it isn't the most entertaining anime at the moment, but I do expect it will improve after a couple eps/seasons.

Overall (3/10):
Everything about this adaptation isn't the best so far, and I hope it'll l become a praiseworthy show that make me reconsider my current score with it.

Or not...

This review has minor spoilers and is written from the perspective of someone with Asperger's syndrome.

Here I am recreating my Komi Can't communicate review. I initially had mixed feelings about KCC (this is how I'll refer to the anime), after some thinking, I now understand how I see KCC. I have Asperger's syndrome, so I can relate to the struggle of socializing. The fact that an animes' main plot point was about someone with social anxiety making 100 friends gave me certain expectations. I was expecting and hoping for a very serious anime showing the struggle people with social anxiety, or anything similar, go through on a daily basis. Unfortunately, KCC wasn't what I was expecting, it is a pure comedy slice-of-life, with hopeful romance in the future episodes.

For the average viewer, KCC will be a very good watch because pretty much anyone can watch it. The comedy is very good! For anyone who has the same expectations I had, unfortunately, this isn't the one you're looking for. But certainly, you should put KCC into your plan-to-watch list, it is a good anime after all. I should say that I haven't read the manga, so I have no idea how the future of KCC looks. Without further ado, I'll get a more elaborate detailing of what I liked and didn't like in KCCs' first 4 episodes. Also, my scores are certainly biased, almost everything I say is in my opinion.

Note that I score from 1/5 instead of 1/10
Story: 3/5
Art: 4/5
Sound: 4/5
Character: 3/5
Enjoyment: 4/5
Overall: 4/5

Since KCC is primarily a comedy, the story isn't the greatest factor in my opinion. It's about a girl with social anxiety who wants to make 100 friends. It is a very cute idea, that I think could have been expanded on. Not much else to say after only 4 episodes, I just hope a little romance or serious talk about social anxiety is added.

I never know what to say about the art in reviews, does anyone really care that much about the art? I think it's just a preference thing, so I'll try my best to look for the unmentioned. Firstly I have to say the bodies of the characters are very well made, the proportions are very realistic. Noone is unrealistically tall or fat (at least so far), everything is kept to a sensible proportion. The colors used are usually vibrant in my opinion, which I think complements the comedic nature they are going for.

The voice actors are very good at their job, they do a great job at assimilating the characters personallities. Since KCC is a comedy, there are of course sound effects added for comedic effect. Something I haven't liked so far would be the repetitive stand-stills used, all noise will stop and characters will freeze because something happened.

The characters all seem to be exagerations of a stereotype, especially Komi-san, her social anxiety doesen't feel real to me at all. I don't mind the classic approach to mix a bunch of quirky or eccentric characters to create bizare outcomes, it's a pretty indomitable approach to comedy. I really hope the romance between the main characters blooms into something more than just friends. I do however dislike that the main character is the overused cliché, an overly average and boring person.

I wasn't exactly happy with what I watched, as my expectations/hopes were for something slightly different, but regardless I enjoyed KCC. It's hard to fail a comedy, so this anime gets a passing grade from me. I was constantly smiling while watching, and at certain points I was a little disquited. The comedy is for all ages, so anyone can watch.

Overall KCC is a must watch comedy. I've heard online that the manga has romance, so I'm definitally going to check it out sooner or later. I look forward to upcoming episodes and hopefully a second season. Thanks for reading my review!

Forget this show exists, and imagine I'm pitching you a new story I want to write:

"A heartwarming comedy about a low-confidence student who befriends his classmate with crippling social anxiety. Everyone thinks they're just unfriendly or quiet, but really they're incredibly anxious but desperately want to make friends. Slowly he helps them come out of their shell and learn to socialize"

Now you're thinking, maybe it's this nerdy guy who gets bullied, or a chubby person who's extremely self-conscious about their weight. It's a wholesome story where they help each other develop confidence and integrate into normal social life.

Now I give you the final detail: it's an anime. Now it all falls into place. The awkward classmate? Big boobs, hottest girl in school. The main character? Completely generic self-insert male. Does she actually end up making friends? Maybe like two, but it's a gay guy or something (anime still does not understand trans people), and some social outcast girl, so we don't actually have to worry that Komi-san will outgrow the main character.

That's the problem I have with this show: it presents itself as this wholesome story about helping a poor girl who just wants to make friends, while it actually has no intentions of making her any less reliant on Tadano's support. Because it's really a self-insert fantasy about taking care of a helpless, beautiful girl. If she actually made progress and learned how to talk to people, she wouldn't need Tadano (our self-insert) anymore.

When you look at it this way, all the contrivances become impossible to ignore. How is that Komi-san has gone ~16 years of her life without ANYONE noticing her anxiety, even though Tadano figured it out in 5 minutes? Because we're supposed to feel special when we project ourselves onto Tadano: only a down-to-earth guy who doesn't care about popularity (just like you!) would be able to see her as a person. Somehow Tadano is the only decent human being in the entire school, and the anime expects us not to question that, because that's how it assumes we think about ourselves.

Literally everyone else in the school, even the teachers, have to act like they're from another planet in order to maintain this fantasy. Every guy is so obsessed with Komi that they won't let each other interact with her, every girl worships Komi so much that they don't feel worthy to speak to her, and even the adults rationalize her antisocial behavior as some kind of enlightened nobility.

Now, I know what you might be thinking, "but that's the joke! It's ironic that everyone thinks she's perfect!" And yes, it is humorous for a bit, but the joke overstays its welcome. Why keep doing the exact same joke episode after episode, stretching its believability way beyond the breaking point, when all it does it prevent the story from making any progress? Why not let Komi break out of her shell, let the students realize she's not some superhuman goddess, and let her actually start speaking to her classmates and making friends?

Because, that would ruin the fantasy. If Komi-san learns to speak, suddenly she doesn't need Tadano anymore. She's the most desired girl in school, she'll have a hundred friends instantly. She'll have a dozen boys trying to date her. Tadano won't be able to maintain his monopoly over her, because his entire character is that he's a mediocre guy. The anime is forced to keep Komi isolated, only give her one or two friends who are carefully established to be incapable as romantic rivals, so that we can keep her to ourselves.

That's why I don't like Komi-san (the show). The need to maintain a wish fulfillment, self-insert, power fantasy poisons every joke, every interaction. The humor is alright, the characters are cute, it's an exciting premise, but because its priorities are so clear, any hope of it turning into a genuinely heartwarming story are gone. And just to prove my point MINOR SPOILER: I skimmed ahead to chapter 200 of the manga and Komi-san is still not speaking, while Tadano has to supervise all of her social interaction.

So there is a negative review out there, and it's getting likes after likes because some people can't stand a popular ongoing slice of life. And you know what? I used to be like that; hating on every single popular thing just because I’d find it “cringe” to be with the 99% rather than that 1 elite percent, but those days are all gone, and I’m here to review a decent piece of art.

I don't care if you think this anime is an average slice of life with too many flaws and boring pacing, and the reason for its popularity being a bunch of nasty pervs.

To be honest with you, I like this series because it's so far greater than what that “popular” review on this page is trying to say.

I'm not trying to say that Komi-san wa, Comyushou desu has a greater meaning than a girl who is not able to communicate properly (like many 2D girl fans) because it is literally about a girl who cannot communicate -just as the name suggests. And to keep it real with you, it's not going to get any greater than that.

But on the other hand, we have these top-notch graphics, great voice acting and OST, and a proper way of story narration, which is considered a cliché nowadays, but believe me, most of the time it’s way better than those weird narrative styles.

The only thing you have to consider here is that's literally how a slice-of-life anime is supposed to work. You don't get anything complicated or a devastating storyline with lots of side stories and complex characters with dark backgrounds.

I mean, Komi is similar to Nagatoro in many ways. The only difference between these two in my opinion is exactly the difference between these two girls. While Nagatoro has enough potential to turn into a you-know-what anime, Komi is way gentler than that (I like them both, no one is judging anything here).

That gentleness is essentially the core part of this anime. Many may think Komi-san is yet another popular rom-com (like I did), but this is not a comedy. I haven’t laughed even once after 5 episodes, but I have enjoyed watching these classmates getting engaged with each other and expanding Komi’s social circle.

And again, this is all you’re going to get: a girl with an expanding social circle. But this simple concept is applied so masterfully that you will never feel left out; everything is chained to each other and nothing is rushed (considering how each episode is introducing a new character while switching between older characters and also making sure that it remains as a slice of life).

Yes, I may update this review when I’ve watched the final episode, and yes, you have the right to not like this anime, but instead of getting/making other people misinformed about Komi-san wa, Comyushou desu, accept it as the unique thing it already is, not as the anime you were expecting to see.

For me, Komi-san wa, Comyushou desu is a 9/10 but to be totally unbiased, it deserves a lovely 8 out of 10.

Also, I have to say that this anime has one of the best art styles I've seen in years, it's just too great to be described but it gives me cherry blossom vibes idk.
