To speak about this OVA it's important to acknowledge first of all the huge difference between the manga of Kamisama Hajimemashita and it's anime adaptation. The adaptation has never been completely faithful to the manga, and the same happens with this OVA. If you haven't read the manga, this OVA is just a great addition to the anime. However, if you already read the manga, you won't be able to help feeling a little disappointed.

The OVA is very well done, just like the regular anime; good art, good cast, good rythm, good everything. The love between the main characters is reinforced and some secrets are revealed. BUT! (And this is a huge "But") The feels one gets in the manga are 300% more intense than in the OVA, so as an adaptation, it's just mediocre.

I'll try to explain briefly what we are missing in the OVA without giving any explicit spoiler:

First of all, the time-travel logic is far better depicted in the manga, and Nanami goes through way more hardship to accomplish her mission. Besides, with regard to the whole story, it is in this Past Arc where we are supposed to discover most of the important information about the nature of Tomoe and Nanami's relationship, as well as Tomoe and Yukiji's, which is not explaind in the OVA at all, when it really shoud have been because it is by far the most interesting part of the story and it really changes everything. (It is there where you finally say "Hey! This is clearly not Inuyasha!" ) But not trace of it in the OVA.
Last but not least, within this arc of the manga, Nanami gets to know quite well Akura-ou, who becomes a major character in the following arcs, and the absence of the Akura-ou part in the OVA, added to the fact of condensing everyone's favorite part of the manga (4 volumes of it actually) in barely 4 episodes, only makes me fear that there might not be next anime season in the future.

The past arc of Kamisama Hajimemashita has been one of the most pasionate stories I've read in a manga, and one of the most coherent time-travel stories I've ever come across. So it saddens me considerably to see it cheapened like this.

However, as I said at the begining of the review, if you haven't read the manga, go ahead, the story is undoubtably enjoyable and sweet.

Before I start, this is my first review, so it might suck. Also, I've read the manga volumes that this four part OVA covers, so take that how you want to.

Story 9/10- At the time of writing this, only the first of the 4 OVAs have been released. Like I said before, I've read the manga, and I really love the story. I think that this animated version will do a good job of pacing, if the first one is anything to base my judgment on. Can't wait for more!

Art 7/10- I felt like the art wasn't as sharp as the actual show is, but when you think about how this is an OVA, I guess it's okay. Some parts of the art didn't look that great, like Tomoe's curse marks (that texture was so ugly). You could tell that the animators didn't really add that much actual movement into the animation. Still, the art was above average for sure, and didn't go off model or anything.

Sound 8/10- Not much to say. They used a lot of the songs from the OST of the show, which I thought was nice. Voice acting was pretty good.

Character 9/10- Again, not much to say. The "new" characters introduced (I say that because Yukiji isn't really new, but this is the first time where she gets developed) are good, and will probably get some character development in the future.

Overall, I had a really good time watching this. I didn't want it to end, and I wish I didn't have to wait so long for the next one. Give this a shot if you are a fan of the original.

I fell deeply in love with all the episodes of Kamisama Hajimemashita from Season 1 to 2 to its OVA version. I love the development of the two main characters; from enemies to friends, and eventually, to lovers. Unlike, the many animes that I have seen, the streak of independence, wit, kind-heartedness, positivity, and charm in Nanami was a welcome change from the dimwitted and damsel-in-distress qualities that female leads in this genre have been stereotyped with. Same with Tomoe, who, though initially had difficulty wearing his heart on his sleeve, still managed to overcome the usual cold and steely demeanor, which often characterized anime male leads even when they are, supposedly, in the euphoric and passionate state of being in love. Though, I must admit, that compared to Takumi Usui of Kaichou wa Maid-Sama, Tomoe still came across as a bit restrained. But that's understandable, considering the different plots and conflicts from which their characters were anchored on.

Moreover, I like how some of the loose ends were tied together in the OVA version. How the premise of having just one true love for youkais was faithfully kept even when the name Yukiji cropped up. I've seen hints of how this would be resolved through Mizuki's time-turning incense and when the wind god, Otohiko, mentioned about the great intertwining bonds in Season 2 but how the two factors would come together to weave a tight story, was of course, entirely of Ms. Julietta Suzuki's doing.

For me, Kamisama Hajimemashita is one of the few anime I would never ever get tired of watching (fact is, I've watched it just a little less than a hundred times already), while hoping that there'll be more in Nanami and Tomoe's story, that it'll continue until both are old and grey.

[Spoilers ahead, don't read unless you've finished this completely]

These 4 episodes of OVAs undoubtedly, for me, carry this whole show. Ignoring the fact that S2 introduced the character of Kirihito (aka Akuraou) and never continued on that story, these 4 episodes were peak for me.

Don't get me wrong, I liked Season 2 and Nanami's development as a God, but we FINALLY got to see Yukiji properly. Well, not really Yukiji, but you know what I mean. The romance in this was so so good, I wish it had been a whole season instead. I feel like they could've made another season with 12 episodes to properly address everything, but I wasn't mad with how it ended.

Only one problem. Kamisama Kiss is built off of a time paradox, and I'm the kind of person that can't overlook these little plot holes. Right off the bat it's clear that something doesn't add up, when we see Tomoe fall in love with Nanami as Yukiji. Time. Paradox. She shouldn't have been there, it doesn't make sense. If Kamisama Kiss had properly made it so there was no plot hole (and it's a pretty big one that affects the entire story), then I'd have to properly commend the writers and creators, but that's not the case.

Time-travel is always a sticky thing to dive into. I feel like an anime has to be extra confident they can tackle and address it properly, because if you're like me, it will change the tone of the entire anime. Although Kamisama Kiss isn't centred around time-travel, it is most certainly built of the whole premise of this time paradox. If it made sense, then everything would change.

Though I can appreciate that the way the story is, is really sweet, so I can overlook it (though it will forever stay in my mind). Other than that tiny (but huge) problem, I loved the OVAs. Nanami as Yukiji looked gorgeous and there is no doubt that Yukiji was known for her beauty, they captured that so well.

The OVAs felt like strictly Tomoe and Nanami time, and I loved it. I probably enjoyed these 4 episodes more than the whole 1st season (and maybe even arguably 2nd). This was the romance I wanted in both seasons, and this is where I got it. I'm not complaining much though, because it left a really good taste in my mouth for Kamisama Kiss.

Overall, these 2 hours of Tomoe and Nanami deserve a solid 8/10. I can't stress enough how good the romance is in this.

**Edited**

After some thorough research, I realised that the one fatal flaw I named wasn't a flaw, and is ACTUALLY a proper theory that is NOT full of holes (despite what one would thing). Time paradox's are a thing, and the one Kamisama is built off of is called pre-destination (I think). That being said, time travel hurts my brain and I'm not fond of the theory of pre-destination in time travel, therefore my overall score will not change. (But definitely worth noting that I don't actually have any complaints about how they tackled time travel, I just have 3 brain cells)
