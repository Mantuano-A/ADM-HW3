Sol Levante was fun yet disappointing at the same time. I definitely enjoyed looking at what I saw, but disappointment that there wasn't more. (Note - it gets a one on rating because there was no story, and five on character because we don't get to know them well.

However, I think here is a good place to discuss the significance of this particular piece in that it shows what can be done with digital animation. Historically, before digital animation came about, everything was drawn by hand and could take a few years to get a project done, such as a movie. Then, the digital age occurred, with studios like Studio Ghibli using it to churn out works faster, which makes sense given the studio's style which is simplistic in nature.

The problem is, with digital the route in both Japan and the West is to churn out more product of the same quality we've always been getting rather than churning out the higher quality product which takes more time to produce. Basically, this short showcases what could be done with the old industry standards using new technology, yet does the consumer base want say a movie that takes a few years to make over a movie which is made with a few clicks of a button because the computer does the work for the artist?

It's difficult to say, though I think this short shows the level of detail which could be done, but makes one wonder if the industry is where it should be - and by this I mean all around the world.

The second I read the description, I knew this was going to be bad. It wasn't a surprise. "The world's first hand-drawn 4K UltraHD anime." What the fuck is wrong with you?

So I set my expectations very low, and was willing to be forgiving because it was only 4 minutes long. This is the posture from which I was willing to at least enjoy it for what it is: "You can just throw a whole bunch of lines and obviously digital coloring/effects at me if you want to. Fine. Whatever. It's your art. But, for the love of God, please do not focus so much on making sure the hair is constantly waving at the screen. It's going to look terrible and I won't be able to pretend that you made your bad decisions with good intentions."

Cut to Willow Smith: "I WHIP MY HAIR BACK AND FORTH. I WHIP MY HAIR BACK AND FORTH."

...

It's like Netflix and, I hope to a lesser extent, Production I. G. don't understand anime and its appeal to most people at all. Everyone likes sakuga, or likes to fixate on it from time to time, but essentially nobody is here strictly for how high-fidelity the anime looks. If they are, they're watching the wrong style of animation—most anime is cheap as fuck and it has developed an architecture within itself to sustain being cheap as fuck. And that's great. I love watching it and seeing where they cut corners and use good direction to patch it up. If you want to see something really impressive, watch food porn scenes from 1980s anime movies.

This... This looks like a polished turd with a spritz of glitter. The action doesn't read properly, so you can't enjoy it as a fight scene. The art looks like it's sliding around, so there's no weight to any of the hits. The main character's hair is constantly flapping around in the wind, so you can't focus on anything else because you asked for ONE THING and they just rammed through with a terrible idea.

How many resources were wasted on this garbage? How do you justify all the time that must have gone into every one of these frames of terrible animation? How do you sleep at night after trying to put this forward as some kind of advance in the medium?

If anyone tries to pass this off as a glimpse of the future, smack them in the face because they want anime to die. I just hope they're actually wrong.

Sol Levante (2020)

4 minutes and yet one of the most difficult anime shorts to score. Why? Because it is a test show more than anything to display anime in HDR 4K.

The story, therefore, is there and it is a good little story but ultimately it has no real meaning since it was just to fill out the time with something worth watching as a showcase.

The art is spectacular to the reason it should be as a showcase of HDR 4k. It is crisp, clear and beautiful.

Sound is impactful, it has depth and is a great example of SFX.

Characters do not matter since its a showcase. Mainly 1 main with a familiar.

Enjoyment in terms of the HDR 4k experience it is outstanding. In terms of the anime itself, it is dull since again it is purely to showcase not really storytelling.

A better option should have been to remake a 4-minute scene of a flashy anime like perhaps No Game No Life Zero so you could see the difference between 1080p and HDR 4K.

5/10 as an anime, 10/10 as an example of the future.
