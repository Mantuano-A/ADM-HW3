before reading my review of Kengan Ashura S2:
I'm not a major fan in the fighting scene of anime and as such, I'm not the most conscious of fighting style criticism and the such however, I really enjoyed the second season of this anime adaptation. Although not incomplete, it brought a lot of interesting characters, plot progression and MC's development as a less capable character. This season didn't fully rely on Tokita Ohma's main power, advance which can be seen in the last 2 episodes - instead it revises the Niko techniques he learned much earlier than season 1. If your question is "is it worth watching" or "would you recommend watching this series as a whole?", my answer is YES of course it's worth trying! Now, I will go in depth with my review on Season 2 of Kengan Ashura!

Story:
Season 2 follows the continuation of the first season where we follow Tokita Ohma and Yamashita Kazuo in their journey to dominate the Life or Death Kengan Tournament against the most powerful corporations and fighters in Japan. Although fairly repetitive and weak plot structure, the series opts to use a variation of fighting techniques as well as a wider character palette especially noticeable in season 2; Despite there being a large number of characters it wasn't difficult to watch match after match because a match usually consisted of at least a portion of an episode and sometimes extended to 2 episodes - The wider purpose of this was to allow the viewers to invest in the wider character palette as well as their development and contribution to the narrative. More so than Tokita, the characters surrounding him allow the viewer to grasp the intense atmosphere between the extremely competitive corporations as they rise to the 'president' of the Kengan Association which is rumored to be a powerful underground entity among corporations.

TL;DR: The overall narrative appears to be basic and repetitive to the average viewer; However, if you invest in the plot and flow with the matches this series will appear much more interesting. As such, I will be rating Story a fair 8/10 - Not the best plot but certainly very addictive to watch and invest time in.

Art & Sound:
As soon as learning Netflix had been licensed another fighting series (Kengan Ashura Season 1) I was extremely hectic as to whether this series would surpass Baki, which was also licensed to Netflix earlier in 2018. I felt that they shared the similar detailed, worn art style as each other yet Kengan Ashura had opted to use CGI for many of the action scenes and even casual character movement scenes - I've pretty much adapted to any art style and 3D animation so I don't really notice the difference in quality to Baki - However if you aren't much invested in 3D anime, I'd suggest watching this to try get you into 3D anime, there is a lot of detail in characters despite being a CGI series. As such, I'll rate this series (even season 2) a well deserving 8/10 in terms of it's distinctive yet unique style.

Character:
It's a known fact that fighting series can include large character palettes but Kengan Ashura manages the characters extremely well for a 24 Episode (for now) series - It not only focuses on the characters but it also maintains most of their contribution to the narrative and develops support characters as the narrative progresses. I think this series executes character development miles better than Baki because it manages the character palette extremely well with nearly no effort. We see that opponents in matches are friends behind the scenes, we also see that despite being defeated they contribute their thoughts to future matches. Because of this, I will be rating this season in terms of character development a solid 9/10, executed great for a series picked up by Netflix.

Enjoyment:
As mentioned before, I'm not much of a fist to fist fighting anime fan because it isn't really interesting to me; However, after completing season 2; it has led me to crave series with similar character development and plot progression. As such I will be rating my enjoyment of this series an amazing 10/10. Loved every bit of season 2 and I hope the sequel will surpass this season by a long shot!

Overall: 8/10
