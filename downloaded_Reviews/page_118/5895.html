*Light spoilers ahead*

TLDR Below.

Imagine having Your Name combined with The Girl Who Leapt Through Time, stretch it infinitely long, and mix 1 or 5 genres of your choice.

You get this mess. This excruciatingly long boring mess of just silly characters with melodrama that doesn't even fit the frame it is presenting.

I must stress there is nothing wrong with generic titles. Not everything has to be innovative of the past, a push of simplicity is beautiful anyway, but quality matters regardless. KimiKana doesn't even understand that basic fact. Story's out here having a bigger identity crisis than Bloom Into You, seriously, this turns into a Disney musical halfway through with some action sequences to follow on. The only important character here is Mio, you can forget anything to do with Arata, the dude doesn't even take at least half of the screen time, but apparently, he is a very important person (you can tell because he has superpowers). There are also people who you won't care about having relationship ties as if we've seen 20 episodes of this nonsense before. Just one big ambitious mess.

I have to give praise to the audiovisuals though, well, for the trailer, because that is one of the reasons I watched this. Everything in the movie does not correlate to the trailer. It is just basic plain budget-cutting stuff you see in mid-tier seasonals, which, doesn't mean it is bad, but doesn't mean it is unique or anything to praise. This movie probably has one of the few times where I can say something has "bad audio", in the sense that the use was bad not the actual sound that I could hear. I am going to have some very strong words with Sena Yoshinobu for allowing me to know some wild direction could even exist.

Anyways, this was too boring for me to enjoy, chances are it will be the same for you. I will give it praise in that the only thing I liked about this was the flamboyant moments and the wholesomeness that draws later on. Other than that, I don't recommend watching this one. Watch the two titles mentioned prior. Your Name for a good supernatural drama. The Girl Who Leapt Through Time for life lessons that are actually coherent.

TLDR: The movie is a mess of story and characters you don't really care about. Audiovisuals are mid. So everything adds up to be boring as hell but has some good flamboyant scenes.
----

I have to say I was eager to watch KimiKana anyway. In addition to a hot trailer, the lack of talk from it was something that made me more interested in it, plus some said it was bad so I had to verify that myself. My curiosity is satisfied however and I have to say, even though it was just bad, I am happy to have seen it anyway.

It was July 2020, I was just starting watching anime and exploring MAL and found a movie that looked very promising. It looked similar to Your Name, which I loved, so I wanted to give it a try. I waited patiently until the release, in November. The day thay movie aired... nothing happened. Not the day after, not in a week, not for a long time. "It was badly received" "It was only showed two times on cinemas" "Rakuten only sells it raw and within Japan" was what I could see from different pages and the official website. I was losing all hope until some days ago I happened to look for it again. And to my great surprise, it was there, it was not fake. So I decided to watch it at night yesterday, which I did. Was it worth to look for it so much? Let's find out.

*SPOILERS AHEAD*

Story: 6.5/10 - The story centers arround Mio and Arata, the typical childhood confession and an accident that transported her to a parallel world. So far so good. What happens then is a very rare mix of everything possible. A colourful world with spirits reincarnated on things she remembered, her worst fears personified in giant monsters that chased her for the entire world and a lot, really lot of metaphoric uses. All of this which would have been perfect if there weren't so many plot holes and, mainly, non-explained things. Especially at the end. But hey, we won, right? Dale campeón.

Art: 8.5/10 - As most movies that inspired in beautiful works like Your Name and A Silent Voice, Kimi wa Kanata has amazing landscape design. Each of the scenarios were beautifully drawn, and you could feel they were very inmersive. Also the character design were very charming and easy to like. Animation was fine, being great at little things like Mio's moving hair and fair at moments like her flight over the fish. An overall great thing that with a little more budget could have been even more amazing.

Sound: 8.5/10 - Soundtrack was also something very special. All the orchesta was very nice and according to the movie. And both songs, To You (Mio's song) and Shunkan Dramatic (Ending) were incredible. I'm even listening to Shunkan Dramatic in loop as I write this. This is the part that creates great memories, and they achieved it by a lot.

Character: 7/10 - Not very amazing but still nice. Mio and Arata were the typical two main characters that everyone like, and then we had the different supporting characters and the main 'antagonist' (was it one though?). They made the movie enjoyable without shining too much. Fair enough.

Enjoyment: 8/10 - Being extremely strange and without sense for some times doesn´t make it less enjoyable. Even more, it makes it even better sometimes as I already knew this could be like that and wanted to feel something like this. I´m glad to find another 'falopity' show, those anime so strange that make you wonder about everything in their world.

Overall: 7.5/10 - I don't really know how much to score this. I feel a 6 is definitively low, a 7 misses something but an 8 is too much for a movie that was so inconsistent for moments. Maybe I will give it an 8 in support for the movie, as 6.55 rating is not fair at all. But the real score for me is 7.50.

Story: 6.5/10

Overall: 7.5/10 --> 8/10
Should you watch it? Yeah, it's a very nice movie.

Was it worth the wait? I would say YES.

Let me guess how you ended up here: you thought the movie title was similar to 'Your Name'? Because that is exactly how I ended up with this one.
WARNING: slight spoilers ahead (doesnt really matter though, trust me).

In short, if you want to watch something just to pass your time and don't care about plot or animation, you're at the right spot. I was intrigued at first and expected some cute romance with some supernatural elements, and on the surface, this is what this movie has to offer. In the beginning I thought it was cute, but as soon as the spiritual stuff started, something felt off. This is mainly because the animation becomes questionnable at times, one scene/character in particular that I found awful to look at was Requiem and his whole chasing or fighting scene. It was just so bland and felt like somebody hodgepodged everything together in MS Paint and a basic editing software.
The music was quite unremarkable, comes across like some royalty-free track you can find online. The scene with Mio singing did shift the tone a bit, but felt like a number in a musical. Also, in my opinion this scene happened too early on plot-wise, as it felt like the conclusion type of deal, however, there was still like half of the movie left.
Regarding the plot itself, there were just way too many details that didn't make sense, were rendered unimportant towards the end or just felt too shallow, making it that you would sometimes have to play a guessing game of whether this or that is happening now, because you tried to piece everything together by yourself. For example, during the hospital scene towards the end when Arata seemed to have picked up Mio's presence in the hallway, only for her to disappear, really had a sense of finality to it and I legit thought she was gone. Only for her to reappear in some other place, resulting in me going 'oh so it isn't over yet, aw man'. Also sidenote, her crying before that scene got really awkward, I don't usually cringe at crying scenes, but this one just made me uncomfortable.
If you are wondering why I haven't said anything about the characters yet, it is because they are really bland and have only vague relationships. They are pretty to look at though, so at least their design is not half bad.

So yeah, it is a movie full of clichés and a stretched-out, half-explained, trying-to-be-deeper-than-it-is plot. I found myself picking up on elements derived from 'Your Name' and 'Spirited Away' and I sort of started describing the movie as a cross between the two, but only if you take all the good stuff from those movies and throw it out the window. Go watch those instead for a more enjoyable time.
Again, if you don't care about plot, characters, animation or music and really just want to have something to shut your brain off and waste 95 minutes of your day, sure, go ahead. Won't watch it again though.
