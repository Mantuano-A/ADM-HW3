I've been waiting for RE: Infinite Darkness for quite a while now. I've followed all the trailers and announcements, often occurring at the same time as the Resident Evil Village announcements. RE8 came out earlier in the year, I loved it, and now Infinite Darkness (ID from here on out) is out, and I have... rather mixed thoughts on it. These are all fresh in my mind, having just binged it all on release day, so bare with me as I collect all of my thoughts and try and make them cohesive here.

Story - 6/10
The story was actually really enjoyable, I thought. I feel as though I would totally benefit from a rewatch soon so that I can maybe get all of the little convoluted bits I missed. While I would rather get my Leon and Claire CGI fix from Resident Evil: Degeneration, I think they're handled well enough here. I would have liked for there to have been a little more balance between the individual A and B-plots involving Leon and Claire, respectively, but I did enjoy it plenty.
My only gripe is that I feel like Infinite Darkness would have worked so much better as another movie. Each episode feels like an excuse to disconnect Claire's side plot from the main plot as a cold open. Maybe it is just me needlessly complaining, but I do personally prefer the nonstop action in the three CGI films that preceded this series.
Bottom line is, good story, some pacing issues.

Art - 8/10
I don't really have any issues on this front either. Not much of a difference from the CGI in Resident Evil: Vendetta, but even there, the CGI was near-perfect. Some characters look a little choppy in motion (For example, the closing shot of Leon walking towards the White House) and some characters do look a little stiff sometimes, but I think that's to be expected. All of the environments look fantastic, arguably better than they did in Vendetta, and in ID, I noticed that there seems to be a little more experimentation with visuals (Such as shadows dancing on walls to signify graphic deaths, lighting changes in the submarine in the second episode, etc.) Overall, really solid.

Sound - 7/10
I thought the opening was badass, and the soundtrack really suits action sequences well, but I thought it was a little out of place for scenes that try to go down the tense, survival-horror route. I can't recall the soundtrack from the CGI movies but, just from my experience of the games, even the action-centered games, not to mention the genuine horror titles in the franchise have fantastic soundtracks that know when to be loud, and when to subdue a little bit. Considering Infinite Darkness seems to toe the line between full-on action and quieter horror at times, I feel like this balance would have benefitted the series more.

Character - 9/10
It's Claire Redfield and Leon Kennedy, what more do you need? (Might I add, played to perfection by RE2 Remake alums Stephanie Panisello and Nick Apostolides.) They merit a 10/10 alone, in my opinion, but I also found myself liking characters like Shen May too, even if she does seem like something of a knockoff Ada Wong. Otherwise, the other characters were rather forgettable, even the President, father of RE4's Ashley Graham, but many characters were super boring in the three CGI films and I still found myself loving those despite it all.
I didn't like that they left Leon and Claire on something of a sour note, though. The only issue when it comes to making a piece of media that takes place midway through the timeline of the franchise is that it has to gel with everything that comes afterwards, chronologically. At the moment, this is the last chronological appearance of Leon and Claire together, and so I just sort of wish that they didn't leave one another with any sort of issue or quarrel between them.

Enjoyment - 8/10
Despite my few issues, I did find myself really enjoying this. For the most part, it riffs off of both the horror and action elements of the games that we've come to know and love. Even in comparison to RE Village, you can clearly see stark influences with the way that the story progresses. I thought that this was an enjoyable, if a little mismatched, experience.

Overall - 7/10

And I've pretty much said my piece. Sorry for rambling!! I'd totally recommend this if you're a fan of the games, or if you enjoyed the CGI films that came before. It is a fairly short watch so, even if you're not a fan, if you have a couple of hours spare, maybe give it a try.

English Version:

This review comes from someone who has the Resident Evil franchise as his favorite, I follow practically everything that is released and I've had contact with almost everything in the saga, games, books, movies, etc.

That said, Resident has always been a great franchise with undeniably amazing titles, notably the revolutionary Resident Evil 1996 and Resident Evil 4, but I've always seen its story as a good B-movie that uses what's influential in its time for each. game, the originals took inspiration from the Romero movies, the sequels, the exaggerated 2000s action movies, and so on, but there was a time when the franchise got lost, more precisely in Resident Evil 6, after that to Capcom spent a lot of time trying to find a way to make the saga come back to strength, and it had some successes as Resident Evil Revelation 2, but it only got back on track by abdicating its main characters and using claustrophobia and mystery with Resident Evil 7.

Since then there has been a lot of talk in the community what would be the future of the characters we already know and love so much, and when Resident Evil Vendetta was announced, we fans rejoiced immensely, Chris and Leon together in an affair for the first time? Rebecca returning to the franchise after decades? Couldn't go wrong right? But it happened, with the exception of a few Fan Service the film commits all the sins that RE6 had committed in the past. That was the last straw for many, however, thanks to some remakes and good sequels some of our hopes were raised, so Resident Evil: Infinite Darkness is announced.

And is he good? No, it doesn't even live up to expectations, the plot while interesting is obvious and clichéd to the point of annoyance, and even though it's a B-Movie franchise in terms of story, it's no excuse for such a weak narrative. All the curiosity that we have in its first episode ends up erased by the plot at no time working the suspense or the characters that compose it, loaded with scenes that alternate between flashbacks and current times, the series manages not only to be extremely expository, but also treats the viewer like an ass. for repeating the same scene over and over again from the same perspective, and don't think that just because the second or third time they repeated the scene and we ended up discovering something new that causes some difference in the plot, quite the opposite, it just takes screen time so we don't deliver nothing that adds to the story.

Some moments are also completely disposable, without spoilers, but anyone who has watched the series knows that Claire's existence makes no difference to the plot, if the character was not present the story would still be the same. Infinite Darkness also goes wrong in wanting to discuss sensitive subjects like politics and turf war using catchphrases, weak speeches and silly justifications of idealistic and revolutionary characters.

This series being bad worries me a lot, because if not even going back to the past Capcom is able to deliver us something minimally decent with these characters, what will become of our dear protagonists in the future of the franchise? Until they realize just how much this show needs maturing not only for unidentified characters like Ethan Winters but also for its main characters, all that's left is to wait for 70-year-old Leon, Chris and company punching rocks and somersaults in underground caves.

Unless you're a big fan of the franchise I don't recommend watching this, settle for Resident Evil Degeneration, Damnation and go into the gaming world.

Ayyyyyyyyyyye! I finally got around to watching this series. I've been hyped as heck since it was announced back in May or so and I'm finally here...6 days after it was released and probably after it was relevant. Anyways, time for the review:

Story: 6/10:
Let's just get this out of the way. I don't think the story is complete and total trash like some people think it is. That being said, it's still cookie-cutter and a bit generic, which is bizarre considering a lot of the RE games have some rather out-there premises at times. I do like how the story between Leon and Claire connects at the end, with the political and physical tensions and all, but that's about all it's got going for it and that part doesn't even really happen until the end, so for 3 out of the 4 episodes, it still feels rather fragmented. It's fine enough, but for RE, it really needed to be something more.

Visuals 8/10:
Okay, visually, this show looks AMAZING! This is by far some of the best-looking CG RE stuff we've seen (which would make sense since the last thing we saw that was RE and CG was 2017 with Vendetta, which is also good) in a while. I actually really like how Leon looks in this show, it makes sense how he looks given that this is after RE4 but before RE5 and even longer before he'd have the look he gets in RE6. Claire looks about how I'd expect here to look, reminding me of her later designs in the RE spinoff games around the late 2000s-early 2010s (minus Mercenaries 3D but uhhh....yeah, I think most people forgot that game existed sooo...yeah.) The original characters look great too, they don't look out of place at all [SPOILERS] (though the B.O.W. that Jason turns into looks about as generic as you can get for an RE monster.) Overall, good animation, liked the fight-scenes (the few that were in there), and the characters look good. Solid B effort.

Sound 7/10:
Well, given the fact that I watched the Dub and not the Sub because Japanese Leon and Claire sounds like a cursed idea to me, I can say with confidence that the voice-acting is...pretty solid. Yeah, conversations are a bit stilted and weird at times due to the pacing, but for the most part, the voice-work is solid. I'm surprised at how well they made Leon RE2 Remake VA actually work really here and I could see him over the course of some time being the next solid voice for Leon like Paul Mercier and Matthew Mercer were over time.

Character 6/10:
Well....the characters are...fine, I guess? I dunno. Something about how Leon and Claire in particular just kinda feels off, their chemistry really isn't the same as it is in the games and hell, probably the movies (though I haven't gotten to those yet, but I sure as hell will once I'm done with this review.) They act fine enough, but comparing them to their game counterparts just makes me wish they were more themselves, especially Leon since he was such a good character in RE4 and Claire has shown good character, especially in the spinoff games, which is why it's kind of disappointing to see them kinda misrepresented here.

Overall 6/10:
This show was...an alright watch as an RE who knows the context for when this show takes place. But if you were coming into this show only having played the Remakes of RE2 and RE3 along with RE8...you're gonna be lost. This is definitely a show for only those who played RE4 & RE5 (and I'd honestly say 6 too otherwise you won't get the reference I think they were going for with the costume.) It's fine enough for what it is, it would've been nice to see more things expanded upon in a second season or maybe a longer Season 1 where Leon decides on what he does with the chip but...it is what it is. Good for RE fans who are semi-hardcore or at least have played RE4 & RE5, otherwise, just pass on this one.

Since I am a pretty big RE fan I have been looking forward to this show even though it being a Netflix Original was quite concerning. Most of their shows tend to be pretty bad and sadly this one isn't any different. NO SPOILERS AHEAD!

The series takes place after RE4 but before RE6, so knowledge of RE2 and 4 is needed. Storywise the show is pretty mediocre and doesn't really add anything to the overall lore of the RE-Universe. The pacing is also all over the place because the show likes to switch not only between characters but also time, sometimes showing the same scene up to 3 times only to add another little bit onto it which becomes pretty tedious real fast.

Speaking of characters, the series brings back both Leon and Claire as the main characters, except that Claires plotline is completely unnecessary and she doesn't really do anything worthwhile. Even removing her from the story wouldn't change anything which is just really frustrating since all of the advertisement portrayed both Leon and Claire as equals, yet she again gets to do nothing like in Degeneration. The new characters also aren't anything to write home about. The asian girl is pretty bland and I already forgot her name, despite finishing the show like an hour ago. The main "bad guy" is pretty much the main character from Final Fantasy Origins who repeatedly screams CHAOS, except that this guy likes to say terror. I really couldn't take him serious. Leon on the other hand is awesome, though that mostly comes from him simply being Leon.

The Animation as a whole is pretty good, most of the time looking amazing and sometimes being pretty bad. It basically gets worse as the show goes on but never really becomes downright awful. I would say its simlply inconsistent. You can also recognize which characters are gonna appear more often than others because some character models reminded me of the old PS3 days.

Overall I would say that if you enjoyed the other CGI RE movies then you will probably get some enjoyment out of this. The show definitely could have been much better and I was really disappointed by it. It's pretty obvious that this was originally meant to be just another movie, that was then split into 4 parts to create a series and gather more hype since the previous movies didn't perform too well. Hopefully an eventual second season can fix some of the afore mentioned problems and thus turn out better in the end.
