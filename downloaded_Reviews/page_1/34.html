This is my first ever anime review, I feel I have to give this anime credit where credit is due because it is the most underrated anime I have ever had the pleasure of watching, do not let the lack of reviews/popularity fool you, it is one of the best animes I have seen.

This review does NOT contain any spoilers.

Story 10/10- The story of kingdom is quite simple, Xin wants to become the greatest General of all time, in doing so you are taken on a journey between China's waring states, and how that came to be. Kingdom brings an array of emotions. You will laugh, you will cry(I did anyways), you will be pumped, you will be angry and you will be mind blown by its badass nature. It's very rare you come across an anime where you can have the massive scale of war and so many characters yet be able to simultaneously focus on a main protagonist effectively. I can only compare this type of well done world building to one piece. I feel like the story's pacing is great, it never feels rushed if anything it can be a bit on the slow side which in my opinion works great, its a slow burn type of anime and that makes waiting for the heart pumping wars all the more enjoyable. If you do not like war/strategy anime do NOT watch this, it is not for you.

Art 8/10- Kingdoms art is a HUGE improvement from the very first episode, it is almost not even comparable and feels like a totally different anime, in a good way. It still keeps its classic art style which suits an anime set in China but it is way smoother and not nearly as choppy. Is it the best art of all time? No. Is it good? Absolutely.

Sound 9/10 - Not much to say here, it gets you hyped up and is very loud and an opera type, it suits this anime perfectly as this is meant to have a very boisterous type of soundtrack.

Characters 10/10 - Along the way we encounter many characters of which you grow to love. This show has quite a few people coming in and out but the main characters usually always stay. It will make you love characters you once hated and hate characters you once loved. There really is so much character development for Xin and the people surrounding him. The enemies we encounter and the strongest people in this Kingdom universe are always interesting and very intriguing. Every general on Qin's army is really badass, likewise with every general in the other states. They really nailed the "mighty general aspect" but also really nailed the common common soldiers with a higher goal just like Xin.

Enjoyment 10/10 - I have never in my life screamed and shouted and had my heart pumping in an anime before, that is until kingdom came along. You will just feel like a man watching this anime, what is more manly than running into battle with your sword pointing at your enemy agter a huge speach you had just given your comrades, an army behind you screaming ready to die just for you. You are going to shout when they are running into battle too, especially when you don't know the outcome. You just feel great after watching this anime, its on a much greater scale compared to for example Vinland Saga, so a 500 thousand man battle really gets your heart pumping, when Xin jumps into the middle of it since he has no fear of anything that's when it gets even more enjoyable.

Overall 10/10 - Do yourself a favour and watch this anime, it will make you feel like a real man man and bring out a bag of emotions in you, it really annoys me that this anime is not huge. It should be up there with naruto, bleach and one piece its really impressive, my only hope is that it comes more popular. Again I say do NOT think because its not popular that its not good, most underrated anime of the decade in my opion, a true hidden gem.

A state that was well-known for its power, its history, and its tremendous influence over the rest of the six states to suddenly face a coalition army that required months, if not years of detailed planning from its supplies down to the very man that even a developed nation today can’t ever hope to meet. The amount of politics, resources, and devotion that went into this coalition required every participant’s sacrifice. A coalition that no other kingdoms or states could ever dream of. Even after the warring states period of China. Qin, a state that has lost its six great generals system, one of the main components that contributed to the peak of its power. Has a king that was unheard of by anyone, a child that was not born within the royal palace is sitting on a throne of an enormous amount of history and power. His great-grandfather, a king that built the very country that he is sitting on from a merely regular state to a powerhouse. A country that demonstrated its prowess so much that it forced all other six states to bend its knees to pay in respect. While simultaneously, struck fear into them to keep them in check.

El Sei, despite being a king of a grand state, never allowed that pressure to crumble him. El Sei’s previous life as a peasant shaped his world view. Due to this, he doesn’t view that being a king is a privilege that he can use to exploit off of his people. But, rather, just a mere step to his grand goal of unifying China under his rule and to end centuries of infighting within China. The infighting that has caused millions of lives, resources, and bloodshed. Similarly, the abuse he received during his childhood shaped the person that he is now. When facing a crisis, he does not crumble into nothingness. He becomes the shining light for everyone. The perfect example was when everyone in the council around him began to panic and crumble when they first learned the coalition army was advancing towards their doorstep. Instead of using their heads to counter the overwhelming forces, they were already believing that they were done for good. El Sei was the one who pulled them together and inspired them to keep hammering at their heads no matter the light of day. His priority was the citizens of his state. Thus, he orders the safety of the people first while they devised strategies to counteract the forces. Further demonstrating that he was not detached from reality. He was not a king that only cared about his power, status, fame, or royalty. But the people of his state.

What Kingdom demonstrates well is portraying the reality of the war during the warring states period. Every single goddamn panel that involved men clashing on the battlefield were clearly drawn out in the manga. The bodies, blood, sweat, and facial expressions give the overall sensation of hell on the battlefields. Whether they were at the center or just to the corner of the panels, the brutality of war was expressed clearly on their faces. Furthermore, the manga was not afraid to demonstrate how civilians were impacted. Numerous times the manga accurately depicted how civilians were killed, buried, and burned to death -- those panels were disgusting. Now, I don’t know how the anime will handle these scenes, but so far they have shown the willingness to show off a bit of gore from the manga.

Shin, the main protagonist of this series is one the best part of Kingdom along with El Sei. Due to the nature of the story, Shin cannot be written as just a main character that is angry about his best friend’s death, and therefore drives forward throughout the story because he is the main protagonist. The author understands what Shin needs to be. Yes, Shin’s main purpose is to become one of the great generals of Qin. But, he also has a human side. Shin isn’t a dumb brat that is good at swing his sword and cutting down his enemies because the series needs him to be. The author has already presented this with other generals on the battlefield. Especially with Ou Ki, who Shin is destined to be. What the author chose to focus on were Shin’s ideals. Various times in the story, Shin was shown particular care for people that were harmed. Whether it was the killing of innocent civilians or a wounded man from a battlefield. At times, he even takes his belief to the extreme. Just as an example, when he discovered that Kan Ki (Yi Huan) massacred a village of people that Kan Ki just conquered, Shin lost it. Not only did he challenge Kan Ki to a duel, but he also banished one of his close friends and comrades from his unit without any consideration just because that friend was drinking with them. That friend of his wasn’t even informed about the massacre.

When you are a leader of a unit, there are times when a battle lasts so long that your men are just exhausted, hungry, and on edge, because they could die the very next battle. During that time, you need to be able to inspire your men to continue fighting. Generally, in anime, we have seen that our main protagonist can inspire their comrades by just yelling “let’s go!” and they were instantly fired up with no further talking. These protagonists had no doubt whatsoever about his or her abilities and were able to finish the job in no time. Shin isn’t, and doesn’t do that type of shit. He yells to his men that the majority of them will die in battle. He tells them the truth and the reality of their situation. But, he can lift their spirits by articulating how far they have come as a unit. He goes deep on their emotions, feelings, and turns those into a raging fire inside of them. And before you know it, they were charging at their enemies like wild beasts that put the enemies, who were relatively carefree, into a panic mode. Often not realizing it, Shin’s bravery, spirit, and actions on the battlefield inspired the men behind him to continue fighting when they were overwhelmed.

It is a shame that this season, which adopts the best arc in the manga, won’t get much attention in the west due to the gap between season 2 and season 3. Instead, we have anime such as Kami no Tou, Kaguya-Sama, and Fruits Basket taking the spotlight for this season. I’m not saying these anime aren’t going to be good shows, I’m saying that if Kingdom was popular in the west, it would have beaten all the currently airing popular shows by a mile. So, do yourself a favor, if you haven’t watched kingdom. Don’t bother watching season 1 or 2. Just read the manga before watching this season.

Score: 8/10

Season 3 of Kingdoms absolutely nailed it!!! S3 starts almost immediately after the military and political events after S2 so i consider it a direct sequel to S2 a few years back. What suprised me in S3 is that the anime jumps right into the plot and military action, no unnecessary fillers, or overly emotional moments. The animation is even better than that of S2 which was already a lot better compared to the bad animation of the first Season of Kingdoms.

Kingdoms as a series, focus on the warring States Period of medieval china. What's unique here
is that despite the story following mostly our main protagonist Xin, it also displays the grand scale of the military and political setting of the warring states period through the use of a proper narattion, making it easy to understand the tactics and politics of the period.

We also get to see great generals and tacticians from both enemies and allies and how the impact they have on troop morale and battles. Another thing i like is that Great generals appear to
be a lot bigger than the average person and of course a lot bigger than our protagonist Xin, who is supposedly legendary strong, this gets to show you in a straightforward way the scale of power between people and how they grow after a lot of experience. Makes me wonder if Xin will grow huge if/when he becomes a Great General.

All in all Kingdoms is an epic anime with great characters and setting!

If you decide to watch S1, don't get put off by the first 15 episodes or so, they had bad animation and they were boring, just keep going and it will improve a lot.
