Not in a million years would I ever believe hentai could have any deep or profound meaning behind the infamous walls of haram panty shots, obscene angles, and buckets of discharge but after watching Aki- Sora Yume no Naka..... I've realized.... I've been ignoring the obvious metaphors, symbolism and the absolute beauty behind the walls structured by mainstream, uncultured anime watchers have built over the past decades by our ancestors.

this. is truly a masterpiece.

Aki Sora is a deep and well-crafted story following our protagonist Sora, who has developed a budding sexual relationship with his direct sibling, Aki. He has a twin sister who does not have big boobs. ( i don't know her name ) There is also the nerdy girl who has a love interest in Sora. However, the classic shy anime girl trope is present in this hentai. I feel like it is well implemented and works beautifully to draw a comparison to real life. It reminds me almost of Shakespeare when he wrote the declaration of independence. It truly moved me as that was such a great piece of literature and the chemistry between Aki, sora, and big boob black hair girl was obviously based on the budding tale of romance, teen angst, sexual repression, and rebellion followed by the declaration of independance.

How you may ask? i will explain to you.

The whole story follows a story depicting the real grim nature of depression and mental illness in adolescent teenagers. Showing the darker and honestly sad side of love, which is never explored in the anime genre of television. The flat-boobs girl loves the girl she pared with sora. She realizes her grave mistake when she witnesses the two fornicating in the school classroom.... Pain. absolute pain. It had obviously broken her. The tears bled through her eyes as a form of artistic expression and resembled the cum on the floor. While the camera pans the cum on the floor and back to the flat girl's face.. I felt a sense of disgust... not for the anime, instead, for human beings in general. The world is so cruel.... it shows us that sometimes, we may never really receive what we want from the bottom of our heart no matter how long we wait.

I'm not going to spoil the ending as I feel like it moved me. and it would have moved you as well, so i wouldn't want to spoil it.

Not ever did I feel this was ever rushed, or instances were random. I never questioned why this was happening, or how things happened. the story was so great I cried after it was over.

10/10.
