*This review contains spoilers in a marked section*

All it takes is one glance at Cautious Hero’s title to know everything you need to know about it. It summarizes the story, the main character, and the anime’s one and only joke.

The titular cautious hero is the worst part of the show. Seiya is the hero, but he has no personality. He's everything you'd expect from an edgy isekai protagonist—an overpowered asshole. He's accompanied by the much more colorful goddess of healing, Ristarte. She summons Seiya to help her defeat the demon king. Compared to the potential heroes, Seiya has significantly higher stats, but there's a catch. He's impossibly cautious. Ristarte thought there's no way his flaw would outweigh his skills, and she needed a hero desperately. She fucked up.

I don’t know what I expected, Seiya sucks as a protagonist because you can never tell what he’s thinking unless he says it aloud. The few times he does say something, it’s some bitchy comments about Rista being a dumbass. No matter how many kind things she does for him, he treats her like crap. She's nice, really, yet the show never allows her to gain confidence. He keeps her around to heal him and cook—but eventually, he just uses healing potions. Seiya could be replaced with an angry plank of wood and there’d be no difference. He's motivated to protect himself first, then save the world, no one else matters. He almost qualifies as a villain because of how he chooses to use his overpowered stats. Needless to say, he’s not a hero. Which is the point of this Satirical Comedy: It tries to imitate Konosuba in many ways, maybe a little too much.

There was a fight early on where Seiya burnt down an entire village of innocent people. What was his reason? Caution. It was never mentioned later on. After that incident, any chance of he would become a ‘good guy’ was off the table. No nuance, no clever writing, he is just a humorless asshole. The jokes never evolve because Seiya never changes. This wouldn’t be a big problem if the story progressed. So does it? Well, kind of... Like most isekai fantasies, the goal is for the hero to save the world from a demonic threat. Seiya levels up so fast that no enemy could stand in his way. Forget power scaling, they only use MMO terms for the target audience. For humor's sake, this is fine. For the story, it is detrimental. A threadbare story is expected from a gag comedy. However, there’s nothing to the show other than the stale jokes, it gets tedious to watch very fast


I enjoyed the first episode, but after that, I realized there would be nothing to it but that one joke—and it didn’t take long for me to get sick of it. I started to wonder why. Why is this guy so cautious? Why was he the one summoned? Why does he have the personality of a potato? I waited for an explanation, I waited for character development. It did not come until the final episodes.

(Spoilers Below)

In the eleventh hour, there's a twist that reveals Seiya wasn't always a shitty person. He was summoned to fight in the same world once before, but this time he was overly ambitious and kind. Every fight left him with 1 HP. He would run into impossible fights and always win—until he and his party got killed by a boss with two lives. When he is reincarnated, he gets told to always be cautious, even though people will hate him. In some way, this twist is supposed to excuse how he treats everyone. He made his companions, Mash and Elulu, mules to carry equipment, not because they're useless but because he was trying to protect them! He shit talked Ristarte and treated her like his maid because he wanted to protect her! So all along, Seiya treated everyone like objects because objects are easy to keep safe. Uh, huh.

The twist adds nothing to him. He’s still a cowardly asshole, yet his caution isn't a flaw because it always works out in the end.

In my opinion, all good twists should be surprising, but upon reflection, you should see where it was hinted. Cautious Hero misses this. The twist comes out of buttfucking nowhere and it adds nothing but transparent emotional manipulation. This anime breaks the basic rule of film, Chekov’s Gun. In other words, set up and pay off. This rule gives a story exceptional rewatch value. In Cautious Hero’s case, I found the majority of the show repetitive and underwhelming. Even after the twist, I felt like I wasted my time. All pay off, no setup. Although I wouldn’t call the twist satisfying in any respect, it at least led to a heartfelt ending. Or maybe not quite. Without the build-up, the tear-jerker ending rang hollow. Ending aside, there are still many more problems with this show that I need to address—and the commendable ways it stands out from other isekai.

(Spoilers End)

The jokes are derived from Rista’s reactions to Seiya being a jackass. He annoys her with his cowardliness. What riles her up the most is his lack of care for anyone. She’s so relatable because of how frustrated she gets. Who wouldn’t be annoyed by this guy? Also, it was pretty funny how horny she was for him. Even with all the stupid stuff she does, she still tries to maintain her nobility as a Goddess. Rista is a clone of Aqua from Konosuba with slight differences. Her over-the-top personality is the highpoint of Cautious Hero. She made it worth watching the boring parts. We see the narrative from her first-person point of view, almost like she is the protagonist. Completing Rista and Seiya’s party is Mash and Elulu, two kids tagging along out of respect for Seiya. I don’t have much to say about them. One is a girl who was there to be cute. The boy acts like a standard shounen character, he is determined to Do His Best. Seiya is so overpowered that he doesn’t need a party. The other members are useless compared to him.

As for the animation, Cautious Hero adopts the loose animation style of Konosuba. With this art style, the animators can get away with making the designs can look a little off-model for the sake of humor. They draw faces to look stupid a lot. When the art looks a little unintentionally bad, it’s still goofy because it rarely takes itself seriously. This style doesn’t extend to the background art or storyboards, which are both mediocre. I loved how colorful this show tried to be, but there were times colors clashed hideously. Thankfully the character designs are pretty good.

Ristarte and her fellow Goddesses wear beautifully decadent outfits. Per isekai standard, they have pieces of cloth instead of bras. I liked that Seiya made fun of how impractical Rista’s outfit was rather than just letting it fly (hey DanMachi). The one design that stood out to me the most was the Goddess of war and her bikini made of chains; it suits her hostile demeanor so well. Conversely, Seiya’s character design is quite generic—it adds nothing to his personality. Where the humor shines is Ristarte’s many ridiculous faces. She is a folder of reaction gifs in the form of a character. The music is fine; I think the OP is one of Myth & Roid’s weakest songs, above Isekai Shit Magician, of course. The ED is alright if you shield your eyes from the ugly CGI dance.

Cautious Hero would have been another Konosuba clone, but the ending made it slightly different. They're both self-aware isekai comedies with asshole protagonists and useless female characters. If only this show wasn’t shackled Seiya, it could have been more enjoyable. He prevents any other character from developing—yet he has even less personality than Kirito. There are one, maybe two jokes, and they're repeated over and over. If you want a watchable isekai gag comedy, this won't do.

I seem to be in the minority here, but I just... yeah.
When have standards fallen so low than anything remotely subverting a dead genre becomes 'groundbreaking'? Is something really good just by virtue of making fun of something else, regardless of execution?
It's not a secret that this show is heavily... borrowing... from other shows. Comparisons have risen. Defenders say 'but ___ doesn't have this joke or that!' which only makes one wonder: if this is so good, why do you feel the need to constantly say the shows it rips off aren't as good?
Given the concept and premise, I'm not going to criticize this based on the characters or setup itself, but rather, the execution.
I'm pretty easy to make laugh. I can and will enjoy all sorts of trash for what they are, too. I must say, to this show: exaggerated faces shouldn't be the peak of your comedy. What else is there? The protagonist is extremely OP and way too cautious. I mean, it's what the title says. Other than that... what else is there? The protagonist destroying the world more than the things he's supposed to save it from given his nature? I mean, yeah, it could work, and it could work pretty well, but repeating this exact same joke violates the principles of 'jokes become less funny with each successive repetition'. You can pull the running gag tirade if you're clever about it, but this show seems to believe that the hero causing nuclear levels of destruction to make sure a slime is dead while explaining this with a deadpan expression and the goddess freaking out was hilarious the first time, and so it ought to happen every other episode the exact same way, with the exact same timing and punchlines.
Comedy and horror are two sides of the same coin because they both rely on surprising the audience; with horror, you scare them and with comedy, you make them laugh. Essentially, though, to make these genres work, you have to play on expectations and keep them at the edge of their seat, and if the joke/scare is too obvious it should be because that's what the audiences look forward to as opposed to thinking 'oh no, they're not going to do THAT, are they?'.
Like I said, I'm not hard to please. I'll be cackling at the most inane things, which shocked me when I realized, halfway through the fourth episode, that I smiled only then (I don't remember for what, though it was with the show, not at it). Some of the jokes had a prety good foundation, but the punchlines fell flat by virtue of being things we have seen so many, many times already. Again: if your comedy relies on some character making exaggerated expressions, then you've failed at it.
With the serious but strange and expressive but relatively level-headed duo, it's to be expected that one of them does outrageous things while the other one reacts. That's fine. The problem is, all she does is scream and wiggle around as if to distract the audience from the fact no amount of wit was put into the joke. If she's not doing this, she's doing that classic 'scream into the sky' reaction which, quite frankly, tends to kill jokes for me more often than make them funnier. Compare and contrast to the how it's most obviously ripping off, KonoSuba, which isn't exactly guilt-free when it comes to repetitive gags but keeps finding different ways to set them up. Another show which I think suffers from the same issue is Seitokai Yakuindomo, but even that one managed to bring a surprise every now and then. Moreover, both of those shows actually made me laugh.
At some point, i wondered, too, what can I actually get from watching this show? It's depressing me and it obviously has nothing else to offer beyond the attempted comedy given it falls into the trap of 'make everything as cliché as possible and make fun of it so people won't notice' as well. I'm not saying clichés are bad or that a generic premise dooms the show... unless that's all there is.
Again, have our standards fallen so low that any piss-poor attempt at satire is to be applauded and defended for 'not being like the rest'?
