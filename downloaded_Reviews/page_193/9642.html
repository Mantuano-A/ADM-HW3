I feel like the best way to describe Aoi Sekai is as follows:
Say you're at a party scouting for a nice girl. You see some nice ones, you see some slightly less nice ones but nobody that really grabs you. And then it happens. This [insert your favorite hair color]-ed vixen appears and draws you in. And after getting to know her better a bit, you're drawn in even further. So when you wake up after what you hoped would be a great night with her, you instead wake up disappointed and wondering if it was all worth it.

Don't get me wrong, Aoi Sekai certainly has a good concept behind it. Personifying the 90s console wars, what could go wrong? Well, lots of things sadly.

The major problem lies with the characters. Gear, our protagonist, decides to join the Segua army after losing his brother (a character so memorable and important that I can't remember his name) in a fight against the Ninteldo empire. And here we run into our first problem.
Avenging a fallen family member is as good a reason as any to go on a crusade but Aoi Sekai forgets the crucial ingredient to this; actually introducing the characters and building them so we can actually give a shit. Literally the first thing that happens is Gear's brother is dead, boom. We don't know why or what their characters are like, he's the hero, the likable one. Hell, for all we know Gear and his brother are actually gigantic asshats who committed grave crimes against the Ninteldo empi-okay, I'm just gonna call them by their proper names now, this is getting annoying. Anyways, for all we know Gear and his brother are actually gigantic asshats who committed grave crimes against Nintendo and Sega are the real bad guys!

Next up, purple-haired useless damsel du jour, Nel. Holy shit, what a useless character. Literally all she does, LITERALLY ALL SHE DOES is stand around and make sounds like she's orgasming at whatever exciting business is going on around her or spouting random superfluous nonsense. You could replace her with a sex doll with a tape recorder cellotaped to her face and you'd get an equally deep and useful character.

Then comes....Titties McArcher, I forget her name. The primary fanservice character. Cute? Check. Big....tracts of land? Check. Skimpy and incredibly self-defeating outfit? Big check! So called "professional" and "incredibly skilled character" who gets her ass handed to her by the main character first chance he gets? You guessed it!

But now we get the big one. Tejilof. My god, this guy is awful. I mean, REAL awful. Why? For starters, he's a walking Tetris reference with all the subtlety of a raging bull in a china shop. Ear ring shaped like a Tetris block that's on screen all the time. And if it isn't, you'll DEFINITELY notice how his sword handle looks suspiciously like....A TETRIS BLOCK! HA! DIDN'T SEE THAT COMING, DID YOU? LOOK AT ME, I'M A TETRIS REFERENCE, CAN YOU TELL?!
Now now, this won't be THAT much of a problem if he's funny, right?

Oh.

Yeah, not only is this guy a walking Tetris reference but his dialogue consists of nothing but stupid double entendres. In fact, let's just cut this short and say that the only good thing about this character is that his VA is also Keima Katsuragi, one of my favorite characters of all time.

This is the part where I normally talk about the plot and such but there's nothing. And for an anime that fails to properly introduce its characters or make us sympathize with them, that's a pretty big feat.

I suppose the nicest thing I can say about Aoi Sekai is that it looks actually kind of decent, considering how bad everything else is. But to hop back on the other hand, the action looks disjointed and messy as hell. Characters seem to randomly move between shots at times.

What I'll simply leave as my final words is that Aoi Sekai is probably the most disappointed I've ever been in an anime. An interesting idea that had true potential is driven into the ground by characters that make a piece of paper look deep and action that is beyond unsatisfying.

Another thing that i found lacking is all the settings seemed really bland and simple. The three major settings i can clearly remember were forest,training hall and dungeon and they all look exactly as what you'd expect when somebody says those titles. The artists played it really safe and didn't put all that much effort into making a really interesting visual experience.

The Story's main problem is despite it possibly being tolerable it is done poorly. It starts without a prologue at a scene where you have to question why it starts that way. It stems from the problem you don't care about there characters so all the meaning in this scene is empty and pointless, they should have used a prologue to that scene. The biggest problem facing this short series is the fact that it is a short series, three 20 minute episodes don't take you very far. I actually would have liked to see the characters go off into the different kingdoms and face off against Marcus(Mario). The pacing just could not be done right for this series so they tried to emphasis emotional scenes hence why they start and end with one. It just did not work out though with the lack of character development.

Being a really big classic game fan and a even bigger fan of anime i kinda hoped this fusion would turn out at least alright. My hopes were dashed though and i ended up having a really boring and uneventful time. I did not really enjoy it and some people may but it feels more like a time waster, just something to watch when you got nothing else too. So ultimately this gets a 3 and i feel i might be a little generous with that.

Since I recently finished what is now my favorite anime, xxxHolic, and I have a tendency to start a show I end up hating after I discover what becomes my favorite anime, I decided to try and watch something short that looked really bad so I wouldn't waste too much time; so, I chose to watch this show. Man what a mistake that was!

What is Aoi Sekai no Chuushin de (which translates to mean World War Blue, and as such, I will be referring to the series as it's translated name), you might ask? Well, have you seen Excel Saga? Have you seen Wreck-It Ralph? Have you ever inhaled paint fumes to get high? If the answer to those 3 questions is "Yes", then congratulations! You've seen World War Blue.

The story of World War Blue is basically a fantasy version of the console war between Nintendo and Sega, with Nintendo being the villains. It's pretty basic from there.

For characters, we have Gear, our main character who is supposed to a parody be Sonic the Hedgehog (insert joke about going fast here), standard strong female character Oppai-I mean Opal (who's supposed to be a parody of one of the characters from Fantasy Zone; I guess she's supposed to be the love interest for Gear; and if you couldn't figure it out from the "nickname" I gave her, she's also the show's "Ms. Fanservice" character), Nel (Gear's younger sister who's an extremely useless character, and a parody of Nei from Phantasy Star 2), Til (who's a parody of Tails from Sonic; he dies at the start, so we feel no connection to his character due to the fact that we didn't get to know him, and we are unaffected by his death) and easily the most bizarre character of them all, Tejilof, a gigantic pervert who's a parody of Tetris of all things (I'm not kidding) - in fact, Tejilof is such a pervert that one of the first things he does is literally grope Ramsus, the general for the army of the country that represents Sega.

The only action scenes that were actually remotely interesting were the ones involving Opal, mainly because she seems to undergo the closest thing this show has to character development - she decides to push her ability, which allows her to fire bursts of energy from her fingers, to her limit, which causes her pain.

One of the few good things about this show is that it actually does look good - and the sound isn't bad, either. It's a shame that the rest of the anime doesn't look as good.

Lastly, there were 2 franchises that seemed absent that had a good reason to be present - the first was Mortal Kombat; I feel it was absent, since both Sega and Nintendo got a copy of Mortal Kombat, but Sega did not censor their version. It would have made much more sense to have a character who's a parody of Mortal Kombat instead of one who's a parody of Tetris - and some might argue that having a living parody of Mortal Kombat would make this show too violent, but the amount of fanservice already makes this inappropriate for kids.

The other franchise that seemed absent was Megaman - but I actually consider this a good thing, since I grew up playing Megaman. And I would have hated for Megaman to be associated with this piece of work.

I guess if I had to say one good thing about this anime, it has made me kind of curious to try the manga, which looks a lot better.

Alternative recommendations: Log Horizon, Excel Saga, Wreck-It Ralph, Ixion Saga DT

I can sum this anime up with the 8 deadly words: I don't care what happens to these people.

Oh boy, this OVA. Let me tell you, I am not going to be pleasant towards it. I'm honestly glad this only had 3 episodes. Have you ever wanted to see an anime about the 90s console war between Nintendo and Sega, even though we should be way past that now? I kinda hope you aren't, because this will be just a waste of your time.

Story: I'll admit, despite it being ahead of its time, I was a bit intrigued by the premise, especially since I love video games and I have fondness towards Nintendo and Sega, especially Nintendo. However, that intrigue was lost when it became obvious what it really was. This anime, for some reason, chooses to be really biased towards Sega while painting Nintendo as the villains. We get most of the screen time with Segua characters, while there isn't nearly enough screen time with Ninteldo characters. I think this would've been a bit more interesting if it ended up focusing on both sides, like there being good and bad apples on both sides and their motivations. But nope, you're not gonna get ANY of that.

Another problem is that there actually isn't that much plot, or at least not one I found interesting. The only plot I can get out of this is our main protagonist joining and winning this war against an empire. NEVER HEARD OF THAT BEFORE. Now, this wouldn't be so bad, except it's not done well at all. Any plot that's there is cliche and predictable. to the point the show ends up becoming more of a chore to watch than it should be. If you don't know what I mean, look at any fantasy story ever and that's pretty what you'll get out of this.
RATING: 2

Art and Sound: I'm grouping up these two because I can say the same thing about both of them. The art and music aren't bad, per say, but nothing stands out at all about them. The characters just look like your typical anime characters, the environments and backgrounds are really basic fantasy, and the music is not necessarily something I want to listen to again and again.
RATING: 5

Characters: I'm sorry, was there character development in this anime? I think I forgot. These characters are about as bland as you can get. Their ONLY intrigue you'll get from them is that they're based on Nintendo and Sega characters, but even then, are you really gonna be intrigued by them in the end? This show doesn't really bother to build them up properly. Gear (Sonic), our main protagonist, is introduced as his best friend/sibling, who is a stand in for Tails, dies, is angered by this, and is driven to become stronger because of this. But since neither of them are really interesting, I just ended up not caring about them.

Opal is the "main heroine", but is really just there to be the fanservice character that most of the characters lust over and who fans can get their rocks off to and I guess she's Gear's love interest. She starts off as your typical man-hating tsundere who mellows down after Gear shows up in her life. Again, you're not given much screen time of their relationship, so why should you care? Also, gotta have her hardly wear anything and make her look like a walking sex object. Opal? MORE LIKE OPPAI AMIRITE? (Ok, that just sounded dumb...) But in all seriousness, I honestly wish anime would do this less and less, because that's something I was always annoyed by. That's pretty much all you need to know.

There are other characters like Niel, Gear's sister who pretty much does nothing and is just there to be the cute sister character, and Teijlof, who is a....Tetris parody that's a pervert.....WHY??? Then you have Marcus (Mario), who's the tyrannic leader of Ninteldo, Zelig (Link), who is for lack of a better word, insane, and other characters who are just background characters, like...a Kirby parody who is a pink haired girl that has sizable breasts....okay, I'm done talking about the characters. Nice representation of Nintendo, anime (Not really. Then again, this kinda applies to Sega too).
RATING: 1

Enjoyment: At best, I was bored since there's mostly just talking, and at worst, I was annoyed and disgusted, especially with the fanservice and the obvious bias towards Sega. Any jokes there are, I mostly just react like "UGGHH, I get it, it's a sexual joke about Tetris." Surely, you won't get sick of that joke the 10th time we tell them. The action sucks too, especially since there's really hardly any of it. I was just waiting for it to end, and I'm really glad it did. There was hardly anything I enjoyed in this anime.
RATING: 1

Overall: This feels like a Sega fanbrat/Nintendo hater's wet dream come true. This is one of those anime that disappoints me on a personal level. It's an anime that sounds interesting, but it doesn't really seem like there was that much effort put into it. This OVA just ended up being worse than I thought it would be. You can read fanfiction of the Console War and enjoy them more than this anime. I don't recommend watching it at all.
