Rescue Me! is a single episode borderline-hentai OVA (meaning it shows everything except genitalia). The story is about as simple as you can get for an ecchi show: an average high school boy is surrounded by a harem of girls (well, one isn't actually after him aside from wanting to slit his throat).
The girls each fit into a specific archetype and aren't very creative (not saying they're necessarily bad, though):
- Busty female lead who takes care of the protagonist as his maid
- Underclassman who looks up to the previously mentioned busty girl
- Younger girl with a flat chest who calls the protagonist "onii-chan"
- Yandere girl who likes the flat-chested girl and is jealous of the protagonist

The girls all have some physical characteristics that make them unique, though they don't serve much of a purpose other than to be included in the guy's harem. Still, the character designs are good and the animation overall actually very good aside from a couple shots that are used twice in a row.

Despite being borderline-hentai, there is no actual sex and the only "sex scenes" are the ones that are shown in the girls' imaginations. These are all pretty short which was a shame because they had potential to be great in ways other than to just make the viewer laugh for a little bit. Outside of the girls' imaginations, there are still plenty of breasts on display, but that's as far as it goes. There is a lot of comedy in this OVA (which is it's strongest point aside from the animation). It's mostly comedy based on accidents and misunderstandings (which usually annoy me in similar things like "To-Love Ru"), and while they are sometimes annoying in this, they can also be very funny.

Check this out if you just want a quick, funny, and very ecchi show. If you're looking for great plot/character development or if you're looking for something that shows "everything," then you should skip this. Rescue Me! has good animation and decent comedy, but it's bad in basically every other category (even compared to hentai).

Some anime just can't help but come close to crossing the border between anime and hentai. Rescue Me is one of those anime. I actually tried to watch it a few months ago, but I stopped about 5 minutes into it. After that, I kept trying to tell myself, "I'm not going to watch the whole thing." Boy, do I wish that I listened to myself!

Rescue Me is a one-episode OVA about a boy named Masayuki Mizutani. Apparently, his parents are out on vacation or something, so he is now living alone. At least, that's what he thinks. He walks into his house and is suddenly greeted by a girl wearing nothing but an apron. This girl is Sayaka Shimizu, who is in the same grade as Mizutani, and she was hired by Mizutani's parents to be his maid. Insert ecchi situation after ecchi situation here.

The story of Rescue Me is practically non-existent. You would think that an OVA with one episode in it would have at least a bit of a plot, but nope! All there is in this OVA are scenes that would make you think that this is a hentai! Apparently, the female characters, especially Sayaka, think that this is a hentai, too. What I mean is that a lot of the ecchi moments come from the "imaginative" minds of the female characters (and even one male character). It's like all of the characters, except Mizutani, are perverts, and if EVERY character in an anime is a pervert, there's definitely a problem.

Well, at least the animation is decent. Rescue Me has some bright, rather colorful animation. The voice acting is rather decent, as well; while they don't do a great job, they don't do terribly, either. The opening theme is a cute song; not a good song at all, but certainly cute. As for the ending theme.... wait a minute, the opening and ending themes are the same song! Well, not that I mind that too much, since this is just an OVA.

Even so, Rescue Me is a less than enjoyable anime. Giving it a 4 is me being extremely generous; it actually deserves a 3 or something. If you like borderline-hentai anime, and ONLY if you like borderline-hentai anime, I'd tell you to watch this. I warn you, though; this anime, believe it or not, is actually worse than another borderline-hentai anime that I've watched and reviewed.

It is one episode ova that used to be released as a bonus to a manga, I believe.

There isn't really much to say about this title - it is a fanservice comedy through and through, with gratitudous nudity but no actual porn. It doesn't really stand out in any aspect, which is why I rated it as 5/5/5/5/5/5

If anything, the formula used by the story is similar to "Ane Log: Moyako Neesan no Tomaranai Monologue", except that quality of art is worse, and there's actual nudity.

Basically, you have a protagonist that is trying to be a normal person with uneventful life (a fairly common trope), and couple of characters that have some interest in him, except that a lot of them tend to drift off into "ecchi delusion" at a drop of a hat. Meaning they start imagining stuff, then they speak what they imagined as if it actually happened, and the main(?) character will hopelessly try to diffuse the situation. So, same comedy device again and again. Except that unlike ane log they actually draw the suggestive content, and there's nudity.

The art isn't anything exceptional, it is a fairly standard stereotypical manga drawing, without unusual features that would make it stand out... for example, "Jitsu wa" had rounded eyes that made every character adorable. Ane log was surpisingly well detailed and had cool designs on characters. This anime... has nothing exceptional. it isn't poorly drawn by any means, it is just the visual style is very very standard.

Similar thing happens in all other aspects. Because it was a manga bonus, there are cost cutting tricks employed (usually panning scenes), but they aren't horrible, and are used sparingly. Voice actors do their job, but that's about it. The story follows the same pattern, and you something similar elsewhere (if you ever watched harem animes, that is), but it isn't exactly horrible.

One interesting thing about it is how surprisingly light hearted it is. There's no dark stuff in here (like in Valkyria Drive, Queen's Blade, Masou Gakuen HxH, etc). No emotional abuse, etc.

So, basically... it is a 5/10 anime that is neither bad or good, and usually you'd pick something like this up if you want an ecchi comedy without dark elements, aren't offended with nudity and can roll with silliness of the premise.

It is not something like To Love Ru, but it might be worth a watch. Then again, you won't lose much if you skip it.
