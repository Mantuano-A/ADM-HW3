Due to my review getting taken down because of some bullshit reason I decided to rewrite it completely instead.


This review is basically gonna be a review for season 2 of One Punch Man, as well as a review for this special. Let me give a little resume, the story of OPM is about this guy named Saitama who's legit the strongest being in the entire universe. After training for 3 years to become a hero, he one-day loses all his hair and can suddenly kill anyone with ONE punch. Later he starts to get really bored of being as strong as he is and now his lifelong dream is to one day find a worthy opponent who can truly give him the fight that he wants.


Now season 1 was kinda just Saitama and his sidekick Genos going around doing all kinds of shit every now and then. Season 2, however, is much different. Saitama isn't as much a focus anymore, which at first I was a bit bothered by but I've later understood that this decision was necessary and actually really good. We get to see a lot more of the other heroes, we get to learn more about their backstory and what really drives them to be heroes.


Okay now let's actually get to the damn review, shall we? Let's get the good stuff out of the way first. I really like the new OP song but the actual OP itself is pretty bad. The voice acting is pretty damn good, the voice for Garou is exactly how I imagined he sounded when I was reading the manga.

Unfortunately, this is where the praise stops. Because this season and this special, overall are just absolutely horrible and are a disrespect to ONE and Yusuke Murata. The animation is abysmal, the art looks like something I could have drawn on a fucking napkin. The pacing is also horrible, it just feels like the J.C Staff wanted to get this season and these specials out as quickly as possible and make some sweet money in the process. This wouldn't surprise me at all since they are one of the worst studios out there.

The passion that was there from season 1 is gone. It feels completely dead and lifeless. The same goes for the special, I gave it a chance because I thought that maybe it would actually be better but no... I would almost say it's even worse.

So would I recommend this? hell no fam... go read the manga instead, it's 10x better than this shit.

If a season 3 ever gets made, I hope it's done by an actual good studio. Like Ufotable or BONES, they could make something amazing.

Oh One Punch Man, how the mighty have fallen.

This will not be a long review detailing the failings of Season 2 of One Punch, there are plenty already and it would just be a waste of time—yours and mine. I will say, that despite the issues with animation season 2 faced, there was a definite improvement over time with the Garo fight single handedly saving the seasons' MyAnimeList score.

These OVA specials though? Yikes.

Not only do they look as bad as OPM2 did on a bad day, but they also fail to evoke the emotions from the viewer that ONE's writing normally does. Very little of it was funny, all of the jokes that were clearly meant to be jokes fell flat, especially the last episode. The scenarios for all of the specials were either predictable or unmemorable to the point of annoyance. OPM is not merely known for having fantastic visuals (at least in manga form) but also having strong characters that are fun to watch, easy to relate to and root for, and have interesting interactions with our titular main character. But these specials don't really provide much of that in any substantive way that you couldn't get elsewhere. In fact, I believe the specials even mention content that isn't even covered in the anime. Which is a great hint to go read the manga, something everyone should do it's fantastic.

I'm not going to talk about the art too much because it's already done to death but wow the specials look bad. It never gets to the level that certain episodes of the show do, which feel like they were largely drawn on four's and fives, but it doesn't mean they look good: Inconsistent and ugly character art, blah colors, low visual fidelity, no real directorial or cinematic flair. All characteristics of things that the laymen would define as "bad." And I would wholeheartedly agree. I can't normally watch pretty things that are poorly written anyways so how am I supposed to feel about this? You would think with the months in between the season and the releases of the Blu Rays that the team would have the time to make these specials look the best they can, but very clearly their schedules were just as garbage as the McDonalds that destroyed my intestines a few days ago.

I anguish for the animators that were murdered in the making of these works that were definitely not worth their blood sweat and tears; as well as the anime only viewer who will miss out on one of the best looking action manga of all time. Which is ultimately why I watched them all despite their poor quality, I love One Punch Man, I think ONE is a great writer and Yusuke Murata is a fantastic artist. The fact that this was released in its current state is an insult to them and all the super talented people caught up in the OPM production committee's attempt to strike while the iron is hot, instead of waiting for Shingo Natsume to finish their mediocre Boogiepop Adaptation.

I'm just glad they're all out so I can mark them as completed and move on with my life.

One Punch Man and Mob Psycho are very similar shows by the very same author, but have you ever wondered what would happen if each one took the other's formal and did a spin on it? That is precisely what these specials were, short skits with self-centered mini-stories that revolves around Saitama and gang, sometimes other characters that never got the amount of screen time they deserve (Puri Puri Prisoner).

I can see why they did not have this content cramped into the main story, they do jump off the timeline of the main plot quite often, your typical sitcom that doesn't respect the nitpicks of the plot, but it's fine for what it is.

English version:
Short, light and funny episodes from distinct One Punch Man characters.
1°: After stopping in a public bathroom, Saitama finds himself facing three outlaws who have a plan of villainy, they frame him to test their superpowers.

2°: Atomic Samurai and Bang go fishing after the events in city A, where they were attacked by aliens. The two talk about life, acquaintances, younger heroes and youth. The two longtime friends are in agreement that a hero doesn't just have to have courage and determination, what's lacking is just one thing: strength.

3°: Upon losing his memory, Genos tries to remember who he is, taking for himself personalities he finds in magazines and on television.

4° Saitama enters on a game tournament where people from all over the world can play online, but he is just aiming for the prize. The game features real fighting moves, and Saitama finds more people with great fighting skills, isn't he the only one with an absurd strength in the world?

5th Puri-puri prisoner escapes from prison and some inmates take the opportunity to escape together, the problem is that Puri-Puri prisoner always goes after those who escape from prison, thus showing that even being a prisoner, the hero is still an agent of the law.

6° A small group of heroes is sent to exterminate a monster that lives in the mountains. Caught by a blizzard, they are forced to stop at an inn. There, they come across a possible assassin who can kill everyone who is staying in the house, including Genos and Saitama who are there.

Portuguese version:
Episódios curtos, leves e engraçados de personagens distintos de One Punch Man.
1°: Após parar em um banheiro público, Saitama se vê enfrentando três marginais que possuem um plano de vilania, estes o enquadram para testar seus superpoderes.

2°: Atomic Samurai e Bang vão pescar depois dos acontecimentos na cidade A, onde foram atacados por alienígenas. Os dois conversam sobre a vida, os conhecidos, os heróis mais jovens e sobre a juventude. Os dois amigos de longa data estão de acordo que um herói não precisa apenas ter coragem e determinação, o que está faltando nos ademais é apenas uma coisa: força.

3°: Ao perder sua memória, Genos tenta se lembrar de quem é, tomando para si personalidades que ele acha nas revistas e na televisão.

4° Saitama se inscreve em um jogo onde pessoas de todo o mundo podem jogar online, mas ele apenas está visando o prêmio. O jogo conta com movimentos de lutas reais, e Saitama encontra mais pessoas com grande capacidade de luta, será que ele não é o único com uma força absurda no mundo?

5° Prisioneiro puri-puri foge da prisão e alguns presidiários aproveitam para escapar junto, o problema é que prisioneiro puri-puri sempre vai atrás daqueles que fogem da prisão mostrando assim que mesmo sendo um prisioneiro, o herói ainda é um agente da lei.

6° Um pequeno grupo de heróis é enviado para exterminar um monstro que mora nas montanhas. Pegos de surpresa por uma nevasca, eles são obrigados a pararem em uma estalagem. Lá, eles se deparam com um possível assassino que pode matar a todos que estão hospedados na casa, inclusive Genos e Saitama que se encontram ali.
