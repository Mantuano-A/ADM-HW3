Training with Hinako is obviously some kind of ecchi anime, thus I went into it with really low expectations, but to my surprise, it actually starts off somewhat unoffensive.

The story starts off with our main character Hinako, getting sucked into a TV, and then breaking the fourth wall by talking to all of the viewers, which came off super charming. However, it quickly devolves into what it really is, an ecchi tape disguised as a workout video. We are soon treated to clips of Hinako working out, and counting reps. Meanwhile, the camera is, naturally, focused on her boobs & butt.

The way they present these exercise routines in terms of the story is pretty decent in the first half, having a little dialogue before & after the workout. However, eventually this just becomes a collection of bonus footage of Hinako counting reps and wearing different outfits without any dialogue in-between. This became painfully mind-numbing, and was really difficult to sit through.

Anyway, Hinako is a very generic anime character. Even if they do have the conceit of her being somebody who was sucked into a TV, she still acts like the majority of empty-headed, ditzy, cute anime girls out there, and doesn't have any kind of defining personality traits, leaving her as a very standard and forgettable character.

As for the art and animation, it's passable, as is the sound design and the music, but all of it comes off as kind of sub-par, and once again... generic.

Overall, there isn't much originality to anything in this OVA, aside from the initial premise. However, the creators also fail to capitalize on that initial set-up, and thus the whole thing ends up feeling kind of unfulfilling and empty. Then, after they abandon the story, they give you 15 minutes of bonus footage, that I ended up sitting through, hoping that perhaps there was more at the end. There wasn't.

This wasn't very enjoyable as a whole, but the beginning is kind of funny, even if it's still not very good at all. However, If anybody wants to watch this out of curiosity, I recommend you only watch it for the first 10 minutes or so and then just shut it off, the rest is not only bland, but absolutely pointless.

Oddly enough, she even talks about the scheduled appearances in the show she's in; treating it like it's a job or something. Not to sound cynical but this is actually riveting for me. Not a lot of anime does this and for good reasons. So, when everything’s established, something must be shown in the remaining 20 minutes ─hence the “Training” in the “Training with Hinako” title (which is basically masturbation material). The rest of the OVA is just you watching Hinako doing 3 exercises: push-ups, sit-ups, and squats (after, of course, teaching you how to do them), from various angles (and for all the sick bastards, various outfits) for 3 “days” (days is what they use to indicate a training session). That’s it. Nothing much really. Also, this OVA has sequels. I’m not joking.

Everything else checks out. The art department gets +1 because jiggle physics, the sounds are just 4 things: background sound (which is not a lot because you’re in a room), Hinako’s VA (who did a fantastic job), that music they use when she starts training, and the ending credits song ─all of which are not bad.

So for everything that this anime is, what’s my say to it? Well I say it’s not for everybody. If you’re looking for something that would “spark your imagination”, then watch other ecchi shows (or you know, actually watch porn). What I won’t deny is Training with Hinako did something that made me interested to it (and no, it’s not her well-endowed body) and by the end of it, I had fun. There’s not a lot in this anime so it’s hard to recommend it. As for their sequels, well, I haven’t watched them ─make of that what you will. Word of advice: watch it with your friends. I think that's how it was meant to be watched.

Today, I decided to challenge myself.

I may have got an A in P.E. class last semester, but workouts bore me to no end. I can listen to music or browse the Internet on the treadmill, but sometimes I drop my phone and that's no fun. (Pretty sure I already broke the internal recording audio thingy from dropping it too many times.)

Suddenly, I had an idea. What if I could be more motivated to exercise if a cute anime girl would do it with me? I'd known about this "Training with Hinako" OVA for quite a while, and maybe it was the answer to my prayers.

I ate my pre-workout snack (cinnamon roll flavored protein bar) and eagerly clicked "Play".

STORY (4/10):

Training with Hinako's plotline is paper-thin - a girl named Hinako gets sucked into her TV one day and becomes an anime character because...plot. (I'd really like to see a psychological version of this where Hinako struggles with herself and starts to have a mental breakdown over becoming an anime character. Or maybe she's actually in a coma! The theories are endless...)

She's starring in a new anime, but she's been told she needs to lose 5kg or they'll cut her part. (Classic example of the 'Hollywood Pudgy' trope - Hinako is far from needing to lose 5kg.) She'll also call you a little chubby (which I am not, thank you very much - Oprah thinks this will be the year of my best body, which to me means getting more muscle on my frame) and asks you to do weight-training with her.

So, yeah, Training with Hinako lacks a substantial story. But really, that's like complaining there was no substance to the story in your Richard Simmons tape.

ART (6/10):

The artwork to Training with Hinako isn't half-bad - the boob physics and booty close-ups get a little ridiculous if you're legitimately trying to work out, though.

SOUND (5/10):

The music's barely worth mentioning. The voice actress for Hinako does a decent job, and the boob-jiggling has its own special sound effect. Lovely.

CHARACTER (5/10):

Hinako is the only character in the OVA we see, but those five minutes or so we get of her character development are decent. I'm sure she's somebody's waifu, which is what they were going for.

ENJOYMENT (5/10):

Believe it or not, Training with Hinako is a decent basic workout. I couldn't keep up with the supposedly out-of-shape girl at times, which I'm just going to blame the video on to save myself embarrassment.

The second half gets very repetitive, and the timers are confusing. I was on the floor utterly confused and changing positions as fast as possible. "Why are we doing so many push-ups? How many different outfits do you feel the need to wear while doing the same set of push-ups?"

Towards the end Hinako stops exercising and sits in some suggestive positions while counting. I assume this is the point where the stereotypical otaku is supposed to stop exercising and start...y'know.

OVERALL (5/10):

Lack motivation to exercise and like anime boobies? Maybe Training with Hinako is your video. If you were one of those weird kids who freeze-framed your mom's workout videos whenever she left the house, this is even better.

Are you pretty good at exercise already? Try an Insanity video or something. This will likely bore you.

Don't want to exercise and just want to admire the...artwork? Sorry if I disappoint you, but this is only PG-13. You're on the Internet, you can find something better.
