Everyone has heard the catchy Spanish folk song, "La Cucaracha," about a cockroach unable to walk because he has lost one of his six legs. While the song may be loved by children and fun to sing along to, it is highly unlikely that anyone feels the same affection for this icky-inspiring and disease-spreading pest. Cockroaches are believed to have originated more than 280 million years ago, in the Carboniferous era. Making them one of the oldest legends on earth, Cockroach the Legend.

In all honesty, I don't know why I even bother to start watching this series. Maybe just because it looks cute? funny? or the sad reality that I have so much free time at hand? Nevertheless, after giving this series a shot, it saddens me to say that I'm quite disappointed with its pure potential. It had a whole lot of potential in front of them, as it could be both cute and entertaining for us, but sadly it doesn't. It had a fair share of lovely moments, but more than that, it left me with its dried-up jokes and slapstick comedy that were far from funny. The show's existence is more direct to say about the torturing life of one cockroach. Each episode is all about how depressed our little cute cockroach can be, and that wasn't really what I call fun or good.  

Story 
The story of Gokicha! Or can be called CockGirl for short, is undeniably a heartwarming series at first. The premise that it holds shows that the series, perhaps, can go much further even though it is a little bit short. A story about a cockroach named Gokicha, who wants to be friends with a human without any hint of what you could say as common sense. Unfortunately, every human in the series treats her so badly that calling it a simple abuse would be an understatement. I mean, I'd think we're all like that, but hey, what's even the difference between this series and us beating cockroaches to death in real life? Nothing. It was not an enjoyable moment and I wouldn't say it was fun either.
 
Art and Animation
It’s not that bad, but it is also not that great either. It's still fine though. You have my words.

Conclusion
I had such high hopes for this series, but I guess it was too much. It doesn't go much further than the cycle of abuse thingy. Although I'd say it was a bit tad interesting in the first half. You might enjoy this series a lot more if you have fewer expectations of what this series should be about. In a sense, I'd say it was fine, albeit it could be much better.

This was one of the most simple anime I've ever seen, but it's nevertheless heart warming. The story's about an anthropomorphic cockroach girl who wants to befriend humans, but gets treated as all cockroaches get treated by humans. The episodes are very short, each about 8 minutes long, there are only 2 episodes released so far, and the animation is basic. The characters don't actually move their mouths when they talk, and a lot of "action" scenes are repetitive. Overall, the simplicity of the story, the characters, and the situations is surprisingly heart warming. If you want to feel good, and have 16 minutes to spare, you should definitely check this out.

As always my reveiw will be short and simple, much like this anime.

Gokicha!! Cockroach Girl! Is very short only 2 episodes at 8 minutes each, it is "ongoing" but episodes haven't been released in awhile so this anime might have died out.

The animation is extremely simple and almost made me stop watching, the few action scenes were looped or at least seemed that way, the characters rarely moved their mouths to speak.

I feel the basic idea behind the anime is great, viewing the world from from a cockroaches perspective was very interesting and kinda funny sometimes.

There doesn't seem to be a central story but since it's only on episode two it's kinda soon to make that assumption.

I hope this anime will be continued at some point, despite its many flaws if they play their cards right I think this could be a hit for a niche group.

This is a show which could have been really good, given the kind of care and funding other shows get. The premise is simple but easily enough to fill a twelve-episode series, as opposed to the short two episodes we got. The animation is janky and underproduced, but at its core all the problems being faced by this series are the result of factors outside of its control, which is oddly fitting given the theme.

The factors within its control are all well-done. The show manages to be emotionally heavy despite its brevity, and the main character is interesting and amicable. There's room for a lot of ethical philosophy within the writing of the show which they seem keen on exploring. But inn the end we get only two episodes, so that's all.

All in all this was a good show hamstrung by its funding and the realities of corporate media manufacture.
