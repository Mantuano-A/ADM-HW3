So I don't write reviews because I don't know how to do it, but it hurts me how badly Itazuraguma no Gloomy is being reviewed. So, I'm taking its defense.

Personally, I think that the reviews on this "anime" are too harsh and misleading.
Of course, this is not a masterpiece or anything similar to that, but I don't believe it deserves such bad reviews.
I mean, what do you really expect from a 1 minute episode?
Plot? Of course not, it's too short to have a decent plot.
Sound? I'll emphasize this: 1 MINUTE!!
You can see Itazuraguma no Gloomy as something small like an ad. It's funny, short and it's mostly there to sell Gloomy's merchandise.

Most people who don't like this, they talk a lot about the violent tendencies of this series, but they don't really know the premises of Gloomy.
Gloomy was created as the antithesis of the fluffy characters that we see in Disney and other similar companies. The creator of Gloomy believes that animals are wild by nature, and would not mix well with humans the way they are portrayed to in many cartoons and movies. So it's normal to Gloomy be violent towards Pity and be all bloody, since it's part of its character. So, I feel like it's a little unfair to criticize this series for this, the violence between Gloomy (wild animal) and Pity (human) is the main point in the whole Gloomy's merchandise.

So... uhh.. I know it's not the cup of tea for everyone, but I don't think 1 minute will really waste your time, so yeah, I recommend this to people who don't expect something serious or just want a small break.

I mean...yeah. It's pretty much what it says on the tin. Pity takes care of Gloomy and tries to treat him like a pet, Gloomy mauls Pity because he's a full-grown bear and bears can't be treated like household pets. There is a point to it though--Pity's design makes him appear young and naive, and yet no parent is ever seen. You catch yourself wondering how the hell he's allowed to keep a bear--where are the people who would keep that from happening? And yet that's a real situation being conveyed. Sometimes our support systems fall through, we aren't protected by the people who are "supposed to" shelter us, and we bring home bears. They'll even tell us they're bears--Gloomy's call, that he typically makes before an attack, is literally the word "bear". But even when we're told something's motivations, even when its nature is so clear, projection is powerful. We bring dangerous things into our lives because of nostalgia (Gloomy looks almost identical to a stuffed bear Pity had growing up that he feels guilty for losing) or loneliness (we only ever see Pity and Gloomy in the house). And with a name like Pity, I can imagine our relentlessly saccharine, relentlessly passive protagonist could get mauled for an entire One-Piece length series before he finally learns to not let bears in the house (though that would get incredibly boring).

This series has the potential to get old fast, but I know nothing about how long it's supposed to run for, and the episodes are only 1 minute long--the creators know that for such a simple plotline any longer would be tedious. The artstyle is smartly made cutesy enough to ensure that the constant maulings aren't unwatchable. With the booming popularity of sanrio characters it's nice to see some darker simplified designs too. So far I'm under the impression that this show knows what it is and isn't trying to be anything else (much like how a real bear cannot try to be a child's teddy bear....), and that in itself has value.

Fun fact: I checked out this series because I just had to do bear safety awareness training for a summer job, and one thing we learned is that there's 2 situations where you are supposed to fight back against a bear--when it's mauling you to kill, or if it's trying to get into where you live. Pity has terrible bear safety awareness.
