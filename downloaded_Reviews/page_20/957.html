So this anime used to air here in Portugal with spanish dubs and since there's only one review about it i thought i'd hop in and give my impressions on it too.

Story 6/10

Story revolves around Ryotsu and his shenanigans as a police officer along with his colleagues in order to make easy money or get out of crazy situations he got himself to.
The episodes are mostly standalone, you can start anywhere you want, although i recommend you watch the first batch of episodes (10 -12) so you can get used to the characters.

Art 7/10

I mean it's not something amazing but it fits the show nicely for what it tries to portrait.

Sound 6/10

It didn't make much of an impact to me, but the OP and the ED were quite nice, although they aired them on Spanish.

Character 8/10

Personally i don't care much about the characters. Most of the police officers are pretty generic (rich boy, police chief that always gets mad, etc...) but i absolutely adore Ryotsu and the way he plans his schemes or escapes, and pretty much the reason why most would watch this show I'd say.

Overall 7/10

It's not bad.The way Ryotsu handles most of his situations are what makes this show worth of watching , what i believe would boost this show's popularity is an improvement on the characters's personality to make them less generic. The art and sound fit the show the way they are supposed to so i don't think they need an improvement and sadly the fact that it's mostly standalone stories there's barely any space for some sort of plot progression in the show. The show is indeed catered at a younger demographic but i'd still recommend it if you like animes like sergeant keroro.

So, this anime was very nostalgic for me. While there is no complete English sub for this anime because it is pretty old and containing close to 400 episodes (manga came out in 1975 and Artist is in his 60s). Fortunately, I was able to watch this anime when I was a kid on a Chinese streaming website where they would dub the anime in Chinese (a lot of anime came to Taiwan from Japan where they would get either subbed or dubbed in Chinese) which allowed me to understand it. The anime showed the Roppongi and Asakusa district in Tokyo during the 1970-1980 (really touristy spot nowadays) and sometimes flashbacks to the 1950s-1960s since that was the year the mangaka was born. That for me, made me feel really attached to the setting even though I wasn't even born at that particular time. Furthermore, the huge cast of characters and the different settings made this anime feel like an actual world for me as if I was part of it (really weird but that's the level of attachment I felt). The music was very 90s and 2000s that you don't hear nowadays for anime. Some episodes can be very funny and some can be bittersweet and, most importantly, some if not, many of the episodes taught me many life lessons as a kid that I wouldn't get from shows like Pokemon or Doraemon. It taught me about Japanese culture and made me got me attached to it even though I came from a Chinese family. Overall what I'm rambling about is that this anime is a very good slice of life anime that impacted me as a person through childhood and was a gateway for me to many other great animes, but this one will always hold a special spot in my heart. Right now, one small goal I've set for myself is to learn Japanese, and one huge goal I've set for myself is to fly to and visit Japan. The very next thing I would do after getting off the airport with my suitcase would be to eat at an authentic Japanese ramen restaurant (Hopefully by 2030).

Character 9
Enjoyment 9

Lastly, I just want to say thank you to the awesome Taiwanese dubbers, the mangaka, and my aunt who showed this anime to me.
