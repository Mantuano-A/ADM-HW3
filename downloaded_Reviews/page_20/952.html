Sing For You, as the title implies, is an OVA that heavily incorporates the character songs associated with the series. The story is mostly based on one of the manga chapters published a few years ago, so it really cant be considered an ORIGINAL video animation (OVA).

I've seen plenty memes on popular forums and boards jokingly stating how GochiUsa is too "high IQ" for the average viewer, but they're actually unironically correct. GochiUsa is different from other anime of the CGDCT sub-genre because there's a subtle undercurrent of sadness running through many of the episodes and it's even more apparent in the manga.

Sing For You is about Chino moving a step closer to coming to terms with her mother's death. To better understand this, it should be known from watching earlier episodes that Chino's mom used to be an avid singer with a lot of passion. Chino doesn't mention her mother often because it brings up painful memories, but after being convinced by her friends to sing a solo for her class, it helped her progress past her painful emotions. At the end of the OVA, Chino reminisces to a time when she was playing in the park with her mother and father. Her eyes flicker for a brief moment but it goes away and she simply smiles and says "mom, i'm spending time with my wonderful friends". It's actually quite sad, charming, and emotional at the same time. In the end credits you see Chino singing while clutching a photo album with pictures of her mother.

GochiUsa manages to portray such feelings that CGDCT titles rarely can and this is such a fabulous adaptation which perfectly captures Koi's incredible vision. To see how the above mentioned themes have completely flown over many viewers heads is unfortunate. GochiUsa truly is high IQ.

I suppose I don't have anyone else to blame apart from myself to expect this to be anything more than an average Gochiusa episode.

From the PVs, the premise and all, I was expecting a more intimate, a more heartfelt story with Chino and her mother. This is after all, after several years of buildup and we've known so little of this figure beyond her name which was only revealed via an April Fool's gag on the official website earlier this year. To have so little of the episode dedicated to the mother-daughter relationship or even the ultimate fate of Chino's mother is a tad bit disappointing.

Still, for better for worse, it's a Gochiusa episode with your standard trappings and structures and plots. Not even one of the better episodes, just around par with Dear My Sister. This is especially true with regards to the animation quality which is a cut rougher than the first two seasons and even Dear My Sister at times.

More Gochiusa is always fine, and this is far from a betrayal of the entire series, but one wishes more could have been done than what we have gotten in a 25 minute OVA where ultimately not much happens.

Once the end credits start rolling and Gin no Spoon ~Blend of Memory~ starts playing, I was moved if only for a bit. It's a beautiful song that serves as a bridge between a mother and her daughter from past to present that is heartwarming and a tad melancholic.

And a sad reminder of what could have been.

Also it's slightly disappointing there was an enka song and Nana wasn't the one singing it. It wouldn't have made much sense in context, but it would have been nice to see. Oh well.

5/10

This ova, tells us how Chino got herself a position in singing solo in recital at her school.
