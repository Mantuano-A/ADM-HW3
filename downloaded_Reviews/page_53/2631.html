This review has minor spoilers, no major plot points are talked about.
Remember most everything is my opinion.
Please only read this review if you're fine with my opinion influencing you're judgments on Selection Project.

So this is my firsts ever anime that focuses on idols, and it completely shocked me. I've always had a very biased opinion on idol animes, "it's probably just reality tv simulation". After watching 5 episodes, I was mentally forced to make a re-examination on my perspective of idol animes. Admittedly I do still see why some people might like the theme, I will cover that in this review as well. Please remember that I'm working with little material, only 5 episodes of 13 have been seen, but I'll try my best to inform you on "what is Selection Project" is.

For those who haven't read the synopsis, I'll give a brief summary here about the main plot. The story follows several aspiring girls who want to become idols, who are competing in the "Selection Project". Selection Project is a competition that makes idols, the girls have to compete and evolve to make it through the competition. From my perspective so far, the winner will be the girl that evolves, or adapts, the most to the competition. On to the nitty-gritty details!

Note that I primarily score from 1-5 instead of 1-10.
Story: 5/5
Art: 5/5
Sound: 5/5
Character: 5/5
Enjoyment: 5/5
Overall: 4/5

You might've realized already that everything is a 5/5 except the overall evaluation, I'll explain that as well.

The story as of episode 5 has been a masterpiece. I was expecting the back-and-forth "progression" reality tv shows use to make the viewer feel like a lot of progressions were made, when in reality not much happened. Selection Project isn't at all like reality tv, though it does have some similar aspects, it has a very clear objective, that is to make an idol! The story is easy to follow and doesn't force much onto the viewer. The start of the anime does feel a little forced, and I can't say why, it's something you just have to force yourself through.

Okay, for an idol anime, expectations are up for the art and sound industries. Of course, they mastered the art, not just in the singing and dancing scenes either. They did a great job at creating a cute little vibrant world with cute girls. I'm not going to talk about the dancing scenes because it's hard to find any flaws in the animation.

Ah, the sound, I can just bop my head to whatever those girls play. Hell, even the opening and ending made it into my favorite OP/ED list! The voice actors are breathtaking! Kudos to the voice actors! Each character has been paired with the perfect voice actor for the job. Outside of the singing/music scenes, the sound does a good job of showing off the characters' natural lives. Since who wins each round of Selection Project is decided on popularity, the sound does a good job at having the viewer also focused on the girls.

The characters are masterfully built. There are some cliche, or stereotypes among the girls, but it feels kinda natural to me. Each character shows a real enthusiasm for being an idol but seems to lack pride in their voice/body. My only disappointment would be the lack of male characters, I'm a huge fan of romance you see. I think the opportunity to show the creation of an idol would be a perfect chance to show romance.

I have really enjoyed Selection Project so far. I can't really explain the target audience, maybe teenage girls? I personally say looks aren't everything, this goes for a biased judgment before watching an idol anime as well as for the idols in the anime. I think the only way for you to decide whether you'll watch or not is to just start watching, and if you don't like it, you can simply drop the anime.

Overall Selection Project is amazing, the only thing it's lacking is a lasting impression and I predict, rewatch value. Starting with the lasting impression, every episode I watched I immediately forgot existed after 20 minutes. The anime did a wonderful job at investing me into the story, but it didn't make me think about it afterward. The rewatch value is just a prediction made by myself. The way I see things developing, this anime is most likely going to be a boring re-watch. I'm definitely going to forget about Selection Project after I finish it, while that's sad, that's how it'll be. Anyway thanks for reading my review on Selection Project, I hope I influenced your opinion on idol anime!

Selection project. Out of all the seasonals I never expected this one to be my favorite. Comparing this to more known shows including the love live franchise, what really caught my eye is how good the production quality for this anime is. As of writing this review there are about 6 episodes released so allow me to explain why I believe this anime is a 10.

Firstly, I would like to put out that I find it great when idol MCs show/vent out their frustrations because in reality life isn't always great. Having an MC that isn't always happy can present more humanity towards the anime. I absolutely loved the first few episodes, it's clear how they identified what the "challenges" the mc would have to face as the plot progresses, and opening the anime showing the vulnerable side of the MCs life, well you'd just want to cheer for her more. Character dynamic isn't that uncommon when it comes to these types of anime, there is always a "rival" or a "best friend". Well in this one, although Suzu and Rena may have the "rival" aspects, it can just be seen as 2 people who aren't particularly friends but both look up to someone dear to them, namely amasawa akari, and its great how they addressed that connection later on. It's quite obvious that Rena will eventually mellow out and become clear friends with Suzu however this is still something to look forward to.

Story- 10 (contains minor spoilers for ep 1)

It has the classic rags to riches kind of story where the mc starts out as a singer/dancer aspiring to be an idol. What stood out to me is the fact that our mc was hospitalized in her childhood due to a chronic illness, overcoming such illness would be considered the plot of the anime however as we move on later into the episode at the qualifiers where we can say that "tragedy strikes" I can really see a connection with Shibuya kanon (Love live superstar MC) as they have similar events that occur. The first few episodes were amazing to open the anime as we get to know our MC, her story, and what inspires her to become an idol. I really liked the competition foundation that they laid out in the beginning and I'm excited for what they plan to do going forward.

Art- 10
Personally the best CGI I have ever seen for the Idol genre, the quality is superb and I really think this should set the standard for the future idol anime.

Sound- 10

B.B cover by Suzu.Rena was honestly what got me hooked into this the most, the symbolisms were also a great touch for the overall song and I got goosebumps all the way. I believe the songs really captured aspects of the characters who sung them as the episodes they were molded around made you get to know the idols a bit more and I found this really great. Overall the ost is amazing and I could sit here and listen to them the whole day.

Character- 9

We have the generic go-getter idol mc type of character, despite this we were able to see her character development in the earlier episodes and I loved how they depicted her story to the audience. It was great how they made it so you could sympathize and relate to her story in the beginning about having someone we look up to and one day hoping to be in the same "stage" as them. We could also notice generic types among the other idols but their chemistry and how there is a whole episode devoted to the development of each "sub-group" is really what made me like them more.

Enjoyment- 10

Could probably rewatch it again and again without getting bored. Really love the characters and the songs as well.

Overall- 10
I would recommend this to anyone who is a fan of the idol genre, truly a hidden gem.


Would update my review as the anime goes on :)

To be an idol.

Selection Project takes an interesting concept on the whole idol franchise, and looks to find the world's next top idol. Taking the idea of holding contests through different regions of Japan. Holding a nation wide singing contest, in order to get the most voted one, and move on to the next round and so on. Making it look like a mix of their own X Factor, or American idol. Quite the spin.

We start through Suzune Miyama, a poor sick girl, who's want to sing, mainly came from watching, and listening to said legendary idol Akari Amasawa. Taking it as her inspiration, and part of her drive to sing. A dream and motivation can really push people beyond themselves. In this case, with how it's presented, it's put it to a point, where you want to root for her, and ope that she achieves such a dream.

The characters of course, are a huge part of this show. The large cast can seem a bit overwhelming at first, but that slowly goes down to look a bit more normal, and allows the watcher to follow a certain number, rather than trying to get to like a dozen faces. Along with that these faces each have their own backgrounds, and personality. You pretty much will have a girl that might end up being your favorite, and as the episodes go on, we'll get to know them more and more. Making for a much more interesting set of cast. Not just the main girl we met at the start.

The art is fresh and colourful, just what you would expect from an idol show such as this. Much like the songs, and other sounds. I honestly cannot say anything wrong about it. Idol shows are normally known for these kinds of things the most, and while it won't be super top tier, but it's very good in terms of sounding, and top notch quality. In both ends of the field.

Selection Project so far seems like a mix of Idoly Pride meets Love live. Two idol shows/series that made their own mark, one more so than the other. We'll just have to see, who comes out on top. So let's all enjoy the ride! Give this one a shot, as you won't regret it.

I'll input a review right now and I can tell you it will be valid until the end of the season, because we are talking about that kind of anime. The kind chain-produced and easily forgotten.

Story 3 : The story is very simple, we have idols that we put in the same house on their road to become stars. So they took a basic Reality Show in real life and made an anime from it.

Art 5 : SoL art like in so many anime the last few years.

Sound 6 : The sounds are average in terms of OSTs, but also in terms of music. The music that the idols sing are really run-of-the-mill idol song you can hear anywhere.

Character 3 : The characters are really basic templates with the usual suspects, the tsundere, the ojousama, the pain in the ass, the MC, the glutton, the loli, etc. Really basic characters who stay on the script of their own basic templates, without any originality or any risk taken in their definition.

Overall 3 :

So now that we did a tour of the different category, let's talk about why that kind of anime is always so bland.

22/7, Love Live, ZombieLand Saga, The IdolM@ster etc. Why idols anime are so boring to watch ? And before you attack me, yes, ZombieLand saga is trash, it has just added the zombie to the algorithm and that's it.

So, I would say there are four main problems with that kind of anime that revolves around MUSIC. It's the lack of story, quality characters, good MUSIC and good ART. You need to have a little bit of everything to make a music memorable. You would think that it's useless in an idol anime centered around music but today, we listen to music everyday. So you cannot "hook" a viewer just playing a music randomly without any context.

To understand my point, think about anime who focused at music at one point and was incredibly well received. We have BECK, Suzumiya Haruhi no Yuutsu, Angel Beats or the last concert in The World God Only Knows.

The common point in those anime is simple.

First the music come with a direct link to the story that the viewer can easily understand. In BECK, Ryusuke wrote a song for his sister and Koyuki sings it, who is in love with said sister. In Angel Beats, the girls sing to evacuate the frustration they feel locked into their dimension. In Haruhi, it's mostly to revolt against the all world and show who's the boss, cause she is. In TWGOK, it's a break up recovery.

In idol anime, there is a song at every episode and it's just here because it needs to be here, with no link to said story.

Second, the MUSIC itself. In all the example I said, the music's actually written to fit this particularly situation. in BECK the music is named "Face", literally talking about his sister. In Angel Beats, Girldemo songs like Alchemy or My Song actually describ how the characters want to escape their condition. in Suzumiya, the song God Knows is the definition of this character. Especially when she's gritting her teeth and singing at the top of her lungs, the anime tells us "This is Haruhi Suzumiya". Finally, in TWGOK, the song is about the sourness of love and has a special pause in it for Chihiro to take a step back.

In all those anime, the song was the EDGE, the song brought an impact to the anime.

In idol anime, the songs are always about the same empty subjects like not giving up or staying strong because there is still a tomorrow, and they are not linked to the story at all. It's just the same song with close lyrics and close instrumental but at the end of the day, it's a template.

Take any song of 22/7, you can put it in Love Live, take any song of Love Live, you can put it in ZombieLand Saga.

Third, the characters. If you are not attached to the characters, you cannot possibly be touched by the music that concerns them. Like we said, in all anime that did it good, the character is still central. The character is linked to the music, that's what makes the music so impactful.

And fourth, the art. How can you get hyped up watching this ugly CGI gibberish ? That's really disgusting. Never a CGI concert has been considered great in the anime community, and for a good reason. It's not possible to watch it without being distracted by the low quality art. In some idols anime, the effort was made on that point, but if you lack all other key point, it stays bland. Selection Project is clearly not doing an effort until now.

Selection Project is your basic template idol anime. So mark my words, everything I said will be verified in the next episodes you will see in this season.
