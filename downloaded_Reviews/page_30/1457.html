Shoukoku no Altair is a historical fiction centered around the young Mahmut Tuğrul as he struggles with his desires for peace while simultaneously having to get himself deeper in the war that is raging between the two major powers of the Türkiye Stratocracy and the Balt-Rhein Empire. My general opinion of the anime is that it is a fantastic take on this particular type of show where politics, war & war tactics are at the forefront of the show's focus. Also I think that the show is criminally underrated if I'm being honest. Most of the criticisms of the show I've heard of were mostly pertaining to the show's pacing so keep that in mind if you are looking to get into Shoukoku no Altair.
I thought the story for Shoukoku no Altair was outstanding. The premise is similar to some shows that have come before it but the way everything was executed, was interesting. I especially liked how the politics of the show was handled and how it connected well with the war aspect, whether it be with the two major powers or the many smaller nations/empires involved.
The art and animation were nice and clean. It was nothing spectacular but at the same time it was above average. The soundtrack for Shoukoku no Altair was great and I thought it fit really well with the tone as well as the general setting of the show.
The characters of the show is probably it's weakest point. It's fun to see the dichotomy of Mahmut Tuğrul's character. A pacifist that often has to lead the frontlines of battle and has to take responsibility for the lives of many. The supporting cast around Mahmut as well as the antagonists are good in their own right, it's just that there isn't any deep characterization to be found from them in my opinion.
Shoukoku no Altair was enjoyable from the first episode to the last through its various arcs. It took some things that I like from its genre and went a bit deeper with it and I think the show was well done overall. I highly recommend you give this an anime a watch.

I don't understand why this show has such a low ranking and review score on MAL.

It's not the most epic show ever, but it's an interesting take on a pretty generic concept: the main character who with a tragic past tries to stop warring nations with one applying more nefarious actions to achieve their goals, usually to capture more land and take over more countries. The setting is now in fictional interpreted historical Türkiye and the main character is a Pasha with an eagle counterpart. So as an avid anime watcher it's a good show to relax and watch. Of course many are probably more critical than me, but sometimes you need to just ease up and enjoy what a show does well with instead or else you begin to find little enjoyment in watching anime anymore.

It's not on par with huge popular shows playing right now like My Hero Academia, but that doesn't mean it's any less enjoyable. It's a slow burning start, but it sets up an interesting political system and society as well as establishing the beginnings of the characters.

Just because the studio isn't releasing a new season of YOI doesn't mean this show is bad. Yes it needs work, like most shows. But I think this is a show with good potential, if it were to get a higher budget and could take a longer time to plan out and animate everything, then I think this show could achieve even better results.

Overall, watch this show, it's good, the MAL ranking and stars don't serve it justice. I hope MAPPA releases another season should this first season only release 12 episodes.

I’m a sucker for historical-based fantasy, so Altair should’ve been the highlight of the year - but it ended up falling flat. While it did some interesting things, it ran into speed bumps that shows why anime rarely attempts epic scale fantasy stories.

The political in Altair interplay is on a level most anime don’t come near touching. The countries all feel unique and like a good deal of thought went behind their creation. The leaders and military commanders have refreshingly realistic stances that usually make sense and don’t feel like they were contrived to create drama or follow an oversimplified black/white, good/evil paradigm. However, a lot of the details get lost in the mix.

Altair moves at a breakneck pace. Sometimes, it feels like you’re meeting a full new cast of characters every episode. The nuanced political play ends up getting lost in a sea of strange names and terms that frustrates the viewer.

Because the details are there.

This show isn’t about flash and bombastic moments, filled with borderline illogical plots in order to satisfy the “rule of cool.” There’s real thought behind everything that happens, but it moves way too fast to pick up on everything. Often, you end up feeling lost and only seeing the surface-level of the story.

The pace ends up hurting the characters too. Supporting characters start blending together because there’s just too many of them and not enough time to differentiate them. Even the characters that show up in the majority of the episodes don’t get the chance to develop because they’re too busy jumping from one country to the next.

Altair could greatly benefit from a few scenes where characters just sit around drinking and having fun rather than always being in the middle of a political discussion or battlefield. Because I want to care about them, but it’s hard too when the anime never attempts to make an emotional connection with the viewer.

The animation itself leaves something to be desired. While the cities and clothing are varied by country, the animation can’t keep up and often skimps on details. Likewise, fight scenes usually fall flat for the same reason.

You still have to appreciate what Altair attempted. If doubled the number of episodes, or halved the content covered, Altair would’ve been a masterpiece. Every single country visited is rich in detail with the occasional Easter egg is thrown in to the delight of history fans like myself. Leadership and countries getting portrayed in mostly grey-and-grey morality is a refreshing and change from the notion of simple “good” and “bad.” And when the anime does slow down or spends more than two episodes on a conflict, the end result is highly engaging despite the lackluster emotional connection to the cast.

I haven’t read the manga, so I can’t confirm this, but I highly suspect this is a case of a poor adaptation of great content and that Altair will go down like Kingdom - skip the anime and read the far superior manga instead.

If you’re looking for anime that rely on detailed storytelling rather than flash and fanservice, then you might enjoy Altair. Sadly, the details fall through the cracks thanks to pacing issues that also end up hurting the characters.

I greatly detested this series, so maybe this review will help people to understand why some watchers may not like it. THIS REVIEW CONTAINS SPOILERS, because I analysed plot turns and characters' motivation in detail here. I was really indignant by all the injustice and inconsistency I saw in this series, so I tried to minutely expound arguments against it.

I’ve seen many mary sue characters, but not a single one of them came even close to the MC of Altair. Mahmut is not only a mary sue, he is like the worst and cheapest collection of possible cliches the main character could be. Before reading Altair I thought that such type of a character could be possible only in bad fanfiction about the all-powerful author's self-insert. But to make the entire series around such type of ‘hero’ is definitely a new low. He actually reminds me of shoujo heroines. Those girls that do nothing in particular, but have all the handsome boys they meet in anime/manga fall for them for no reason. You can see exactly this pattern in Altair, only instead of handsome guys the whole countries and their rulers fall for Mahmut when he comes there. So, so much nonsense in this anime, I don’t even know where to start.

I think the first moment which left a bad impression of MC as a shameless mary sue was when he fought with a professional assassin in the beginning of the series and won easily. But it was nothing compared to what convenient plot twists he and his beloved Turkiye got in the future. What I noted firstly, Mahmut is not some shounen MC with special abilities. There is no magic in his world. That means author can’t simply justify his winnings against stronger, older and more experienced opponents just because he is a chosen one and awakened some kind of special power. He doesn’t have it. That means he wins against all those smarter and more powerful men and outsmarts them only because he is some kind of genius teenager. That’s already a big stretch that seems absolutely unrealistic, or rather even goes against a common sense. He doesn’t even have a physical strength to fight against a professional soldier if you look at his complexion. How did he manage to fight evenly against Glalat for example? He doesn’t have any kind of magic ability, so how can you explain it? Only thanks to plot shield.

But there is also an awful nonsense concerning his country and their side in war as a ‘good guys’. I’ve never detested any fantasy country as much as I detest Turkiye in this anime. I was left in a deep shock after the arc about Balaban, which perfectly demonstrated all the incredible hypocrisy concerning Turkiye and their way of settling matters. I can tell you how it looks from the side of someone who doesn’t care about cute Mahmut and pairings with him. The whole arc tells us how one country, Turkiye, wanted to establish its dominance in the region and for that reason subjugated the neighboring countries and deprived them of their independence. They did it by arranging a conspiracy with traitorous relatives of the rulers of said countries. Those relatives then proceeded to murder their own kin only because they didn’t want to submit to Turkiye. Turkiye here is an obvious evil in my eyes. Are you against independence and self-determination of nations? Do you support dictatorship? Because Turkiye was acting like a typical dictatorial state here.

To implement their plan Turkiye ordered Mahmut to arrange assassinations of the leaders who didn’t want their countries to become a part of Turkiye and give up their independence. Now remind me, why on earth we must support Turkiye here? Only because the MC is on its side? So, not only Mahmut with the renegades from the royal families murdered Balaban and other ‘rebellious’ rulers, they also straight out brainwashed ordinary citizens with a propagandist plays. They even said directly that they need to make people support them and deceive them somehow, because people obviously wouldn’t endorse some bunch of murderers who annex their country. Now, what were Balaban’s intentions and those other sultans who joined him? He wanted an equal rights for his country with Turkiye. An equal rights, that’s all. He didn’t want to conquer Turkiye. He wanted his country to be free from it. He also wanted to make a trade treaties with Baltrhein empire, because it was more profitable for his country than their dependent condition under Turkiye’s authority. And you know what, the author doesn’t even try to cover this up or challenge Balaban’s reasoning.

What shocked me the most is how Balaban’s brother justified his betrayal. He said, okay, our country would become rich and prosperous if we had independence and can make treaties with Baltrhein. He admitted it. But he thought that if they become rich some other country would try to attack them. So, making your own country prosper is bad by that logic. What is this I don’t even– Firstly, a rich country can hire an army to protect itself. And their country was already under attack from Turkiye even without the riches. So, this faulty logic just doesn’t work at all! Then why doesn’t the author claim that Venedik is also wrong in becoming rich due to the sea trade? Or is it okay for them because they became allies with Turkiye? If you sided with Turkiye that makes you ‘the good guys’ by default, and if you just don’t want to side with it - that makes you a villain and you need to be murdered. How Baltrhein are the bad guys here? It weren’t them who tried to conquer Balaban’s country. They wanted to trade with it. And Turkiye wanted to subdue all the neighboring territories and annex them.

Another reasoning which Balaban’s brother gave while murdering him, was that sometime in the future his decision to ally with Baltrhein will prove to be actually wrong. That’s it. He somehow knows, or feels, or maybe he can see the future, that the alliance with Baltrhein will be bad for their country and Turkiye’s dictatorship will be good. He - and the author - couldn’t even came up with the reason why is that, they just know. Perfect argumentation here, isn’t it? And because of that nonsense he murdered his brother and gave his country to Turkiye. And Mahmut had a nerve to make a sad face and whine that he is sorry for the blood was spilled. Really now, it wasn’t just spilled. Mahmut spilled it by organizing the assassinations and straight out attacking Balaban’s country. How on earth am I supposed to cheer for this absolutely evil and hypocritical MC and his tyrannical country with a bunch of dirty traitors who killed their relatives for the sake of it?

For his great achievements in annexation and murders Mahmut has been promoted again and became a minister of foreign affairs. Because really, there are no other candidate in the whole Turkiye who suits the role better than a 17 years old mary sue. Mahmut begins his tour around Europe and almost every country he visits gladly unites with Turkiye even if they didn’t want to be dragged into the war with Baltrhein before. I’m talking about Venedik primarily. I was left speechless from that kind of nonsense, just enjoy the ‘logic’ here. So, Lucio, the ruler of Venedik, has stated very clearly that he didn’t want his country to take part in any conflict. Then Mahmut dragged Venedik into the war against Baltrhein by deceit. He used their ships in one of his ‘genius’ machinations and after that Venedik had no choice but to fight Baltrhein because empire considered them enemies from that point. What was Lucio’s reaction? Maybe he became angry at Mahmut and Turkiye? Or tried to contact empire and reestablish his country’s policy as non-interference? No, he praised Mahmut for pushing his country into the war by deceit. And why is that? Because Mahmut just did it so smartly. Okay, yeah, he smartly dragged your country into the war while you tried to maintain peace in it. That’s certainly a reason to praise someone and to make alliance with this deceiver. Because now you trust him, right? What is this? I can't take this motivation seriously. It’s unbelievable how the author twists logic and common sense to make everything go into Mahmut’s favor.

Another great nonsense that pissed me off happened when even not-Romania of all countries decided to make an alliance with Turkiye. And as always the author pretended that there were some ‘difficulties’ for Mahmut because at first not-Dracula refused to cooperate with Turks. But of course it was solely a cheap plot pretense. Literally a couple of chapters in manga after he ‘firmly’ refused to make a deal with Turkiye he changed his mind. No one can resist our little mary sue. The reason for his change of mind was another ‘genius’ idea Mahmut had that not-Romania can become rich by selling bird’s crap. What a story. Firstly, there was no reason at all for that country to close its borders. If its ruler didn’t open them and started to trade with other countries even when his people began to die from hunger, he is just a degenerate and a tyrant. There is simply no justification for this, it’s just tyranny. But the author tried to present this man as a troubled person who just didn’t know what to do. This reasoning didn’t work at all. Nothing was stopping not-Romania from trading with other countries apart from its ruler’s stupidity and stubbornness. Mahmut wasn’t some kind of genius savior of their nation because they didn’t need his plan in the first place. They even had Baltrhein ambassador that made them a trade offer. Guess what did they choose? Of course they chose Mahmut with his bird’s crap and executed the empire’s ambassador. Even though not-Romania also didn’t want to be dragged into the war and their fear of wars was exactly the reason for closing borders in the past. Guess they just stopped caring and decided to provoke Baltrhein and show aggression to them first. For no reason at all.

Actually what makes all this worse besides Turkiye and Mahmut’s hypocrisy about ‘not wanting to start a war’ while conquering another countries and organizing a war alliance, is that cheap false pretense when some countries sort of don’t want to unite with Turkiye but then immediately change their decision. Author obviously use this primitive plot device to create a false sense of ‘troubles’ Mahmut has on his way. But they are not real troubles. Because every freaking time everything goes in his favor in end. Another example of this being the case with Florence. Again its ruler at first refused to let Turkiye’s army pass. But literally in the next episode she not only let them pass but joined their alliance. Who does the author try to deceive here with these forced difficulties which are solved by the convenient plot every time? In case of Florence this county had especially good reason to refuse Turkiye's proposal because Turkiye invaded them in the past. So all other ministers were against the alliance with such barbarians and rightfully so. Of course they were presented as some kind of evil or narrow-minded people. In this story if you don’t like Turkiye and Mahmut you are the bad guy by default, don’t forget. Even if you have perfectly good reasons for hating them, you must forget the past and work with them now. By the very convenient coincidence Florence ruler just happened to be Suleyman’s lover. Because of that she didn’t give a heck about her country’s safety or its citizens opinion. She was ready to do anything her lover asked and was just waiting for the pretext to act. And the author gave us that pretext.

Of course it was stupid to expect that in such kind of story there will be two sides of conflict with their own truth. There can be no other right side besides Mahmut and Turkiye. So Baltrhein is presented here as cliche bag guys who are ‘evil’ just because. Seriously, they were given no reasoning for their actions. Moreover they even have some parallels with nazis, for god’s sake. Can you get any lower and more cheesy with your bad guys portrayal? Like ambassador’s quasi-nazi uniform with the freaking armband. In the middle ages. I actually laughed when I saw it first. And almost all Baltrhein officers are blonde. Seriously, it feels like the author has something personal against Europe. Their motivation in this war is as vague and unconvincing as it could be. At first author stated that Baltrhein wants to expand empire borders because they need more taxes from the conquered lands. Then Louis said something like that empire needs to grow or it would stop existing. What? Again, nothing definite, just some vague phrases that make no sense. Why would it stop existing without war? No answer. It just would. Author can’t give them better motivation. Okay, in case with taxes it was at least some kind of argumentation. But what are their soldiers doing? They are not just conquering new lands and imposing taxes on them. No, they completely destroy and burn down the entire cities. Excuse me, but who would pay taxes if you burn down their fields and cities and kill all the citizens? It makes no sense. And I’m not blaming Baltrhein for that. I’m blaming the author because she imposed on them this plot induced stupidity so they would look like cartoony bad guys. She couldn’t came up with a convincing motive for them to burn down and ruin countries when they supposedly want to profit from these lands. Baltrhein just needs to play the role of token bad guys that the hero Mahmut could defeat.

So when Baltein took another city (and didn’t even kill its citizens this time, just asked them for cooperation), Florence’s ruler used this as an excuse to make alliance with Mahmut, as if something unthinkable has happened that immediately crossed out their long lasting feud with Turkiye. But nothing of sort has happened if you think about it. She wanted to create her own alliance with neighboring countries at first and literally nothing has changed since that moment. She knew from the start that Baltreain is trying to expand its influence and waging war. But as I said she just wanted a pretext to do a favor for her lover. What a ‘good’ ruler she is and what another convenient development for Mahmut. Also there was this extremely cheap and trite scene when she was saved literally in the last moment by Suleyman from ‘evil’ ministers who were against alliance with Turkiye. I think I haven’t seen this kind of lazy script with banal ‘save lady in distress in the last second’ scene for years.

Listen Brody, this show is slept on. Simple.

If you enjoyed shows like Fullmetal Alchemist Brotherhood (FMA:B) or Vinland Saga, then keep reading.

Like FMA:B, this show is a shounen. Unlike many shounens howeer, this show subverts typical shounen tropes and does a pretty good job of exploring the themes of a shounen without relying on gimmicks like filler episodes and excessive fanservice. Throughout the story, the main character matures and grows from his mistakes and experiences, and ultimately becomes a Chad. Guess what teenagers go through? Character development (and puberty). Part of growing up is realizing that it's okay to make mistakes. This show subverts the trope of having an infallible hero who is perfect and incapable of failing during the most important set pieces, keeping the viewers invested during important climaxes in the story. This show doesn't boast any magical superhuman powers and instead prefers to keep itself grounded in realism. The only "powers" the MC has is his prestigious military background as a pasha (minister/high ranking officer), his enduring convictions, his ingenuity and wits, and his trusty companion eagle whom he uses as an offensive attack and as a messenger/scout. The grounded realism serves to make it much more entertaining to see how the protagonist overcomes his obstacle, and makes it also more relatable and believable. This leads to the next aspect of the show I really liked.

This show is also really historical, and being a history buff myself, this tickles my pickle plenty!!! How many animes actually explore the Sultanate days or even involve Middle Eastern/Turkish culture and history? The show definitely reminded me of Vinland Saga, FMA: Brotherhood, and Log Horizon as I was watching the political mind games being held between our protagonist and antagonist. The show has a lot of behind the scenes planning, such as secret plots and assassinations, power struggles, political/economic implications and consequences resulting from the loss of territories/battles, etc.. The main character himself is politically and personally motivated by his own convictions, and undertakes actions befitting of one who is on a personal and political mission. In one episode he may be seen being gathering intel using spy networks and sneaking around enemy compound, while in the next episode he may be seen on the field leading troops. To top it off, the conflicts that occur in each arc of the show are all part of a bigger political theater involving the entire region, pitting nations against each other.

I like watching isekais partly because of the feeling of discovering something grandiose. When you get sent to another world different from yours, a whole new frontier opens up and you get loads of potential and opportunity to go on adventures and do grand things. So when I was watching the MC travel across Arabian deserts under the cover of night and sail across the seas on his adventures of self discovery and heroic ambitions, I was deeply immersed and invested with the story, characters, and the journeys they took to get to where they wanted to be. It felt as if I was adventuring alongside the cast as an outside observer. I can definitely say the show successfully manages to appeal my desires to explore and adventure. So if you're like me and you enjoy open world games for the exploration, adventuring/quests, and vast landscapes offered, then you'll probably like this show.

So if you like adventure, exploration, cross-cultural exchanges, and political/military themes in anime, then this show is definitely up your alley. It's not perfect or a masterpiece like Fullmetal Alchemist Brotherhood, but it's a damn good show for what it's worth and what it shoots for. Also the ending song for the first half of the show just slaps.

TL;DR: Watch this show if you want to feel like Assassin's Creed Ali-Baba Origins meets Aladdin meets Mount and Blade meets Arabian James Bond
