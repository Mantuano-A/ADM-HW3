AlterSilverBird (All reviews)
2 people found this review helpful
Overall 7

Enjoyment 6
So the strengths yet the same time weaknesses of OVAs are there short run time and pacing. A lot of OVAs due to having limited screen time are really good at trimming the fatty filler that some anime tend to be inflicted with, centralizing the main points and constantly focusing on them, which also can be a hindrance if the things you want to focus on doesn't interest the watcher or what you congest in these short runs didn't feel big enough to feel whole.

Hori-San to Miyamura-Kun is an anime that focuses on the romance of two high school students, a standard affair of anime, but one where the entire anime is really only about that. There are no other subplots, world-building, or moments of reprieve, just the sweet and lovely tale of the two. Something definitely a lot of romance junkies wanted, but also an anime that does show what setbacks this could have,

STORY:

So the story is incredibly simple. Two young highschoolers who at first glance don't seem compatible at all, Hori being a social butterfly and Miyamura as a loner. Of course there more then meets the eye between the two, as both of them actually have sides of them that are complete opposites of each other, which they soon learn about each other and became the catalyst of this romantic story. Spending more time together, at first because Miyamura was a good brother figure to Hori's younger brother, the two develop a romantic interest between each other which isn't so easily attainable due to their public personas.

The story is all about the struggle they face in their journey of love, from friends to public perception, to misunderstandings and even realizing their own feelings, we see the two have a full conflict on their incoming romance as they tackle each issue one at a time for each episode. Because each conflict has a full episode dedicated to them, they are really fleshed out and feel whole, with some of these being quite relatable to real life, such as opening up yourself to others. I also love seeing how the couple develops in character from each story beat. Sadly for everything good the couple did, other parts end up suffering, such as the subplots of other characters with some being drop like the potential romantic interest of Hori's best friend to Miyamura, others feeling out of nowhere, like Hori's enemy suddenly becoming her friend, with the only good one being about Miyamure's new best friend and Hori's unsuccessful suitor. Overall the anime does what it did set, but due to lack of time, the things beside it with a few exceptions are not well executed. It even has a cliffhanger of an ending which we'll never see, which is saddening since it seemed interesting.

ART:

Now, this is where the anime shines. Relying on a simple palette of pastel color, the animation looks like a color book coming to life. Easy on the eyes, yet relaxing, it's clear the animation wanted to be simple and easy but do it in a way that still looks good to see. The animations are great as well, being very fluid to the point the movements feel genuine yet expressive enough to show character. I also have to praise the background, because everything looks so relaxing like a warm blanket snuggling you. And lastly the wardrobe, there seems effort in visual storytelling with clothing reflecting each character, like Miyamura appearing quite geeky at school but a bad boy in public with his piercings and tattoo or Hori's conservative appearance at her house. I also like how the characters pop from the background to further give focus on them. However for all the praise, the biggest weakness has is faces, the facial expressions are good if typical, but the faces are all the same which isn't unusual, but it really looks like some of the artists just copied a single body frame and just some details to make different characters and it so hard to see when you actually notice.

SOUND:

Now here's the interesting part, the sound isn't always present. What I mean is that there's a number of scenes which have no music, only some sound effects and the dialogue of the characters. These moments are really well-done, the atmosphere strong since the silence can give us a feeling of awkwardness and discomfort which fit a lot of the scenes involving Hori and Miyamura. There's also moments of silence where it's just the two doing the mundane thing, which I like since it does show the couple being normal, something not a lot of romance do since they keep the characters acting lovey-dovey. And the moment there is music, it's quite excellent. It helps emphasize the scenes there with simple guitar riffs and gentle sounding music, which again compliments the simplistic narrative. I also have to give props to the voice actors for conveying emotion to these characters, but in a way, it feels like something you can see in everyday life.

CHARACTER:

Now, this is where some of my gripes start to show up. The duo of Hori and Miyamura are wonderful and full of depth. Miyamura is an introvert with the hobbies of an extrovert like having a tattoo and doing rebellious acts like jumping fences or even having a fistfight, but the introvert side isn't completely fake since he also does a number of personal activities like cooking and housework. His struggle and confusion of his feelings are actually quite relatable due to his own past, and how he overcomes feels realistic. I'll admit his confusion can be funny when he thought he might've had feelings for Tooru though it can be equally frustrating when he denies his feelings for Hori. Hori herself is also a great character, someone who cares about outer appearance but is really a genuine sweetheart who is insecure about her housewife like lifestyle. I also like how quiet and close she can be, being monotonic in some of her talk with Miyamura and sharing her issues with her friends. This works since it enhances her main flaw, her fear of her public image and attaching herself too close to someone with the danger of them leaving her, which explains some of the conflicts she faces, and her past as a bully which can back to haunt her.

But aside from that, their the only good characters in this anime besides Tooru and Kouchi with Tooru having his own development and Kouchi just being a delight to watch. The rest though are just so underdeveloped and bland, who only serve as background dressing which would be fine if there wasn't so much focus on them. I also have to admit while I like the dual nature of the two main characters, I admit some times there feels a little too much on them, like Hori being a former bully, but also quiet, then emotional, then responsible which jumbles around. Ultimately the main cast are good but with the exception of a few, the cast isn't really that strong.

ENJOYMENT:

I'll admit that the enjoyment factor is a little mixed. What I love to see was the dynamic relationship between the two leads and their emotional turmoil and relatable issues. I also enjoyed some of the regular things they do as high schoolers like play games or even pranking each other. However, I cannot deny that some of the scenes outside these feel too typical and goes by the number. I also think there's a lack of drama or subplots that were set-up that didn't go anywhere which would've made the anime more interesting like the council member who tried to frame Hori for messing up paperwork. And the big romance between the two can sometimes lose its luster especially at the final episode where it felt dragged on. It was a show I enjoyed at the start of being simple yet enjoyable, but as time goes on it started to feel kinda boring lowering my enjoyment factor.

OVERALL:

This is ultimately a good short anime to watch, especially for a sucker for romance. That said it doesn't have the same weight as other better OVAs and especially since while the focus on the show did seem too kinda drag on and the things they could've done to have the show feel fresh were just dropped. It will take only less than 2 hours to finish this, but sometimes it will feel like it lasted longer. Overall I do think the show is worth watching, but it's just a simple fix instead of a brand new experience that will change you. So just warm snack to sate your craving for a romantic story, especially one that feels down to earth.

I really do not care whether it is publicly bad or not, I read the manga and it was euphoric. I would've rather had a complete anime series but even if these episodes are OVA's I still loved it. The art was not as good as the manga however it is an insightful view of what the anime would/could look like.

Currently, I wish for an anime adaptation, the OVA's are a way to satiate the desire for one which really helped me and it's pretty much my method of getting over depression from waiting for the anime for so long. Overall I love the characters and the relationship-plots and the humor put into this.
