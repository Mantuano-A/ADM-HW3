“Fiction is the lie through which we tell the truth.”

Every once in awhile an anomaly occurs which shakes to our very foundation. For the masses the distinction is straightforward, whether love or hate, brilliant or idiotic, trascend. For example, when the Dragon Maid got a second season , it was immediate and sustained adoration, Tokyo Revengers on the other hand was bombed with Negative Reviews written by intellectually sane people. But to explain why the public supports or denounces a particular thing is next to impossible, as it culminates from the attitude of the era and chance. One thing, however, is certain: ingrained perceptions are difficult, if not impossible to change.
Once a Garbage, always a Garbage. Once a Masterpiece, always a Masterpiece. Once considered an Isekai, always an Isekai.

The problem with the love/hate dynamic is that critical evaluation is sparse, and if an outsider decides to scrutinize the work with a critical eye, he is often castigated for not falling in line or ridiculed for not understanding the core themes.

Such in case with Mushoku Tensei. It's often hailed for deconstructing the basic Tropes of the infamous Isekai genre.
The stigma of the Isekai genre is not only to attributed to corny power fantasies and harem, but also poor worldbuilding and flat generic Protagonist.
Recently, writers have attempted deconstruction to remove the negative stigma. Ranging from worldbuilding power fantasies like Overlord to comical parodies like Konosuba to edgy revenge garbage like Rising of the shield hero. Possibilities are infinite, however many have fell short due to industry's disposition to quick cash grabs.

Mushoku Tensei is reowned for its great worldbuilding, realistic magic system, great characterisation and unorthodox use of Isekai tropes however it fails to execute it properly.

CHARACTER 4/10
Starting from the Characters, we have Rudeus who masturbated in his parents funeral, an unlikeable personality trait that can easily make the audience dislike him in his first impressions.
But, for the audience to sympathize with his antics the author attempts to create a tragic backstory, he was bullied and tormented in his previous life. I expected him to change his insatiable lust in his "SECOND LIFE" but it gets worse as the story progresses. Apparently, there is an attempt to give him some depth however, when he doesn't overcome his fatal flaws, how can we even consider him well written Character?

Let's ignore the MC for a while and talk about the MC's harem, I mean the secondary cast-

Roxy: She's the one who taught our MC Magic and apparently she's a Mary Sue intelligent, wise, kind and humble. No interesting personality otherwise.
Eris: How can a harem be complete without a tomboyish Tsundere who hits every male that comes around her vicinity.
Sylphiette: Here we are finally the girl who loves the MC with extreme loyalty. Her only trait is that she's childish and brave. As her age is same as our Pedo Protagonist, she's often the victim of his uncomfortable antics.
Unfortunately, all of them adhere to a certain character archetype and lack necessary depth. No need of any reminder that they're also tools for fanservice for majority of the screentime.

The Antagonists are all one dimensional, predictable and generic that it can even make Genthru of Greed Island look very complex in comparison.

STORY 3/10
I expected a coming of an age story with groundbreaking levels of Character development and great exposition to worldbuilding but sadly this turned out to be another male power fantasy. The magic system is quite simple consisting of simple spell chanting instead of RPG power levels.

The worst thing about this show is how it's core central theme is diverted and it suffers from a identity crisis, whether it's a Ecchi or adventure story. It disguises itself as a redemption story but fails to deliver any subtle acceptable Character development. Male domination (Just like every other Isekai or Harem) is the main issue of this show. Every other female character are degraded and reduced to either tools of fanservice or MC's Harem sextoy. The fact that he never changes his core flaws but instead becomes worse or according to Isekai Standards STRONGER (Bigger Harem) makes this nothing but rather a wish fulfilment Male Power Fantasy.

The worldbuilding is boring in the current and subsequent arcs with generic dungeons and fantasy creatures (Sex Toy) introduced with even more generic elements. There's no novelty or creativity present.
In my opinion, even Black Clover (One of the worst Shounen) has better female cast and fight scenes with actual tension than this poor man's attempt of Re:ZERO and Konosuba. The Standards of Isekai have degraded so low that even a pedophilic Edgy Protagonist is adored by the community. Even Itachi's redemption which was one of the worst written parts of Naruto was better than this Male Power Fantasy.
Even Togashi, the greatest Shounen writer can write a decent Isekai better than 90% Isekai writers of the Industry. Go check out Yu Yu Hakusho or re-watch the Greed Island Arc of HunterXHunter it's way better.

Overall 33/100

I usually don't write more than one preview per season, but this anime is SOO hyped that I have to break my rule. Mushoku is quickly becoming one of the most popular series on MAL. So why is this series so popular? I'll try break down some of the pros and cons.

Pros:

For an isekai series, Mushoku does invest fairly heavily in world building. Compared to the average isekai, it's pretty impressive that Mushoku created countries, noble houses, a little bit of history, and even hired a linguistics graduate to create a fantasy language. That happens pretty often in the West ever since Tolkien blazed that trail. Klingon was created in the 1960s and is a full language with fleshed out vocabulary and grammar rules. Same goes with Dothraki and even Na'vi from Avatar. However, that really doesn't happen much in anime. Garzey's Wing attempted it to disastrous results. So kudos for Mushoku.

The animation and OST are actually pretty darn good. I really have no complaints on a technical level. Mushoku was a very successful light novel series in Japan, so this anime received a generous budget. They didn't have to worry about this making money with Japanese otaku. Mushoku is going to run another 5 seasons at least.

Mushoku actually cares about its magic system. Us geeky anime fans love to know how stuff works. How much energy and experience is needed for a certain spell. What is the spell's recharge time? What is the effective range? How does magic work in this world and where does it come from? Can anyone learn magic? This is why fans will constantly bug people like Akira Toriyama and Hirohiko Araki with these sorts of questions and those 2 will usually respond with "I don't remember, I was drunk when I wrote that shit. Why do you even care? It was funny!" The author of Mushoku cares and he's happy for you to ask all the questions you want.

Cons:

Whether or not you like a character is entirely subjective. However, I will say I greatly dislike the MC in this series. It's not just that he's a bad person. There are plenty of characters who do horrible things and yet are compelling and interesting characters. A great character doesn't need to be some goody two-shoes who is immediately loveable. The issue is that Mushoku presents itself like a redemption story and it honestly isn't. In life, Rudeus was a loathsome piece of shit who would rather fap to loli porn than go to his own parents' funeral. However, he gets a second chance at life and vows to become a better person. He uses his adult knowledge and formal education to completely outpace his peers, learn the fantasy language by 2, and become a powerful mage. However, he's still a pedophilic degenerate who makes no effort to improve morally. He isn't a latent pedophile who struggles against his body's natural desires and feels tremendous guilt. That would actually be interesting. Instead, he grooms and bangs all the girls in the novel series because he's a power fantasy for the male readers. He doesn't improve and there are no negative consequences for his actions. Ever.

Halfway through season 1, the God of the Mushoku universe comes to Rudeus and starts telling him what he needs to do to advance to the next part of the story. Whenever Rudeus is stuck, we get literal deus ex machina to save the day and keep the plot moving. In the first episode of season 2, Rudeus needs a power up. So God tells him to buy some food and give it to an obnoxious loli begging in an alley. It turns out she is actually a bratty demon lord and knockoff Disgaea character who rewards Rudeus with the Sharingan from Naruto. Now that Rudeus got a free power up, he can instantly defeat Eris and completely undermine all of her training. This is necessary though because he needs to be the alpha if he's going to groom and bang her. Which he will. I'm going to come out and say it. The plot of Mushoku is pretty bad. The fact this is a novel series is embarrassing. There are plenty of 1990s JRPGs that are better written than this.

This is another subjective factor, but I have no love for either the comedy or echii content in this series. The English dub at least tried to improve the comedy, but when I watch it subbed I don't even smile once per episode. I am a very easy person to make laugh. I laughed at Peter Grill at least twice an episode and those are 10 minute episodes. This anime is comedically getting its ass kicked by Peter Grill. It also entirely fails to titillate or arouse me, since almost all the fanservice is from loli characters. I'd rather masturbate to the characters in Plus Sized Elf than this garbage.

I gave this a 4 purely for technical reasons. I like to have at least a little objectivity in my ratings. However, I can honestly say that I'd rather be watching Goblin Slayer season 2. I had a lot more fun with Redo of Healer. I've already alluded to the fact that on an enjoyment level, I think Peter Grill is better than Mushoku. If I didn't have horrendous anime taste myself, it would physically hurt me to see a series this mediocre get so hyped. So no, I don't recommend watching Mushoku. If you have to see what the hype is all about, I would at least wait for the dub to come out.

Mushoku Tensei is amazing but certainly is not for everybody. Having already read the light novels and web novels I am very impressed with how they are handling the adaptation and the direction in which they are taking it. Certainly, it is not perfect, but there is much to be commended.

Story: 9
The story of Mushoku Tensei is nearly perfect, as it is very interesting to see how what was a worthless piece of trash decided to live in this world and use their second chance. What I find to be great, but a little lackluster in some moments is the pacing, most of the time the anime makes sure to spend time fleshing out the story and events, but sometimes things that should have taken longer feel like they flash by in an instant, this isn't much of a problem though as it is only apparent in some episodes. It is really interesting to see the background of the world and how it is built, all of the issues and moral injustices that may be reflecting Rudeus' personality and acting as the catalyst for his character progression. The different situations in which Rudeus finds himself are all relevant to his character growth and they all cause some sort of conflict (either internal or external) which is great to see. The comedy is also really appealing to me, and I find myself audibly laughing to most of the jokes or out of pure excitement of what has been created, something I really rarely do otherwise. What makes this even better, is how suddenly the mood shifts, contrasting the much more positive outlook Rudeus has on this world than it actually is.

Art: 10
The art is phenomenal, no complaints there. Every single location feels alive, and the character designs are also slowly progressing to look older, a really great detail. The art greatly accentuates the awesome worldbuilding in Mushoku Tensei and makes it much more interesting to see. The fight scenes are fluid and pleasing and are usually well choreographed. I personally like seeing the different fighting styles of the different types of swordsmen and how they overcome their opponents.

Sound: 10
Everything about the sound in this anime is great, the OST is really good and it makes for a great viewing experience. Usually I skip both the beginning and end of most of the stuff I watch, but for Mushoku Tensei I watch the whole thing through which says a lot.

Character: 10
Each character in MT is unique and entertaining to unravel and observe, the progression of the characters also makes perfect sense and does not pose any problems. Rudeus is most interesting to watch as he slowly grows and begins to develop throughout the series, beginning to understand that this is a real world in which he lives with real people, not just NPC's. His actions affect those around him in negative ways, and he tries to change himself to counteract that. All of the cast of Mushoku Tensei is entertaining in my opinion, and I've grown to root for some aspects of the characters to improve, which makes it all the better to see when they actually change in the direction which I want them to.

Overall: 10
Mushoku Tensei is what I would consider being perhaps the best Isekai, but it is certainly not for those who are looking for something lighthearted or even looking for the main character to be the epitome of perfection. It starts off rough, but slowly you grow to appreciate parts of it as it grows into something more complex. The art is spectacular and there are no moments of awful CGI that will ruin scenes. The sound is perfect, and fits the anime really well. And if you enjoyed Season 1, then I am sure you will find Season 2 even more enjoyable.
