I love this smug gremlin to death.

As she was initially described, the phrase "Annoying! Cute! But Annoying!" hits the nail on the head. The illegally Dekai Sugoi's might be the initial point of interest, but I honestly believe letting yourself be swayed by design preferences into max horny or max disgust will make you miss out on one of the best characters in a good while.

While it might be just another ecchi rom-com SoL in its core, Uzaki-chan wa Asobitai! brings one of the best character dynamics to table yet, blowing its competition, and their cardboard cutout counterparts right out of the water. And though the cover girl definitely plays her role as such, the series just would not work without the other half. Uzaki-chan is a perfect example of a simple series that is greater than the sum of its parts.

While Uzaki is a great character on her own, the series just wouldn't work without Sakurai. Unlike many other serieses with a clear main pair, the bickering isn't one sided bullying with meak retorts which hold no weight, but rather genuine back and forth banter. Uzaki is a force of nature and will do what she wants to, but Sakurai ain't no slouch either, and always responds accordingly. You never know what either of them is going to do, and their reactions are even more unpredictable. This is the first time the main pair genuinely feel like best friends, rather than strictly limited to being love interests. I can not stress how much that adds to the charm.

The supporting cast is also full throttle on supporting the development between the main pair, rather than act as obstacles to it. There is no forced NTR-bait, or fake drama for the sake of furthering the relationships. The entire series is just dumb, lovable fun. I've yet to find a character I didn't like, a joke that was too far for comfort, or a scenario that didn't bring a smile to my face. I find it hard to digest that out of all things anime, this was the one to rile people up.

The voice actors are on point. None of the earlier wouldn't work without their delivery. Just Uzaki screaming "Senpai" won my heart over. And to be honest, the quality of the acting definitely saves a few scenarios where the shots pan on for just a bit too long. On the topic of voice actors, the OP absolutely slaps ass as well, and fits the series like a glove.

I am tremendously biased. The series was practically made for people like me. If I didn't give the series a 10, who would?

Endless praise aside, the Anime is Outstanding, but by no means perfect. As I mentioned earlier, some of the charm from reading the gags from the panels of a manga are lost in bringing the manga into motion. The author usually crams such massive amounts of text in when Uzaki makes fun of her senpai, that they can't read the whole line through and still keep the delivery of the joke fluid.

The art quality also isn't quite on par with the source material, but it definitely isn't a low budget atrocity like what some anime unfortunately end up like. I don't really know how to feel about TAKE's KUSOCAT-mascot making sudden appearances every now and then, it wouldn't quite fit into the anime-only experience, but at the same time, it's also kinda wholesome. Really gives the anime a passion-project vibe. For better or worse.

The manga was my absolute favorite, and I'm glad to say that I like the anime just as much. It's all I asked for, and then some.

"You can hate whatever you want, but don't diss what someone else likes."
