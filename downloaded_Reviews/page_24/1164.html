This review has minor spoilers, no major plot points are talked about.
Remember that almost everything I say is my opinion.

I believe Taishou Otome Otogibanashi was made to bring happiness into the world, so my scores will be hooked to that belief. I'll start off by saying this is one of those "feel good" anime, there is positivity everywhere. I know this will turn some people off straight away. It's very easy to tell if you'll like the anime or not, so trust your gut feeling on this one. Please read the synopsis before continuing.

Note that I primarily score from 1-5 instead of 1-10.
Story: 4/5
Art: 4/5
Sound: 4/5
Character: 4/5
Enjoyment: 4/5
Overall: 4/5

The story is not the focus of the anime. This anime is more focused on the characters and their emotions/outlooks on life. So I discourage you to say "the story is bad" because you shouldn't be looking at the plot. The part you want to focus on would be as follows: Tachibana Yuzukis' influence on Shima Tamahikis' worldview and self-worth. I believe the story as of 4 episodes has been well constructed. The anime seems to be using the opposites attract formula, which I personally like, as it lets the characters see what they are missing in themself. The story is fairly slow-paced so far.

The art style is very cute and fits the old-school setting. The environment has just enough detail to create a little world as if we were seeing through the lens of a pessimist; through the eyes of Tamahiko.

The sound in the anime is average, except for the voice actors, they did a good job. The sound wasn't ever something I picked up on, it's not really a strong point of the anime. The voice-actors however, did a brilliant job at portraying their character's pessimism and optimism.

The characters have been well crafted. Tamahikos' family gives a good contrast on happiness in social standing; the rich family is satisfied with their place in society while the poorer brides being sold have positive perspectives on life. I didn't really do a great job at explaining that, I mean to say the poorer people seem to be more positive than the plush rich family. I do feel a little bit of a forced "waifu vibe", I hope they aren't just trying to make waifus for popularity.

I've enjoyed Taishou Otome Otogibanashi so far, I thoroughly look forward to seeing the characters develop more. This anime is something I think anyone can watch, though the younger demographic will most likely find it boring. The way things are going now, the anime looks like it'll be great, I encourage you to watch it.

Overall this anime is great. It has a pretty large target audience and displays the strong effects of positivity/negativity on other people. So far the only genre that is inaccurate would be comedy, I haven't laughed much as of episode 4, I've only smiled. Thanks for reading this review, have a good day everyone!

Taishou Otome Otogibanashi is a tone-deaf masterpiece, completely contradictory by nature, the anime proposes a romance that is barely believable and a sense of having to completely suspense your disbelief against the strange forces of wholesomeness and eye candy relationships. While the anime itself have a bunch of extremely respectable features that I will explain later, the final taste is that this is something that shouldn't exist, or at least, it shouldn't exist as it is.

The anime is not offensively bad, honestly, the comedy works extremely well with the usage of exaggerated emotional reactions, the characters are somehow charismatic, and the energy that the anime radiates does its job at letting us fully enjoy the relationships and quirks of them, it sometimes goes into predictable territory, but you can lose yourself into it without any problem. While the anime just has broadcasted 4 episodes, in each of them we have an interesting perspective over the pain provoked by authoritarian and almost evil family figures, and at the same time it let us go into detail about the perspective of the common people towards those that are above them. I don’t really know if this is historically true, but it works in the concept of the anime and it is a coherent motive about how the other can affect our own sense of development and sentiments.

And that is probably one of the main strong motives of the anime, to forge our own path is not just a thing that we do in the solitude of our own world, but it is a shared experience with all of those who can affect us, be it not just our friends but also our context, the world as an entire idea. Proposing the idea of caring to the one next of us, a positive idea. To extend such proposal the anime uses an interesting way of expressing the mental images of the characters, developing not only a coherent aesthetic that fits the historical period but also a pleasing way to truly understand the sentiments of them.

Why is this anime a 1 then? Because the concept is broken by design. The anime is not oblivious about its lack of touch, during the fourth episode a doctor tell the main character about what everyone who live in a developed country in the 21st century would think, about how buying an underage bride and using her as a servant is just an abuse of power and a completely bankrupt way of treating another human being. The problem is that while this anime is self-aware about how toxic the message is, it doesn’t turn around and make a reflection out of it, but instead just tries to silence those voices and try to go forward with the inhumanity of the main duo. Contradiction. The anime tell us something but does other.

And why is this a toxic message? One could say that this is a historical piece, and then whatever happens in that context is just a historical antecedent about something that existed, equivalent of doing a historical documentary. That would work, and I would love to see a realistic depiction of the reality about arranged child marriages, imagine yourself being a coin of exchange because your family were poor, that is strong. This anime lacks touch at treating such topics, it is tone-deaf, the main struggle presented in the anime is not by the perspective of the bride that was sold but the people who are in power, the equivalent of telling the story about a slave in a cotton farm being obedient and brightening the life of the slave owner. It is wrong, because it uses a deeply controversial topic that is still relevant to the present day, an issue about gender inequality, power distribution and poverty, and romanticize it to create a romcom, a narrative that instead of trying to portray it humanly it does a wish fulfillment manner. The caring sense of this anime just go in one direction because the main girl is not a human character, and the eventual struggle of the child bride by her condition is just used to sold us the character growth of the people in power, or to say, the husband and his family, instead of being a topic on its own.

I could add more negative factors of this anime but it is probably not worth it. The characters face extremely questionable struggles, the main character is not subtle at all about anything, and a bunch of other negatives, but anything that I can say wouldn’t be as problematic as the exposition of bride arrangements. Now, I will gladly delete this review if I sense a change of direction over this, but there is no business about this anime having a score higher than 1 in this situation.

Taishou Otome is one of those romance animes that has the power to give you diabetes because of its sweetness overdose: from the characters, plot, storyline, dialogue, etc.

The manga is on par with this anime adaptation. Since the chapters in the manga are very short and it only has one major arc, Taishou Otome really exceeded my expectations. People compare this anime to Tonikawa Kawaii, which I find it interesting, because of how sweet of our two beloved main characters. Their blossoming romance and cute romantic moments, including dialogue, really strikes my heart to invest to watch this anime more. Common thing between those two shows is simple: hardcore romance and undeniable sweetness. it is obvious that they are really different, but from what I can tell you, the high level of romantic fluff is the same as Tonikawa Kawaii. And of course, Yuzu is a powerful contender for being the best girl of this season.

I also want to point out the emotional aspect of this anime, because it greatly affects the emotions of the audience, just as in the manga. With its dialogue matching with the music's somber tone, it really puts you on a situation that you are witnessing the tragic backstory of Tamahiko, where his family abandoned him, thinking of himself as a useless person. Yuzu on the other hand, is the person who can bring him back to the light.

In our real world, we cannot avoid our own ups and downs. Admit it, everyone has, not just me and you. There are moments that we think that "I am useless, guess I'll die then" because of some tragic and traumatizing moments. At the end, someone is there to save you from darkness. There is always light at the end of the tunnel and someone is there to help and overcome your problems in life. This is present in Taishou Otome, where Yuzu wants Tamahiko to release all the burdens of his life and be happy with her, as they will marry each other in the future (not a spoiler much, but foreshadowing since it is part of the plot). Yuzu is the saving grace from Tamahiko's uselessness. She wants him to be happy and solve the problems together. This is what I like from the manga and anime.

Overall, this show has a special place in my heart. Compare it to Tonikawa Kawaii or not, emotional impact is important and one of the factors of a great anime. With romance at its core, Taishou Otome is one of the hidden gems this year's Fall Season. I hope everyone appreciates this anime.

Ye it's good.
A very cute yet detailed concept of a love story, being relatively unique, at least compared to most modern day romance shows.
It's only been 3 episodes, so I can't definitely express as much as I would love to, however, after watching these 3 episodes, I'm already engaged and interested in this story.
I think what's great about this is how polar opposite the 2 main characters are. They're both pretty stock and default, being just very monotonal all the time [even though this might change in the future, idk, hope so]. Obviously, 2 opposites attracting is not at all original and is over done as heck, but it's the way these 2 are opposites and yet similar, with this very different idea. Tachibana, a sold bride to pay off a debt, is this overly cute, soft speaking, "perfect girl" who basically just does all the house work and looks after Shima; a boy who survived a car crash that resulted in his mother's death, that ended up concluding with his family disowning him. He, like Tachibana, has 1 personality trait or 1 personality type. He's typically to him self and quite confused character. Tachibana is very much happy with the idea of marrying Shima, whilst Shima is still unsure yet clearly flustered at parts.
They are both opposites in personality, but they're similar in story. In a way, they were both forced to abandon their lives to go live off somewhere else, now isolated from the rest of the world nearly, trying to craft a working relationship that feels natural rather than forced.

What feels unique about this story though is just the time period it's set up. It's set around early 1920 [I think?], and because it's in Japan, a lot of stuff that happens is a lot more traditional. Normally, teenage romance happens in high schools, but not this time, this happens because 2 teenagers got sent off in a house. The story takes advantage of the fact it's set in this time period to give a creative take on teenage romance. It could have just been another high school romance just in the 1920s, but no. Instead, it cleverly takes ideas from this era, that could not at all work if set in a much more modern age. I'd love to see how much more this story progresses around this type of environment, as it may just feel like a reskin of typical romances but set in the 1920s [like when they went shopping, granted what they went shopping for isn't so much a modern thing, but you could easily just have the same thing done in a modern setting. Whilst I'm not saying the story can't take these typical realistic ideas, it needs to be able to separate it self more from other romances by being centred around it's time period and having one of a kind situations].

Music and visuals are great, Shima's sister is also interesting, and allows for a setup in the future to connect back to the family.
Overall, I have really high hopes for this series, and I will be disappointed if it doesn't live up to the expectations I am giving, otherwise it will just be another rehash romance but skinned in a different time period.
