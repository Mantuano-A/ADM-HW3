Title: Baccano! OVA

Anime: The Baccano! OVA was produced by Brains Base (who also produced Natsume Yuujin-Chou and the Kimi ga Nozomu Eien ~Next Season~ OVA) and was directed by Takahiro Omori (director for Jigoku Shoujo Futakomori and Natsume Yuujin-Chou). The three episodes were released on DVDs 5 (released February 27th, 2008), 7 (released April 23rd, 2008), and 8 (released May 28th, 2008) as bonus episodes in Japan; it is unclear if, in Funimation's licensing of the series Stateside, the OVA is included in what was licensed.

The Baccano! OVA picks up on some of the plot lines from the series that, despite being wrapped up, weren't quite complete and needed some more explaining, and also introduces some new characters.

I watched Baccano! almost a year ago now, and watching this reminded me of why I liked the series so much. There's an amazing amount of detail in this production, especially in the story and backstories of the characters, and how things in the past and future dovetail into each other to bring things full circle and fill out and finish the last of the plot lines that weren't quite perfect. The new characters also make their entrances nicely within all this, fitting in perfectly.

Art style is pretty much unchanged from the original series; still a great amount of detail given to the character designs, which is where it matters most, and the animation in general is smooth and well-done.

Music is still mostly jazz-based and good, while the OP and ED stays the same as from the series.

The seiyuu all returned for the OVA, and Tomokazu Sugita (famous for his roles as Mayama in Honey and Clover and Soldier Blue in Toward the Terra) makes an appearance as the new OVA-exclusive character Graham Spector.

The length was pretty good on this, though I would've liked to have this expanded into a second season. There's a rather metatastic moment at the end of this, though, and it very much leaves the possibility for a continuation open, and I would hope that they pick up on this.

All in all, an excellent OVA, though not quite as good as the series itself, that expands and brings the series full-circle while leaving the possibility for continuation more than open.

As many of you should know, in Japan, Baccano! did not do well at all. Many factors are to blame for this (Awful timeslot, the anachronic nature does not lend well to weekly viewings, the DVDs and Blu-Rays were horrendously overpriced and it ultimately did nothing to improve the sales of the Light Novels, which nowadays generally do well in their first week, selling about 8K copies, but likely won’t be making it into 18K per month). Thus I am surprised Brain’s Base decided to make three OVAs that serve as Epilogues to the series. So how did they turn out? Let’s find out:

Story:

Technically the story is still anachronic, but it has a much more focused structure, namely due to it mostly being an adaptation of Volume 14 with some minor elements from Volume 5. As of writing this review, I have yet to read Volume 14, as I am following Yen Press’ Release of the Novels (Illegally of course as I am not spending the equivalent of almost 60 dollars for a single Volume simply because those suckers aren’t sold in my country) and those are only by Volume 10. So I can’t say how accuate it is to the Light Novel, but from what I’ve heard it’s a fifty/fifty sort of thing in terms of accuracy.

Setting that aside, the story works as a decent epilogue to the 1931 Storyline, with its two biggest subplots finally coming together in a somewhat unconventional fashion. There are some rather amusing bits of black comedy, and in general it does work as a satisfying conclusion to the arc. That said, I am kinda getting tiered of the 1931 Subplot to be honest. I like it, but I feel that due to the Anime skipping ahead a few Volumes, it feels like nothing but plot development on this subplot and nothing else. Also, for the record, while I don’t know much about the Changes to this particular Volume of the Novels, I do know that there are enough changes for the rest of the Novels that it’s clear Brain’s Base no longer had plans to adapt the rest.

There are however a few too many issues. Surprisingly the Gecko Ending isn’t the biggest issue, as its clear this isn’t the Ending, and the show acknowledges that. It still pisses me off, but hey, it’s better than in other stuff. No the biggest issues are anything that doesn’t have to do with Jacuzzi and anyone who interacts with his gang. All of it feels like if it was just thrown in to pad out the running time, like the stuff with Elmer or Sylvie. Both interesting characters in their own right, but here they aren’t allowed to show any of their layers due to how tacked on they feel. Also the few changes I am aware of kinda piss me off, like completely changing the way Ladd and Graham met for no reason. So overall, these OVAs aren’t perfect, but they’re fine for what they are.

Characters:

Since Eve is no longer around I can give this show the 10/10 character marc it deserves. The cast is fantastic, yet due to me already having said everything there was to say back in my review of the show, I will only focus on a few of them. First is Claire Stanfield, as I am no longer prevented from talking about him due to spoilers. This guy is his own brand of crazy, believing the world is nothing but an illusion going on in his head, and he has the ego to booth. It’s great.

The highlight of course is Graham Specter, Ladd’s self-proclaimed little brother. Like Ladd, he’s insane, but in a different way. He doesn’t have Ladd’s strange views on killing, yet he is extremely bipolar, with people often having no idea what’s his deal. Finally is Elmer C Albatross; or simile junkie as Huey rightfully calls him. He’s fine enough here, with it being clear there’s something else going on with him, though due to the 2002 Story never being properly adapted, we never get to see exactly what is so wrong about him.


Animation and Sound:

Grouping them together as they’re the same as in the show. Moving on!

Voice Acting:

Since last time it was more of a joke than anything, I will now play this section more straight. The whole cast is great, putting a lot of energy and quirkiness into their performances, yet never so much that it would start getting annoying. As for the newcomers for this OVA, the most obvious ones are Ohata Shintaro, whom I have never heard about before but is more or less exactly how I imagined Elmer sounding like (Then again, whenever I read Elmer’s lines in the Novels, I could only hear Italy’s voice from Hetalia so… yeah).
And of course there is Sugita Tomokazu, who looked at his BFF Nakamura Yuuichi and his performance as Hazama in the first Blazblue game (That was coming out in a few months from this OVA’s release) and said “Yeah, I can do that too!” and thus Graham Specter was brought to life wonderfully. Besides him, there’s also Yoshino Hiroyuki, Kobayashi Yuu, Sakaguchi Daisuke, Hirohashi Ryou, Koyasu Takehito, Saito Chiwa and the Late Great Arimoto Kinryu.

Overall:

So yeah, these OVAs are pretty good. They aren’t perect, but hey, nothing can truly be perfect. That said, if Baccano ever truly continues in Anime form, this thing more or less makes it so they’ll have to start from scratch. If so, I think doing something similar to what they did with the Monogatari series would be their best bet, as that’d give them enough time to sort the whole thing out. Still though, for what it is, the Baccano! anime was a fun ride, and I do recommend it.

Final Score: 8/10

Considering my opinion of the main series, I have mixed feelings about these three additional episodes.

On one hand, there are those who want a bit more closure and have come to love the characters. These specials could be just what they need, as the episodes delve further into some of the people from the main anime and attempt to tie up a few loose ends.

On the other hand, you have people like me, who enjoy Baccano! because of its seeming ambiguity and lack of a beginning and end. To me, it is not the actual content of the story or the characters that stand out in the original anime, it is the structure of said story and the way it’s broken down and put together. It’s about what that structure says about the nature of stories and storytelling. Because of this, I’m okay with the characters originally being, at best, functional yet distinctive. I don’t mind that they don’t have backstories, as that would just slow down the pace of the anime, especially since it’s just 13 episodes. The introduction of the specials creates, I think, a certain tonal disjointedness.

The bottom line is I see the main anime as a finished product and the specials as an addendum of sorts. To me, they’re an addendum that’s unnecessary and does not fit in with the nature of the original anime. But to some, they’re an addendum that has added value. Where you lie in this spectrum is up to you.

Expanding on its highly praised and successful prequel, Baccano! Specials give us something new but still remain quite as nostalgic and familiar, satisfying our hunger for knowing more about the beloved rich characters and making even more sense of the prequel. What we wanted from a sequel was delivered in a mere 3 episodes (approximately 30 minutes each) of content, and nobody's complaining.

- Story (8.0/10):
These animations tend to dive more into the characters, giving them more depth and backstories, driving their motives out, and even giving them more treats and characteristics we never knew they had.
The specials follow the same path as their ancestor, jumping back and forth between timelines and places, but this time around as we already know the parent story, it's not as hard to keep up with, the thing that made it really good to invest in.
In addition to the main timelines, 1711, 1930, 1931, and 1932, other time periods were added in a purpose of presenting certain important and influential past events that made a lot of sense of other stuff. It's undoubtedly the pairing and ships that were the focus of this specials, while they went through telling how the couples made up after the events of the main story, while also shading some light on other initial immortals that we never got to see more about them.


- Art (7.5/10):
The animation was decent, or a better rephrase, above average, nothing to complain about. I would argue that the main show did it best with better quality, but the fast-paced moments also retained their flashiness.

- Sound (8.5/10):
They went back at it again with the perfect accent of the era, you can't help but blend in with the characters just focusing on their voice alone, the music was amazing as always, you can never get fed up with the classic jazz soundtracks that kick in every now and then.

- Character (10.0/10):
Baccano! has always managed this on itself with no backing up, the characters were always interesting and engaging, you can help but side with both the good and bad people. but with this specials, they had a lot more to them to show, there was the focus on Chane, Nice, and Jacuzzi, now she got those burn marks and how he got his tattoo and what for, stuff like this really make the interactions worth it.
There was a yet astonishing maniac introduced that compliments perfectly Ladd, it makes you wish he was present on the Flying Pussyfoot and lead the action.

- Enjoyment (9.0/10):
I enjoyed this more than I accounted for, I signed in for a sketchy watch, thinking I would just be lost in a great mess again, but it really was easy on the mind this time around, feeling like having the ultimate experience.

- Overall (8.6/10):
I bet if you enjoyed the parent series, and actually understood the story, that would be all the assurance that you have several affections to multiple characters, these specials where like an expansion pack with the greatest features you could think off to your favorite game, why miss on it if you have the time and energy?

There is no difference. There is only... a DVD!

The Baccano! specials should be considered part of the original anime. Seemingly, the only reason why these three episodes were moved onto DVD is because anime episodes always come in multiples of 13.

Actually, my first sentence is a lie. There is a difference... the plot has been mostly explicated in full, so the viewer can now follow the jumbled story-telling style of Baccano!. Whereas with the first episodes, viewers are likely to get confused by the ruckus of unknown events and unknown characters. Now, everything is clear.

Therefore, I enjoyed the last three episodes of Baccano more than the first three. If anything, watching Baccano! is much like a jigsaw puzzle: At first, putting all the pieces together is a frustrating task. However, when the last pieces are fit into place, looking at the complete picture is quite fulfilling.
