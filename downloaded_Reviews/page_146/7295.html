What's up with the title? For starters, most of the anime takes place during the day. And the only reference to anything with three sides is the trio of men with paranormal abilities.

Story: Kosuke Mikado—or Mr. Triangle, since his name is written as "triangle"—has the ability to see ghosts. Meanwhile, Rihito Hiyakawa has the ability to hear and exorcise them through a medium. Obviously, Mikado is that medium, so the two team up to track down a girl named Erika Hiura who curses others. 2/10

Art: Mediocre at best. Even the paranormal stuff doesn't stand out. Madoka Magica and Vanitas no Carte both show wonderful, creepy worlds through their art, but this anime fails to emphasize the horror of what Mikado and the cursed victims see. 3/10

Sound: Subtle and eerie, doing a far better job than the art to create the atmosphere. 6/10

Character: None of the characters are compelling. Mikado is called out for letting Hiyakawa use him, and Hiyakawa isn't attractive enough—either by appearance or character—to justify Mikado submitting to him. Keita Mukae, a fake fortune teller, is pretty cool, though. 4/10

Enjoyment: The erotic exorcisms were entertaining... the first couple times. Mukae's comment about Mikado being on a leash suggests potential character development, but given Mikado's and Hiyakawa's personalities, that probably won't happen. 4/10

Overall: I don't recommend this anime. It's an interesting premise, with two men teaming up on paranormal mysteries and falling in love, but it has poor execution. There are a lot of better BL anime out there. 4/10

As a fan of the manga who loves it very, very much, I'm honestly rather disappointed by the adaptation. Yes, only 4 episodes have aired, so I am hoping my enjoyment will increase since I know the plot will eventually pick up.

Story: 8
Obviously I'm biased because I read the manga so I appreciate the plot more than others and know what is to come. The general premise as mentioned by others is that two guys go around and do exorcisms and are also in the process of trying to help a detective solve a long-running murder case. There is a large element of BL in the way the exorcisms are conducted, and in how submissive Mikado is to Hiyakawa in all elements of their "relationship". I admit that I myself wasn't too into the story around this part of the manga and didn't really know what direction the plot was going in, but I know very well it will get better, thus why I give a generous 8. I will update if I don't feel the anime does the entire story justice.

Art: 5
Honestly, was expecting a lot better. I loved the art in the manga and the anime just does not cut it. It seems cheap and just...off in a way. I hate to be so critical but I'm just so disappointed. The creepy, dark moments in the manga are drawn beautifully but the anime is just not as effective.

Sound: 8
Nothing major to say, I overall think the OST is quite good and fits the tone very well. Definitely a highlight of the anime. Not too big of a fan of the opening, but I think the ending is great. Really good choice of voice actors too.

Character: 6.5
At this point of the anime, the two leads definitely don't seem too remarkable. I don't know what it is about the anime, but I don't enjoy Hiyakwa's character as much as in the manga. He comes off as controlling, creepy, and a little too weird. Mikado is very submissive and sometimes it's a little ridiculous to see how easily he goes along with what Hiyakawa says. I know that their characters will become better once their backstories are delved into, and I really hope the anime does a good job on that.

Enjoyment: 8
I'm generous with this because seeing it animated makes me happy nonetheless, but man do I wish it were better executed. My main issue is the pacing being wayyy too fast. I get that they only have 12 episodes but it's clear that they want to cram all 10 volumes into the season and I just don't think it's working that well. I think the characters need more time to expand upon their chemistry together and to flesh out the world in general. It just feels like you're thrust right into it all and there's just one thing after another happening so fast. But, it is still entertaining and interesting to watch. If I was unaware of the story, I would be quite curious to see what is going to happen next.

Overall: 7
If more time, care, and money were put into the anime, I feel like it could have been soooo much better than what we got. It's decent overall and it's nice to have a BL anime that has an interesting supernatural plot. But...it genuinely feels lacking. I hope that it will become better as it goes on, and will for sure update this review if I believe it does. Or if it gets worse. In general, at this point I wouldn't really recommend it to anyone unless you've read the manga or just enjoy watching any and all types of BL.

For shows like these, no matter the concept and story telling. If you're going to have a duo lead the line, then you best make sure that they mesh well together, and can really push the show forward. In this case, it falls flat and misses so many marks. If the characters don't mesh together, or the chemistry is seriously lacking. Then certain scenes are going to be left awkward, and uninteresting by default. If you wanna see a better driven BL kind of show, then you are in the wrong place, and it would be better to watch something else.

Now that's not to say the show is a complete awful mess. When it really isn't. The art and animation that goes along with it, is one of it's more stronger points. Even if it is let down from the story, that makes it less tense, and honestly, it doesn't give off any tense feeling at all. Imagine a Saw Movie, and in the middle of it, they just suddenly went and made flirting on one another in a sunny setting. It feels out of place. It works for some shows, but for this, it doesn't work at all. But I will have to give props to some very creative and interesting backgrounds, and how the spooky stuff looks overall.

The sounds for this show aren't anything to scream at, but they do their job in each and every setting. Which is not only what you expect within an anime, but what should be within an anime. Can't say anything more about that.

As for the characters themselves? We have Kosuke Mikado, who has the ability to see ghosts. Meanwhile, Rihito Hiyakawa has the ability to hear and exorcise them through a medium. Which of course is Mikado. As I've already spoken about through this review, is that the characters don't really mesh well. It's as if one sees it as they were destined to be partners, while the other disagrees kind of vibes. Sure, it could get better. But it just feels poor. Just take the first episode for instance. Of how weird and awkward it seems through their interactions. It's...Something lmao.

I might be a bit too harsh on this, as I do find that it does have an interesting premise, but it just lacks of certain emotions, that other shows do so much better. Even a show like Pet had a bit of BL, but that had more tension then this does. Be careful with this, I'd give it a shot, but if it's not for you after a few episodes, then move on from it.

Ok so just for context, I haven’t read the manga and I went into this with absolutely no idea what to expect.

I won’t go as far as saying this anime is bad. I am only 4 and a half episodes in at the time of this review so to be too critical as of right now probably isn’t justified. With that being said I really only have continued watching this far because I feel that they’ve done a really great job setting up a world with potentially interesting characters, story, and supernatural abilities.

Now, to get into why I think this anime is struggling (at least as of right now). For starters, the animation is not as great as it could potentially be. I am unsure about what the supernatural abilities look like in the manga, but I would certainly hope they are more interesting than what we see in the anime. It’s not terrible, but it just seems so lackluster. I feel like they do put more effort into the characters and environment which makes for a pleasant overall viewing experience, but it’s hard to get excited about the supernatural aspect of this anime when it’s not really delivering onscreen.

Another frustrating aspect is the BL aspect of this anime. *sigh* I have seen a lot of BL and there’s nothing more frustrating than seeing two characters, set up to be love interests, only to fall into a very unhealthy relationship dynamic. I will avoid spoiling the exact details of the BL dynamic presented in this anime but in summary it presents as being unhealthy and quite uncomfortable to watch in many scenes. One character knows very little about supernatural abilities other than his natural ability and yet his counterpart seems to know an incredibly vast amount, yet never shares. It wouldn’t be that big of an issue if he didn’t frequently use his knowledge and experience to keep his partner in the dark and literally manipulate him into performing how he desires. Thankfully there hasn’t been any non consensual sexual activity depicted, however, some scenes involving the use of their supernatural abilities are very uncomfortable to watch and would most definitively be considered non consensual within the context of sexual activity.

My final critisicm would be that the characterization is really lacking so far. As I said earlier the characters themselves seem very interesting but from the perspective of someone who hasn’t read the manga, I know literally nothing about them. Despite not doing a great job of showing the nature and intentions of the characters we are observing onscreen, they somehow do a fairly good job at showing interesting conversations/moments between characters. The issue with that is that i’m left with the feeling of ‘oh that seemed really meaningful’ without really knowing why it should have felt meaningful.

I will continue watching through the end of the season because I feel like there really is a lot of potential for great things. I don’t even personally need the animation to be anything amazing as long as these characters are given some much needed backstory and development. The story really only has the potential to be great if the characters that drive it are likeable or at least intriguing to watch develop and grow so I really hope things improve.

TL;DR
Not terrible, but it is a somewhat confusing watch through so far and it does suffer from some unhealthy & very annoying BL tropes. With that being said, these issues and the story/characters have potential to improve and develop into something really cool!
