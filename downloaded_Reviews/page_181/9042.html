The best way I can describe Val x Love is this. This show wants to be a hentai, but fails at being a hentai; so it is not a hentai, even though it should be a hentai. That’s right, I honestly think this show would have been better as a hentai rather than the show we got instead. It’s a show that always feels like it’s scared to go all the way and only teases it. But that isn’t the only reason why I think this show is underwhelming and the bad kind of trashy.

Reason number 1: The show’s story feels completely forced. It felt like the show just contrived some way to justify why these girls are living with our main protagonist. So the reason being is that Odin sees our main protagonist Akustu Takuma as being the catalyst to allow his Valkyries to grow stronger and fight Akumas, daemons serving under Fenrir. This is about the only reason we get and it happens for less than a minute, in a flashback, at the end of the first episode. I can’t say really I’m convinced as it feels like the show didn’t care about explaining and just wanted to get to the juicy harem bits. But the reason just feels weak and there isn’t enough justification. It just happens and we are just meant to accept it.

Reason number 2: I can’t say I like the main protagonist Takuma. I get that with Takuma, they were trying to go with a gentle giant character. Someone who looks scary but has a kind heart but due to his social anxiety he comes across as intimidating. I’m fine with that. But when you are surrounded by girls that want to be with him, then I can’t really feel sympathy for him because he has plenty of people that care for him and talk to. So they try to play it off as comedic but for me at least, it comes across as flat.

Reason number 3: The animation is flat. For a show that is also trying to be a shounen series, this is a bad thing if the action you are portraying feels uninspired and lazy. It uses the more cheap tactics like still images and constantly cutting away to break the pacing. So they just come across as flat. Then there is the show’s censorship. Now it does do the usual censorship like hair covering the breasts but that is when they actually put effort into the censorship. The show does an inconsistent job of censorship. In a shower scene, they do a poor job when comes to using another tactic, steam, to cover the private parts as there was barely any steam; so they just don’t bother animating anything there, private or not. Now good censorship leaves the viewer using their imagination to think what’s beyond there but not having anything there leaves the scene feeling...flaccid. And you don’t want a scene like that come across as flaccid.

Honestly, the whole show has come across as flaccid. I can’t say I personally feel any sense of arousement or being humoured when watching this show even though the show tries to do that. I heard that the show was censored heavily compared to the original material which is fine if you do it right, which they don’t. I honestly would say that you should skip or drop this show. There really isn’t anything to keep you watching or to get you to watch. I will probably drop this show not long after I post this review as I already have four other shows on Saturday to watch that are more interesting than this show and I will probably be less arsed to watch this show as time goes on.

There's something about these battle harems that I find really enjoyable, but it's really hard to put my finger on what precisely it is. Maybe it's the base level titillation from the abundance of fan service. it could be the absurd and roundabout ways shows like these justify their fan service. Perhaps it's because of it's simple portrayals of characters.There's also the paper thin plot it has until it falls into the deep end and simply becomes a joke. Or just a culmination of all the above which this show more or less is.

Val x Love is kind of a monster of the week type of show with a thin coating of Norse mythology hence the Val in the title standing for Valkyries. Valkyries being the nine girls, each on fitting into a specific archetype such as the loli, kuudere, himedere, etc etc. Although the blonde haired tsundere also facilities as being the "main girl". All bounded to the main male protagonist who they must form a special/romantic bond with in order to level up like some video game character, and be more capable to fight the enemy and prevent Ragnarok(I think). The way that they bond is usually for the most part risque and mostly serve as the fan service of the show. Doing actions such as kissing, groping, bathing together, or just spending time together which also usually devolves into more fan service.

I'm not sure if there's a word to describe the kind of person that characters like the protagonist are. He's such a heavily flawed in so many ways that it comes off more artificial rather than believable. He has a somewhat scary looking physique but is mostly a wimp who is scared of human interaction. The fact that he has to have intimate contact with the Valkyries exacerbates it. The only thing he is noticeably good at is studying, and even then the slightest bit of pressure can make him panic and fail exams. It’s all done so purposeful to the point of contrivance for the purpose of having easy character development. But that’s the thing, it’s so easy that it’s down right predictable from the very first episodes. Not to mention that if they weren’t to do that, all that would be left is a rather irritating character.

The antagonists aren’t really that note worthy aside that they are also taken from parts of Norse mythology as well. I guess it's because the conflicts are more centered around the main protagonist sucking it up and kissing one of his harem in order for them to fight. Of course from the last paragraph it’s less of a conflict and more of an inevitable amount of time.

There’s probably a lot I could say about the production value, but if you know what you’re getting into when it comes to the story and characters. You probably know what you are getting into when it comes to the visuals and audio. That is to say, it is serviceable for what it is. And that’s something that can also be said about the entirety of the show. It’s a serviceable bad show that isn’t a total joke, but enough that it can be seen in an ironically enjoyable light.
