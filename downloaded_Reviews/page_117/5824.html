The anime is very enjoyable, particularly if you are a dog person, it is also a slice of life to the max, so if you do not enjoy the genre, this anime isn't for you. It is overall fun but the amount of enjoyment very much depends on the person. I am European and so have no understanding of this historical context, therefore the historical context is something the anime could attempt to explain a bit more, although the target audience is likely to be mostly Japanese. The animation is average, the opening is good, and the credits are amazing. If you're looking for a complex action-packed, story-heavy anime this is really not for you, however, if you just want to spend a fun 24 minutes enjoying an original, humour-filled, and entertaining slice of life this is for you.

To start off, its a fun anime! Its light hearted, cute as hell, and its an anime to definitely relax to.

The premise is basically warlords from feudal Japan that have long since been deceased have been reborn into a variety of animals. But mostly dogs.

All of them have long since settled into their new lives well considering they all have owners who spoil them to one degree or the other. And considering how rough their old lives could be its no wonder they fell into this one so well!

Consider having a harem of 20 and all the drama that comes with it! And also the war, can't forget the war.

Its a fun anime, nothing serious in the lightest, and it honestly didn't teach me that much in the long run. Its just a warm slice of life that can make you feel a bit better after a bad day. And me being me I needed this many many a time!

Plus the after credit endings include actual dogs! PUPPERS!!! Its really cute so I'd advise on either skipping the credits (which...at least watch it once bc those RL dogs are in it) or enjoying the credits with the end scene.

Oh my, it only took me one episode to decide that this was not worth sticking around! Look, I came here because I thought the idea was cool, the feared Nobunaga reincarnated as a dog. It looked like it would be a fine comedy and I could learn a little bit more about the history of Japan. But......the first 10 minutes of the episode already threw me off enough to completely drop out and even encouraged me to write one of the few reviews I had. First, the thing that bothered me the most of the way the first episode, just the first one, was handled. The episode looked like it was written by a ten year old and I'm not exaggerating to make a point. So if you familiar the with the plot, the episode basically does it the easiest way possible and that's it. What I mean with this is that you see Nobunaga about to commit suicide like history was told and the next scene, there he is. Yeah, the dog. Everything about this transition was just so, so wrong I'm even having trouble putting into words, but what you see is basically, after the death of the character, the next moment of the anime shows him as the titular dog already fully grown (I'd say a dog 3-5 years old) and not only that, but his mindset is almost like he remembered his life as Nobunaga from a few minutes ago. You don't see the dog growing up, you don't see him remembering little by little about his previous life. He suggest as a punchline, before dying, that after all that he's done, he may as well be reborn as a dog, and the next minute hes already in the body of a grown dog, with memories fresh like it happened yesterday and for some bizarre reason, his owner appears right after, and he reacts like a lot of domesticated dogs would react, but now that he's realizing the way he react is that of a dog. Makes me wonder, what the hell happened here? Did he just now realized that he had a previous life as a human being and even though he tries, he cannot deny his animal instincts?!?! This was so poorly done that only after seeing people can fully comprehend. That was the first major issue I had with the show which made me realize this would be as lazy as it could get; The second one is a little more complicated but I'll try to explain. The way this reincarnated Nobunaga talks in his mind does not suggest at all that of a leader would even if this leader was a goofy one. He talks and acts in his own head like a teenager who suddenly became an adult and not only that, but an influential adult in feudal Japan and he's more concerned with his self-image as Nobunaga than with the actions that Nobunaga took that made him what he was. It doesn't speak "grown mature man" to me at all, it only contributes to make the first minutes of this show look a lot more like a four panel comic joke rather than a story and despite the fact that I wanted to see where this was heading, those things alone made me sure that this wasn't well-thought at all, it didn't bother even with the most basic details and it couldn't even pass a slapstick comedy, much less a anime.

The gist of my review is this: Give this a shot!

I was very skeptical of the premise of this show. Firstly, is YET ANOTHER Nobunaga show? Second, the shtick is that they are dogs? thirdly, comedy is very hit or miss for translations.. leaning toward the miss in most cases. But, after one episode, I was left with a huge smile on my face and I was really surprised how much I enjoyed watching this show.

First, it's not another Nobunaga show. I mean, it is, but not really. It never takes the Nobunaga thing seriously. It's the butt of countless jokes and in all honesty most of them land really well even if you know nothing of Nobunaga.

Second, yes, the shtick is dogs, but they really do a great job at delivering on the cute and funny.

Thirdly, well.. like I said in the first and second points: It's funny.

It might not be Anime of the year: the animation is middling at best and there is no real story progression, it's just "Hey these warlords are now dogs, isn't that funny?" and it is!

A re-incarnation story

Alright so the entire story is that we follow japanese historical figures who got reincarnated into the current world as dogs. Of course like the shows name tells we follow Oda Nobunaga. In every episode we mostly see Nobunaga talking to the other dogs who were either his friends or enemies during the time when they were still humans. Not gonna lie but I like their chemistry together a lot. It is pretty funny.

The characters are nothing special. Like I said the dogs chemistry together is pretty funny.

The art style is quite bland and not detailed at all. Only the dogs has some effort put into them

The opening is pretty good. The visuals are okay and the music is great. The ending is cool since they got real dogs to portray three characters from the show.

It is up to you if you wanna watch this. It is okay is the comedic sense but don't expect much.
