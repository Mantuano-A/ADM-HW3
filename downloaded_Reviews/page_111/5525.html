Before reading this, know that this opinion is coming from somebody who has read the manga, and I believe that my opinion will vastly differ from someone who has never read the manga. This is probably the most disappointing anime I have seen this year. The biggest problem with this anime is that the manga was too good, to the point where I had unreasonably high expectations.

Story: 3/10
Overall, the story follows that of the manga. However, the pacing was an issue, some of the iconic scenes in the manga didn't make it into the anime. Instead, focus on that annoying little valkyrie panicking over every little thing took priority even though it was clearly unnecessary. The same for the guy sounding the horn, nobody gives a shit about him. Gets a bit boring seeing predictable reactions after a while. In the manga, it was a counstant fight -> flashback -> counter -> flashback -> counter...but in the anime there were so many annoying little interruptions that the tension/energy of the show is just gone. Flashbacks of the fights were also dragged out slightly longer than necessary whereas the actual fight scenes were erased and felt like they were over slightly quicker than in the manga.

Art: 1/10
This is one of my biggest frustrations. The manga had amazing, beautiful art. For example Lu Bu vs Thor final clash, the anime just didn't do the manga justice. Plenty of the fights had this weird powerpoint-type "animation" where it's just feels like stop-motion of a bunch of images. It was terrible. Especially for an action scene. For example the part where Thor was doing the spin after catching the hammer he's thrown, the anime had to show that spin in two different cuts (of two half-spins) whereas normally it should be expected that he does an entire spin. What the hell was that? Sometimes the character's voice can be heard and their faces are right in front of the viewers, but the mouth does not move. The finale of the fight between Zeus and Adam, most of it was just spectator reactions because for some reason they couldn't put in any fighting animations. Even the few exchange of blows were just a volley of animated colored-strikes instead of their actual arms/fists. Just read the manga and see for yourself, how the "animation" did it dirty. Words cannot describe how atrocious this "animation" is. Absolutely unacceptable. To put it into perspective, remember seven deadly sins, escanor vs meliodas fight, and how everyone agreed that the animation was kinda bad? This is worse. Way worse.

Sound: 5/10
This is probably the only redeeming factor of this anime. The soundtracks are decent enough, and the voice acting is good. Nothing stands out, but nothing to complain about either. This is the one thing where perhaps I can overlook since the manga has no sounds for me to compare it to in the first place. I don't have much to say.

Character: 5/10
Again, over-exaggeration of reactions of minor characters and onlookers. Instead, some of the more iconic scenes which portrays certain characters attitude towards the showdown are missing. For example, in the manga Zeus the old man was showing off his arrogance by strangling his pet dragon in front of the crowd when announcing the fight, this part was removed from the anime even though would have explained quite a lot. Or the scene where Loki approaches Heracles when he was going up the stairs and he explains that he is actually against ending mankind, and will always be an ally to justice. These are important character developments, especially for one of the fighters. It should not be left out and it should not just jump straight to the fight. But because that fight was left as a cliffhanger, I will give them the benefit of the doubt that they will cover that next season. Though for that to happen, some episode recap will have to be done, which is already annoying enough as is in this season. Still, most of the first 3 main characters were displayed good enough, but I just felt like more could have been done.

Enjoyment: 4/10
Not enjoyable at all. It was quite a letdown. Completely destroyed my expectations of how good the anime could have been. Partially due to the manga.

Overall: 2/10
Please just read the manga. I do not even recommend watching this anime. You will get way better art, more coherent story, better pacing, better understanding of the characters, and overall just a more pleasant time.

Ah yes. Records of boring fights. What a great anime.

I'm not usually into pure fighting anime, and I started RoR only on a whim. It started out very promising, but devolved into a boring plot that dragged on way too much.

The Council of Gods meets every 1000 years to determine the fate of mankind. After seven million years of human history, the gods decided that humanity could no longer be saved and must perish. However, Valkyrie Brunhilde proposed to give humanity one last chance to prove itself. The gods agreed to the Battle of Ragnarok, which pitted the 13 greatest figures in history and the 13 most powerful gods against each other in a duel.

As I mentioned before, I really felt like I was going to have a lot of fun with this anime at the beginning. The premise was really interesting, and I don't think anyone would object to a few spectacular battles between humans and gods. Unfortunately, that's not how it turned out.

In 12 episodes, a total of 3 fights were executed and each fight took up 4 episodes. At the end of each fight even a statistic was shown how long the fight actually lasted and I had the feeling that this statistic really reflected the actual anime duration. The average duration of a fight was between 5-10 minutes and out of a total of 4 episodes the fights took only a fraction.
This is exactly where the biggest weakness of the series lies. The rest of these episodes are filled with boring conversations between the gods or flashbacks of the respective fighters. Don't get me wrong. Learning about the past of some gods and heroes through myths and legends is very interesting, but when that takes up about 80% of an episode, it becomes very tiring to actually follow the plot.
As for the fights themselves, they are nothing too special with simple movement patterns.

I personally found the animation of the characters quite appealing and the facial expressions were very detailed. The fight scenes, on the other hand, were more sloppy than detailed. I didn't get anything from the sound for the most part and it was very unimpressive. The opening theme was not bad, but not to my taste. The Ending Theme was all the better, and I listened to it for hours afterwards.

It's an anime that didn't surprise me much. I would recommend it to anyone who likes shows like Baki or Kengan Ashura, otherwise I would rather leave it alone. I expected spectacular fights but was unfortunately disappointed.
