The waiting period between the OVA episodes were so unbearably long!!! I'll be completely honest, I am in love with the To LOVE-Ru Darkness portion of the series. I love Momo's "Harem Plan" she is trying to fulfill for Rito. Watch these OVAs as a part of the TV series and not just "extra" episodes.

Note : My score represents the To LOVE-Ru Darkness series as a whole.

Story (8) To LOVE-Ru Darkness strays its focus from Lala and Haruna towards Momo, Mea, and Konjiki no Yami (Golden Darkness). It is wrapped around Momo's plan to get all of the heroines to be with Rito, ALL AT THE SAME TIME...!!! How lucky is Rito to attract all of these beautiful girls just by acting himself? All I can say is..."I wish I was him!" Momo makes an appearance in basically every single episode where she tries to come up with a plan for her harem plan to be successful. All of the events tie back into her master plan. In a nutshell, what I want to say is that there is more to this anime than it just being your typical ecchi harem series.

Art (11) Yes, I just rated it an 11/10. I absolutely love the animation! Kentaro Yabuki's character illustrations are just so aesthetically pleasing. All the characters are beautiful and have their own charms. He has actually inspired me to start drawing (look on my profile for some of my works).

Sound (9) Does anybody else love the opening of To LOVE-Ru Darkness? "Saikou no yume wo isshou ni mimashou" from the opening of the anime translates to "let's watch the best dream(s) together." Just from the opening song, I get a sense of just how beautiful this show really is. Aside from the opening song, I enjoyed the mix of heavy breathing and screams from the heroines ;).

Characters (10) Darkness mainly revolves around the interactions between Rito and Yami, Mea, and Momo. If you are a fan of either Lala or Haruna, then you probably didn't enjoy Darkness as much as the first two seasons. Momo is one of my favorite characters of anime, period. Not many girls are willing to lead a harem plan for the guy they love. I love how every single heroine expresses their love for Rito in their own unique way!

Enjoyment (10) I do enjoy the ecchi genre, and To LOVE-Ru is at the pinnacle of it. When I think of ecchi, I think of To LOVE-Ru. Be prepared with a box of tissues (I know what you guys were thinking...Not for fapping! But for the nosebleeds that you will get from the extreme ecchi scenes in the series).

Overall (10) If you are looking for an ecchi anime, then by all means watch To LOVE-Ru Darkness. If you are looking for an ecchi anime with an actual story, then PLEASE watch To LOVE-Ru Darkness.

When it comes to OVAs, this is probably as common, unoriginal and possibly repetitive as it can get. It features a load of fan-service, awkward situations and of course a lot of nudity, as you'd expect from To LOVE-Ru. Not that it's not enjoyable, but it's not for everyone, that's for sure. As for myself, I enjoyed it quite a bit just for the funny and awkward scenes, but once again, it's focused mostly on awkward scenes that involve fan-service. And a lot of it.

The "To LOVE-Ru Darkness OVA" episodes follow the same formula as the Motto season, meaning that each OVA is split into a few "Mini Episodes", the biggest difference being that these episodes are not related to the main story at all. The episodes are however related to chapters from the manga, but from what I can tell (not having read the manga) they don't affect the main story in the slightest. This means that not having watched this won't make a difference, other than perhaps the "Prologue" episode, which covers a bit from before the Darkness season. It really involves our main cast getting into a lot of weird, funny and awkward moments without any plot or real character development.

The art itself is of course the same as the actual series, so in overall pretty good, and it being an OVA, you won't be pestered by steam clouds and whatnot. The same goes for the music, the soundtrack and sounds overall are all pretty much from the main show. The only difference is that it focuses more on the ecchi parts, but what do you expect from a side OVA.

In conclusion, if you're looking for a bit extra after having watched the anime itself, it's quite the amount of fun. However, don't watch this until you've watched the Darkness season, it will spoil it for you if you haven't yet. The characters are still enjoyable, the art and sound is just the same as the show, the story is pretty much non-existant other than the "stories" the mini-episodes have, and it's overall still fun to watch. It is just as ecchi, if not even more than the actual anime, so be careful when watching in a public area.

PS: "Borderline hentai" anime takes it far further than this anime will ever do. Don't worry about it too much, just wear a decent pair of headphones and you're golden.
