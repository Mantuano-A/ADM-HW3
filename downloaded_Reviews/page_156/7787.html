*Spoiler-free Review*
Biggest disgrace to this series anybody could have imagine.

So it's 2019. Avex pictures(The parental company of âge,Muv-luv VN maker studio) announces MLA anime. Fans got mixed opinions. "Why it's started from alternative and not from extra or unlimited?" Avex pictures staff answer: "To bring in more people into Muv-luv. It would've been boring for most of anime onlies to start from the extra, because it's a calm stakeless romcom from 2001. It should've been adapted in 2000s, tbh. Supposedly they were hesitant to start adaptating ML triology, because it would've hindered people from buying the VNs and required a good amount of money. Which was a bad take imho. You ask why? Mechs play a great part into ML, at 2000s, they had a handful of quality options available to adapt a mecha series. Gainax, sunrise, even xebec. They all were in their peaks, mecha wasn't as niche as it is in 2021, when even sunrise isn't that promising of a studio and handling a ton of their projects into the parental company (bandai namco).

In 2021, They finally announced that yumeta company(most famous work? A digimon movie) and graphinica(In charge of ssss. CGI) will be adapting this. This one smelled extremely hit-or-miss adaptation from the get-go, mostly on the miss side of the things.

First episode, being stand-alone, was kinda good-ish. It portrayed the atmosphere of the dire situation the japanese were facing rather well. It was also rather digestable for new people. They had the same experience with the first episode of AoT(which its author, isayama, is a big MLA fanboy). It was also the only episode that Kouki yoshimune(the author) had direct influence in making it. VN readers were ready to see the first moments of their "Dripless" shirogane takeru after this.

Second episode is out. First thing that bothered people was bad animation. Proportions of the bodies, lazy drawings(especially in long-shot situations), weird positioning of limbs and body parts were irksome tbh. There's a good reason why shows like Kimetsu no yaiba and Jujutsu kaisen are massively popular nowadays. When their stories are digestable enough for people to get into, their top-tier animation quality will invite you to a festival of beauty. Look no further from this season, takt.op destiny is massively popular. Its flashy animation is the first thing when you ask about that show's advantages. Dear people from Avex pictures, when you know animation is the first thing people will judge your show, why did you ridiculed youselves and hired this studio? This animation level isn't even acceptable by the standards of 2000s anime.

Another issue that this show suffers from is the pacing. Some genius must've thought that rushing through events to get into the sick parts will make people care about the franchise. Bad news for ya. They adapted the manga and even cut through its content as well. VN as a medium, has the advantage of enough time to flesh out the situations and characters masterfully. Anime simply doesn't allow that if you don't want to invest a lot of money and episodes. Muv-luv, as a franchise, looks the most generic when you see it from a surface PoV. Now when they rushed through a rushed adaptation of the VN, Basically most characters other than takeru were made into cardboard cutouts. The new viewer doesn't have the slightest of clue to care about them. When the build-ups from the former titles and the journey that evolved takeru from the airheaded boy who takes the current situation for granted is cut-out from the narrative and basically shown as a quick slide-show, even the reason the viewer must care about takeru is vanished. That must be the reason why kouki yoshimune, the creator of this series is constantly dropping youtube links about "supplementary materials" for the anime onlies. He clearly ISN'T happy with the way this adaptation is going on, since he also lowkey complained about the situation this anime is on his streams. But why they took this questionable method? Budget issues? Understandable, have a nice day.

Sound is mostly exquisite. OP and ED are less relevant to the story, mostly ED, but OSTs from evan call were nice and pleasing. The VA performances were ok, except the takeru's VA (which was needed more experience to take this role).

So dear people in Avex pictures, Is this how you invest new people in your franchise? You basically took away the slightest reason for an anime only for care about anything. First impression (which is the animation) is nowhere to be seen. Narrative is just blazing through details, which those interactions and infos could've added subtle and powerful touches to the story. If you wanted to advertise your franchise, this low-effort, low-budget adaptation isn't the solution. This adaptation is simply became an "unwanted" one both by anime onlies, and VN veterans. All I can say is Avex pictures basically missed the opportunities were on their way and now they're trying to fix it by this pathetic excuse of an adaptation.

Muv Luv Alternative is the adaptation of one of the most acclaimed visual novels in the west as you may have already read a million times.
It is also a terrible attempt to cram an story made to be told in 80 hours into 4 hours of anime, it fails not only as an introduction by having a lightning pacing with poor exposition, but by having a low production quality and poor direction, meaning that if you want to relive the scenes from the game it would be a better option to just download the game and select the chapter you want.
However I must say that the first episode of this anime was good, it was everything I wish the show was: An introduction of the huge Muv Luv universe that showed unexplored characters and battles that happened off screen in the visual novel, but instead what we are getting is a show that fails to both appeal to old fans and make new people interested since everything happens too fast and there are only crumbs of good animation and direction, outside of the first episode the only department where I'm confident this anime will deliver and has delivered up until now is the mecha action and the CGI department absolutely deserves to be praised.
It is not a question of not being loyal to the source material, the problem is that trying to adapt it would be an impossible mission with their resources, if this show tried to tell an isolated story or an spin-off it would surely be much better.
I'd recommend that you only watch the first episode and then do something else, so in case you want to have the Muv Luv experience but you don`t want to go through 80 hours of powerpoint presentations I will give two recommendations that are literally the inspirations for Muv Luv and will probably take less time to watch, those are:
Gunbuster (1988): A six episode mecha/alien anime that follows a similar plot structure to Alternative, it is also shorter, much better produced and a timeless classic made by Hideaki Anno with an almost perfect pacing.
Starship Troopers (1997): A 129 minutes sci-fi book adaptation that was made by a director that hated the book, it has a nice production for the time and cool sci-fi shenanigans but it is above all a parody and a criticism of fascism, militarism and the pointless wars those two things promote.

As someone who knows not one single thing about Muv-Luv, when I saw this Anime I thought to myself what the hell kinda name is muv-luv!? Piqued my interest...

Then, the first episode blew me away. Maybe I was just feeling sappy or patriotic at the time, but the music, the dialogue, the voice acting, the scenes and sensation of warriors fighting for our species very survival against a ghastly and disgusting alien horde in badass overbuilt mecha... whoosh. Got something in my eye over here, muv-luv!

I've been watching ever since mainly because that first episode cut deep through my callused amygdala and really made me feel like this would be a grand war epic full of desparation and marvelous large-scale mecha battles against aliens. Alas, we must always cynically remind ourselves of trash shows from the past that had a banger first episode and couldn't maintain. We all know the feeling, and unfortunately Muv-Luv Alternative is definitely heading down that road with wild abandon. The production quality dropped in every way, we've been getting an increasing quanitity of pretty subpar slice of life stuff, and the story seems to stagnate more and more with each episode. Frankly I feel hard jebaited with Muv-Luv. But... I am also hesitant to write Muv-Luv Alternative off completely. The first episode really was that good.

But in episodes 2-5, we've put up with a strange, uneventful "power-up" phase that doesn't excite me at all. For some reason, we end up following this weird multiverse hitchhiker guy that literally gatecrashes his way into a (harem) military unit in the Japanese mecha force that cooperates with the UN to fight the aliens. Periodically, he visits the hot researcher that initially let him into the top-secret military base on a whim, who herself is also trying to save humanity. How, you ask? A tiny child with the brain capacity to compute the solution to humanity's predicament, or something like that - like an omnipotent loli sherlock holmes with aspergers. So these two geniuses together in a room hatch magnificent strategems such as secretly reprogramming the mechas to execute melee range combos and cancels "like in a video game". Certainly, no one has tried that my dear watson! Oh boy.

Anyways loli sherlock holmes is cute, and hopefully she can come up with something better than just shuttling a handful of people off planet to gtfo the solar system before the aliens kill us all - the future that our multiversal-man MC is ostensibly trying to avoid from his original verse. That is the sorry state humanity is in, it's just too bad we can't have a show based around THAT. Cause THAT sounds interesting, in the same way that episode 1 was interesting, because it was all about THAT.

I have nothing but questions, and I find the answers lackluster. There is obviously something here, as evidenced by the first episode... but we're getting into weird shots of this guy building relationships with alternate universe versions of girls he used to know in his home universe where the Aliens already wiped us out. Really it's devolving quickly into a slice of life harem Anime with cute military girls that are tusenderes to the last, at least that's what it feels like. So I wanted to let ppl know 5 episodes in we've descended from somewhere in the 8-9 territory to a 5-6, which is basically pure hopium points on top of the 3-4 it really deserves for bungling such a terrific head start. I will leave the rating in the middle for now, and I advise everyone to watch the first episode at least. But the rest is really not worth your time so far.

Great name though! Muv-luv... haha.

Disclaimer: I have played the VN and am a big fan of Muv Luv but I will approach reviewing this show with as little as bias as possible while giving general context to the franchise (NO SPOILERS).

Story:

Episode one gives us a gist of what's going on with the world, main characters aren't introduced yet and we get to see some original characters.

I think they framed the vibe of what Muv Luv Alternative is well in a single episode: Despair and desperation. It may not be as emotionally impacting because we don't really know these characters but the theme is there until the very last second of the episode (see post credits!).

It's great that they broke down the maps, logistics, and units early on. You will enjoy paying attention to the locations of humanity's defenses as well as BETA hives. If you paid attention, you'll remember in ep 1 they were caught by surprised because of their poor intel.

In any battlefield, you always need to be aware of some basic forms of information:

Size, Location, Unit, Time, and Equipment.

These were presented seamlessly in ep 1 by breaking down the BETA size per wave, what type of BETA were part of the wave (Unit), where they're at and direction, and as for equipment that would be the same as unit since each type of BETA has specific capabilities and weaknesses.

These gives the defending command to use the intel and coordinate their logistics to engage the enemy. You'll see very well how they know they're (humans) own capabilities as well as the enemies' which makes them weary of their logistical constraints in case the perceived threat overcomes their arsenal. No power of friendship and flash backs can save you all the time in the Muv Luv verse, either you have some odds of winning or you don't, everything else just shifts the chances of success not create it.

Art:

I am pleasantly surprised by the Art. Nothing groundbreaking or top tier I would say (the walls look fucc) but it doesn't hurt my eyes. Everything looks crispy enough and looks like it belongs 2020+. It's not KyoAni or Mad House but it shouldn't be a detrimental to the viewer's experience (fingers crossed. I hope this ages well)[pun not intended].

Sound:

It's not too hype, haven't seen the OP or ED yet, but the choice of music in the end of the episode gave me some nice chills which leads me to believe they are paying good attention to the emotional impact of the story, and that's what I'm here for.

Character:

Not much characterization out yet but it's pretty standard. The first ep was focused on laying out the groundwork for the universe instead of the cast. This part of my review may be revised once I see more.

Enjoyment:

I watched the first episode completely expecting a train wreck and now I'm slightly hopeful this will work out. Of course, in true Muv Luv fashion, I always expect that
naïve hopefulness to be crushed but I'll be watching each episode as it releases. I AM HYPED>

Overall:

Even if you're not familiar with the VNs, I'd say this is worth watching this season. Coupled with 86, you should get some good mecha-military vibes. While 86 is what I consider a good show, I'd say Muv Luv is vastly superior just because of how detailed it breaks down it's logistics, capabilities of each actor in the battlefield, and the overall grand campaign it wages.

Mandatory mention of Attack of Titan which was inspired by Muv Luv Alternative a lot. If you think for a second that MLA is copying anything that was aired in the past 10 years, just keep in mind this has been around since 2003 so it may be the other way around.

Anyway, don't expect too much out of it, but give it the chance to be part of your weekly views. I'll watch this to the very end even if it ends up being a pile of shit—I'll sink with this ship. Here's to Unit A-01 o7.

As I have played the visual novel trilogy some years ago my judgment will be biased towards the positive I believe. This review does not contain any spoilers.

A bit of a TLDR and comparison to the original works,
If you haven't played the games this adaptation is based on it attempts to cram a 95 hour long VN trilogy into most likely 4 hours. This is not a good idea IMO.

The consequences of compressing a trilogy to 4% of its play time is the story skipping most stuff while being a constant info dump for most of the beginning episodes. Combining this with absolute horrendous to okay 2D art will result in an anime that seems to have no redeeming qualities. Even as a person who has read the trilogy the pace of the anime is nauseating during certain episodes and the budget seems to have gone completely to the action scenes, which currently are a mix between beautiful 3D CG and high quality 2D art.

I would really not recommend it when compared to the original trilogy but it might be watchable as stand-alone anime even if it is going at break neck speed thought the story. I personally really like this anime as I am watching this show with a huge smile on my face because it brings back memories of the trilogy.
End of TLDR

If I were to watch it without any understanding of the source material, the 2D art quality and rushed story would make me question if it was worth watching and the action scenes (even if there only is a few as of episode 4) and characters would make it worth watching.

2D art:
For certain wide-shots they will basically use CTRL and + to zoom in on faces without redrawing. This would perhaps not be such a big problem if not for the awful quality in some wide-shots.
The quality of the faces can sometimes be good but most of the time it is below what would be expected of an anime released today, especially the placement of facial features.
I would say that about 75% of the time the 2D art is good or acceptable and the rest is laughably bad.
In action scenes the 2D part is well made with the exception of some character shots.

3D CG:
As it is mostly used in action scenes where the budget seems to have gone I would say it is some of the best 3D CG combined with 2D that I have seen in anime as of now. (Excluding the first episodes background for certain parts)

Story:
The story is at least in the first episodes an info dump with flashbacks. I seems like it would be hard to follow thanks to the constant new information being given to the viewer that could possibly be seen as random. Though I could be wrong as I might be comparing it to when I read the trilogy.

Voice acting:
I have no problem with the voice acting, seems to be normal for an anime released today.

Sound and BG:
I really like the sound and BG for the anime as it fit's very well with what is happening on screen. It is not as bombastic as modern shōnen anime but it fit's the theme of this show perfectly.

OP and ED:
The OP is enjoyable and contains a nice preview and hints for the show while the ED gives more background to the characters.

Summary,
A horrendous adaptation but it could be enjoyable as a character driven mecha show especially if they fix the animation problem with a BD release and pull-back on info dumps by giving the story more time to develop naturally.
