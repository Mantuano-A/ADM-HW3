First of all, every negative comment about this hidden gem is quite undeserved, IMHO. This is an anime to remember, not forget, as Lunar-Anime mistranslated.

You should pay a lot of attention to those little -albeit very important- details about the story, because there's definitely a story and you have to use both logic and imagination to figure it out as you watch each episode. Having said that, I truly recommend this anime to everyone that is willing to appreciate a good suspense and mysterious drama with romance and comedy.

The director for this series is Yousei Morino, the alter-ego to Shigenori Kagayama, in what I'd call his 'good' side, because unlike all the ecchi-nonsensical series he's made, Kakyuusei 2 stands, with Pia Carrot 2DX and -to a lesser extent- ONE ~Kagayatsu kisetsu he~ (he could only do so much with just 4 episodes), as his best works, all of those he not only directs but screenplays.

The animation ARMS threw together is tolerable, the character designs are quite appealing but beware of the QUALITY because is in almost every episode (this was a low-budget show made in 2004). Nonetheless, the story grows on you as does the music (Repetitive? Yes, but it does the job of keeping the suspense/mystery going throughout the whole series).

Overall, it kinda grew more on me than I thought it would, so I fully recommend it.

This anime starts with a hook. Without giving anything away, it leads you to believe a major event occurs somewhere in the story and it instantly pricks your interest. It suggests the characters formed a unique bond over some event and you can't wait to find out what it is. And so it begins. You sit and you watch; and you wait. Initially nothing happens then a character appears who makes you extremely curious. Who is he? Why is he there? What is he? It's all very mysterious. So far so good, right? Oh how I wish I knew what was to come.

The anime then goes through all thirteen episodes with very little happening. Scenes are repeated ad nauseum and your nostrils will start to twitch. It does however, keep harping back to the hook and you feel you have to keep going because something big is going to happen sooner or later. Alas, it does not. The hook turns out to be merely something that was suggested in passing; and the mysterious character? The attitude is that he is what he is, get over it. He's barely even explained. No major event, nothing. It comes to an end and you're left sitting there with your jaw on your lap, insulted to the core of your being by a story that promised to go clubbing with you all night then went to bed at 9pm with a cup of warm milk instead. It's like waiting all day for the Olympic 100 metres final, only when the moment arrives, the gun goes off to start the race and all the runners slowly get out of the blocks then go home for tea and cake. You feel like you've been conned. If you want to avoid that feeling, avoid this anime at all costs.

This is one of the worst animes I have watched. That's saying a lot since I enjoyed In Another World With My Smartphone greatly.

Story 1/10
I don't even know what the story was even supposed to be about. It's doesn't even follow it's source material. I went into this after watching it's impressive predecessor Kakyuusei TV series from 1999 and was greatly disappointed by it. They like to throw in some unrelated "sad" stories out of nowhere for no reason which also has no relation to the main story and want use to be sad and think it makes sense.

Art 1/10
The art was a quartar decent. The characters have too much angles, too thin, which in term makes them look tall, and overall, kinda look bad, and I really liked Code Geass character designs.

Sound 1/10
The op and ed was pretty bad and even among the soundtracks, there was nothing actually good. Plus the voice acting was just bland and terrible. There was a character who was voiced by a VA who clearly has no experience and has no idea what she was doing. I even wondered if she paid her way into it or used some sort of connections. From the research I did, this was her first and last role. And even the main characters are voiced by a bunch of no names who never really had or ever had any main roles apart from some hentai. And I have no idea what sort of brains decided to use a female VA with a very womanly voice to voice a male character.

Character 1/10
The characters are just unbearable. They showed little to no development over the series. And I had a hard time finding out who was supposed to be the main character. Traditionally, the MC would be Oriya Rouma, since he was the MC in the VN. Then they throw in that otherworldly character Kishida Shun who came out of nowhere for no reason and confuse the hell out of me. And all the other male and female characters are very forgettable. I still can't remember any of the character names and I can remember all the character names from the previous Kakyuusei sereis, Highschool DxD and other similar ones, so that's saying a lot.

Enjoyment 1/10
Every episode of this anime is just unbearable and just had me thinking wtf is this shit. I had little to no enjoyment watching this. That's not even the problem of the source material since a lot of the stuff in here seems to be some original stuff made up by dumb story writers and directors.

Overall 1/10
This anime was an absolute crap and an absolute waste of my time. Even a hentai would have much better story than this. There's absolutely no reason to watch this. If you watched 1999's Kakyuusei and plan to continue into this, I suggest don't. You would be greatly disappointed. It's like some waste of ground and food idiot just played the game one day and thought "I want to be in this story but not as the MC but as a different mysterious character that knows the girls and that the girls like for no reason".
There are plenty of other animes with similar themes to this but are actually 100 or 1000 times better, like this one's predecessor Kakyuusei 1999 TV series, or Da Capo. I suggest you go watch those.
